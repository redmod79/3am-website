    Topics Research¶

    Question¶

    What is the literary structure of Revelation 12-14 and how does the placement of the three angels' messages within this macro-structure determine their meaning? What comes before (Rev 12's cosmic conflict, Rev 13's two beasts), what comes after (Rev 14:13-20's harvest), and how does the Lamb-on-Zion unit (14:1-5) frame the messages? How does Rev 11:19 (the ark of the covenant revealed) open the entire sequence? Are the three angels literal angels or symbolic movements — what does the apocalyptic genre require?

    ANGEL (a spirit) — FUNCTIONS OF¶

    • Rev 14:6
    • Rev 14:8
    • Rev 14:9
    • Rev 12:7
    • Rev 8:2
    • Mat 13:39
    • Mat 25:31
    • Heb 1:14
    • Rev 1:20

    ANGEL (a spirit) — FUNCTIONS OF / APPEARANCES. Angels serve as messengers (Rev 14:6-9), warriors (Rev 12:7), judgment executors (Rev 8:2, Mat 13:39-41), attendants at the Second Coming (Mat 25:31), and ministering spirits (Heb 1:14). In Revelation the seven stars are the angels of the seven churches (Rev 1:20). Nave's lists approximately 80 verse references for angel functions and appearances.

    ARK — IN THE TABERNACLE¶

    • Exo 25:10-22
    • Exo 25:16
    • Exo 25:21
    • Deu 10:5
    • 1 Ki 8:9
    • Rev 11:19
    • Heb 9:4

    ARK — IN THE TABERNACLE. Made of shittim wood overlaid with gold (Exo 25:10-11). Contains the testimony/law (Exo 25:16,21; Deu 10:5; 1 Ki 8:9). Mercy seat with cherubim on top (Exo 25:17-22). God meets with Israel from above the mercy seat (Exo 25:22). Seen in heavenly temple (Rev 11:19). Nave's lists approximately 50 verse references.

    • John 1:29
    • John 1:36
    • Rev 5:6
    • Rev 5:12
    • Rev 14:1
    • Rev 14:4
    • Rev 14:10

    LAMB OF GOD — GENERAL. John the Baptist identifies Jesus (John 1:29,36). In Revelation: the Lamb enthroned (Rev 5:6), worthy of worship (Rev 5:12), on Mount Zion with 144,000 (Rev 14:1,4), present at judgment (Rev 14:10). Nave's lists approximately 18 verse references.

    WORSHIP — TO BE RENDERED ONLY TO GOD¶

    • Exo 20:3-5
    • Deu 6:13
    • Mat 4:10
    • Rev 4:10-11
    • Rev 14:7
    • Rev 13:4
    • Rev 13:12
    • Rev 13:15
    • Rev 14:9
    • Rev 14:11

    WORSHIP — TO BE RENDERED ONLY TO GOD. Central theme in Rev 12-14 conflict: true worship of Creator (Rev 14:7, cf. Exo 20:11) vs. beast worship (Rev 13:4,12,15). Third angel warns against worshiping the beast (Rev 14:9,11). Nave's lists approximately 60 verse references.

    COVENANT — OF GOD WITH MEN¶

    • Gen 9:9-17
    • Gen 15:18
    • Exo 19:5
    • Exo 24:7-8
    • Exo 25:16
    • Deu 10:5
    • Rev 11:19

    COVENANT — OF GOD WITH MEN. The ark of the covenant contains the Ten Commandments (Exo 25:16; Deu 10:5). In Rev 11:19, the ark of his testament (diatheke) is seen in the heavenly temple, reopening the covenant-law theme that threads through 12:17 and 14:12. Nave's lists approximately 40 verse references.

    • Rev 14:7
    • Rev 20:12

    JUDGMENT — THE GENERAL. The hour of his judgment is come (Rev 14:7). God shall judge the secrets of men (Rom 2:16). Every idle word accountable (Mat 12:36). Books opened (Rev 20:12). Nave's lists approximately 50 verse references.

    ZION — GENERAL¶

    • Psa 2:6
    • Psa 48:2
    • Isa 2:3
    • Heb 12:22
    • Rev 14:1

    ZION — GENERAL. Called the city of David (2 Sam 5:7). God's holy hill (Psa 2:6). In Heb 12:22 Mount Sion = the heavenly Jerusalem. Rev 14:1: the Lamb stood on Mount Sion with 144,000. Nave's lists approximately 25 verse references.

    TEMPLE — HEAVENLY¶

    • Rev 3:12
    • Rev 7:15
    • Rev 11:19
    • Rev 14:15
    • Rev 14:17
    • Rev 15:5-8
    • Rev 16:1

    TEMPLE — HEAVENLY. The temple of God opened in heaven (Rev 11:19). Angels come out of the temple (Rev 14:15,17; 15:6). Temple of the tabernacle of the testimony opened (Rev 15:5). Temple filled with smoke, none able to enter (Rev 15:8). Nave's lists approximately 70 verse references for temple topics.

    SATAN — GENERAL¶

    • Gen 3:1-15
    • Isa 14:12-14
    • Ezk 28:12-17
    • Mat 4:1-11
    • Rev 12:7-9
    • Rev 20:2

    SATAN — GENERAL. Identified as the serpent of Eden (Gen 3:1-15; Rev 12:9; 20:2). Fall narrative (Isa 14:12-14). Cast out of heaven (Rev 12:7-9). Accuser cast down (Rev 12:10). Dragon, that old serpent, called the Devil and Satan (Rev 12:9; 20:2). Nave's lists approximately 45 verse references.

    SERPENT — GENERAL¶

    • Gen 3:1-15
    • Num 21:6-9
    • Isa 27:1
    • Rev 12:9
    • Rev 12:14-15
    • Rev 20:2

    SERPENT — GENERAL. The serpent in Eden (Gen 3:1-15). Leviathan the piercing serpent (Isa 27:1). In Revelation: that old serpent = the dragon = the Devil and Satan (Rev 12:9; 20:2). The serpent cast water as a flood after the woman (Rev 12:15). Nave's lists approximately 20 verse references.

    • Mark 16:15

    GOSPEL — UNCLASSIFIED SCRIPTURES. The everlasting gospel (Rev 14:6). Preach the gospel to every creature (Mark 16:15). Go and teach all nations (Mat 28:19). The gospel of the kingdom preached in all the world (Mat 24:14). Nave's lists approximately 55 verse references.

    • Lam 1:15

    WINE PRESS — FIGURATIVE. I have trodden the winepress alone (Isa 63:3). The Lord hath trodden the daughter of Judah as in a winepress (Lam 1:15). The press is full, the fats overflow (Joel 3:13). Cast into the great winepress of the wrath of God (Rev 14:19-20). He treadeth the winepress of the fierceness and wrath of Almighty God (Rev 19:15). Nave's lists 5 verse references.

    ANGEL OF THE CHURCHES — GENERAL¶

    • Rev 1:20
    • Rev 2:1
    • Rev 2:8
    • Rev 2:12
    • Rev 2:18
    • Rev 3:1
    • Rev 3:7
    • Rev 3:14

    ANGEL OF THE CHURCHES — GENERAL. The seven stars are the angels of the seven churches (Rev 1:20). Each of the seven letters in Rev 2-3 is addressed to 'the angel of the church' in a specific city. Whether these are literal guardian angels, human pastors/bishops, or personified spirits of the congregations is disputed.
