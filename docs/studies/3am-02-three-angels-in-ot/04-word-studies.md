# Word Studies

## Question
Is the three-part pattern of the three angels' messages — (1) gospel call to worship the true God, (2) exposure of a false system, (3) warning of judgment — repeated throughout the Old Testament? Examine: Noah before the flood, Abraham and Sodom, Moses and Egypt, Elijah at Carmel, Josiah's reform, Jeremiah's ministry. Do these share the same structure as Rev 14:6-12?

### G4352 — proskyneo

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G4352-proskyneo.md`

**Occurrences:** 65

To kiss the hand toward, prostrate in worship. 54 unique verses in NT. Nearly exclusively translated 'worship/worshipped.' The first angel's core command: 'worship him that made heaven and earth.' LXX equivalent of Hebrew shachah (H7812). Dominant in Revelation for both true worship (God) and false worship (beast/image).

**Semantic categories:** reverence/worship of God, false worship of idols/beast, homage to royalty

**Key verses:** Rev 14:7, Rev 14:9, Rev 14:11, Matt 4:10, John 4:24

**Relevance:** The first angel's call to 'worship him that made heaven and earth' (Rev 14:7) is the baseline vocabulary for the worship element in all six OT episodes. Proskyneo in Revelation contrasts true worship (God/Lamb) with false worship (beast/image) — the same binary Elijah presented at Carmel.

### G2920 — krisis

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G2920-krisis.md`

**Occurrences:** 47

Decision, judicial verdict, judgment. 47 occurrences in NT. From krino (G2919). Used for divine judgment (Rev 14:7 — 'the hour of his judgment is come'), human courts, and the act of evaluating. Key in the three angels: establishes that God's judgment hour has arrived.

**Semantic categories:** judicial process, divine judgment, condemnation, evaluation

**Key verses:** Rev 14:7, John 5:22, Matt 12:36, 2 Pet 2:9

**Relevance:** The first angel announces 'the hour of his krisis is come.' Via TR-riyb-krisis, this word traces directly to the OT covenant lawsuit (rib) tradition — the same formal legal framework that underlies all six OT prophetic episodes.

### G4098 — pipto

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G4098-pipto.md`

**Occurrences:** 93

To fall. Used in Rev 14:8 for Babylon's fall: 'Epesen epesen Babylon' (fell, fell, Babylon). The aorist indicative reduplication intensifies the declaration. Via TR-naphal-pipto, connects to OT uses of naphal for falling empires and cities.

**Semantic categories:** physical falling, collapse of empires/cities, prostration, moral ruin

**Key verses:** Rev 14:8, Rev 18:2, Isa 21:9

**Relevance:** The second angel's declaration 'Babylon is fallen, is fallen' uses pipto twice (aorist indicative). Isa 21:9 uses the same language: 'Babylon is fallen, is fallen.' The exposure element across OT episodes leads to the 'fall' of false systems.

### G2097 — euangelizo

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G2097-euangelizo.md`

**Occurrences:** 61

To announce good news, preach the gospel. The first angel's action: having the everlasting gospel 'to preach' (euangelisai). Via TR-basar-euangelizo, traces to the Hebrew basar (H1319) — to bring good tidings. The gospel call is the first element of the three-part pattern.

**Semantic categories:** gospel proclamation, good news announcement, missionary preaching

**Key verses:** Rev 14:6, Luke 4:18, Isa 61:1

**Relevance:** The first angel carries 'everlasting gospel to preach' — euangelion aionion euangelisai. This establishes that the three-part pattern begins with gospel proclamation, not judgment. The OT basar/euangelizo connection shows this is rooted in OT prophetic tradition.

### G5399 — phobeo

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G5399-phobeo.md`

**Occurrences:** 110

To fear, be afraid, reverence. The first angel's command: 'Fear God' (Phobethete ton Theon). Aorist passive imperative. Via TR-yare-phobeo, connects to Hebrew yare (H3372) — the foundational OT concept of fearing God.

**Semantic categories:** reverent fear of God, terror/dread, respect/awe

**Key verses:** Rev 14:7, Rev 15:4, Prov 1:7, Eccl 12:13

**Relevance:** The first command of the three angels — 'Fear God' — establishes reverent awe as the proper response. This connects to the OT wisdom tradition (Prov 1:7, Eccl 12:13) and to every OT episode where the true God is revealed against false gods.

### H7843 — shachath

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H7843-shachath.md`

**Occurrences:** 164

To corrupt, destroy, ruin. 136 occurrences across 25 books. Key verb in Noah's flood narrative: the earth 'was corrupt' (Niphal) and God will 'destroy' (Hiphil) — same root for both the problem and its consequence. The dominant Hiphil usage (~98 occurrences) means 'cause to be ruined/destroy.'

**Semantic categories:** moral corruption, physical destruction, divine judgment

**Key verses:** Gen 6:11, Gen 6:12, Gen 6:13, Gen 6:17

**Relevance:** The wordplay in Gen 6:11-13 is foundational: the earth was corrupt (shachath Niphal), all flesh corrupted (shachath Hiphil) their way, so God will destroy (shachath Hiphil) them. The exposure of corruption using the same root as the coming judgment is a powerful literary pattern.

### H2555 — chamas

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H2555-chamac.md`

**Occurrences:** 61

Violence, wrong, cruelty. Paired with shachath (H7843) in Gen 6:11 as the dual charge against the pre-flood world: corruption and violence. These are the two exposure terms for Noah's episode.

**Semantic categories:** physical violence, moral wrong, injustice

**Key verses:** Gen 6:11, Gen 6:13, Hab 1:3

**Relevance:** Chamas (violence) and shachath (corruption) are the twin indictments against the pre-flood world — the exposure element in Noah's episode. Gen 6:13 explicitly states that chamas is the reason for the flood judgment.

### H6662 — tsaddiyq

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H6662-tsaddiyq.md`

**Occurrences:** 210

Righteous, just. Applied to Noah in Gen 7:1 ('thee have I seen righteous before me'). Noah's righteousness implicitly exposed the world's wickedness — his life was the gospel call embodied. Connected to the NT keryx dikaiosynes (preacher of righteousness, 2 Pet 2:5).

**Semantic categories:** moral righteousness, covenant faithfulness, judicial righteousness

**Key verses:** Gen 6:9, Gen 7:1, Ps 1:6

**Relevance:** Noah's designation as tsaddiyq connects to dikaiosyne in 2 Pet 2:5 (keryx dikaiosynes). His righteousness functioned as exposure: Heb 11:7 says 'by the which he condemned the world.' The righteous exemplar exposes the false system by contrast.

### H5647 — abad

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H5647-abad.md`

**Occurrences:** 332

To serve, work, worship. 261 unique verses (332 tokens). The core Exodus worship verb: 'Let my people go, that they may serve (abad) me.' ~65% translated 'serve.' The semantic range spans from agricultural labor to religious worship. Overwhelmingly Qal (~95%).

**Semantic categories:** divine service/worship, agricultural labor, servitude/slavery

**Key verses:** Exo 7:16, Exo 8:1, Exo 9:1, Exo 9:13, Exo 10:3, 1 Ki 16:31

**Relevance:** The repeated Exodus formula 'Let my people go that they may serve (abad) me' is the gospel/worship call element in the Moses/Egypt episode. Via TR-abad-douleuo, connects to the NT concept of serving God. The same verb describes Ahab 'serving' Baal (1 Ki 16:31) — false worship using the same vocabulary.

### H7812 — shachah

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H7812-shachah.md`

**Occurrences:** 194

To bow down, prostrate, worship. 166 unique verses (194 tokens). The primary Hebrew worship verb. 99% Hishtaphel (reflexive). ~46% translated 'worship,' ~29% 'bowed.' Via TR-shachah-proskyneo, this is the direct Hebrew equivalent of proskyneo (G4352) in the first angel's message.

**Semantic categories:** worship of God, bowing to royalty, false worship/idol worship

**Key verses:** Gen 18:2, Exo 20:5, 1 Ki 16:31, Jer 25:6

**Relevance:** The Hebrew worship verb that translates to proskyneo in the LXX. When the first angel says 'worship (proskyneo) him that made heaven and earth,' the Hebrew equivalent is shachah. Every OT episode involves the contrast between true shachah (worship of YHWH) and false shachah (worship of idols/Baal).

### H3318 — yatsa

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H3318-yatsa.md`

**Occurrences:** 1371

To go out, come forth. 1,069 tokens across 990 verses — 14th most frequent verb in Hebrew Bible. Overwhelmingly Qal (784) and Hiphil (279). The core 'come out' verb in the vocabulary chain: Jer 51:45, Isa 48:20, Isa 52:11, Gen 19:12-14. Translates to exerchomai (G1831) in Rev 18:4.

**Semantic categories:** physical departure, exodus/liberation, divine command to separate, military going forth

**Key verses:** Jer 51:45, Isa 48:20, Isa 52:11, Gen 19:12

**Relevance:** The core verb in the 'come out' tradition. Jer 51:45 'My people, go ye out (tse'u, yatsa Qal imperative) of the midst of her' is the direct OT source for Rev 18:4's exerchomai imperative. This vocabulary chain is the most direct textual evidence linking OT prophetic messages to Revelation.

### H7378 — riyb

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H7378-riyb.md`

**Occurrences:** 79

To contend, strive, plead, hold a controversy. 59 unique verses (79 tokens). The technical term for the covenant lawsuit — God's formal legal proceeding against covenant-breaking Israel. ~32% translated 'plead,' ~19% 'contend.' Via TR-riyb-krisis, connects to krisis (G2920) in Rev 14:7.

**Semantic categories:** covenant lawsuit, legal contention, interpersonal strife, divine controversy

**Key verses:** Hos 4:1, Mic 6:2, Jer 2:9, Isa 1:18

**Relevance:** The rib (covenant lawsuit) provides the formal legal framework underlying the three-part prophetic pattern. Via TR-riyb-krisis, the Rev 14:7 'judgment' (krisis) vocabulary traces directly to the OT covenant lawsuit tradition. The rib structure has three elements matching the three angels: summons (gospel call), indictment (exposure), sentencing (judgment).

### H8451 — towrah

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H8451-towrah.md`

**Occurrences:** 244

Law, instruction, teaching. 213 unique verses (244 tokens). From yarah (H3384 — to direct, instruct). ~93% translated 'law.' Central to the Josiah episode: the sepher ha-towrah (book of the law) is rediscovered and triggers reform. Via TR-towrah-nomos, connects to NT nomos (G3551).

**Semantic categories:** Mosaic law, divine instruction, specific ordinances, Pentateuch

**Key verses:** 2 Ki 22:8, 2 Ki 23:25, Deut 31:26, Ps 119:1

**Relevance:** The discovery of the sepher ha-towrah in 2 Ki 22:8 is the gospel/covenant call element in Josiah's episode — the rediscovery of God's covenant requirements triggers repentance, exposure of idolatry, and the judgment prophecy from Huldah.

### H430 — elohiym

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H430-elohiym.md`

**Occurrences:** 2689

God, gods. 2,689 tokens in 2,246 verses — the second most common noun in the OT. Morphologically plural but grammatically singular when referring to the true God. ~90% refers to the God of Israel; ~3.8% to pagan deities. Central to the true-vs-false-gods theme across all six episodes.

**Semantic categories:** the true God (YHWH), pagan/foreign deities, angels/heavenly beings, human judges

**Key verses:** Gen 1:1, Exo 12:12, 1 Ki 18:21, 1 Ki 18:39

**Relevance:** The elohim vocabulary is central to the worship binary in every episode: YHWH as ha-elohim (the true God) vs. elohey Mitsrayim (gods of Egypt, Exo 12:12) vs. ha-ba'al (the Baal, 1 Ki 18:21). The Carmel confession 'YHWH hu ha-elohim' (1 Ki 18:39) uses elohim to declare the identity of the true God.

### H8199 — shaphat

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H8199-shaphat.md`

**Occurrences:** 223

To judge, govern, vindicate. 182 unique verses (223 tokens). ~59% translated 'judge.' The noun form shephatim (judgments) appears in Exo 12:12 — 'against all the gods of Egypt I will execute judgments (shephatim).' Via TR-shaphat-krino, connects to krino (G2919).

**Semantic categories:** judicial authority, divine judgment, governance/ruling, vindication

**Key verses:** Exo 12:12, Num 33:4, Judg 2:16, 1 Sam 7:6

**Relevance:** The noun shephatim (judgments) in Exo 12:12 and Num 33:4 explicitly identifies the plagues as divine judgments against Egypt's gods. This is the judgment element in the Moses/Egypt episode — God executing judgment against the false worship system.

### H4941 — mishpat

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H4941-mishpat.md`

**Occurrences:** 448

Judgment, justice, ordinance. One of the most common Hebrew words for justice/judgment. Used across all six episodes for God's judicial actions. The concept of divine mishpat (justice) underlies the entire warning-before-judgment pattern.

**Semantic categories:** divine justice, legal judgment, ordinance/law, rights

**Key verses:** Gen 18:19, Gen 18:25, Jer 7:5, Mic 6:8

**Relevance:** Abraham's appeal 'Shall not the Judge (shophet) of all the earth do mishpat (justice/right)?' (Gen 18:25) establishes that God's judgments are inherently just. The three-part pattern demonstrates this justice: warning is given before judgment falls.

### G2784 — kerysso

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G2784-kerysso.md`

**Occurrences:** 63

To herald, proclaim publicly. The verb form of keryx (G2783). Used for preaching the gospel and making public proclamations. Connected to Noah's role as keryx dikaiosynes (2 Pet 2:5) — the herald who formally announces God's message.

**Semantic categories:** gospel proclamation, public heralding, prophetic announcement

**Key verses:** Matt 4:17, Mark 1:14, Acts 10:42, 2 Tim 4:2

**Relevance:** The verb kerysso connects to keryx (G2783), which describes Noah in 2 Pet 2:5. The angelic messengers of Rev 14 are performing the kerysso function — publicly heralding God's message. Each OT episode features a figure performing this heralding role.

### G897 — Babylon

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G897-babylon.md`

**Occurrences:** 12

Babylon — both the historical city/empire and the apocalyptic symbol in Revelation. The second angel's target: 'Babylon is fallen, is fallen.' Revelation's Babylon draws directly from Jeremiah 50-51, with verse-by-verse parallels especially in the cup imagery (Jer 51:7 → Rev 14:8, 17:4).

**Semantic categories:** historical empire, apocalyptic symbol, false religious system

**Key verses:** Rev 14:8, Rev 17:5, Rev 18:2, Rev 18:4

**Relevance:** Babylon is the paradigmatic 'false system' in the second angel's message. Jeremiah's Babylon oracle (chs. 50-51) is the primary OT source for Revelation's Babylon, establishing a direct textual chain from the Jeremiah episode to the three angels' messages.

### G1391 — doxa

**Occurrences:** 168

Glory, splendor, honor. The first angel commands: 'give glory (doxa) to him.' The purpose of all six OT episodes is ultimately to glorify the true God — declaring His name, power, and justice throughout the earth.

**Key verses:** Rev 14:7, Rev 15:4, Exo 33:18, Isa 42:8

**Relevance:** The first angel's command to 'give glory to him' connects to Exo 9:16's purpose statement — 'that my name may be declared throughout all the earth.' Each OT episode aims to glorify the true God by exposing false worship and vindicating YHWH.

### H3068 — YHWH

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H3068-yhwh.md`

**Occurrences:** 6534

The LORD — the personal, covenantal name of the God of Israel. 6,534 tokens in 5,520 verses — the most frequent proper noun in the Hebrew Bible. From the verb 'to be' (hayah, H1961). The self-existent, covenant God who is contrasted with false gods in every OT episode.

**Semantic categories:** divine name, covenant identity, self-existence

**Key verses:** Exo 3:14-15, 1 Ki 18:39, Exo 12:12, Jer 51:6

**Relevance:** YHWH is the true God in every episode: Noah walked with God; Abraham interceded with YHWH; the Exodus was fought in YHWH's name; Carmel declared 'YHWH hu ha-elohim'; Josiah served YHWH; Jeremiah spoke the word of YHWH. The first angel's call to 'worship him that made heaven and earth' is a call to worship YHWH.

### H6452 — pasach

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H6452-pâçach.md`

**Occurrences:** 8

To hop, skip over, hesitate, limp, dance. 8 occurrences. The root of 'Passover' (pesach, H6453). In 1 Ki 18:21, describes Israel 'halting/limping' between two opinions — spiritual vacillation. In 1 Ki 18:26 (Piel), describes Baal's prophets leaping on the altar — frenzied ritual dance. The Exodus Passover sense is God 'passing over' the blood-marked houses.

**Semantic categories:** divine protective passing-over, limping/hesitation, frenzied ritual leaping, physical lameness

**Key verses:** 1 Ki 18:21, 1 Ki 18:26, Exo 12:13, Exo 12:23, Exo 12:27

**Relevance:** The dual use in 1 Ki 18 is theologically rich: Israel 'limps' (pasach) between YHWH and Baal (v.21), while Baal's prophets 'leap' (pasach Piel) on the altar (v.26). Same root, contrasting uses — one describes divided loyalty, the other frenzied false worship. The Passover connection (same root) links the Carmel episode to the Exodus episode.

### H1168 — Baal

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H1168-baʻal.md`

**Occurrences:** 88

The chief male deity of the Canaanite/Phoenician pantheon. 88 occurrences. Meaning 'lord/master' from the common noun ba'al (H1167). Worshipped as a storm and fertility god. The Carmel confrontation (1 Ki 18) is the locus classicus. Concentration in 2 Kings (30), 1 Kings (13), Jeremiah (13).

**Semantic categories:** Carmel confrontation, cyclical apostasy in Judges, prophetic indictment, Hosea's marriage metaphor

**Key verses:** 1 Ki 18:21, 1 Ki 18:26, 1 Ki 18:40, Jer 2:8, Hos 2:8

**Relevance:** Baal is the primary false deity in the Elijah/Carmel and Josiah episodes, and a major target of Jeremiah's indictment. The Baal-vs-YHWH binary at Carmel directly parallels the first angel's worship call vs. the beast system. Note: Baal was a storm/rain god (the drought context of 1 Ki 17-18), not a sun god as Bohr claims.

### G1831 — exerchomai

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G1831-exerchomai.md`

**Occurrences:** 230

To go out, come out, issue forth. 230 occurrences. From ek (G1537, out of) + erchomai (G2064, to come). The Greek equivalent of yatsa (H3318) — highest PMI score (4.42) with 489 co-occurrences in LXX. Used in Rev 18:4 for the 'come out' command (Exelthate, aorist active imperative 2p).

**Semantic categories:** physical departure, separation from corrupt systems, divine/prophetic origin, eschatological action, missionary movement

**Key verses:** Rev 18:4, 2 Cor 6:17, Heb 13:13, Acts 7:3

**Relevance:** The Greek verb that completes the 'come out' vocabulary chain: OT yatsa (H3318) → LXX exerchomai (G1831) → Rev 18:4 Exelthate. This is the most directly documented lexical link between OT prophetic messages and Revelation.

### G2783 — keryx

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G2783-keryx.md`

**Occurrences:** 3

Herald, preacher. Only 3 NT occurrences. A formal public crier commissioned to make announcements on behalf of a sovereign authority. Used for Paul (1 Tim 2:7, 2 Tim 1:11) and Noah (2 Pet 2:5 — 'keryx dikaiosynes,' preacher of righteousness).

**Semantic categories:** apostolic self-designation, antediluvian herald of righteousness

**Key verses:** 2 Pet 2:5, 1 Tim 2:7, 2 Tim 1:11

**Relevance:** Noah as keryx dikaiosynes (herald of righteousness) confirms his role as a commissioned gospel announcer — not merely a boat-builder but a formal herald calling for repentance before catastrophic judgment. The keryx role parallels the angel messengers of Rev 14.

### H2534 — chemah

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H2534-chêmâh.md`

**Occurrences:** 133

Heat, wrath, fury, poison. 133 occurrences. Dominant in Ezekiel (33) and Jeremiah (18). The word of choice for God's fury in the writing prophets. The cup-of-wrath tradition (Jer 25:15, Isa 51:17) uses chemah as the contents of the cup. LXX translates chemah as thumos (G2372), establishing the direct lexical bridge to Rev 14:10's 'wine of the thumos of God.'

**Semantic categories:** divine wrath/fury, cup-of-wrath tradition, human anger, venom/poison

**Key verses:** 2 Ki 22:17, Jer 25:15, Isa 51:17, Ezek 5:13

**Relevance:** In 2 Ki 22:17, Huldah prophesies: 'my wrath (chemah) shall be kindled against this place, and shall not be quenched.' The chemah→thumos LXX translation is the lexical bridge connecting Huldah's judgment prophecy to Rev 14:10's 'wine of the wrath (thumos) of God.' The cup-of-wrath tradition (Jer 25:15) connects Jeremiah's ministry to the third angel's judgment warning.

### H6663 — tsadaq

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H6663-tsadaq.md`

**Occurrences:** 54

To be righteous, be just. The verb form corresponding to the adjective tsaddiyq (H6662). The righteousness vocabulary connects Noah's characterization (tsaddiyq) to the concept of divine justice (tsedaqah) — God's judgments are themselves righteous.
