# Reference Gathering: "The Hour of His Judgment Is Come" (Rev 14:7c)

## Question
What does hoti elthen he hora tes kriseos autou ("for the hour of his judgment is come") mean? What is this judgment — the cross, the second coming, the investigative/pre-advent judgment, or something else? Why does the aorist elthen present it as an accomplished fact? How does krisis (G2920) differ from katakrima (G2631)? Trace krisis through the LXX to Daniel 7's dina. How does Daniel 7:22 establish this judgment is FOR believers? How can judgment be "good news"? Does the pre-advent judgment undermine assurance? How does Romans 2:16 prove judgment IS gospel content?

## Web Research Findings

The web research agent (00-web-research.md) produced extensive scholarly findings organized into 13 research directives. Key highlights:

**Krisis vs. Katakrima distinction:**
- Krisis (G2920): 48 NT occurrences; "a separating or sundering, a trial or contest, selection, and judgment concerning justice and injustice" — the process of judicial evaluation
- Katakrima (G2631): only 3 NT occurrences (Rom 5:16, 5:18, 8:1); "the adjudication of punishment" — the adverse result including sentence AND execution
- Rev 14:7 uses krisis (evaluation process), not katakrima (condemnation) — the angel announces a judgment process, not a condemnation

**LXX Daniel 7 vocabulary chain:**
- Dan 7:10: dina yitib → LXX: kriterion ekathisen ("the tribunal sat")
- Dan 7:22: dina yehib → LXX: to krima edoken ("the judgment/decision was given")
- Dan 7:26: dina yittib → LXX: to kriterion kathisei ("the tribunal will sit")
- LXX uses kriterion and krima — cognates of krisis from the same krin- root
- This connects Rev 14:7's krisis to Daniel 7's judgment vocabulary family

**Prophetic aorist of elthen:**
- Precept Austin identifies elthen as a "prophetic aorist" — "the time of His judgment is certain and imminent, as if it is already underway"
- Alternative reading: aorist simply presents the judgment as accomplished certainty from John's visionary perspective

**Daniel 7:22 "judgment given TO the saints":**
- Pfandl (BRI): "a judgment was made in favor of the saints of the Most High" — primary function is vindication
- Multiple scholarly sources confirm the preposition indicates favorable verdict

**Evangelical critique engagement:**
- Barnhouse (1956): called investigative judgment "the most colossal, psychological, face-saving phenomenon"
- Hoekema (1963): "a doctrine built on a mistake"
- Ford (1979): 1,000-page manuscript arguing it "lacked firm biblical warrant" and "undermined assurance"
- Glacier View (1980) produced massive denominational upheaval — 182 pastors departed in Australia/NZ
- Scholarly defense increasingly emphasizes vindication/Great Controversy framework

**1 John 4:17 — confidence IN judgment:**
- Parresia (boldness/confidence) at krisis — the same word as Rev 14:7
- Love casts out fear (4:18) but NOT judgment — believer has confidence IN judgment, not exemption FROM it
- Parresia/parousia near-homophony noted by commentators

**Romans 2:16 — judgment IS gospel content:**
- Barnes: "one of the great and prime articles of that gospel was, that God would judge the world by Jesus Christ"
- Multiple commentators confirm judgment is integral gospel content, not addition
- kata to euangelion mou grammatically modifies "God will judge"

**LXX Daniel 7 Christology (Koperski/Reynolds):**
- OG version uses latreuō (reserved for worship of Israel's God) for the Son of Man figure
- OG aligns Son of Man with Ancient of Days — elevated Christology strengthens reading "his judgment" (autou) in Rev 14:7 as divine judgment

**Livingston's assurance framework:**
- "The theological change necessary to remove any qualms about assurance is not changing the timing... but a change in basic understanding of salvation or free will"
- Assurance comes from understanding grace, not from removing judgment

**Key leads for the scoping agent:**
1. The krisis/katakrima distinction is the interpretive key — Rev 14:7 announces a judgment PROCESS (krisis), not a condemnation (katakrima). Rom 8:1 negates katakrima, not krisis. This means believers face evaluation with boldness (1 John 4:17), not exemption from all judgment.
2. The Aramaic dina → LXX kriterion/krima → NT krisis chain connects Rev 14:7 directly to Daniel 7's court scene. The scoping agent should direct the research agent to verify exact LXX vocabulary in Dan 7:10, 22, 26 and trace the krin- root family.
3. Dan 7:22's "judgment given TO the saints" is the pivotal verse for establishing that judgment is FOR believers. The Aramaic preposition le and its interpretation need thorough research.
4. The prophetic aorist of elthen deserves morphological verification and comparison with other prophetic aorists in Revelation (e.g., epesen epesen in 14:8).
5. Ford's strongest critique — that the investigative judgment lacks biblical warrant independent of the 1844 date — should be engaged honestly. The exegetical case for a pre-advent judgment concept (Dan 7's sequence: judgment BEFORE kingdom transfer) may be stronger than the case for the specific timing.
6. Rom 2:16 (kata to euangelion mou) and 1 John 4:17 (parresia in the day of krisis) are critical proof texts for judgment-as-gospel and judgment-with-confidence. Both deserve close grammatical examination.
