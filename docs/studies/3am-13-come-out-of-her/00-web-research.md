### Greek Text Analysis — Revelation 18:4 (BibleHub Interlinear)
**URL:** https://biblehub.com/text/revelation/18-4.htm
**Key claims:**
- Exelthate: Aorist Imperative Active, 2nd Person Plural — a single decisive command, not ongoing instruction
- Ho laos mou: Article (nominative) + Noun (vocative) + Possessive Pronoun (genitive 1st person) — vocative address using covenant designation
- Hina me: Conjunction + Negative particle — introduces negated purpose clause
- Synkoinonesete: Aorist Subjunctive Active, 2nd Person Plural — potential shared participation
- Labete: Aorist Subjunctive Active, 2nd Person Plural — potential receiving
- The verse contains TWO hina-me clauses: (1) hina me synkoinonesete tais hamartiais autes, (2) kai ek ton plegon autes hina me labete
**Greek analysis:** The aorist imperative conveys urgency — a single, decisive action rather than continuous or repeated command
**Relevance:** Full parsing confirms the dual-purpose structure and the urgency of the aorist imperative
**Status:** Lead to verify against Scripture

## Summary of Scholarly Landscape

The scholarly consensus treats Revelation 18:4's "come out of her, my people" as the climax of a biblical tradition stretching from Abraham's call out of Ur through the Exodus, the prophetic calls to flee literal Babylon (Isa 48:20; 52:11; Jer 50:8; 51:6, 45; Zech 2:6-7), and Paul's composite citation in 2 Corinthians 6:14-18. Each iteration applies the same theological logic: God's people must separate from corrupt systems before judgment falls, not because separation saves them, but because continued association means complicity in sin and exposure to its consequences.

On the ecclesiological question — how can God have "my people" inside Babylon? — scholars identify multiple categories. Gill distinguishes "elect ones till now uncalled" from those "secretly called but not publicly professing." Jamieson-Fausset-Brown affirms that "in every apostate or world-conforming church there are some of God's invisible and true Church." The John 10:16 "other sheep" tradition reinforces this: commentators unanimously identify the "other sheep" as Gentiles outside the Jewish institutional structure who nonetheless belong to Christ's flock. The critical Greek distinction between aule (fold = institutional structure) and poimne (flock = living community) demonstrates that belonging to the flock does not require being in the right fold — a principle directly applicable to God's people inside Babylon.

The Greek grammar of Revelation 18:4 reinforces the urgency and logic of the call. The aorist imperative exelthate demands a single, decisive act of departure. The dual hina-me purpose clauses establish a cause-effect chain: remaining in Babylon leads to (1) sharing her sins (synkoinonesete — active co-participation, used only 3x in the NT) and (2) receiving her plagues. The relationship between the fall announcement (Rev 14:8; 18:2) and the come-out call (18:4) is sequential and logical: the announcement reveals Babylon's true condition, making the separation command both possible (now people know) and necessary (judgment follows).

## Research Directives for Downstream Agents

1. **Verify the OT "come out" chain:** Check the exact wording and contexts of Gen 12:1, Isa 48:20, Isa 52:11, Jer 50:8, Jer 51:6, Jer 51:45, Zech 2:6-7, and Num 16:26 — do they share vocabulary, and does Rev 18:4 clearly allude to them?
2. **Verify the aule/poimne distinction in John 10:16:** Confirm that the Greek text uses two different words and that the KJV "one fold" is indeed a mistranslation of "one flock" — this is load-bearing for the ecclesiology argument.
3. **Parse the dual hina-me clauses in Rev 18:4:** Confirm that there are two distinct purpose clauses (not sharing sins AND not receiving plagues) and examine whether these represent two separable consequences or one causal chain.
4. **Examine synkoinoneo across its 3 NT occurrences:** Check Eph 5:11, Phil 4:14, and Rev 18:4 — does the word consistently imply active co-participation rather than mere proximity?
5. **Map the 2 Cor 6:14-18 catena:** Verify which OT texts Paul quotes (Lev 26:12, Isa 52:11, Ezek 37:27, 2 Sam 7:14) and how each functions in the composite argument — does the Isaiah 52:11 "come out" command carry the same force as in Revelation?
6. **Check the Hosea reversal pattern:** Does the ho laos mou designation in Rev 18:4 connect to the Hosea "not my people" / "my people" reversal (Hos 1:9; 2:23; Rom 9:25; 1 Pet 2:10)? If so, the people called out of Babylon may include former "not-my-people" who have been reclaimed.
7. **Verify the fall-then-call sequence:** Confirm that Rev 14:8 (fall announcement) logically precedes Rev 18:4 (come-out call) — does the text require the fall to be recognized before the separation can occur?
8. **Examine Keener's corporate accountability claim:** Check whether Scripture supports the principle that remaining within a judged institution means sharing its judgment, even if individually forgiven — test against Lot in Sodom, Rahab in Jericho, and Israelites in Egypt.
9. **Check the Korah rebellion parallel (Num 16:26):** Verify whether Moses' command to "move back from the tents of these wicked men" functions as a "come out" type, and whether the consequence pattern (proximity = shared destruction) matches Rev 18:4.
10. **Investigate the speaker identity:** Confirm whether "another voice from heaven" saying "my people" must be God/Christ speaking, or whether an angelic messenger could use this covenant designation on God's behalf.
