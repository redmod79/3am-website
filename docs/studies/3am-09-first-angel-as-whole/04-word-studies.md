# Word Studies

## Question
How do the four elements of the first angel's message (euangelion aionion + phobethete + dote doxan + hoti elthen he hora tes kriseos + proskynesate to poiesanti) function as one integrated gospel proclamation? How does Ecclesiastes 12:13-14 demonstrate this is the same message? How does Paul at Athens (Acts 17:22-31) serve as apostolic precedent — element by element? How does Psalm 96 contain every element in OT worship form? Capstone synthesizing studies 04-08.

### G2098 — euangelion

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G2098-euangelion.md`

**Occurrences:** 77

Neuter noun meaning 'good message/gospel.' From eu (good) + angello (announce). 77 tokens in 74 verses across 17 books. Virtually uniformly translated 'gospel' in KJV (94.6%). Concentrated in Pauline epistles (Romans 10, 2 Corinthians 8, Galatians 7). The noun denotes content of proclamation rather than the act of proclaiming.

**Semantic categories:** gospel proclamation, salvation message, good news

**Key verses:** Rev 14:6, Rom 1:16, Gal 1:6-7, Eph 1:13

**Relevance:** The text labels the first angel's content as euangelion aionion. The anarthrous construction is qualitative ('gospel of an eternal kind'), not indefinite. The cognate accusative (euangelion euangelisai) intensifies the gospel-ness.

### G2097 — euangelizo

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G2097-euangelizo.md`

**Occurrences:** 61

Verb meaning 'to announce good news, evangelize.' 52 unique verses (61 tokens) across 12 books. Overwhelmingly middle voice (88%) — the evangelist personally engages as an invested participant. Present tense dominant (25), followed by aorist (20).

**Semantic categories:** proclamation, gospel preaching, heralding

**Key verses:** Rev 14:6, Luk 3:18, Gal 3:8, Luk 4:18

**Relevance:** Rev 14:6 is the only NT verse where the noun euangelion and verb euangelisai co-occur — a cognate accusative intensifying the gospel character. Luke 3:18 uses euangelizeto to label John's judgment preaching as gospel.

### G5399 — phobeo

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G5399-phobeo.md`

**Occurrences:** 110

Verb meaning 'to fear, be afraid, revere.' 90 unique verses (110 tokens) across 14 books. Spans three semantic domains: fear/terror (025), worship/reverence (053), and honor/status (087). Nearly all forms are middle/passive voice — deponent. The aorist imperative phobethete in Rev 14:7 is a decisive call to reverential awe.

**Semantic categories:** reverential awe, terror/alarm, worship, honor

**Key verses:** Rev 14:7, Matt 10:28, Exo 20:20, 1 John 4:18

**Relevance:** First imperative of the triad. Bridges to H3372 yare in the OT. The aorist imperative form calls for a decisive, specific act — not ongoing habit but definitive commitment to fear God.

### G5401 — phobos

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G5401-phobos.md`

**Occurrences:** 47

Masculine noun meaning 'fear, terror, reverence.' 44 unique verses (47 tokens) across 16 books. Spans fear/terror and worship/reverence domains. 77.3% translated 'fear' in KJV.

**Semantic categories:** fear, terror, reverence

**Key verses:** 1 John 4:18, Rom 3:18, Acts 9:31

**Relevance:** The noun form of the first angel's imperative. 1 John 4:18 specifies that love casts out kolasis-fear (dread of punishment), not phobos in its reverential sense.

### G1391 — doxa

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G1391-doxa.md`

**Occurrences:** 168

Feminine noun meaning 'glory, honor, splendor.' 151 unique verses (168 tokens) across 23 books. Root shifted from classical Greek 'opinion/reputation' to LXX sense of visible, luminous majesty — particularly the manifest presence of God (Hebrew kabod). Carries both visible-radiance and relational honor senses.

**Semantic categories:** divine radiance, honor/status, praise, visible splendor

**Key verses:** Rev 14:7, Rom 1:21-23, Rom 4:20, John 17:4, Rev 16:9

**Relevance:** Second imperative object — 'give him glory.' The didomi + doxa + dative construction appears in Rev 14:7, Rom 4:20, and Rev 16:9 (the refusal echo). LXX equivalent of kabod (H3519).

### G1392 — doxazo

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G1392-doxazo.md`

**Occurrences:** 71

Verb meaning 'to glorify, render glorious, honor.' 54 unique verses (71 tokens) across 13 books. Spans praise, honor/status, and value domains.

**Semantic categories:** glorification, praise, honor

**Key verses:** Rom 1:21, John 17:4, Matt 5:16, 1 Cor 6:20

**Relevance:** Rom 1:21's edoxasan (aorist active) with negative — 'they glorified him NOT as God' — is the exact failure the first angel's dote doxan reverses.

### G1325 — didomi

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G1325-didomi.md`

**Occurrences:** 430

Primary verb meaning 'to give' with extraordinarily wide application. 377 unique verses (430 tokens) across 22 books. Spans giving, lending, dedicating, and assigning domains. Aorist dominant (59%).

**Semantic categories:** giving, granting, appointing, consecrating

**Key verses:** Rev 14:7, Rev 16:9, Rom 4:20

**Relevance:** The verb in 'give him glory' (dote auto doxan). Same verb in the Rev 16:9 refusal echo (dounai auto doxan) and Rom 4:20 (dous doxan to theo). The didomi + doxa + dative construction links Rev 14:7, Rom 4:20, and Rev 16:9.

### G4352 — proskyneo

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G4352-proskyneo.md`

**Occurrences:** 65

Verb meaning 'to worship, prostrate, do homage.' 54 unique verses (65 tokens). From pros (toward) + probable derivative of kyon (dog — kissing the hand). LXX equivalent of shachah (H7812). Almost exclusively translated 'worship' in KJV.

**Semantic categories:** worship, prostration, homage

**Key verses:** Rev 14:7, Rev 13:15, Matt 4:10, Rev 4:10

**Relevance:** Third imperative — the behavioral expression of the triad. The worship contest between God and the beast (Rev 13:15 vs 14:7) makes proskyneo the central issue of the three angels' messages.

### G4160 — poieo

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G4160-poieo.md`

**Occurrences:** 597

Verb meaning 'to make, do.' 518 unique verses (597 tokens) across 26 books — the broadest Greek action verb. 50% translated 'do/did,' 15% 'make/made.' The LXX equivalent of asah (H6213).

**Semantic categories:** making, doing, creating, performing

**Key verses:** Rev 14:7, Acts 17:24, Exo 20:11 LXX

**Relevance:** Deliberately chosen over ktizo in Rev 14:7 to invoke Exo 20:11 LXX (asah = poieo). Rev 4:11 and 10:6 use ktizo; only 14:7 uses poieo. Acts 17:24 also uses poieo (poiesas) — confirming the creation-verb parallel.

---

### G2936 — ktizo

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G2936-ktizo.md`

**Occurrences:** 16

Verb meaning 'to create, found, form originally.' Only 12 unique verses (16 tokens). Technical creation vocabulary — more specialized than poieo. Used in Rev 4:11 (ektisas, ektisthesan) and Rev 10:6 (ektisen) but NOT in Rev 14:7.

**Semantic categories:** divine creation, founding, originating

**Key verses:** Rev 4:11, Rev 10:6, Col 1:16, Eph 2:10

**Relevance:** The verb Revelation does NOT use in 14:7. The deliberate switch from ktizo (Rev 4:11, 10:6) to poieo (Rev 14:7) is the strongest evidence for the Exo 20:11 allusion. Mueller (BRI): 'By using poieo instead of ktizo, the connection to Genesis 1-2 is even more clearly established.'

### G2920 — krisis

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G2920-krisis.md`

**Occurrences:** 47

Feminine noun meaning 'judgment, judicial process.' 47 NT occurrences. From krino (to separate, judge). Spans court of justice, judging activity, evaluation, and punishment domains. 55% translated 'judgment' in KJV.

**Semantic categories:** judicial process, evaluation, court, day of judgment

**Key verses:** Rev 14:7, Matt 12:36, John 5:24, Dan 7:22 LXX

**Relevance:** The PROCESS word — the angel chose krisis (47 NT occurrences, judicial evaluation) over katakrima (3 occurrences, adverse verdict + execution). This linguistic choice shapes the entire theology of Rev 14:7: judgment is investigation, not condemnation.

---

### G2631 — katakrima

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G2631-katakrima.md`

**Occurrences:** 3

Neuter noun meaning 'condemnation, adverse sentence.' Only 3 NT occurrences, all in Romans (5:16, 5:18, 8:1). From kata (against) + krima (verdict). 100% translated 'condemnation' in KJV.

**Semantic categories:** condemnation, adverse verdict

**Key verses:** Rom 5:16, Rom 5:18, Rom 8:1

**Relevance:** The word the angel did NOT choose. Rev 14:7 uses krisis (process), not katakrima (condemnation). Rom 8:1 negates katakrima for believers: 'There is therefore now no condemnation to them which are in Christ Jesus.' Judgment IS gospel because it is krisis, not katakrima.

### G5610 — hora

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G5610-hora.md`

**Occurrences:** 108

Noun meaning 'hour, time, season.' 108 occurrences. 70.4% translated 'hour.' Concentrated in Gospels (John 26, Matthew 23, Luke 16, Mark 12) and Revelation (10).

**Semantic categories:** time period, appointed time, eschatological moment

**Key verses:** Rev 14:7, John 12:23, Rev 3:10, Rev 18:10

**Relevance:** The eschatological timing marker: 'the hour of his judgment IS come.' The articular construction (he hora) makes this a specific, appointed hour — not a general time but THE hour.

### G166 — aionios

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G166-aionios.md`

**Occurrences:** 71

Adjective meaning 'eternal, everlasting, perpetual.' 71 tokens in 69 verses across 19 books. From aion (G165). 92% rendered 'eternal' or 'everlasting' in KJV.

**Semantic categories:** eternal duration, age-spanning quality

**Key verses:** Rev 14:6, 2 Tim 1:9, Titus 1:2, John 17:3

**Relevance:** Qualifies the gospel as 'everlasting' — not a new gospel but the same gospel proclaimed from the beginning (Gal 3:8 proeuangelizomai). The anarthrous construction with euangelion is qualitative: 'gospel of an eternal kind.'

### G4102 — pistis

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G4102-pistis.md`

**Occurrences:** 244

Feminine noun meaning 'faith, trust, persuasion.' 244 occurrences. From peitho (to persuade). Spans belief, trust, and promise domains. 68.9% translated 'faith' in KJV.

**Semantic categories:** faith/trust, belief, faithfulness, the faith

**Key verses:** Rev 14:12, Rom 4:20, Gen 15:6, Heb 11:1

**Relevance:** Rev 14:12's 'faith of Jesus' (pistin Iesou) — subjective genitive ('the faithfulness OF Jesus') or objective genitive ('faith IN Jesus'). Both work; the subjective genitive adds christological depth: the saints exhibit Christ's own faithfulness.

### G1785 — entole

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G1785-entole.md`

**Occurrences:** 71

Feminine noun meaning 'commandment, precept.' 65 unique verses (71 tokens). From entellomai (to command). 93.8% translated 'commandment(s)' in KJV.

**Semantic categories:** commandment, divine directive, precept

**Key verses:** Rev 14:12, Rev 12:17, 1 John 5:3, John 14:15

**Relevance:** The commandment inclusio: Rev 12:17 (entolas tou theou) and Rev 14:12 (entolas tou theou) bracket the three angels' messages. In a passage concluding with 'commandments of God,' the creation formula's strongest link is to the commandment memorializing creation.

### G3340 — metanoeo

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G3340-metanoeo.md`

**Occurrences:** 36

Verb meaning 'to repent, change one's mind.' 32 unique verses (36 tokens). From meta (after/change) + noeo (to think). Fundamentally cognitive-volitional — deliberate reorientation of thought and will, not merely emotional remorse. Concentrated in Revelation (10), Luke (9), Matthew (5).

**Semantic categories:** repentance, changed thinking, moral reorientation

**Key verses:** Acts 17:30, Acts 2:38, Rev 2:5, Matt 3:2

**Relevance:** The Acts 17:30 imperative ('commands all people everywhere to repent') parallels Rev 14:7's 'fear God and give him glory.' Repentance and fear-of-God are two angles on the same moral reorientation — metanoeo is the Athenian equivalent of phobethete.

### G5083 — tereo

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G5083-tereo.md`

**Occurrences:** 75

Verb meaning 'to keep, guard, watch over.' 75 occurrences. From teros (a watch). Differs from phylasso (preventing escape) — tereo implies keeping the eye upon, noting, fulfilling. 17.3% translated 'keep,' 12% 'keepeth.'

**Semantic categories:** guarding, observing commandments, maintaining, watching

**Key verses:** Rev 14:12, Rev 12:17, John 14:15, 1 John 5:3

**Relevance:** Rev 14:12's hoi terountes (present active participle) is substantival — describing who the saints ARE. The word implies watchful, careful guarding of commandments, not mere external compliance.

### G5281 — hupomone

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G5281-hupomone.md`

**Occurrences:** 32

Feminine noun meaning 'patience, endurance, steadfast perseverance.' 32 occurrences. From hupo (under) + meno (remain). Pictures bearing a weight from below — active endurance, not passive resignation. 78.1% translated 'patience' in KJV.

**Semantic categories:** endurance, steadfastness, patient perseverance

**Key verses:** Rev 14:12, Rev 13:10, Rom 5:3-4, Heb 12:1

**Relevance:** Rev 14:12: 'Here is the patience (hupomone) of the saints.' The hupomone inclusio (Rev 13:10 to 14:12) brackets the three angels' messages as a single literary unit. Active endurance under eschatological pressure, not passive waiting.

### G2919 — krino

### H3372 — yare

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H3372-yare.md`

**Occurrences:** 414

Primitive root verb meaning 'to fear, revere.' 317 unique verses (414 tokens) across 34 books. Qal dominant (85%) covering both natural fear and reverent awe. Niphal participle nora means 'awesome, terrible' applied to God.

**Semantic categories:** reverential awe, terror, reverence

**Key verses:** Eccl 12:13, Deut 10:12, Josh 24:14, 1 Sam 12:24, Exo 20:20

**Relevance:** The Hebrew root behind phobeo. The farewell-address pattern traces yare through Moses (Deut 10:12), Joshua (Josh 24:14), Samuel (1 Sam 12:24), Solomon (Eccl 12:13), and the first angel (Rev 14:7). LXX yare maps to phobeo with 241 co-occurrences.

### H3519 — kabowd

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H3519-kabowd.md`

**Occurrences:** 214

Masculine noun meaning 'glory, weight, honor.' 189 unique verses (214 tokens) in 27 books. From kabad (to be heavy). Root idea is literal heaviness applied metaphorically — substance, importance, radiance. 82% translated 'glory' in KJV.

**Semantic categories:** divine radiance, weight/substance, honor, character

**Key verses:** Psa 96:7-8, Exo 33:18, Exo 34:6-7, Isa 42:12

**Relevance:** The Hebrew background for doxa. Psa 96:7-8 'give unto the LORD glory (kabod)' maps to Rev 14:7 'give him glory (doxa).' When Moses asked to see God's glory (kabod, Exo 33:18), God proclaimed His character (Exo 34:6-7) — glory-giving = recognizing God's full character-weight.

### H4941 — mishpat

**Key verses:** Eccl 12:14, Dan 7:22, Psa 96:13, Isa 42:1

### H6213 — asah

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H6213-asah.md`

**Occurrences:** 2934

Primitive root verb meaning 'to do, make.' 2,283 unique verses (2,934 tokens) — one of the most frequent OT verbs. Present in every OT book. The semantic default all-purpose action verb. 39% 'do/did,' 21% 'make/made.'

**Semantic categories:** making, doing, creating, acting

**Key verses:** Exo 20:11, Gen 1:7, Psa 96:5

**Relevance:** Exo 20:11: 'the LORD made (asah) heaven and earth, the sea.' LXX translates asah as poieo. Rev 14:7 uses poieo (poiesanti) rather than ktizo, mapping to asah via the LXX. Psa 96:5: 'the LORD made (asah) the heavens' — same verb in the same creation-worship context.

### H120 — adam

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H120-adam.md`

**Occurrences:** 575

Noun meaning 'man, mankind, human.' The word in Eccl 12:13's kol-ha-adam ('the whole of man'). Three interpretive options for kol-ha-adam: (1) 'all mankind' — universal scope, (2) 'whole duty of man' — KJV adds 'duty' in italics, (3) 'man's all' — the totality of human purpose/being.

**Semantic categories:** humanity, mankind, human being

**Key verses:** Eccl 12:13, Eccl 3:13, Eccl 5:19

**Relevance:** The kol-ha-adam phrase defines what the first angel's message IS — if it means 'man's all' (entire purpose), then Eccl 12:13 says the fear-commandments-judgment triad IS the totality of human existence. This parallels euangelion aionion as the comprehensive message.
