# Verses

## Question
What does 'everlasting gospel' (euangelion aionion) mean in Rev 14:6? Is this the same gospel Paul preached, or a different one? What does the anarthrous construction imply? How does euangelisai connect to the OT basar vocabulary chain (Isa 40:9, 52:7, 61:1)? What is the content of a gospel called 'everlasting'? How does the imperial euangelia thesis relate? Does it pass the Galatians 1:6-9 test?

### The First Angel: Everlasting Gospel Proclaimed with Judgment-Hour Content

> And I saw another angel fly in the midst of heaven, having the everlasting gospel to preach unto them that dwell on the earth, and to every nation, and kindred, and tongue, and people, Saying with a loud voice, Fear God, and give glory to him; for the hour of his judgment is come: and worship him that made heaven, and earth, and the sea, and the fountains of waters.

**Context:** Rev 14:1-5 introduces the 144,000 with the Lamb on Mount Zion. The first angel in v.6 appears immediately after this scene of the redeemed. The three angels' messages (vv.6-12) form a unified proclamation identified by the term euangelion aionion. This is the crux text for the study: the phrase 'everlasting gospel' appears nowhere else in Scripture.

### Second Angel: Babylon Is Fallen — Structural Component of the Everlasting Gospel

> And there followed another angel, saying, Babylon is fallen, is fallen, that great city, because she made all nations drink of the wine of the wrath of her fornication.

**Context:** The second angel follows the first, extending the everlasting gospel proclamation. The doubled 'is fallen, is fallen' (epesen epesen) is emphatic aorist. The perfect tense pepotiken ('has made drink') presents Babylon's corrupting influence as a completed state with ongoing results.

**Notes:** The second angel's message is structurally part of the euangelion aionion announced in v.6. The mirrored genitive chains in vv.8 and 10 (wine of wrath of fornication / wine of wrath of God) link Babylon's cup with God's judgment cup.

### Third Angel: Warning Against Beast Worship — The Testing Truth

> And the third angel followed them, saying with a loud voice, If any man worship the beast and his image, and receive his mark in his forehead, or in his hand, The same shall drink of the wine of the wrath of God, which is poured out without mixture into the cup of his indignation; and he shall be tormented with fire and brimstone in the presence of the holy angels, and in the presence of the Lamb: And the smoke of their torment ascendeth up for ever and ever: and they have no rest day nor night, who worship the beast and his image, and whosoever receiveth the mark of his name.

**Context:** The third angel's warning completes the three-part proclamation. The conditional protasis (ei tis proskynei... kai lambanei) uses present indicatives describing characteristic/ongoing action. The apodosis uses future tenses for judgment consequences. The articular present participle hoi proskynountes identifies beast-worshipers as a community by their ongoing practice.

**Notes:** The third message is the testing truth — it distinguishes two communities by their worship allegiance (beast vs. Creator). EGW identifies this as 'the messages by which the world is to be tested' (2SM 111.3).

> Here is the patience of the saints: here are they that keep the commandments of God, and the faith of Jesus.

**Context:** Rev 14:12 follows the third angel's warning and precedes the harvest scene (vv.14-16). It functions as a descriptive conclusion identifying the saints by their character: hoi terountes (the ones keeping) is an articular present active participle — substantival, not imperative. The saints are described, not commanded.

### OT Herald: Mevasseret Zion — 'Behold Your God!'

> O Zion, that bringest good tidings, get thee up into the high mountain; O Jerusalem, that bringest good tidings, lift up thy voice with strength; lift it up, be not afraid; say unto the cities of Judah, Behold your God!

**Context:** Isaiah 40 opens the 'Book of Comfort' (chs. 40-66). The herald (mevasseret, feminine Piel participle of basar) is commissioned to announce God's arrival to Zion and Jerusalem. The content is 'Behold your God!' — a theophanic announcement. The feminine form mevasseret may personify Zion/Jerusalem as the herald.

### Paul's Gospel: Received by Divine Revelation, Not Human Origin

> But I certify you, brethren, that the gospel which was preached of me is not after man. For I neither received it of man, neither was I taught it, but by the revelation of Jesus Christ.

**Context:** Paul defends the divine origin of his gospel against the Judaizers. This parallels the angel of Rev 14:6 — both the Pauline gospel and the everlasting gospel have divine, not human, origin.

**Notes:** To euangelion to euangelisthen hyp emou (v.11) — double article construction with aorist passive participle. The gospel has a divine source (di apokalypseos Iesou Christou, v.12).

### Paul's Gospel Confronted: Circumcision as the Specific Heresy

> And I went up by revelation, and communicated unto them that gospel which I preach among the Gentiles, but privately to them which were of reputation, lest by any means I should run, or had run, in vain. But neither Titus, who was with me, being a Greek, was compelled to be circumcised: And that because of false brethren unawares brought in, who came in privily to spy out our liberty which we have in Christ Jesus, that they might bring us into bondage: To whom we gave place by subjection, no, not for an hour; that the truth of the gospel might continue with you.

**Context:** Paul identifies the specific issue: circumcision. Titus (a Greek) was NOT compelled to be circumcised. The pseudadelphoi (false brethren) sought to spy out their liberty in Christ and bring them into bondage — the bondage of requiring circumcision for salvation.

**Notes:** The Galatian heresy is specifically circumcision-as-salvation-requirement, not the addition of eschatological or ethical content to the gospel message.

### Circumcision vs. Faith Working Through Love

> Stand fast therefore in the liberty wherewith Christ hath made us free, and be not entangled again with the yoke of bondage. Behold, I Paul say unto you, that if ye be circumcised, Christ shall profit you nothing. For I testify again to every man that is circumcised, that he is a debtor to do the whole law. Christ is become of no effect unto you, whosoever of you are justified by the law; ye are fallen from grace. For we through the Spirit wait for the hope of righteousness by faith. For in Jesus Christ neither circumcision availeth any thing, nor uncircumcision; but faith which worketh by love.

**Context:** Paul draws the conclusion: circumcision for justification = fallen from grace. But the positive formulation in v.6 — 'faith working through love' (pistis di agapes energoumene) — shows that Paul's gospel is not antinomian. Faith produces love, and love fulfills the moral law (Gal 5:14).

**Notes:** Gal 5:6 is the Pauline parallel to Rev 14:12. Both texts combine faith and active obedience: pistis di agapes energoumene (faith working through love) parallels hoi terountes tas entolas tou Theou kai ten pistin Iesou (those keeping God's commandments and the faith of Jesus).

### Paul's 'First Importance' Gospel Summary — Priority, Not Exclusivity

> Moreover, brethren, I declare unto you the gospel which I preached unto you, which also ye have received, and wherein ye stand; By which also ye are saved, if ye keep in memory what I preached unto you, unless ye have believed in vain. For I delivered unto you first of all that which I also received, how that Christ died for our sins according to the scriptures; And that he was buried, and that he rose again the third day according to the scriptures:

**Context:** Paul's most concentrated gospel summary. En protois (v.3) means 'among the first things' or 'of first importance' — dative plural of protos (G4413). This is a priority statement, not an exclusivity statement. Paul gives a DIFFERENT gospel summary in Rom 1:1-4.

**Notes:** En protois: en (preposition, 'among/in') + protois (dative plural masculine of protos, G4413). The dative plural with en indicates 'among the first things' — what is most important, not what is exclusively definitive. If 1 Cor 15:3-4 were the COMPLETE definition of the gospel, Paul would be preaching a 'different gospel' in Rom 1:1-4 — which fails his own Gal 1:6-9 test.

### Paul's Second Gospel Summary — Descended from David, Declared Son of God

> Paul, a servant of Jesus Christ, called to be an apostle, separated unto the gospel of God, (Which he had promised afore by his prophets in the holy scriptures,) Concerning his Son Jesus Christ our Lord, which was made of the seed of David according to the flesh; And declared to be the Son of God with power, according to the spirit of holiness, by the resurrection from the dead:

**Context:** Paul's opening to Romans provides a different gospel summary from 1 Cor 15:3-4. Here the gospel includes Davidic descent, prophetic fulfillment, and declaration as Son of God through resurrection. This proves the gospel is broader than any single summary.

**Notes:** Euangelion Theou (v.1) is anarthrous — same pattern as euangelion aionion in Rev 14:6. The genitive Theou identifies source/origin. Verse 2 explicitly ties the gospel to OT prophetic promises ('which he had promised afore by his prophets').

### Gospel as Power of God unto Salvation — Righteousness by Faith

> For I am not ashamed of the gospel of Christ: for it is the power of God unto salvation to every one that believeth; to the Jew first, and also to the Greek. For therein is the righteousness of God revealed from faith to faith: as it is written, The just shall live by faith.

**Context:** Paul identifies the gospel as God's saving power for all who believe. The gospel includes the revelation of God's righteousness (dikaiosyne Theou) — a concept far broader than death-burial-resurrection alone.

**Notes:** The gospel includes righteousness-by-faith theology. This connects to Rev 14:12's pistis Iesou — if the faith of Jesus is the means by which righteousness is received, then Rev 14:12 is expressing core Pauline soteriology.

### Judgment 'According to My Gospel' — Paul Includes Judgment in Gospel Content

> In the day when God shall judge the secrets of men by Jesus Christ according to my gospel.

**Context:** Paul explicitly includes judgment in 'my gospel.' If judgment is part of Paul's gospel, then the judgment announcement in Rev 14:7 does not violate the Pauline gospel — it IS the Pauline gospel applied eschatologically.

**Notes:** Kata to euangelion mou — 'according to my gospel.' This is one of three texts (with Luke 3:18 and Rev 14:6-7) that explicitly label the combined gospel+judgment message as 'gospel.' Triple witness.

### Gospel as Mystery Kept Secret Since the World Began

> Now to him that is of power to stablish you according to my gospel, and the preaching of Jesus Christ, according to the revelation of the mystery, which was kept secret since the world began, But now is made manifest, and by the scriptures of the prophets, according to the commandment of the everlasting God, made known to all nations for the obedience of faith:

**Context:** Paul describes the gospel as a mystery with pre-creation origin. The phrase 'since the world began' (chronois aioniois) uses the same adjective (aionios) as Rev 14:6. The gospel is made known 'to all nations for the obedience of faith' — universal scope paralleling Rev 14:6's 'every nation.'

**Notes:** Chronois aioniois (v.25) uses the dative plural of aionios — the same adjective that modifies euangelion in Rev 14:6. The 'everlasting God' (tou aioniou Theou, v.26) uses the same adjective again. The gospel is eternal because the God behind it is eternal.

### Chosen Before the Foundation of the World — Pre-Creation Gospel Purpose

> According as he hath chosen us in him before the foundation of the world, that we should be holy and without blame before him in love:

**Context:** Paul traces election to before the foundation of the world (pro kataboles kosmou). This is one of four independent NT witnesses to the gospel's pre-creation origin (with 2 Tim 1:9, 1 Pet 1:20, Rev 13:8).

**Notes:** Pro kataboles kosmou — 'before the laying down of the world.' The gospel plan is not a reaction to sin but a pre-creation purpose.

### Lamb Foreordained Before the Foundation of the World

> Forasmuch as ye know that ye were not redeemed with corruptible things, as silver and gold, from your vain conversation received by tradition from your fathers; But with the precious blood of Christ, as of a lamb without blemish and without spot: Who verily was foreordained before the foundation of the world, but was manifest in these last times for you,

**Context:** Peter confirms the pre-creation origin of the gospel plan. The Lamb was foreordained (proegnosmenou) before the foundation of the world (pro kataboles kosmou) but manifested in the last times.

**Notes:** Independent witness to the pre-creation gospel plan, confirming the 'everlasting' character of the gospel in Rev 14:6.

### Lamb Slain from the Foundation of the World

> And all that dwell upon the earth shall worship him, whose names are not written in the book of life of the Lamb slain from the foundation of the world.

**Context:** The Lamb is described as 'slain from the foundation of the world' (apo kataboles kosmou). This text immediately precedes the three angels' messages in Rev 14, connecting the beast-worship crisis to the pre-creation gospel purpose.

**Notes:** The phrase 'from the foundation of the world' links directly to the 'everlasting' character of the gospel in 14:6. The gospel is everlasting because the Lamb was slain (in purpose) from the world's foundation.

### Eternal Life Promised Before the World Began — Second Pro Chronon Aionion

> In hope of eternal life, which God, that cannot lie, promised before the world began; But hath in due times manifested his word through preaching, which is committed unto me according to the commandment of God our Saviour;

**Context:** The same phrase pro chronon aionion appears here as in 2 Tim 1:9. God promised eternal life before eternal times — confirming the backward reach of aionios. The gospel was 'manifested through preaching' at the right time.

**Notes:** Second occurrence of pro chronon aionion in the Pastorals. Zoes aioniou ('eternal life') uses the same adjective that modifies euangelion in Rev 14:6. If aionios means merely 'age-long,' then the life God promises is temporary — which no interpreter accepts.

### Jesus Quotes Isa 61:1 — 'This Day Is This Scripture Fulfilled'

> The Spirit of the Lord is upon me, because he hath anointed me to preach the gospel to the poor; he hath sent me to heal the brokenhearted, to preach deliverance to the captives, and recovering of sight to the blind, to set at liberty them that are bruised, To preach the acceptable year of the Lord. And he closed the book, and he gave it again to the minister, and sat down. And the eyes of all them that were in the synagogue were fastened on him. And he began to say unto them, This day is this scripture fulfilled in your ears.

**Context:** Jesus reads Isa 61:1-2a in the Nazareth synagogue and declares its fulfillment in himself. He uses euangelisasthai for Isaiah's levassar, creating the personal basar-euangelizo bridge. He stops before 'the day of vengeance' — distinguishing his first advent mission from the judgment aspect.

### Harvest Scene Following the Three Angels — Proclamation Leads to the Second Coming

> And I looked, and behold a white cloud, and upon the cloud one sat like unto the Son of man, having on his head a golden crown, and in his hand a sharp sickle. And another angel came out of the temple, crying with a loud voice to him that sat on the cloud, Thrust in thy sickle, and reap: for the time is come for thee to reap; for the harvest of the earth is ripe. And he that sat on the cloud thrust in his sickle on the earth; and the earth was reaped.

**Context:** The harvest scene immediately follows the three angels' messages (vv.6-12). The Son of man comes 'on a white cloud' to reap the earth. The everlasting gospel proclamation directly precedes and prepares for the second coming. The sequence is: gospel proclaimed → world divides into two communities (v.12 vs. v.11) → harvest/judgment.

**Notes:** The structural sequence: everlasting gospel (v.6-7) → Babylon's fall (v.8) → beast-worship warning (vv.9-11) → saints identified (v.12) → Son of man comes to harvest (vv.14-16). The three angels' messages prepare for the eschatological harvest.

### Paul's Pre-Conversion Zeal — Context for the Galatian Heresy

> For ye have heard of my conversation in time past in the Jews' religion, how that beyond measure I persecuted the church of God, and wasted it: And profited in the Jews' religion above many my equals in mine own nation, being more exceedingly zealous of the traditions of my fathers.

**Context:** Paul describes his pre-conversion life in Judaism — zealous for 'the traditions of my fathers.' This provides context for understanding the Galatian heresy: the Judaizers wanted to impose the same traditions (circumcision, ceremonial law) on Gentile believers as a salvation requirement.

**Notes:** Perisspoteros zelotes hyparchon ton patrikon mou paradoseon — 'being more exceedingly zealous of the traditions of my fathers.' Paul's former zeal for Jewish traditions parallels the Judaizers' demand for circumcision. The 'different gospel' he condemns is a return to this works-based system.

### Glorious Gospel of Christ — Gospel Vocabulary Diversity

> But if our gospel be hid, it is hid to them that are lost: In whom the god of this world hath blinded the minds of them which believe not, lest the light of the glorious gospel of Christ, who is the image of God, should shine unto them.

**Context:** Paul uses another gospel designation: 'the glorious gospel of Christ' (to euangelion tes doxes tou Christou). The genitive chain (gospel of the glory of Christ) connects glory and gospel — the same connection as Rev 14:7's 'give him glory.' The gospel is described as 'light' that illuminates.

**Notes:** To euangelion tes doxes tou Christou — anarthrous euangelion in some manuscripts, articular in others. The 'glory' (doxa) connection links to Rev 14:7's dote auto doxan ('give him glory'). Another of the 22+ NT gospel designations.

### Striving Together for the Faith of the Gospel — Pistis + Euangelion

> Only let your conversation be as it becometh the gospel of Christ: that whether I come and see you, or else be absent, I may hear of your affairs, that ye stand fast in one spirit, with one mind striving together for the faith of the gospel;

**Context:** Paul combines pistis (faith) and euangelion (gospel): te pistei tou euangeliou ('the faith of the gospel'). This connects to Rev 14:12's ten pistin Iesou coordinated with the everlasting gospel. The gospel has a 'faith' that believers 'strive together for.'

**Notes:** Te pistei tou euangeliou — the faith of/belonging to the gospel. Euangelion here is anarthrous in the genitive (tou euangeliou), like Rev 14:6. The combination of faith + gospel in Phil 1:27 parallels the combination in Rev 14:6+12.

### Gospel of God + Word of God — Gospel as Divine Word, Not Human Invention

> So being affectionately desirous of you, we were willing to have imparted unto you, not the gospel of God only, but also our own souls, because ye were dear unto us. For ye remember, brethren, our labour and travail: for labouring night and day, because we would not be chargeable unto any of you, we preached unto you the gospel of God. For this cause also thank we God without ceasing, because, when ye received the word of God which ye heard of us, ye received it not as the word of men, but as it is in truth, the word of God, which effectually worketh also in you that believe.

**Context:** Paul uses 'the gospel of God' (to euangelion tou Theou) twice (vv.8-9) and then identifies it as 'the word of God' (logon Theou) which is 'not the word of men.' The gospel has divine origin — paralleling both Gal 1:11-12 (not after man) and Rev 14:6 (an angel carrying the gospel from heaven).

**Notes:** To euangelion tou Theou (vv.8-9) — the gospel belongs to God, not to human inventors. Logon Theou (v.13) — the gospel IS the word of God. Divine origin confirms the gospel is one and unchanging — the same whether preached by Paul or proclaimed by the angel of Rev 14:6.
