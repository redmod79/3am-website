4. **Graduated accountability — Luke 12:47-48, Acts 17:30, 1 Timothy 1:13:**
Luke 12:47-48 is the clearest graduated-punishment text — the servant who KNEW (gnous) his master's will and did not do it receives many stripes (daresetai pollas); the one who did not know (me gnous) receives few (oligas). Run greek_parser.py on Luke 12:47-48. The question: does 'few stripes' mean SOME punishment or REDUCED punishment? Acts 17:30 introduces hupereidon (G5237, hapax) — God 'overlooked/winked at' times of ignorance. READ context: this does NOT mean ignorance was sinless, but that God withheld active punishment during the ignorance era. Compare with Rom 3:25 paresis (passing over) — similar concept with different vocabulary. For 1 Tim 1:13, Paul says he obtained mercy (eleethein) BECAUSE he acted 'ignorantly in unbelief' (agnoōn en apistia). Run greek_parser.py on 1 Tim 1:13. The commentators distinguish Paul's ignorance (sincere but wrong — vincible ignorance) from the Pharisees' willful blindness. Cross-reference with John 9:41 ('If ye were blind, ye should have no sin') and John 15:22 ('If I had not come and spoken, they had not had sin'). Also check Jas 4:17 — sin defined relationally to knowledge.
**Verses:** Luke 12:47-48, Acts 17:30, 1 Tim 1:13, John 9:41, John 15:22-24, Jas 4:17, Rom 3:25, Rom 2:12-16
**Strong's:** G5237, G52, G1097, G1922

5. **Can the mark be resisted? — Revelation 15:2 and the nikao pattern:**
Rev 15:2 describes tous nikōntas ek tou thēriou kai ek tēs eikonos autou kai ek tou arithmou tou onomatos autou — 'those overcoming out of the beast and out of his image and out of the number of his name.' READ $WORD_STUDIES/G3528-nikao.md. The present active participle nikōntas indicates ongoing conquering. Note the triple ek (out of) — they overcome the beast, the image, AND the mark (number). Run greek_parser.py on Rev 15:2. Cross-reference with Rev 12:11 — the MEANS of overcoming: 'by the blood of the Lamb and by the word of their testimony.' The prior study (3am-14) noted that Rev 13:15-17 uses hina + subjunctive for the coercion — poiei pantas hina = 'he MAKES all that they should' — purpose clause, not result clause. The mark is ATTEMPTED on all, not successfully imposed on all. Also check Rev 13:7-10: the beast is given authority to 'make war with the saints and to overcome (nikēsai) them' — but this is military/political defeat, not spiritual capitulation. Rev 20:4 confirms the binary: those who reign are defined by 'did not worship...did not receive' — three aorist negations. READ $WORD_STUDIES/G5480-charagma.md and $WORD_STUDIES/G5281-hupomone.md (endurance of the saints).
**Verses:** Rev 15:2, Rev 12:11, Rev 13:7-10, Rev 13:15-17, Rev 20:4
**Strong's:** G3528, G3534, G5480, G5281
