    Topics Research¶

    Question¶

    What does 'everlasting gospel' (euangelion aionion) mean in Rev 14:6? Is this the same gospel Paul preached, or a different one? What does the anarthrous construction imply? How does euangelisai connect to the OT basar vocabulary chain (Isa 40:9, 52:7, 61:1)? What is the content of a gospel called 'everlasting'? How does the imperial euangelia thesis relate? Does it pass the Galatians 1:6-9 test?

    PREACHING — The act of exhorting, prophesying, reproving, teaching. The Gospel of the Kingdom of God is the subject of Christ's preaching (Mrk 1:14-15; Luk 8:1). Christ crucified and risen is the burden of Paul's (Act 17:3). Unclassified Scriptures include 1Co 1:17-18,21,23,27-29; 2:1-8; Col 1:23-29; 1Th 1:5; Tit 1:3.

    CIRCUMCISION — Institution of (Gen 17:10-14; Lev 12:3). A seal of righteousness (Rom 2:25-29; 4:11). Necessity of, falsely taught by Judaizing Christians (Act 15:1). Abrogated (Act 15:5-29; Rom 3:30; 4:9-11; 1Co 7:18-19; Gal 2:3-4; 5:2-11; 6:12; Eph 2:11,15; Col 2:11; 3:11). Paul's argument against continuance (Rom 2:25,28; Gal 6:13). Characterized by Paul as a yoke (Act 15:10). Figurative (Exo 6:12; Deu 10:16; 30:6; Jer 4:4; Rom 2:28-29; Php 3:3; Col 2:11).
