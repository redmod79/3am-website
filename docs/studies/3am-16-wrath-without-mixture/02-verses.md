# Verses

## Question
What does 'the wine of the wrath of God, which is poured out without mixture into the cup of his indignation' (Rev 14:10) mean? How do thumos (G2372) and orge (G3709) function together? What does akratos (G194, unmixed) mean? What does 'tormented with fire and brimstone in the presence of the holy angels, and in the presence of the Lamb' (Rev 14:10) mean? What does 'the smoke of their torment ascendeth up for ever and ever' (Rev 14:11) mean — eternal conscious torment or permanent destruction? Address using OT prophetic convention: Isaiah 34:10 (Edom), Jude 7 (Sodom). How does Malachi 4:1-3 define the final outcome? What does 'no rest day nor night' describe?

### The Third Angel's Warning — Primary Text

> And the third angel followed them, saying with a loud voice, If any man worship the beast and his image, and receive [his] mark in his forehead, or in his hand,
The same shall drink of the wine of the wrath of God, which is poured out without mixture into the cup of his indignation; and he shall be tormented with fire and brimstone in the presence of the holy angels, and in the presence of the Lamb:
And the smoke of their torment ascendeth up for ever and ever: and they have no rest day nor night, who worship the beast and his image, and whosoever receiveth the mark of his name.

**Context:** Revelation 14 presents three angels delivering sequential messages. The first angel proclaims the everlasting gospel and calls for worship of the Creator (14:6-7). The second angel announces Babylon's fall (14:8). The third angel delivers the most severe warning in Scripture — those who worship the beast receive God's unmixed wrath. The polarity flip from 14:8 to 14:10 is structural: Babylon's wine (seductive deception) becomes God's wine (undiluted judgment). The chapter then contrasts the wicked's fate with the blessed dead who 'rest from their labours' (14:13).

**Notes:** CRITICAL TENSE DATA: Rev 14:10 basanisthesetai is Future Passive Indicative 3rd Sg. Rev 14:11 anabainei is Present Active Indicative 3rd Sg, and echousin is Present Active Indicative 3rd Pl. The shift from future (torment) to present (smoke ascending, no rest) is confirmed by parser output. The genitive chain in 14:10: tou oinou tou thymou tou Theou tou kekerasmenou akratou en to poterio tes orges autou. Wine (oinos) associates with thumos (genitive of content); cup (poterion) associates with orge (genitive of characterization). Bengel's distinction confirmed: wine = thumos (fierce passion), cup = orge (settled judicial anger). The participle proskunountes (present active, v.11) describes habitual action — those who worship (as ongoing practice).

### Blessed Dead REST from Labours — Rest Vocabulary Contrast

> And I heard a voice from heaven saying unto me, Write, Blessed [are] the dead which die in the Lord from henceforth: Yea, saith the Spirit, that they may rest from their labours; and their works do follow them.

**Context:** Immediately following the third angel's warning. The contrast is deliberate: those who worship the beast have 'no rest' (14:11), while those who die in the Lord 'rest from their labours' (14:13). The same anapausis vocabulary family appears in both verses.

**Notes:** The verb in 14:13 is anapausontai (future middle indicative of anapauo, G373) — the cognate verb of anapausis (G372) used in 14:11. The righteous REST; the beast-worshippers have NO rest. This inversion is a structural argument.

### Thumos and Orge Paired Again — Cup of the Wine of Fierceness of Wrath

> And the great city was divided into three parts, and the cities of the nations fell: and great Babylon came in remembrance before God, to give unto her the cup of the wine of the fierceness of his wrath.

**Context:** The seventh bowl judgment. Babylon receives the cup. The genitive chain here: to poterion tou oinou tou thymou tes orges autou — four genitives (cup of wine of thumos of orge of his). This is the same thumos-orge pairing as 14:10 but with escalated genitive stacking.

**Notes:** Parser confirms: poterion (G4221, cup), oinou (G3631, wine), thymou (G2372, fierceness), orges (G3709, wrath). Four-genitive chain. In 14:10 the structure is wine-thumos-God plus cup-orge-his. Here all four are stacked in a single chain. The escalation continues to 19:15.

### Five-Stacked Genitive Chain — Thumos Climax at the Winepress

> And out of his mouth goeth a sharp sword, that with it he should smite the nations: and he shall rule them with a rod of iron: and he treadeth the winepress of the fierceness and wrath of Almighty God.

**Context:** Christ's return. The longest genitive chain in Revelation: ten lenon tou oinou tou thymou tes orges tou Theou tou Pantokratoros. Five stacked genitives: winepress of wine of thumos of orge of God of Almighty. This is the climactic expression of the wrath trajectory that began in 14:8.

**Notes:** Parser confirms: lenon (G3025, winepress), oinou (G3631, wine), thymou (G2372, fierceness), orges (G3709, wrath), Theou (G2316, God), Pantokratoros (G3841, Almighty). Five genitives. The escalation from 14:10 (3 genitives) to 16:19 (4 genitives) to 19:15 (5 genitives) is structural. Thumos appears in all three; orge appears in all three. They are not interchangeable but complementary — thumos = the burning intensity, orge = the judicial implementation.

### Living Creatures 'Rest Not Day and Night' — Identical Construction to Rev 14:11

> And the four beasts had each of them six wings about [him]; and [they were] full of eyes within: and they rest not day and night, saying, Holy, holy, holy, Lord God Almighty, which was, and is, and is to come.

**Context:** Heavenly throne room vision. The four living creatures praise God ceaselessly. The phrase 'rest not day and night' uses identical vocabulary and grammar as Rev 14:11's 'no rest day nor night.'

**Notes:** Parser confirms exact parallel: Rev 4:8 has anapausin ouk echousin hemeras kai nuktos — same word anapausis (G372, accusative singular feminine), same verb echo (G2192, present active indicative 3rd plural), same temporal genitives hemeras kai nuktos. Rev 14:11 has ouk echousin anapausin hemeras kai nuktos. IDENTICAL construction. Same word, same tense, same grammar, same book, same author. If the construction in 4:8 describes uninterrupted activity quality rather than ontological eternal duration (the living creatures are not praised into eternal existence — they praise as an ongoing quality of activity), then the same construction in 14:11 need not require eternal conscious existence either.

### The Cup of Wine Full of Mixture — OT Source that Rev 14:10 Reverses

> For in the hand of the LORD [there is] a cup, and the wine is red; it is full of mixture; and he poureth out of the same: but the dregs thereof, all the wicked of the earth shall wring [them] out, [and] drink [them].

**Context:** A psalm of Asaph. God is the judge (v.7) who puts down one and raises another. The cup in God's hand contains mixed wine — the Hebrew mesek (H4469) meaning mixture. The wicked must drink even the dregs. This is the foundational OT cup-of-wrath passage that Rev 14:10 deliberately reverses.

**Notes:** Hebrew parser confirms: kowc (H3563, cup), yayin (H3196, wine), male (full), mesek (H4469, mixture). The Psalm describes wine 'full of mixture' — Rev 14:10 describes wine 'mixed unmixed' (kekerasmenou akratou). The reversal is deliberate: OT judgments were tempered (mixed with mercy); the eschatological judgment is untempered (akratos, unmixed). This is confirmed by the word study on G194 akratos.

### Jerusalem's Cup of Wrath — Trajectory Passage

> Awake, awake, stand up, O Jerusalem, which hast drunk at the hand of the LORD the cup of his fury; thou hast drunken the dregs of the cup of trembling, [and] wrung [them] out.
Thus saith thy Lord the LORD, and thy God [that] pleadeth the cause of his people, Behold, I have taken out of thine hand the cup of trembling, [even] the dregs of the cup of my fury; thou shalt no more drink it again:

**Context:** Isaiah addresses Jerusalem. God gave Jerusalem the cup of His fury to drink, but now removes it. The trajectory: Jerusalem drank the cup and survived — God removed it. In the eschatological application (Rev 14:10), there is no removal. The cup is poured out akratos (unmixed).

**Notes:** Hebrew vocabulary: kowc (H3563, cup), chemah (H2534, fury/heat — the Hebrew equivalent of Greek thumos). The LXX renders chemah as thumos in wrath passages, establishing the Hebrew-Greek bridge. The cup-of-wrath trajectory: Psa 75:8 (mixed) -> Isa 51:17 (drunk but removed) -> Jer 25:15 (given to nations) -> Rev 14:10 (unmixed, no removal).

### The Cup of Wrath Given to All Nations

> For thus saith the LORD God of Israel unto me; Take the wine cup of this fury at my hand, and cause all the nations, to whom I send thee, to drink it.
And they shall drink, and be moved, and be mad, because of the sword that I will send among them.
Then took I the cup at the LORD'S hand, and made all the nations to drink, unto whom the LORD had sent me:
Therefore thou shalt say unto them, Thus saith the LORD of hosts, the God of Israel; Drink ye, and be drunken, and spue, and fall, and rise no more, because of the sword which I will send among you.
And it shall be, if they refuse to take the cup at thine hand to drink, then shalt thou say unto them, Thus saith the LORD of hosts; Ye shall certainly drink.

**Context:** The broadest OT cup-of-wrath passage. God commands Jeremiah to take the cup of fury and give it to all nations. The phrase 'rise no more' (v.27) indicates finality. The cup cannot be refused (v.28).

**Notes:** This passage establishes the universality of the cup-of-wrath tradition. Key vocabulary: yayin (H3196, wine), chemah (H2534, fury), kowc (H3563, cup). The phrase 'rise no more' is destruction vocabulary, not torment vocabulary.

### Babylon's Golden Cup — Source for the Polarity Flip

> Babylon [hath been] a golden cup in the LORD'S hand, that made all the earth drunken: the nations have drunken of her wine; therefore the nations are mad.

**Context:** Jeremiah's oracle against Babylon. Babylon herself is described as a golden cup in God's hand that intoxicated the nations. This is the direct OT source for Rev 14:8 (Babylon makes nations drink) and the polarity flip to 14:10 (God makes beast-worshippers drink).

**Notes:** The polarity flip documented in 3am-11: Babylon's cup (Jer 51:7 / Rev 14:8) contains seductive wine; God's cup (Rev 14:10) contains judgment wine. The terminal genitive swaps from 'her fornication' to 'God's wrath.'

### The Sister-Cup Passage — Cup of Astonishment and Desolation

> Thou hast walked in the way of thy sister; therefore will I give her cup into thine hand.
Thus saith the Lord GOD; Thou shalt drink of thy sister's cup deep and large: thou shalt be laughed to scorn and had in derision; it containeth much.
Thou shalt be filled with drunkenness and sorrow, with the cup of astonishment and desolation, with the cup of thy sister Samaria.
Thou shalt even drink it and suck [it] out, and thou shalt break the sherds thereof, and pluck off thine own breasts: for I have spoken [it], saith the Lord GOD.

**Context:** Ezekiel's allegory of Jerusalem and Samaria as sisters. Jerusalem must drink the same cup Samaria drank — a cup of desolation and destruction. The imagery of drinking every last drop and breaking the cup itself emphasizes thoroughness.

**Notes:** Adds 'desolation' vocabulary to the cup tradition. The cup produces astonishment and desolation — destruction imagery, not eternal torment.

### Edom's Smoke Ascending Forever — Primary OT Parallel to Rev 14:11

> And the streams thereof shall be turned into pitch, and the dust thereof into brimstone, and the land thereof shall become burning pitch.
It shall not be quenched night nor day; the smoke thereof shall go up for ever: from generation to generation it shall lie waste; none shall pass through it for ever and ever.

**Context:** Isaiah's oracle against Edom. The destruction is described with maximal 'forever' language: smoke ascending forever, fire unquenched night nor day, waste from generation to generation. Yet verse 11 describes animals dwelling in the ruins — proving the 'forever' language is rhetorical convention for completed, permanent destruction, not literal endless burning.

**Notes:** Hebrew parser confirms: le-olam (H5769, forever) for the smoke ascending, and le-netsach netsachim (H5331, perpetuity of perpetuities) — the STRONGEST Hebrew 'forever' form — yet animals dwell there (v.11-17). Verbal parallel to Rev 14:11 confirmed by cross-testament parallels tool (Jesaia 34:10 scored highest at 0.401 for Rev 14:11 OT parallels). LXX of Isa 34:10 uses aiona chronon while Revelation uses eis aionas aionon — intensified form, but both referring to completed destruction.

### Animals Dwelling in Edom's Ruins — Proof the 'Forever' Was Rhetorical

> But the cormorant and the bittern shall possess it; the owl also and the raven shall dwell in it: and he shall stretch out upon it the line of confusion, and the stones of emptiness.
They shall call the nobles thereof to the kingdom, but none [shall be] there, and all her princes shall be nothing.
And thorns shall come up in her palaces, nettles and brambles in the fortresses thereof: and it shall be an habitation of dragons, [and] a court for owls.
The wild beasts of the desert shall also meet with the wild beasts of the island, and the satyr shall cry to his fellow; the screech owl also shall rest there, and find for herself a place of rest.
There shall the great owl make her nest, and lay, and hatch, and gather under her shadow: there shall the vultures also be gathered, every one with her mate.
Seek ye out of the book of the LORD, and read: no one of these shall fail, none shall want her mate: for my mouth it hath commanded, and his spirit it hath gathered them.
And he hath cast the lot for them, and his hand hath divided it unto them by line: they shall possess it for ever, from generation to generation shall they dwell therein.

**Context:** Continuation of the Edom oracle. After describing 'forever' fire and smoke (34:10), Isaiah describes animals making nests and dwelling in the ruins. The same phrase 'for ever, from generation to generation' now describes animal habitation — the land is desolate, not still burning. This is the decisive proof that 'smoke ascending forever' is OT prophetic convention for permanent, completed destruction.

**Notes:** The 'rest' word in v.14 (the screech owl 'rests' there) uses Hebrew nuwach — the same rest vocabulary family. Even animals find rest in what was supposedly 'forever burning.'

### Who Dwells with Everlasting Burnings? The Righteous.

> The sinners in Zion are afraid; fearfulness hath surprised the hypocrites. Who among us shall dwell with the devouring fire? who among us shall dwell with everlasting burnings?
He that walketh righteously, and speaketh uprightly; he that despiseth the gain of oppressions, that shaketh his hands from holding of bribes, that stoppeth his ears from hearing of blood, and shutteth his eyes from seeing evil;
He shall dwell on high: his place of defence [shall be] the munitions of rocks: bread shall be given him; his waters [shall be] sure.

**Context:** Isaiah asks who can dwell with the devouring fire and everlasting burnings. The answer is not 'the wicked in eternal torment' but 'the righteous' — those who walk uprightly. The fire characterizes God's nature, not a punishment chamber. The righteous dwell safely with the consuming fire; the wicked are consumed by it.

**Notes:** EGW corpus lead verified: 'Same fire is consuming fire to wicked, sun and shield to righteous' (CFL 88.4). Confirmed by this passage: the fire is one; what varies is the relationship to it. Cf. Mal 4:1-2 where the same 'day' burns like an oven for wicked but brings healing for righteous.

### The Sodom Paradigm — Fire Falls, Smoke Seen Next Morning

> Then the LORD rained upon Sodom and upon Gomorrah brimstone and fire from the LORD out of heaven;
And he overthrew those cities, and all the plain, and all the inhabitants of the cities, and that which grew upon the ground.
But his wife looked back from behind him, and she became a pillar of salt.
And Abraham gat up early in the morning to the place where he stood before the LORD:
And he looked toward Sodom and Gomorrah, and toward all the land of the plain, and beheld, and, lo, the smoke of the country went up as the smoke of a furnace.

**Context:** The foundational fire-and-brimstone judgment. Fire falls in v.24 (the agent). Abraham sees smoke the next morning in v.28 (the witness). The cities are destroyed — not eternally tormented. This establishes the fire-as-agent / smoke-as-witness distinction.

**Notes:** The smoke vocabulary in Gen 19:28 uses qiytowr (H7008), which shares the q-t-r root with qatar/qetoreth (burn incense / incense). Judgment smoke and worship smoke share the same root — both ascend 'before God.' Sodom is now ashes (2 Pet 2:6, tephroo G5077) — the paradigm for 'eternal fire' producing a completed result.

> And a mighty angel took up a stone like a great millstone, and cast [it] into the sea, saying, Thus with violence shall that great city Babylon be thrown down, and shall be found no more at all.
And the voice of harpers, and musicians, and of pipers, and trumpeters, shall be heard no more at all in thee; and no craftsman, of whatsoever craft [he be], shall be found any more in thee; and the sound of a millstone shall be heard no more at all in thee;

### The Definitive Eschatological Outcome — Ashes Under Feet

> For, behold, the day cometh, that shall burn as an oven; and all the proud, yea, and all that do wickedly, shall be stubble: and the day that cometh shall burn them up, saith the LORD of hosts, that it shall leave them neither root nor branch.
But unto you that fear my name shall the Sun of righteousness arise with healing in his wings; and ye shall go forth, and grow up as calves of the stall.
And ye shall tread down the wicked; for they shall be ashes under the soles of your feet in the day that I shall do [this], saith the LORD of hosts.

**Context:** Malachi's eschatological prophecy. The final chapter before the 400 silent years. The day burns like an oven (tanur, H8574). The wicked are stubble (qash, H7179). Neither root (sheresh, H8328) nor branch (anaph, H6057) remains. The wicked become ashes (epher, H665) under the feet of the righteous. Context (3:14-18) distinguishes righteous from wicked in a manner unfulfilled in history, establishing this as eschatological.

**Notes:** Hebrew parser confirms key vocabulary: tannuwr (H8574, oven/furnace), qash (H7179, stubble), sheresh (H8328, root), anaph (H6057, branch), epher (H665, ashes/dust). The root-and-branch idiom means total elimination. EGW interprets root = Satan, branches = followers (FLB 358.3). Whether or not one accepts that specific identification, the idiom's meaning is clear: NOTHING remains. The righteous tread on ASHES — not tormented beings. This is the most explicit OT statement of the final outcome.

### Sodom Suffered Vengeance of Eternal Fire — Yet Now Ashes

> Even as Sodom and Gomorrha, and the cities about them in like manner, giving themselves over to fornication, and going after strange flesh, are set forth for an example, suffering the vengeance of eternal fire.

**Context:** Jude uses Sodom as an example (deigma) of divine judgment. The cities 'suffer the vengeance of eternal fire' (puros aioniou). Yet 2 Peter 2:6 says God 'turned them to ashes' (tephroo). Eternal fire produced ashes — not eternal burning.

**Notes:** Parser confirms: puros (G4442, fire, genitive), aioniou (G166, eternal, genitive), diken (G1349, vengeance/justice, accusative), hupechousai (G5254, suffering/undergoing, present active participle). The present participle hupechousai ('suffering') describes the ONGOING EXAMPLE status — the ruined cities continue to serve as an example. The fire itself is not still burning. 'Eternal fire' = fire from the eternal God producing permanent results, as established in the eternal-fire prior study.

### Sodom Turned to Ashes — The Result of 'Eternal Fire'

> And turning the cities of Sodom and Gomorrha into ashes condemned [them] with an overthrow, making [them] an ensample unto those that after should live ungodly;

**Context:** Peter's parallel to Jude 7. The verb tephroo (G5077, hapax — to turn to ashes) describes Sodom's fate. The aorist participle tephrosás indicates completed action. The result is an ensample (hupodeigma, G5262) for the ungodly.

**Notes:** Parser confirms: tephrosás (G5077, aorist active participle — completed action of reducing to ashes), katastrophe (G2692, overthrow), hupodeigma (G5262, example/pattern). Peter selects tephroo rather than generic burning verbs precisely because the ash-result is the theological point: ashes are what 'eternal fire' produces.

### Subject Differentiation at the Lake of Fire — Devoured vs. Tormented

> And they went up on the breadth of the earth, and compassed the camp of the saints about, and the beloved city: and fire came down from God out of heaven, and devoured them.
And the devil that deceived them was cast into the lake of fire and brimstone, where the beast and the false prophet [are], and shall be tormented day and night for ever and ever.

**Context:** Revelation 20 narrates the post-millennium events. Human followers of Satan (v.9) are DEVOURED by fire from heaven. The devil, beast, and false prophet (v.10) are cast into the lake of fire and tormented. Different verbs for different subjects.

**Notes:** Parser confirms the vocabulary differentiation: v.9 uses katephagen (G2719, katesthio — aorist active indicative: 'devoured') for HUMAN followers. v.10 uses basanisthesontai (G928, basanizo — future passive indicative: 'shall be tormented') for the devil, beast, and false prophet. The subjects of 'tormented forever' are non-human/symbolic entities. The beast is explicitly destined for 'destruction/perdition' (apoleia, G684) in Rev 17:8,11 — yet later 'tormented.' Stott argues the beast and false prophet are 'powers of evil, not individual persons.' Rev 20:14-15 uses 'second death' (thanatos, G2288) for human wicked.

> And death and hell were cast into the lake of fire. This is the second death.
And whosoever was not found written in the book of life was cast into the lake of fire.

**Context:** The lake of fire is explicitly defined as 'the second death.' Human wicked are described with death vocabulary, not torment vocabulary. Death and Hades themselves are destroyed — the last enemy abolished (1 Cor 15:26).

If death is the last enemy destroyed, then death ceases to exist — and those in the 'second death' cease to exist.

### Perish vs. Eternal Life — The Fundamental Binary

> For God so loved the world, that he gave his only begotten Son, that whosoever believeth in him should not perish, but have everlasting life.

**Context:** The most familiar verse in Scripture. The binary is explicit: PERISH (apollymi, G622) vs. ETERNAL LIFE (zoe aionios). Not 'eternal torment vs. eternal bliss' but 'destruction vs. life.'

**Notes:** Apollymi (G622) — 'to destroy fully, to perish.' The fundamental binary in didactic teaching is death/destruction vs. life, not torment vs. bliss.

### Wages of Sin Is Death — Not Eternal Torment

> For the wages of sin [is] death; but the gift of God [is] eternal life through Jesus Christ our Lord.

**Context:** Paul's clearest statement of the binary. The wage earned by sin is DEATH (thanatos, G2288), not eternal torment. The gift counterpart is eternal LIFE. The contrast demands that death means the opposite of life.

**Notes:** Thanatos (G2288, death) as the wage. The opposite of eternal life is death — cessation of life, not continued existence in torment.

### Fear Him Who Can Destroy Both Soul and Body in Gehenna

> And fear not them which kill the body, but are not able to kill the soul: but rather fear him which is able to destroy both soul and body in hell.

**Context:** Jesus teaches his disciples about true vs. false fear. Humans can kill the body. God can destroy (apollymi, G622) BOTH soul (psyche, G5590) AND body in gehenna. If God destroys the soul, the soul ceases to exist.

**Context:** Paul describes the final state: ALL enemies destroyed (including death itself), God 'all in all.' If evil persists eternally in a torture chamber, God is not 'all in all' — evil coexists eternally with God.

**Notes:** Parser confirms 1 Cor 15:26: katargeitai (G2673, katargeo — present passive: 'is being destroyed/abolished') applied to thanatos (G2288, death). Death is the LAST enemy destroyed. If death itself ceases to exist, then the 'second death' is not an eternal state but a final act of destruction.
