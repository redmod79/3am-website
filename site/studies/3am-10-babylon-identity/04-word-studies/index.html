    Word Studies¶

    Question¶

    What is Babylon in Revelation 14:8? Is it Rome, a past empire, a future rebuilt city, or a religious system? Why does the harlotry vocabulary (porneia, porne) require a prior covenant relationship with God — making Babylon a corrupted form of the true faith rather than mere paganism? What does the zanah/porneia word chain from the OT prophets (Ezek 16, 23, Hosea 2) reveal about Babylon's character? What does the mother-daughter structure (Rev 17:5, 'MOTHER OF HARLOTS') reveal about Babylon's reproductive capacity? How does the balal etymology (Gen 11:9, 'confusion') establish mixture/confusion as Babylon's essential character? Address the preterist reading (Babylon = 1st-century Rome) and the futurist reading (Babylon = literal rebuilt city) — show why the harlot-bride bracket structure (Rev 17:1 and 21:9 using the same deictic formula) requires a symbolic religious-system reading.

    H2181 — zanah¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H2181-zanah.md

    Occurrences: 107

    Core OT verb for harlotry/fornication. 107 tokens in 81 verses across 19 books. Dominant in Ezekiel (19x) and Hosea (11x). Semantic range spans literal prostitution, covenant-unfaithfulness (going after other gods), and prophetic metaphor for spiritual adultery. In the prophets, zanah is almost always used for entities in covenant relationship with YHWH who become unfaithful. The two possible exceptions — Tyre (Isa 23:15-17) and Nineveh (Nah 3:4) — may both have had treaty/covenant relationships with Israel.

    Semantic categories: literal prostitution, covenant people going after other gods, prophetic metaphor for spiritual adultery, broader seductive corruption

    Key verses: Ezek 16:15, Ezek 23:3, Hos 1:2, Isa 1:21, Jer 3:1-3, Nah 3:4

    Relevance: The backbone of the study. If zanah requires prior covenant relationship, then Babylon must be a corrupted form of the true faith, not mere paganism or a purely secular empire.

    G4202 — porneia¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G4202-porneia.md

    Occurrences: 25

    NT noun for harlotry/fornication. 25 verses, 26 tokens. Highest concentration in Revelation (7 occurrences, 1.7 per 100 verses). Semantic range: marital exception (Matt 5:32, 19:9), vice list (Matt 15:19, Gal 5:19), sexual ethics (1 Cor 5-7), Jerusalem Council decree (Acts 15:20), and figurative/idolatrous (Revelation). In Revelation, porneia consistently denotes idolatrous infidelity — the NT equivalent of the OT zanah tradition.

    Semantic categories: marital exception, vice list, sexual ethics, Jerusalem Council, figurative idolatry (Revelation)

    Key verses: Rev 14:8, Rev 17:2, Rev 17:4, Rev 18:3, Rev 19:2

    Relevance: The NT continuation of the zanah word chain. Porneia in Revelation's Babylon passages carries the full weight of OT covenant-unfaithfulness vocabulary.

    G4204 — porne¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G4204-porne.md

    Occurrences: 12

    NT noun for harlot/prostitute. 12 verses, 6 books. 5 of 12 occurrences in Revelation chs. 17-19 — all referring to Babylon. Outside Revelation, used for Rahab (Heb 11:31, Jas 2:25), repentant harlots entering the kingdom (Matt 21:31-32), and the body-harlot analogy (1 Cor 6:15-16).

    Semantic categories: repentant harlot, Rahab faith exemplar, sexual union warning, Babylon great whore

    Key verses: Rev 17:1, Rev 17:5, Rev 17:15-16, Rev 19:2

    Relevance: The noun identifying the woman in Rev 17. Combined with the METER TON PORNON construction in 17:5, establishes the mother-daughter structure.

    G4203 — porneuo¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G4203-porneuo.md

    Occurrences: 7

    NT verb 'to commit fornication.' 7 verses, only 2 books (1 Corinthians and Revelation). 71% of occurrences in Revelation. Aorist tense dominates (6 of 8 tokens) — describing completed acts: Israel's historical sin, Babylon's seduction, kings' participation.

    Semantic categories: sexual ethics (1 Cor), nations committing fornication with Babylon (Rev)

    Key verses: Rev 17:2, Rev 18:3, Rev 18:9, Rev 2:14, Rev 2:20

    Relevance: The verb describing the active participation of nations and kings with Babylon — they 'commit fornication WITH her.'

    G4205 — pornos¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G4205-pornos.md

    Occurrences: 10

    NT masculine noun for fornicator/whoremonger. 10 verses across 5 books. In Revelation, appears in exclusion lists (Rev 21:8, 22:15). Concentrated in Pauline vice lists and Revelation exclusion lists.

    Semantic categories: exclusion from church, exclusion from kingdom, exclusion from New Jerusalem

    Key verses: Rev 21:8, Rev 22:15, 1 Cor 5:9-11

    Relevance: The male counterpart — those who participate in Babylon's fornication are excluded from the New Jerusalem.

    G897 — Babylon¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G897-babylon.md

    Occurrences: 11

    Greek proper noun for Babylon. 12 tokens in 11 verses across 4 books. More than half (6/11) in Revelation 14-18 as prophetic-symbolic entity. Matthew's 3 uses are genealogical (the Exile). Acts 7:43 is an OT citation. 1 Pet 5:13 is debated (literal or cipher for Rome). The word never appears in Mark, Luke, John, or any Pauline epistle.

    Semantic categories: historical city of exile, OT citation, apostolic cipher, apocalyptic harlot, object of final destruction

    Key verses: Rev 14:8, Rev 17:5, Rev 18:2, Rev 18:10, Rev 18:21, 1 Pet 5:13

    Relevance: The name itself. In Revelation, Babylon is marked as MYSTERION (symbolic) and functions as a theological category, not merely a geographic identifier.

    H894 — Babel¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H894-babel.md

    Occurrences: 264

    Hebrew proper noun for Babel/Babylon. 264 tokens in 233 verses across 14 OT books. Jeremiah dominates (149 occurrences, 64%). Etymology from balal (H1101, 'to confound/mix'), giving folk-etymology 'confusion.' Akkadian bab-ilu ('Gate of God') is the native etymology; Scripture reinterprets polemically.

    Semantic categories: origin/primeval city, imperial power, instrument of divine judgment, proud oppressor marked for judgment, place of exile

    Key verses: Gen 10:10, Gen 11:9, Jer 51:7, Isa 13:19, Dan 4:30

    Relevance: The OT foundation. The babel/balal wordplay establishes mixture/confusion as Babylon's essential character — what claims to be 'the gate of God' is actually 'confusion.'

    H1101 — balal¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H1101-balal.md

    Occurrences: 45

    Hebrew verb 'to mix, confuse.' 45 tokens in 41 verses across 7 books. Overwhelmingly cultic-legal: 88% in Numbers and Leviticus (grain-offering formula: 'fine flour mingled with oil'). Only 5 uses outside ritual law carry the theologically significant weight: Gen 11:7,9 (confounding language), Hos 7:8 (mixing among peoples), Isa 64:6 (withering), Ps 92:10 (anointing).

    Semantic categories: mixing flour with oil (grain offering), confounding language (divine judgment), illicit mixing among peoples, withering/fading

    Key verses: Gen 11:7, Gen 11:9, Hos 7:8

    Relevance: The etymology of Babel/Babylon. Primary meaning is 'to mix' (oil with flour). The confusion sense derives from mixing languages. Applied theologically: Babylon's character is mixture — mixing truth with error until saturation.

    G2372 — thumos¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G2372-thumos.md

    Occurrences: 18

    NT masculine noun for passionate wrath/fury. 18 occurrences, 56% in Revelation (10/18). Distinguished from orge (G3709): thumos is the hot, passionate eruption; orge is settled, judicial anger. In Revelation, thumos describes divine eschatological fury (seven bowls, winepress) and appears in the polarity flip: Babylon's thumos (14:8) → God's thumos (14:10, 19).

    Semantic categories: divine eschatological fury, human passionate outburst, vice list item, intoxicating passion

    Key verses: Rev 14:8, Rev 14:10, Rev 14:19, Rev 15:1, Rev 16:19, Rev 19:15

    Relevance: Central to the genitive chain in Rev 14:8. The thumos of Babylon's porneia (14:8) is answered by God's thumos + orge (14:10) — divine justice through exact vocabulary correspondence.

    G3709 — orge¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G3709-orge.md

    Occurrences: 36

    NT feminine noun for settled/judicial wrath. 36 occurrences across 11 books. Romans has the highest count (12). Dual semantic domain: emotion of anger (Louw-Nida 88.024) and judicial punishment (38.001). Distinguished from thumos as deliberate/settled vs. passionate/boiling.

    Semantic categories: divine judicial wrath, eschatological wrath to come, human anger (vice lists), God's revealed wrath against sin

    Key verses: Rev 14:10, Rev 16:19, Rev 19:15, Rom 1:18, 1 Thess 1:10

    Relevance: In Rev 14:10, thumos + orge combine as God's response to Babylon. The combination intensifies: 'the wine of the wrath (thumos) of God poured out without mixture into the cup of his indignation (orge).'

    G946 — bdelygma¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G946-bdelygma.md

    Occurrences: 6

    NT neuter noun for abomination/detestable thing. 6 verses across 4 books. Half in Revelation (17:4, 17:5, 21:27). In LXX, renders both Hebrew towebah (H8441) and shiqquwts (H8251). The semantic range collapses three distinct OT terms into one Greek word, carrying the combined force of moral abomination, idolatrous defilement, and ritual uncleanness.

    Semantic categories: abomination of desolation (Dan quotation), contents of Babylon's cup, exclusion from New Jerusalem

    Key verses: Rev 17:4, Rev 17:5, Rev 21:27, Matt 24:15

    Relevance: The contents of Babylon's golden cup: bdelygmata (abominations) — the full OT range of things God finds detestable: idolatry, sorcery, false worship, profanation of the sacred.

    H8441 — towebah¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H8441-towebah.md

    Occurrences: 127

    OT feminine noun for abomination. 127 tokens in 112 verses across 14 books. Concentrated in Ezekiel (41), Proverbs (20), and Deuteronomy (16). Covers idolatry (Deut 7:25), sorcery/necromancy (Deut 18:10-11), unjust weights (Deut 25:13-16), sexual sin (Lev 18:22), and general moral-religious loathsomeness.

    Semantic categories: cultural taboo, idolatry and foreign gods, sexual sin, sorcery and necromancy, unjust weights, general moral loathsomeness

    Key verses: Deut 7:25, Deut 18:10-11, Deut 27:15, Ezek 16:22

    Relevance: The OT source vocabulary for bdelygma through LXX translation. The categories of towebah (idolatry, sorcery, false worship, law-rejection) match the contents of Babylon's cup in Revelation.

    H8251 — shiqquwts¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H8251-shiqquwts.md

    Occurrences: 29

    OT masculine noun for detestable idol/abomination. 29 occurrences concentrated in prophetic literature (Ezekiel 8, Jeremiah 5, Daniel 3). Used as a title for foreign deities (Milcom, Chemosh, Molech) and for idolatrous objects defiling sacred space. In Daniel, combined with shamem to form 'abomination of desolation' (shiqquts meshomem).

    Semantic categories: title for foreign deities, idolatrous objects in sacred space, abomination of desolation

    Key verses: Dan 11:31, Dan 12:11, 1 Ki 11:5-7, Jer 7:30

    Relevance: The specific OT term for 'detestable idol.' Through the LXX bridge (shiqquwts → bdelygma), this vocabulary enters Revelation's description of Babylon's cup contents.

    G3466 — mysterion¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G3466-mysterion.md

    Occurrences: 27

    NT neuter noun for mystery/secret. 27 verses across 10 books. Concentrated in Paul's prison epistles (Col+Eph = 10/27). In Revelation, appears 4 times including Rev 17:5 (on the harlot's forehead) and Rev 17:7 (the mystery of the woman). The word signals that the name requires symbolic/theological interpretation, not geographic identification.

    Semantic categories: mysteries of the kingdom, Christ as mystery, church as mystery, mystery of lawlessness, harlot's name as mystery

    Key verses: Rev 17:5, Rev 17:7, 2 Thess 2:7, Eph 3:3-6

    Relevance: MYSTERION on the harlot's forehead signals that 'Babylon' is a symbolic designation requiring spiritual interpretation. The connection to 2 Thess 2:7 ('mystery of lawlessness already at work') links the harlot to an ongoing, concealed corruption within the community of faith.

    G5331 — pharmakeia¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G5331-pharmakeia.md

    Occurrences: 3

    NT feminine noun for sorcery/witchcraft. Only 3 occurrences in 2 books (Galatians, Revelation). In Gal 5:20, listed as a work of the flesh paired with idolatry. In Rev 18:23, it is the instrument by which Babylon deceives all nations. In Rev 9:21, listed among sins from which the impenitent refuse to turn.

    Semantic categories: vice/work of the flesh, apocalyptic deception mechanism, impenitence

    Key verses: Rev 18:23, Rev 9:21, Gal 5:20

    Relevance: Pharmakeia is Babylon's deceptive mechanism — 'by thy sorceries were all nations deceived' (18:23). This is cognitive/spiritual poison, not merely intellectual error. Connects the wine metaphor to sorcery: doctrinal intoxication.

    G4098 — pipto¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G4098-pipto.md

    Occurrences: 93

    NT verb 'to fall.' 93 tokens in 85 verses across 10 books. Very broad semantic range spanning 8 Louw-Nida domains: physical falling, prostration/worship, death, destruction, loss of status, change of state, cessation. In Rev 14:8, the doubled prophetic aorist epesen epesen announces Babylon's fall as an accomplished fact.

    Semantic categories: physical fall, worship prostration, death, destruction/ruin, loss of status

    Key verses: Rev 14:8, Rev 18:2

    Relevance: The doubled epesen epesen (prophetic aorist) in Rev 14:8 and 18:2 echoes Isa 21:9 ('Babylon is fallen, is fallen'). The prophetic aorist treats the future fall as already accomplished — certainty of fulfillment.

    H2534 — chemah¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H2534-chemah.md

    Occurrences: 133

    OT feminine noun for heat/fury/poison. 133 tokens in 117 verses across 20 books. Concentrated in prophetic-judgment literature (Ezekiel 30, Jeremiah 16, Isaiah 12 = 51%). The LXX translates chemah as thumos (G2372) 74% of the time, creating the direct vocabulary bridge into Revelation.

    Semantic categories: divine fury poured out, human rage, snake venom/poison, wine-cup of wrath

    Key verses: Jer 25:15, Jer 51:7, Isa 51:17, Ezek 22:22

    Relevance: The OT source word for thumos (G2372) through the LXX bridge. Jer 25:15 ('wine cup of this fury/chemah') → LXX thumos → Rev 14:8 (oinou tou thumou). The chemah→thumos bridge documented in TR-chemah-thymos.md (PMI 6.18).

    G3631 — oinos¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G3631-oinos.md

    Occurrences: 33

    NT masculine noun for wine. 33 tokens in 25 verses across 9 books. Revelation has highest raw count (8). In Revelation 14-19, wine is used figuratively: Babylon's wine of porneia (14:8, 18:3), God's wine of wrath (14:10, 16:19, 19:15), and as commodity (18:13).

    Semantic categories: literal beverage, parabolic (new wine), figurative intoxicating doctrine, figurative divine wrath, commodity

    Key verses: Rev 14:8, Rev 14:10, Rev 16:19, Rev 18:3, Rev 19:15

    Relevance: The wine metaphor is central: Babylon gives wine (intoxicating false doctrine) and receives wine (divine wrath). The vocabulary chain: yayin (H3196) → oinos (G3631) → the cup imagery of Rev 14:8.

    H3196 — yayin¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H3196-yayin.md

    Occurrences: 151

    OT masculine noun for wine. 151 tokens in 134 verses across 31 books. Root means 'to effervesce/ferment.' Used in Jer 51:7 ('the nations have drunken of her wine') — the direct OT source for Rev 14:8 and 18:3.

    Semantic categories: literal fermented beverage, sacrifice/offering, figurative judgment cup, figurative joy/celebration

    Key verses: Jer 51:7, Psa 75:8, Hos 4:11

    Relevance: The OT wine vocabulary that becomes oinos in NT. Jer 51:7 'mi-yeinah shathu goyim' (from her wine the nations drank) is the direct source for Revelation's Babylon-wine imagery.

    G4352 — proskyneo¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G4352-proskyneo.md

    Occurrences: 65

    NT verb 'to worship/do homage.' 65 tokens in 54 verses. LXX translation of shachah (H7812). Central to Revelation's worship binary: true worship of God (Rev 14:7) vs. false worship of the beast (Rev 13:4, 8, 12, 15). The first angel calls for proskyneo of the Creator; Babylon's system produces proskyneo of the beast.

    Semantic categories: worship of God, worship of Christ, false worship of beast/dragon, refused angel worship

    Key verses: Rev 14:7, Rev 13:4, Rev 13:8, Rev 19:10

    Relevance: The worship binary established by the first angel (Rev 14:7 'worship him that made') is the standard against which Babylon's corruption is measured. Babylon's wine leads to beast-worship instead of Creator-worship.

    H7812 — shachah¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H7812-shachah.md

    Occurrences: 194

    OT verb 'to bow down/worship.' 194 tokens in 166 verses. Overwhelmingly Hishtaphel stem (99%). LXX translates as proskyneo (G4352). Spans social prostration before rulers and worship of God. Central to the Decalogue prohibition (Exod 20:5; Deut 5:9).

    Semantic categories: social prostration, divine worship, worship prohibition

    Key verses: Deut 5:9, Exod 20:5, Psa 96:9

    Relevance: The OT source vocabulary for the worship binary. The shachah→proskyneo chain connects the OT worship prohibition to Revelation's worship conflict.

    G2920 — krisis¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G2920-krisis.md

    Occurrences: 47

    NT feminine noun for judgment/decision. 47 occurrences across 8 books. Dual semantic domain: judicial process (Louw-Nida 56.001) and punishment/consequence (38.001). In Rev 14:7, 'the hour of his judgment (krisis) is come' — the first angel's proclamation that the gospel includes judgment as evaluative vindication, not merely condemnation.

    Semantic categories: court of justice, judicial decision, evaluative process, punishment

    Key verses: Rev 14:7, Rev 18:10, John 5:24

    Relevance: The first angel proclaims krisis (Rev 14:7) as gospel content. Babylon's fall (14:8) follows immediately — the judgment is simultaneously vindication for God's people and exposure of the false system.

    G3735 — oros¶

    Occurrences: 65

    NT neuter noun for mountain. 65 total occurrences, 11 unique translations. Translated 'mountain/mount' 59 times (91%), 'hill' only 3 times (all in Luke, never in Revelation). In Revelation: 7 occurrences (6:14, 6:16, 8:8, 14:1, 16:20, 17:9, 21:10) — ALL meaning 'mountain,' NONE meaning 'hill.' The word bounos (G1015), the actual Greek word for 'hill,' never appears in Revelation. Woods' observation verified: translating oros as 'hill' in Rev 17:9 requires a unique meaning shift in that one verse.

    Semantic categories: literal geographic mountain, symbolic mountain (kingdom/power), eschatological removal of mountains

    Key verses: Rev 17:9, Rev 21:10, Rev 6:14, Rev 8:8, Rev 14:1, Rev 16:20

    Relevance: Critical for the seven-mountains debate. If oros means 'mountain' (not 'hill') in Rev 17:9, the 'seven hills of Rome' reading is weakened. Rev 17:9b immediately identifies the mountains as 'seven kings' — symbolic of powers/kingdoms, not geographic hills.
