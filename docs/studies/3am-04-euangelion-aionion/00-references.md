## Web Research Findings

The web research agent (Phase 0) produced extensive scholarly findings in `00-web-research.md`. Key items:

**Anarthrous construction debate:**
- **Alford** (Greek Testament Commentary): Despite the anarthrous construction, euangelion still means "the Gospel" because "no argument can be derived" from the missing article "in the case of a word which had become so technical." Cites Romans 1:1 as a parallel anarthrous use. Claims euangelion "appears nowhere in the NT except in the technical sense."
- **Vincent's Word Studies**: Takes the opposite position — emphasizes the anarthrous form, rendering "an eternal Gospel," highlighting qualitative/universal character rather than identity with a previously known message.

**Imperial euangelia thesis:**
- **DeSilva (BBR 18:2, 2008)**: Proposes translating euangelion aionion as "an age-long accession announcement" paralleling Roman imperial accession proclamations. Notes Vespasian's accession (68-69 CE) provides a "recent display" of a three-announcement sequence. Acknowledges "many are clear to distinguish this 'gospel' from 'the Gospel.'"
- **Priene Calendar Inscription (9 BC)**: Augustus's birthday called "the beginning of the good tidings [euangelion] for the world." Augustus called "god manifest," "Savior," credited with ending war. Scholars connect to Mark 1:1 as a deliberate counter-proclamation.
- **Nick Cady (2019)**: Euangelion functioned as political terminology — "history-making, world-shaping reports of political, military, or societal victories."

**Basar-euangelizo vocabulary chain:**
- **BiblePortal (G2097 word study)**: LXX occurrences of euangelizo: 17 instances. Three key Isaiah passages: 40:9, 52:7, 61:1. Rev 14:6 is the ONLY place in the NT where the noun (euangelion) and verb (euangelisai) appear together in the same clause.
- **HebrewWordLessons**: Basar (H1319) Piel participle mevasseret functions as a technical term for an authorized herald. Luke 2:10-14 uses euangelizomai for angelic announcement, demonstrating LXX equivalence.

**Galatians 1:6-9 objection:**
- **Answering Adventism**: Argues the everlasting gospel must be identical to 1 Cor 15:1-4; any "addition" (judgment, Sabbath, etc.) corrupts the gospel. Accuses Adventism of "changing what the gospel message is."
- **Cambridge Bible for Schools**: Claims the "eternal gospel" is "pure, natural theism" — broader than Pauline soteriology.

## Prior Studies

### From Semantic Search — Direct Matches

**gsp-12-everlasting-gospel** (score: 0.722) — "The Everlasting Gospel: One Message, All Ages"
- Question: "Is the gospel one continuous message from Genesis to Revelation?"
- Key finding: The gospel was planned before creation (Eph 1:4; 2 Tim 1:9; 1 Pet 1:20; Rev 13:8). Four independent NT texts place its origin before creation.
- Key finding: Aionios (G166) appears 71 times in 69 verses. In Rev 14:6 it modifies euangelion — the ONLY time this combination appears in Scripture. The backward reach of aionios is confirmed by 2 Tim 1:9 (pro chronon aionion).
- Key finding: Rev 14:6 is the last occurrence of euangelion in the Bible — the final time the word "gospel" appears in Scripture. Its placement is deliberate.
- Key finding: The gospel chain runs from Adam to angel: protoevangelium (Gen 3:15) → Abraham (Gal 3:8) → sacrificial system (Lev 17:11) → prophets (Isa 53:11) → cross (Rom 3:24-26) → intercession (Heb 7:25) → judgment (Dan 7:22) → three angels (Rev 14:6-12) → second coming (1 Thess 4:16-17).
- Key finding: The Eccl 12:13-14 structural parallel provides an exact triad match: fear God + keep commandments + judgment.
- Word study: basar→euangelizo bridge PMI 29.44; chashab→logizomai bridge PMI 31.41; heteros/allos distinction in Gal 1:6-7 distinguishes qualitative vs. numerical difference.

**gsp-10-three-angels-gospel** (score: 0.644) — "Are the Three Angels' Messages the Gospel, or an Addition to It?"
- Question: "Are the three angels' messages of Revelation 14:6-12 the gospel, or an addition to it?"
- Key finding: The legon construction in Rev 14:7 (present active participle of lego) introduces the direct discourse of the euangelisai from v.6. What follows legon IS the content of the everlasting gospel — structurally parallel to Mark 1:14-15, where keryssōn + legōn introduces Jesus' gospel content.
- Key finding: Paul at Athens (Acts 17:22-31) contains every element of the first angel's message: Creator God (v.24 = Rev 14:7), universal scope (vv.26,30 = Rev 14:6), judgment (v.31 = Rev 14:7), repentance (v.30 = Rev 14:7). Element-by-element correspondence is exact.
- Key finding: Rev 14:12 describes gospel RESULTS (what saved people look like), not additional salvation requirements. Tērountes (present active participle of tēreō) is descriptive, not imperative.
- Key finding: The pistis Iēsou construction in Rev 14:12 may be subjective genitive ("Jesus' own faithfulness"), connecting to the Pauline pistis Christou tradition (Rom 3:22, Gal 2:16).
- Vocabulary: basar→euangelizo bridge PMI 29.44 is near-exclusive equivalence. The angel uses the same word family as Isaiah's mevasser.

**gsp-01-gospel-defined** (score: 0.409) — "What Is the Gospel According to the Bible Itself?"
- Question: "Is the gospel limited to 1 Cor 15:3-4, or does the biblical definition include more?"
- Key finding: Paul gives DIFFERENT gospel summaries in Rom 1:1-4 and 1 Cor 15:3-4. If 1 Cor 15:3-4 were the complete definition, Paul would be preaching a "different gospel" in Romans. Both are summaries of a gospel broader than either.
- Key finding: Jesus preached "the gospel of the kingdom" (Mark 1:14-15) BEFORE his death, burial, and resurrection had occurred. If the gospel is solely those events, Jesus never preached the gospel during his ministry.
- Key finding: Euangelion (G2098) appears 77 times across 74 verses in 17 NT books. The verb euangelizo (G2097) adds 52 more occurrences across 12 books. Total gospel-family vocabulary: 130+ NT occurrences.
- Key finding: Galatians 1:6-7 uses heteron (G2087, different in kind) for the false gospel, then clarifies with allos (G243, another of the same kind). Paul condemns adding SALVATION CONDITIONS (circumcision), not broader gospel content.
- Key finding: Nave's catalogs 22+ distinct NT designations for the gospel — each revealing different dimensions. If the gospel were a single-point formula, it would not require 22 names.
- Key finding: En protois (1 Cor 15:3) means "among the first things" or "of first importance," not "the only things." Greek parsing: en (among) + protois (first things, dative plural).

**gsp-02-gospel-in-ot** (score: 0.408) — "Was the Gospel Present in the OT, or a NT Invention?"
- Key finding: The basar→euangelizo lexical bridge. Hebrew basar (H1319) appears 27 times in the OT, Piel stem in 96% of occurrences. LXX translates as euangelizo in 18 of 21 occurrences — PMI 29.44.
- Key finding: Three defining OT basar passages: Isa 40:9 (mevasseret — "Behold your God!"), Isa 52:7 (mevasser — four gospel elements: shalom, tov, yeshuah, malakh), Isa 61:1 (levassar — Spirit-anointed herald, mashach root of mashiyach).
- Key finding: NT confirms the bridge by direct quotation: Rom 10:15 quotes Isa 52:7, Luke 4:18-19 quotes Isa 61:1-2, Acts 10:36 echoes Isa 52:7.
- Key finding: Paul coined proeuangelizomai (G4283, hapax legomenon, Gal 3:8) — "gospeled-beforehand" to Abraham. The word itself IS the argument that the gospel predated the NT.
- Key finding: Heb 4:2 uses kathaper kakeinoi ("just as also they") — explicit comparison: the same gospel delivered to the wilderness generation as to NT believers.

**3am-03-three-angels-in-nt** (score: 0.661) — "The Three Angels' Pattern in the NT"
- Key finding: Three independent NT texts label the combined gospel+judgment message as "gospel": Luke 3:18 (John's judgment preaching = euangelizo), Rom 2:16 (judgment "according to my gospel"), Rev 14:6-7 (judgment IS the everlasting gospel). Triple witness.
- Key finding: The anticipation-vs-announcement distinction: NT preachers used future-oriented language (mellei/mellon — "about to"), while Rev 14:7 uses elthen (aorist — "IS come"). The pattern is the same; the temporal frame shifts from anticipated to arrived.
- Key finding: Paul at Athens provides the bridge between NT preaching and the first angel — identical elements, only the temporal frame differs. Acts 17:31: "appointed a day" (future); Rev 14:7: "the hour IS come" (arrived).
- Key finding: The Elijah chain — Elijah at Carmel, John the Baptist, and the three angels deliver the same three-part pattern: gospel call → expose false system → judgment warning.
- Vocabulary: euangelizo (G2097, 61 occurrences) connects all instances. krisis (G2920, 47 occurrences) carries the OT covenant lawsuit tradition (riyb→krisis). hora (G5610) vs. hemera (G2250) encodes the anticipation-vs-announcement distinction.

**law-17-paul-and-law-in-galatians** (score: 0.532)
- Key finding: Paul's argument in Galatians targets Judaizers who demanded circumcision and the ceremonial system as salvation requirements (Gal 2:3-4; 5:2-3; 6:12-13; Acts 15:1).
- Key finding: Paul distinguishes: law as justification mechanism (rejected), ceremonial system (superseded — "circumcision is nothing," 1 Cor 7:19), moral law content (fulfilled through love, Gal 5:14).
- Key finding: Gal 5:6 — "faith which worketh by love" — faith is not antinomian; it produces love, and love fulfills the moral law.
- Key finding: Gal 5:19-21 condemns Decalogue violations (adultery = 7th, murder = 6th, idolatry = 1st/2nd), presupposing the Decalogue remains the moral standard.
