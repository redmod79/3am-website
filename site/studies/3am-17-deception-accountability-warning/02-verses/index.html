    Verses¶

    Question¶

    Does everyone who receives the mark of the beast end up lost? What about those who were deceived (Rev 19:20)? Does God guarantee fair warning before the mark crisis? Can the mark be resisted (Rev 15:2)? How does 2 Thessalonians 2:10-12 explain the relationship between deception and accountability — is the "strong delusion" a cause of truth-rejection or a consequence? Trace the judicial delusion pattern: Romans 1:24-28, Psalm 81:11-12, Isaiah 6:9-10. How does graduated accountability work (Luke 12:47-48, Acts 17:30, 1 Tim 1:13)? How does the OT ignorance-vs-presumption distinction (Lev 4, Num 15:22-31) establish the framework? Is the close of probation taught in the Bible — how do Rev 22:11, Rev 15:8, and Daniel 12:1 converge?

    The judicial-delusion causal chain: truth-rejection precedes divine delusion¶

    [Even him], whose coming is after the working of Satan with all power and signs and lying wonders, And with all deceivableness of unrighteousness in them that perish; because they received not the love of the truth, that they might be saved. And for this cause God shall send them strong delusion, that they should believe a lie: That they all might be damned who believed not the truth, but had pleasure in unrighteousness.

    Context: 2 Thessalonians 2 addresses the day of the Lord. Paul identifies a man of sin who operates by Satan's energeia (v.9). The causal chain in vv.10-12 is explicit: truth-rejection (anth' hon ouk edexanto, v.10) CAUSES divine delusion (dia touto pempei, v.11). edexanto is aorist middle indicative of dechomai -- active refusal. pempei is present active indicative -- God actively sends. The compound energeian planes is a genitive of quality: 'a working characterized by delusion.'

    Notes: Grammar reference at 2thess-2-1-12.md provides complete parsing tables. Key confirmations: edexanto = V-ADI-3P (Aor Mid Ind), pempei = V-PAI-3S (Pres Act Ind). The causal connectors anth' hon ('because') and dia touto ('for this cause') establish explicit double causal link. v.13 contrast: God chose others 'to salvation through sanctification of the Spirit and belief of the truth.'

    Three-fold paradidomi: progressive judicial abandonment pattern¶

    Wherefore God also gave them up to uncleanness through the lusts of their own hearts, to dishonour their own bodies between themselves: Who changed the truth of God into a lie, and worshipped and served the creature more than the Creator, who is blessed for ever. Amen. For this cause God gave them up unto vile affections: for even their women did change the natural use into that which is against nature: And likewise also the men, leaving the natural use of the woman, burned in their lust one toward another; men with men working that which is unseemly, and receiving in themselves that recompence of their error which was meet. And even as they did not like to retain God in [their] knowledge, God gave them over to a reprobate mind, to do those things which are not convenient;

    Context: Romans 1:18-32 describes the downward spiral. Three times paradidomi (G3860) appears with identical form: v.24 paredoken (V-AAI-3S), v.26 paredoken (V-AAI-3S), v.28 paredoken (V-AAI-3S). Escalation: lusts of hearts / uncleanness -> dishonorable passions -> debased/reprobate mind (adokimon noun). Wordplay in v.28: ouk edokimasan (did not approve) -> adokimon (unapproved).

    Notes: Parser confirms all three paradidomi as V-AAI-3S. Andrews (2025) argues it carries 'legal and punitive weight' and 'never implies divine indifference.'

    OT prototype of judicial abandonment -- 'I gave them over to their stubborn heart'¶

    But my people would not hearken to my voice; and Israel would none of me. So I gave them up to their own hearts' lust: [and] they walked in their own counsels.

    Context: Psalm 81 recounts God's deliverance and Israel's refusal. v.12 (Heb v.12): lo-shama ammi liqoli -- human rejection FIRST. v.13 (Heb v.13): va'ashallchehu bishriyrut libbam -- divine response: shalach (Piel Wayq 1s+3ms = 'I sent him away') in the stubbornness of their heart. Conceptually parallel to paradidomi in Rom 1.

    Notes: Parser confirms: shama (Qal Perf 3ms), abah (Qal Perf 3ms), shalach (Piel Wayq 1s+3ms). Nave's SELF-DELUSION explicitly cross-references Psa 81:11-12 with 2 Thess 2:10-11.

    Hiphil imperatives -- judicial hardening of Israel's heart/ears/eyes¶

    And he said, Go, and tell this people, Hear ye indeed, but understand not; and see ye indeed, but perceive not. Make the heart of this people fat, and make their ears heavy, and shut their eyes; lest they see with their eyes, and hear with their ears, and understand with their heart, and convert, and be healed.

    Context: Isaiah's commissioning. Three Hiphil imperatives in v.10: hashmen (Hiphil Impv 2ms of shaman = 'make fat' -- heart), hakbed (Hiphil Impv 2ms of kabed = 'make heavy' -- ears), hasha (Hiphil Impv 2ms of sha'a = 'paste/smear shut' -- eyes). The Hiphil is causative but can function as resultative/permissive. The pen ('lest') clause shows purpose. NT quotations vary: Matt 13:14-15 follows LXX passive; Mark 4:12 uses shorter form.

    Notes: Parser confirms all three as Hiphil Impv 2ms. Mike K. (2014) favors confirmation reading: God confirms people in their chosen state. Isa 35:5-6 hints at reversibility.

    OT framework -- inadvertent sin (bishgagah) vs high-hand sin (beyad ramah)¶

    And if ye have erred, and not observed all these commandments, which the LORD hath spoken unto Moses, [Even] all that the LORD hath commanded you by the hand of Moses, from the day that the LORD commanded [Moses], and henceforward among your generations; Then it shall be, if [ought] be committed by ignorance without the knowledge of the congregation, that all the congregation shall offer one young bullock for a burnt offering, for a sweet savour unto the LORD, with his meat offering, and his drink offering, according to the manner, and one kid of the goats for a sin offering. And the priest shall make an atonement for all the congregation of the children of Israel, and it shall be forgiven them; for it [is] ignorance: and they shall bring their offering, a sacrifice made by fire unto the LORD, and their sin offering before the LORD, for their ignorance: And it shall be forgiven all the congregation of the children of Israel, and the stranger that sojourneth among them; seeing all the people [were] in ignorance. And if any soul sin through ignorance, then he shall bring a she goat of the first year for a sin offering. And the priest shall make an atonement for the soul that sinneth ignorantly, when he sinneth by ignorance before the LORD, to make an atonement for him; and it shall be forgiven him. Ye shall have one law for him that sinneth through ignorance, [both for] him that is born among the children of Israel, and for the stranger that sojourneth among them. But the soul that doeth [ought] presumptuously, [whether he be] born in the land, or a stranger, the same reproacheth the LORD; and that soul shall be cut off from among his people. Because he hath despised the word of the LORD, and hath broken his commandment, that soul shall utterly be cut off; his iniquity [shall be] upon him.

    Context: Foundational sin-classification passage. Two categories: (1) bishgagah (H7684) -- atonement available (vv.22-29); (2) beyad ramah (H3027 + H7311) -- no atonement, cut off (vv.30-31). Distinction is about heart posture, not act nature.

    Notes: Parser confirms: shegagah (Noun fs Abs), yad (Noun s Abs), ramah (Qal Ptcp fs of ruwm). High-hand sinner 'blasphemes' (megaddep, Piel Ptcp) and 'despised the word of the LORD.' Connects to Heb 10:26-29.

    Graduated sin offerings -- priest/ruler/commoner with different sacrifices¶

    And the LORD spake unto Moses, saying, Speak unto the children of Israel, saying, If a soul shall sin through ignorance against any of the commandments of the LORD [concerning things] which ought not to be done, and shall do against any of them: If the priest that is anointed do sin according to the sin of the people; then let him bring for his sin, which he hath sinned, a young bullock without blemish unto the LORD for a sin offering. ... And if any one of the common people sin through ignorance, while he doeth [somewhat against] any of the commandments of the LORD [concerning things] which ought not to be done, and be guilty; ... and the priest shall make an atonement for his sin that he hath committed, and it shall be forgiven him.

    Context: Leviticus 4 builds sacrificial framework for sins bishgagah. Graduated accountability within inadvertent category: priest (4:3-12) = young bullock; ruler (4:22-26) = male kid; common person (4:27-35) = female kid or lamb. Greater knowledge/privilege requires costlier sacrifice.

    Notes: Parser confirms bishgagah (Noun fs Abs) in Lev 4:2. The graduated sacrifice system institutionalizes accountability scaling with privilege.

    'Willful sin after epignosis of truth' -- no remaining sacrifice¶

    For if we sin wilfully after that we have received the knowledge of the truth, there remaineth no more sacrifice for sins, But a certain fearful looking for of judgment and fiery indignation, which shall devour the adversaries. He that despised Moses' law died without mercy under two or three witnesses: Of how much sorer punishment, suppose ye, shall he be thought worthy, who hath trodden under foot the Son of God, and hath counted the blood of the covenant, wherewith he was sanctified, an unholy thing, and hath done despite unto the Spirit of grace?

    Context: NT application of Num 15 framework. hekousios (G1596, adverb) + hamartanonton (Pres Act Ptcp GPM = habitual sinning) + meta to labein ten epignosin tes aletheias ('after receiving full knowledge of truth'). A fortiori: if despising Moses' law = death, how much worse for trampling the Son of God?

    Notes: Parser confirms: hekousios = ADV, hamartanonton = V-PAP-GPM, epignosin = N-ASF (G1922). Present participle = continuous/habitual.

    Many vs few stripes -- graduated punishment based on knowledge¶

    And that servant, which knew his lord's will, and prepared not [himself], neither did according to his will, shall be beaten with many [stripes]. But he that knew not, and did commit things worthy of stripes, shall be beaten with few [stripes]. For unto whomsoever much is given, of him shall be much required: and to whom men have committed much, of him they will ask the more.

    Context: Jesus' clearest graduated-punishment text. v.47: ho gnous (Aor Act Ptcp = 'the one having known') + daresetai pollas ('beaten with many'). v.48: ho me gnous ('the one not having known') + daresetai oligas ('beaten with few'). BOTH receive punishment; ignorance reduces but does not eliminate culpability.

    Notes: Parser confirms: gnous = V-2AAP-NSM, daresetai = V-2FPI-3S, pollas = A-APF, oligas = A-APF.

    God 'overlooked' (hupereidon) times of ignorance -- now commands repentance¶

    And the times of this ignorance God winked at; but now commandeth all men every where to repent:

    Context: Paul's Areopagus speech. hupereidon (G5237, hapax) = Aor Act Ptcp of huperorao -- 'having looked past/over.' Deliberate restraint, not approval. ta nun + apangellei + pantas pantachou metanoein = universal command to repent. Compare Rom 3:25 paresis.

    Notes: Parser confirms: hupereidon = V-2AAP-NSM, agnoias = N-GSF (G52), metanoein = V-PAN (G3340).

    Paul obtained mercy because he acted in ignorance and unbelief¶

    Who was before a blasphemer, and a persecutor, and injurious: but I obtained mercy, because I did [it] ignorantly in unbelief.

    Context: Paul's testimony. eleethein ('obtained mercy,' V-API-1S) hoti agnoOn epoiesa en apistia ('because being ignorant I did it in unbelief'). agnoOn (Pres Act Ptcp of agnoeo) = mitigating factor. Sincere but wrong -- vincible ignorance.

    Notes: Parser confirms: agnoOn = V-PAP-NSM (G50), eleethein = V-API-1S (G1653), apistia = N-DSF (G570).

    'If ye were blind, ye should have no sin' -- genuine ignorance removes guilt¶

    Jesus said unto them, If ye were blind, ye should have no sin: but now ye say, We see; therefore your sin remaineth.

    Context: After healing the man born blind. Conditional: genuine blindness -> no sin. Claimed sight while rejecting truth -> sin remains.

    Notes: Strongest single text for non-culpable ignorance removing guilt. The Pharisees' problem: CLAIMED knowledge while rejecting implications.

    'If I had not come and spoken, they had not had sin' -- knowledge creates culpability¶

    If I had not come and spoken unto them, they had not had sin: but now they have no cloke for their sin. He that hateth me hateth my Father also. If I had not done among them the works which none other man did, they had not had sin: but now have they both seen and hated both me and my Father.

    Context: Two counterfactual conditionals: Christ's words/works eliminated the ignorance defense. Now they have 'no cloke (prophasin, excuse) for their sin.'

    Notes: Directly supports graduated accountability and the EGW principle: 'where these circumstances are or have been wanting, nothing is imputed.'

    Judged by the standard received -- Gentiles by conscience, Jews by law¶

    For as many as have sinned without law shall also perish without law: and as many as have sinned in the law shall be judged by the law; (For not the hearers of the law [are] just before God, but the doers of the law shall be justified. For when the Gentiles, which have not the law, do by nature the things contained in the law, these, having not the law, are a law unto themselves: Which shew the work of the law written in their hearts, their conscience also bearing witness, and [their] thoughts the mean while accusing or else excusing one another;) In the day when God shall judge the secrets of men by Jesus Christ according to my gospel.

    Context: Romans 2 establishes proportional judgment. Standard of judgment matches standard of revelation received.

    Notes: Foundational text for graduated accountability in Paul's theology.

    Proof the mark CAN be resisted -- nikountas ek tou theriou¶

    And I saw as it were a sea of glass mingled with fire: and them that had gotten the victory over the beast, and over his image, and over his mark, [and] over the number of his name, stand on the sea of glass, having the harps of God.

    Context: tous nikOntas (Pres Act Ptcp APM of nikao) ek tou theriou kai ek tes eikonos autou kai ek tou arithmou tou onomatos autou -- triple ek construction. Present participle = ongoing conquering.

    Notes: Parser confirms: nikOntas = V-PAP-APM (G3528). Rev 12:11 provides means of overcoming. Rev 13:15-17 shows coercion they resist.

    Overcoming by blood of the Lamb and word of testimony -- means of resistance¶

    And they overcame him by the blood of the Lamb, and by the word of their testimony; and they loved not their lives unto the death.

    Context: Means of victory: (1) blood of the Lamb, (2) word of testimony, (3) willingness to die. Spiritual victory, not military.

    Notes: Defines how the mark is resisted: faith and testimony up to martyrdom.

    First angel's message -- universal gospel guarantee before judgment¶

    And I saw another angel fly in the midst of heaven, having the everlasting gospel to preach unto them that dwell on the earth, and to every nation, and kindred, and tongue, and people, Saying with a loud voice, Fear God, and give glory to him; for the hour of his judgment is come: and worship him that made heaven, and earth, and the sea, and the fountains of waters.

    Context: euangelion aiOnion to epi pan ethnos kai phylen kai glOssan kai laon. Universal scope guarantees fair warning before the third angel's judgment (14:9-11).

    Notes: Parser confirms: euangelion = N-ASN (G2098), aiOnion = A-ASN (G166), pan ethnos = every nation.

    'Come out of her my people' -- God calls His own out BEFORE judgment¶

    And I heard another voice from heaven, saying, Come out of her, my people, that ye be not partakers of her sins, and that ye receive not of her plagues.

    Context: God addresses 'my people' inside Babylon. The call to 'come out' precedes judgment. God has people inside Babylon and provides escape before destruction.

    Notes: Addresses fairness: God does not judge without providing an escape route.

    Gospel preached in all the world before the end -- universal witness guarantee¶

    And this gospel of the kingdom shall be preached in all the world for a witness unto all nations; and then shall the end come.

    Context: Olivet Discourse. Temporal sequence: gospel to ALL nations FIRST, THEN the end comes.

    Notes: Combined with Rev 14:6-7, establishes universal fair-warning guarantee.

    Temple filled with smoke, no one could enter -- cessation of intercession¶

    And after that I looked, and, behold, the temple of the tabernacle of the testimony in heaven was opened: And the seven angels came out of the temple, having the seven plagues, clothed in pure and white linen, and having their breasts girded with golden girdles. And one of the four beasts gave unto the seven angels seven golden vials full of the wrath of God, who liveth for ever and ever. And the temple was filled with smoke from the glory of God, and from his power; and no man was able to enter into the temple, till the seven plagues of the seven angels were fulfilled.

    Context: Rev 15:8: oudeis edynato (Impf M/P Ind = ongoing inability) eiselthein (Aor Act Inf) eis ton naon. Temple fills with smoke BEFORE plagues (16:1ff). If no one can enter the temple (place of intercession), intercession has ceased. achri telesthOsin = 'until plagues completed.'

    Notes: Parser confirms: edynato = V-INI-3S (Impf M/P Ind), eiselthein = V-2AAN, naon = N-ASM (G3485). Independently supports pre-plague finalization without EGW.
