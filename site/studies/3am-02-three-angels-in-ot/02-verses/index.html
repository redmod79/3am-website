    Verses¶

    Question¶

    Is the three-part pattern of the three angels' messages — (1) gospel call to worship the true God, (2) exposure of a false system, (3) warning of judgment — repeated throughout the Old Testament? Examine: Noah before the flood, Abraham and Sodom, Moses and Egypt, Elijah at Carmel, Josiah's reform, Jeremiah's ministry. Do these share the same structure as Rev 14:6-12?

    First angel's message — everlasting gospel, fear God, worship the Creator¶

    And I saw another angel fly in the midst of heaven, having the everlasting gospel to preach unto them that dwell on the earth, and to every nation, and kindred, and tongue, and people, Saying with a loud voice, Fear God, and give glory to him; for the hour of his judgment is come: and worship him that made heaven, and earth, and the sea, and the fountains of waters.

    Context: Revelation 14 follows the beast system of chapter 13. The three angels' messages (14:6-12) are the divine counter-proclamation. The first angel establishes the baseline pattern: gospel proclamation (euangelion aionion), call to worship (proskyneo), and announcement of judgment hour (krisis).

    Notes: Three aorist imperatives: phobethete (fear), dote (give), proskynerate (worship). The Creation formula (heaven, earth, sea, fountains of waters) echoes Exodus 20:11. This verse establishes the three-part vocabulary chain to trace through OT episodes.

    Third angel's message — judgment warning against false worship¶

    And the third angel followed them, saying with a loud voice, If any man worship the beast and his image, and receive his mark in his forehead, or in his hand, The same shall drink of the wine of the wrath of God, which is poured out without mixture into the cup of his indignation; and he shall be tormented with fire and brimstone in the presence of the holy angels, and in the presence of the Lamb: And the smoke of their torment ascendeth up for ever and ever: and they have no rest day nor night, who worship the beast and his image, and whosoever receiveth the mark of his name. Here is the patience of the saints: here are they that keep the commandments of God, and the faith of Jesus.

    Context: The third angel warns of judgment on those who choose false worship. The warning is urgent and specific. Verse 12 concludes with the character of those who heed the messages: commandment-keepers who hold the faith of Jesus.

    Notes: Proskyneo appears twice in this passage — once for false worship (v.9) and once to describe those who persist in it (v.11). The thymos/orge wrath chain connects to the OT chemah/aph vocabulary via LXX. Fire and brimstone echo Gen 19:24 (Sodom destruction).

    Song of Moses and the Lamb — Exodus-to-Revelation bridge¶

    And they sing the song of Moses the servant of God, and the song of the Lamb, saying, Great and marvellous are thy works, Lord God Almighty; just and true are thy ways, thou King of saints. Who shall not fear thee, O Lord, and glorify thy name? for thou only art holy: for all nations shall come and worship before thee; for thy judgments are made manifest.

    Context: Rev 15:3-4 explicitly combines the Song of Moses (Exodus 15) with the Song of the Lamb, forming a typological bridge from the Exodus judgment-on-Egypt episode to the end-time scenario. The language echoes the first angel: fear, glorify, worship, judgments.

    Notes: Key vocabulary overlap with Rev 14:7: phobeo (fear), doxa (glory/glorify), proskyneo (worship), krisis/krima (judgment). This confirms that Revelation itself treats the Exodus pattern as a type of the end-time three-part message.

    Noah episode: 120-year probationary period — extended gospel opportunity¶

    And the LORD said, My spirit shall not always strive with man, for that he also is flesh: yet his days shall be an hundred and twenty years.

    Context: God's statement limits human probation to 120 years before the flood. This represents extended gospel opportunity — time for Noah's preaching to take effect. The striving of God's Spirit is the gospel element.

    Notes: The 120-year period shows the patience of God before judgment — the same principle seen in all six OT episodes and culminating in the 'everlasting gospel' of Rev 14:6.

    Noah episode: exposure of world's moral corruption¶

    And GOD saw that the wickedness of man was great in the earth, and that every imagination of the thoughts of his heart was only evil continually. And it repented the LORD that he had made man on the earth, and it grieved him at his heart. And the LORD said, I will destroy man whom I have created from the face of the earth; both man, and beast, and the creeping thing, and the fowls of the air; for it repenteth me that I have made them.

    Context: Genesis 6:5 exposes the world's condition: total moral corruption. Every imagination (yetser) of the thoughts (machsheboth) of the heart was only evil (ra) continually. This is the exposure element — the false system is universal wickedness.

    • וַ/יַּ֣רְא () — Qal.Wayq.3ms — 'see') יְהוָ֔ה (YHWH) כִּ֥י (that) רַבָּ֛ה (רב adj.fs 'much') רָעַ֥ת (רעה noun.fs.cst 'evil') הָאָדָ֖ם (human) בָּאָ֑רֶץ (in the earth) וְכָל (and whole) יֵ֨צֶר֙ (יצר noun.ms.cst 'form/imagination') מַחְשְׁבֹ֣ת (מחשׁבה noun.fp.cst 'thought') לִבֹּ֔ו (לב noun.ms+3ms 'his heart') רַ֥ק (only) רַ֖ע (רע adj.ms 'evil') כָּל־הַיֹּֽום (all the day)

    Notes: Hebrew parsing confirms ra'ath (evil, noun fs construct) and ra (evil, adj ms). The double use of 'kol' (whole/every) emphasizes the totality of corruption. This verse is the judgment basis — exposure comes before the judgment announcement in v.7.

    Noah episode: earth corrupt (shachath) and filled with violence (chamas)¶

    The earth also was corrupt before God, and the earth was filled with violence. And God looked upon the earth, and, behold, it was corrupt; for all flesh had corrupted his way upon the earth.

    Context: Genesis 6:11-12 uses shachath (H7843) in both Niphal ('the earth was corrupt') and Hiphil ('all flesh had corrupted his way'). The word chamas (H2555, violence) completes the exposure. These are the two key terms for the pre-flood condition.

    • וַ/תִּשָּׁחֵ֥ת () — Niphal.Wayq.3fs — 'was corrupt/destroyed') הָאָ֖רֶץ (the earth) לִפְנֵ֣י (before) הָֽאֱלֹהִ֑ים (God) וַתִּמָּלֵ֥א (מלא Niphal.Wayq.3fs 'was filled') הָאָ֖רֶץ (the earth) חָמָֽס (חמס noun.ms 'violence')

    Notes: Hebrew parser confirms: tishacheth (shachath, Niphal Wayyiqtol 3fs) — the earth became corrupt; chamas (noun ms absolute) — violence. The LXX translates shachath with diaphtheiro, establishing the Greek connection (TR-shachath-diaphtheiro).

    Noah episode: God announces judgment and commands ark construction¶

    And God said unto Noah, The end of all flesh is come before me; for the earth is filled with violence through them; and, behold, I will destroy them with the earth. Make thee an ark of gopher wood; rooms shalt thou make in the ark, and shalt pitch it within and without with pitch.

    Context: God reveals His judgment plan to Noah personally (Amos 3:7 principle) and commands the ark's construction. The word mashchitham (Hiphil participle of shachath + 3mp suffix) — 'I am about to destroy them' — uses the same root as the corruption in v.11-12.

    • וַיֹּ֨אמֶר () — Qal.Wayq.3ms — 'said') אֱלֹהִ֜ים (God) לְנֹ֗חַ (to Noah) קֵ֤ץ (end) כָּל־בָּשָׂר֙ (all flesh) בָּ֣א (בוא Qal.Perf.3ms 'has come') לְפָנַ֔י (before me) כִּֽי (because) מָלְאָ֥ה (מלא Qal.Perf.3fs 'is full') הָאָ֛רֶץ (the earth) חָמָ֖ס (violence) מִפְּנֵיהֶ֑ם (because of them) וְהִנְנִ֥י (behold I) מַשְׁחִיתָ֖ם (שׁחת Hiphil.Ptcp+3mp 'am destroying them') אֶת־הָאָֽרֶץ (with the earth)

    Notes: Hebrew parser confirms mashchitam (shachath, Hiphil participle + 3mp suffix) — God uses the same verb for judgment that described their corruption. Wordplay: they corrupted (shachath) the earth, so God will destroy (shachath) them. The ark is the gospel provision — a way of escape during judgment.

    Noah episode: flood judgment announced with covenant promise¶

    And, behold, I, even I, do bring a flood of waters upon the earth, to destroy all flesh, wherein is the breath of life, from under heaven; and every thing that is in the earth shall die. But with thee will I establish my covenant; and thou shalt come into the ark, thou, and thy sons, and thy wife, and thy sons' wives with thee.

    Context: The judgment announcement (v.17) is immediately followed by the covenant promise (v.18). This structure mirrors the three-part pattern: judgment is announced, but salvation is offered to those who respond in faith.

    Notes: The contrast between v.17 ('every thing that is in the earth shall die') and v.18 ('But with thee will I establish my covenant') perfectly illustrates the gospel/judgment duality in the three-part pattern.

    Noah episode: final 7-day warning before flood judgment¶

    And the LORD said unto Noah, Come thou and all thy house into the ark; for thee have I seen righteous before me in this generation. Of every clean beast thou shalt take to thee by sevens, the male and his female: and of beasts that are not clean by two, the male and his female. Of fowls also of the air by sevens, the male and the female; to keep seed alive upon the face of all the earth. For yet seven days, and I will cause it to rain upon the earth forty days and forty nights; and every living substance that I have made will I destroy from off the face of the earth.

    Context: The final 7-day warning. Noah is called 'righteous' (tsaddiyq, H6662). The invitation 'Come thou and all thy house into the ark' is the last gospel call before judgment falls.

    Notes: God's address to Noah as tsaddiyq (righteous) confirms the connection to 2 Pet 2:5 (keryx dikaiosynes — preacher of righteousness). The 7-day final warning parallels the pattern of last-chance warnings before judgment in all six episodes.

    And spared not the old world, but saved Noah the eighth person, a preacher of righteousness, bringing in the flood upon the world of the ungodly;

    Context: Peter identifies Noah as keryx dikaiosynes — a herald (keryx, G2783) of righteousness (dikaiosyne, G1343). This confirms that Noah was not merely a boat-builder but a commissioned announcer of divine warning.

And he brake in pieces the images, and cut down the groves, and filled their places with the bones of men. Moreover the altar that was at Bethel, and the high place which Jeroboam the son of Nebat, who made Israel to sin, had made, both that altar and the high place he brake down, and burned the high place, and stamped it small to powder, and burned the grove. And as Josiah turned himself, he spied the sepulchres that were there in the mount, and sent, and took the bones out of the sepulchres, and burned them upon the altar, and polluted it, according to the word of the LORD which the man of God proclaimed, who proclaimed these words. Then he said, What title is that that I see? And the men of the city told him, It is the sepulchre of the man of God, which came from Judah, and proclaimed these things that thou hast done against the altar of Bethel. And he said, Let him alone; let no man move his bones. So they let his bones alone, with the bones of the prophet that came out of Samaria. And all the houses also of the high places that were in the cities of Samaria, which the kings of Israel had made to provoke the LORD to anger, Josiah took away, and did to them according to all the acts that he had done in Bethel. And he slew all the priests of the high places that were there upon the altars, and burned men's bones upon them, and returned to Jerusalem.

    Context: The most detailed account of idolatry destruction in the entire Bible. Josiah's reform systematically dismantles every form of false worship: Baal vessels, Asherah/grove, host-of-heaven worship, idolatrous priests, sodomite houses, Topheth/Molech, sun chariots, rooftop altars, high places from Solomon through Jeroboam. This is the exposure element at its most comprehensive.

    Notes: The catalogue of false worship destroyed includes: Baal (H1168), Asherah/grove (H842), host of heaven, sun/moon/planets, Molech (child sacrifice), Ashtoreth (Zidonians), Chemosh (Moabites), Milcom (Ammonites). The exposure spans from the temple itself (v.4) to the far reaches of Samaria (v.19). Note that sun worship (v.5, 11) is present in Josiah's reform context, even if it was not the primary issue at Carmel.
