    Bible Study: Does the New Testament repeat the same three-part pattern (gospel call → expose false system → warn of judgment) before Revelation 14? Examine: John the Baptist's ministry, Jesus' ministry, Stephen's speech in Acts 7, Paul at Athens (Acts 17:22-31), Paul to Rome (Rom 1-3).¶

    Question¶

    Does the New Testament repeat the same three-part pattern (gospel call → expose false system → warn of judgment) before Revelation 14? Examine: John the Baptist's ministry, Jesus' ministry, Stephen's speech in Acts 7, Paul at Athens (Acts 17:22-31), Paul to Rome (Rom 1-3).

    Prior Research Summary¶

    Three prior 3AM studies establish context: 3am-02 proved six OT episodes (Noah, Sodom, Egypt, Carmel, Josiah, Jeremiah) contain the three-part pattern and documented vocabulary chains (riyb→krisis, shachah→proskyneo, yatsa→exerchomai, chemah→thumos). gsp-10 proved Acts 17:22-31 maps element-by-element onto the first angel's message and that 1 Thess 1:9-10 compresses all three elements. gsp-07 proved judgment is integral to the gospel (Rom 2:16, Acts 17:30-31). Web research identified Dodd's kerygma analysis, Parsons' chiastic structure in Acts 17, Toussaint's three-cord analysis of Acts 7, Andrews' anticipation-vs-announcement distinction, Campbell's rhetorical analysis of Rom 1-3, and scholarly recognition of Matt 23's three-section structure. EGW explicitly connects John the Baptist to the first angel's message via the Elijah chain. Bohr expands this into a Three Elijahs framework.

    Primary Verses (34 — full analysis required)¶

    Revelation 14 reference text: The three angels' messages — the pattern being compared - Rev 14:6-7 — First angel's message — gospel call, fear God, judgment hour - Rev 14:8 — Second angel's message — Babylon is fallen - Rev 14:9-11 — Third angel's message — judgment warning against beast worship - Rev 14:12 — Result clause — patience of saints, commandments, faith of Jesus

    John the Baptist episode: John's preaching in Matthew and Luke - Matt 3:1-3 — John the Baptist's gospel call — repent for kingdom is at hand - Matt 3:7-10 — John exposes Pharisees/Sadducees — generation of vipers, axe at root - Matt 3:11-12 — John's judgment warning — unquenchable fire, winnowing fork - Luke 3:7-14 — Luke's expanded account of John's exposure and ethical demands - Luke 3:15-18 — Luke's account of John's judgment warning and gospel preaching

    Jesus' Matthew 23 discourse: Woes against scribes and Pharisees - Matt 23:1-12 — Jesus warns against scribes and Pharisees — opening exposure - Matt 23:13-33 — Seven woes — systematic exposure of false religious system - Matt 23:33-36 — Judgment warning — how shall ye escape the damnation of gehenna - Matt 23:37-39 — Judgment lament over Jerusalem — your house left desolate

    Stephen's speech: The longest sermon in Acts - Acts 7:2-8 — Stephen's gospel history — Abraham called, covenant promise - Acts 7:9-16 — Stephen — Joseph rejected then exalted, pattern of rejected savior - Acts 7:20-36 — Stephen — Moses rejected by Israel then sent as deliverer - Acts 7:37-43 — Stephen — Israel turned to idolatry despite Moses' ministry - Acts 7:44-50 — Stephen — temple theology exposed, God does not dwell in handmade houses - Acts 7:51-53 — Stephen's judgment indictment — stiff-necked, resist Holy Spirit, betray and murder the Just One

    Paul at Athens: The Areopagus sermon - Acts 17:22-25 — Paul at Athens — Creator God who made the world, does not dwell in temples - Acts 17:26-28 — Paul at Athens — God's universal sovereignty over nations - Acts 17:29-31 — Paul at Athens — idolatry exposed (v.29), judgment announced (v.31), repentance commanded (v.30)

    Paul to Rome: Romans 1-3 progressive argument - Rom 1:16-17 — Gospel declaration — power of God unto salvation, righteousness revealed - Rom 1:18-25 — Gentile corruption exposed — knew God but glorified him not, idolatry - Rom 1:26-32 — Consequences of false worship — God gave them up - Rom 2:1-11 — Jewish hypocrisy exposed — thou that judgest doest the same things - Rom 2:12-16 — Judgment according to gospel — God judges secrets by Jesus Christ according to my gospel - Rom 3:9-20 — Universal judgment — all under sin, every mouth stopped

    Compressed pattern texts: Additional NT texts showing the three-part pattern in compressed form - 1 Thess 1:9-10 — Compressed three-part pattern — turned to God, turned FROM idols, await coming wrath - Acts 24:24-25 — Paul before Felix — reasoned of righteousness, temperance, judgment to come

    Additional Primary Verses: - Mal 4:5-6 — OT Elijah prophecy — links to both John and end-time ministry, the typological backbone of the three Elijahs framework - Isa 40:3 — Prophecy of the forerunner — voice crying in wilderness, applied to John in all four Gospels - Eccl 12:13-14 — OT structural match — fear God, keep commandments, judgment — exact parallel to Rev 14:7,12 - Isa 52:7 — Basar/euangelizo bridge — good tidings proclaimed in context of deliverance FROM Babylon (Isa 52:11)

    1. John the Baptist's three-part ministry pattern: Retrieve the full texts of Matt 3:1-12 and Luke 3:1-20 with chapter context. Run greek_parser.py on Matt 3:2, 3:7, 3:10, 3:12 to parse key terms: metanoeo (G3340), gennemata echidnon (offspring of vipers), axine (G513, axe), pyr asbestos (G762, unquenchable fire). Check whether John's preaching naturally divides into three movements: (1) gospel call — 'Repent, for the kingdom of heaven is at hand' (Matt 3:2); (2) exposure — 'generation of vipers,' denunciation of presuming on Abrahamic descent (3:7-9), reproof of Herod (Luke 3:19-20); (3) judgment warning — 'axe laid to the root' (3:10), 'unquenchable fire' (3:12). READ $WORD_STUDIES/G3340-metanoeo.md and $WORD_STUDIES/G3341-metanoia.md. Run cross_testament_parallels_v2.py on Matt 3:10 and Matt 3:12 to find OT judgment parallels. Verses: Matt 3:1-3, Matt 3:7-10, Matt 3:11-12, Luke 3:7-14, Luke 3:15-18, Luke 3:18-20, Mal 4:5-6, Luke 1:15-17 Strong's: G3340, G3341, G2097, G513, G762, G4442

    2. Jesus' Matthew 23 discourse as three-part pattern: Retrieve the full text of Matthew 23 with chapter context (also Matt 24:1-3 for the transition to Olivet Discourse). The chapter divides into three sections: (1) warning about scribes/Pharisees (vv.1-12) — initial address to the disciples and people; (2) seven woes (vv.13-33) — systematic exposure of hypocrisy, a 'what' vs 'whom' problem (see Parsons on Acts 17); (3) judgment warning (vv.33-39) — 'how shall ye escape the damnation of gehenna' (v.33), blood guilt (vv.34-36), desolation lament (vv.37-39). Run greek_parser.py on Matt 23:13 (ouai/woe), 23:33 (krisis/judgment, geenna), 23:38 (eremos/desolate). READ $WORD_STUDIES/G2920-krisis.md for the judgment vocabulary. Investigate whether Matt 23-25 as a unit forms a macro three-part pattern: exposure (ch.23) → judgment signs (ch.24) → judgment execution parables (ch.25). Run cross_testament_parallels_v2.py on Matt 23:33 and 23:37 to find OT connections (Jer 7:34, Deut 32 links). Verses: Matt 23:1-12, Matt 23:13-33, Matt 23:33-36, Matt 23:37-39 Strong's: G3759, G2920, G1067, G2048, G5273

    3. Stephen's speech as prophetic covenant lawsuit: Retrieve the full text of Acts 7 (especially vv.2-53). Toussaint identifies three thematic cords: (1) God's program shows progress; (2) blessings transcend geography; (3) Israel consistently opposed God's messengers. Map these to: gospel history (vv.2-43 — God's faithfulness from Abraham through Moses), exposure of false system (vv.44-50 — temple theology exposed: 'the Most High dwelleth not in temples made with hands'), judgment indictment (vv.51-53 — 'ye stiff-necked and uncircumcised in heart and ears'). Run greek_parser.py on Acts 7:51 to parse sklerotrochelos (stiff-necked), antipiptete (resist), and Acts 7:52 (dikaios/Just One). Check whether 'stiff-necked' (sklerotrochelos, G4644) echoes Deut 9:6 LXX — if so, Stephen is invoking the covenant lawsuit tradition (riyb pattern) documented in 3am-02. READ $WORD_STUDIES/H7378-riyb.md and the TR-riyb-krisis translation relationship. Hodges argues the Joseph-Moses rejection typology is the unifying theme — verify whether the rejected-then-exalted pattern parallels the gospel element. Run cross_testament_parallels_v2.py on Acts 7:51. Verses: Acts 7:2-8, Acts 7:9-16, Acts 7:20-36, Acts 7:37-43, Acts 7:44-50, Acts 7:51-53 Strong's: G4644, G496, G4151, G1342, G5406, G4396

    4. Paul at Athens — chiastic sermon with three-part pattern: Retrieve the full text of Acts 17:16-34 with context. Parsons identifies a chiasm: A (ignorance, vv.22-23) / B (Creator, vv.24-25) / C (humanity-God, vv.26-28) / B' (idols exposed, v.29) / A' (judgment, vv.30-31). Run greek_parser.py on Acts 17:23 (agnostos/unknown God), 17:24 (kosmos/world, poiesas/made), 17:29 (theion/Godhead), 17:30 (hupereidon/overlooked, metanoein/repent), 17:31 (estesen hemeran/appointed a day, krinen/judge, anastesas/raised). READ $WORD_STUDIES/G3340-metanoeo.md, $WORD_STUDIES/G2920-krisis.md, $WORD_STUDIES/G2316-theos.md. The critical parallel with Rev 14:6-7 is: Creator worship (17:24 ~ Rev 14:7 'made heaven and earth'), universal scope (17:26,30 ~ Rev 14:6 'every nation'), judgment announcement (17:31 ~ Rev 14:7 'the hour of his judgment'). Check the Greek tense of 'estesen' (aorist) vs Rev 14:7 'elthen' — this is key to Andrews' anticipation-vs-announcement distinction. Run cross_testament_parallels_v2.py on Acts 17:31 for both OT and NT parallels. Verses: Acts 17:22-25, Acts 17:26-28, Acts 17:29-31 Strong's: G57, G2889, G4160, G5237, G3340, G2919, G2250, G450

    5. Paul's Romans 1-3 as gospel-exposure-judgment progression: Retrieve the full text of Rom 1:16-3:26 with chapter context. The traditional reading identifies a progressive structure: gospel declaration (1:16-17 — 'power of God unto salvation'), Gentile corruption exposed (1:18-32 — 'knew God, glorified him not as God, changed the truth into a lie'), Jewish corruption exposed (2:1-29 — 'thou that judgest doest the same things'), universal judgment verdict (3:9-20 — 'all the world guilty before God'). Run greek_parser.py on Rom 1:16 (dynamis/power, euangelion), 1:18 (orge/wrath, apokalyptetai/revealed), 1:25 (esebasthesan/worshipped, creature/ktisis), 2:16 (kata to euangelion mou/according to my gospel), 3:19 (hupodikos/under judgment). READ $WORD_STUDIES/G2098-euangelion.md, $WORD_STUDIES/G3709-orge.md, $WORD_STUDIES/G2920-krisis.md. The critical verse is Rom 2:16 — Paul explicitly ties judgment to 'my gospel' (kata to euangelion mou), proving judgment is not separate from gospel but integral to it. This parallels Rev 14:6 where the judgment announcement IS the everlasting gospel. Campbell's prosopopoeia theory does not negate the structural parallel — even if 1:18-32 is speech-in-character, the three-part movement remains. Run cross_testament_parallels_v2.py on Rom 2:16. Verses: Rom 1:16-17, Rom 1:18-25, Rom 1:26-32, Rom 2:1-11, Rom 2:12-16, Rom 3:9-20 Strong's: G2098, G1411, G3709, G601, G2937, G4352, G5228

    6. Pattern synthesis — structural correspondence across all five episodes: After gathering data on all five episodes (John the Baptist, Jesus' Matt 23, Stephen, Paul at Athens, Paul in Romans), build a synthesis comparing how each instance maps to the three-angel pattern. The Love in Truth academic thesis identifies four consistent elements across apostolic sermons: (1) core gospel facts proclaimed, (2) error/sin exposed — preachers 'unfailingly identify the main source of their opposition,' (3) call to response, (4) eschatological warning. Note how the EXPOSURE element adapts to audience while maintaining the same structural position: John exposes Pharisaic presumption, Jesus exposes scribal hypocrisy, Stephen exposes temple theology and prophet-rejection, Paul exposes Athenian idolatry, Romans exposes both Gentile and Jewish corruption. In every case the exposure targets the specific false system the audience is entrapped in — paralleling the second angel targeting 'Babylon' (the false system of the hearer's context). READ the 3am-02 CONCLUSION.md comparison matrix for the OT episodes to model the format. The synthesis should also note additional NT instances: Peter at Pentecost (Acts 2), Paul at Antioch (Acts 13), Paul before Felix (Acts 24:25), 1 Thess 1:9-10.
