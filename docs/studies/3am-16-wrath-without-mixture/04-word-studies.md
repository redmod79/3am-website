# Word Studies

## Question
What does 'the wine of the wrath of God, which is poured out without mixture into the cup of his indignation' (Rev 14:10) mean? How do thumos (G2372) and orge (G3709) function together? What does akratos (G194, unmixed) mean? What does 'tormented with fire and brimstone in the presence of the holy angels, and in the presence of the Lamb' (Rev 14:10) mean? What does 'the smoke of their torment ascendeth up for ever and ever' (Rev 14:11) mean — eternal conscious torment or permanent destruction? Address using OT prophetic convention: Isaiah 34:10 (Edom), Jude 7 (Sodom). How does Malachi 4:1-3 define the final outcome? What does 'no rest day nor night' describe?

### G2372 — thumos

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G2372-thumos.md`

**Occurrences:** 18

Fierce passion, boiling anger. From thyo (to rush, breathe hard). 10 of 18 occurrences in Revelation (56%), where it describes divine eschatological fury. Distinguished from orge as the more passionate, turbulent eruption of fury versus settled judicial anger. In Rev 14:10, thumos characterizes the WINE (content/intensity), while orge characterizes the CUP (judicial container).

**Semantic categories:** divine eschatological fury, human passionate anger, vice-list usage

**Key verses:** Rev 14:10, Rev 14:19, Rev 16:19, Rev 19:15, Rev 14:8

**Relevance:** Core vocabulary for the wrath-without-mixture passage. Thumos appears in the genitive chain of 14:10, 16:19, and 19:15 alongside orge, escalating from 3-genitive to 5-genitive chain. Maps to Hebrew chemah (H2534) via LXX.

### G3709 — orge

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G3709-orge.md`

**Occurrences:** 36

Settled judicial anger, vengeance. Dual-domain: anger (emotional state) AND punishment (judicial action). 12 of 36 in Romans; 6 in Revelation. More theologically loaded than thumos — orge carries the weight of God's judicial verdict. In the genitive chains, orge appears as the container/characterization (the CUP of his orge) while thumos appears as the content/intensity (the WINE of his thumos).

**Semantic categories:** divine judicial wrath, eschatological wrath, human anger

**Key verses:** Rev 14:10, Rev 16:19, Rev 19:15, Rev 6:16-17, Rom 1:18

**Relevance:** Paired with thumos in Rev 14:10, 16:19, 19:15. Bengel's distinction confirmed by parser: wine associates with thumos (genitive of content), cup associates with orge (genitive of characterization). Maps to Hebrew aph (H639) via LXX.

### G194 — akratos

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G194-akratos.md`

**Occurrences:** 1

Unmixed, undiluted. Hapax legomenon (Rev 14:10 only). Alpha-privative + kerannymi (G2767, to mix). In Greco-Roman culture, unmixed wine was considered barbarically strong. Applied to divine wrath: the cup contains thumos at full strength, with no mercy tempering. The deliberate oxymoron kekerasmenou akratou ('mixed unmixed') means God has PREPARED the cup but has NOT diluted it. Reverses Psalm 75:8's 'full of mixture' (mesek, H4469).

**Semantic categories:** undiluted divine wrath

**Key verses:** Rev 14:10

**Relevance:** The key term in the passage. Akratos reverses the OT cup-of-wrath tradition where wine was 'full of mixture' (Psa 75:8). The eschatological judgment removes the tempering element — no mercy, no intercessor (Rev 15:8, Dan 12:1).

### G928 — basanizo

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G928-basanizo.md`

**Occurrences:** 12

To torment, test, distress. From basanos (G931, touchstone for testing metals). Etymology: testing metals -> questioning under torture -> torment. 12 occurrences: 6 in Gospels (physical suffering, demonic encounters), 1 in Epistles (Lot's moral distress), 5 in Revelation (eschatological torment). Semantic range broader than just 'torture' — includes physical ailment (Mat 8:6), difficult toil (Mar 6:48), moral vexation (2 Pet 2:8).

**Semantic categories:** physical suffering, demonic encounter, moral distress, eschatological torment

**Key verses:** Rev 14:10, Rev 20:10, Rev 9:5, Mat 8:29, 2Pe 2:8

**Relevance:** The verb in 'he shall be tormented' (basanisthesetai). Parser confirms future passive indicative in Rev 14:10. The touchstone etymology suggests testing/proving rather than pure torture. In Rev 14:10, the torment is future tense (eschatological), while the 'no rest' clause in 14:11 is present tense — a significant tense shift.

### G929 — basanismos

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G929-basanismos.md`

**Occurrences:** 6

Torment (noun), the process of being tormented. ALL 6 occurrences in Revelation only: Rev 9:5 (5-month bounded torment), Rev 14:11 (smoke of torment ascending), Rev 18:7 (Babylon's torment), Rev 18:10 (kings watch Babylon's torment), Rev 18:15 (merchants fear Babylon's torment). 3 of 6 describe Babylon's COMPLETED destruction. 1 has explicit time limit. Zero occurrences in epistles, Gospels, or OT. Exclusively apocalyptic vocabulary.

**Semantic categories:** demonic torment (bounded), eschatological judgment torment, commercial/imperial torment of judgment

**Key verses:** Rev 14:11, Rev 9:5, Rev 18:7, Rev 18:10, Rev 18:15

**Relevance:** Critical for evaluating Peterson/Beale's claim that basanismos 'never means annihilation.' While technically true (basanismos describes the EXPERIENCE of torment, not annihilation itself), 3 of 6 uses describe an entity (Babylon) whose complete cessation is documented. The vocabulary describes the torment of the destruction PROCESS, not eternal conscious existence.

### G372 — anapausis

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G372-anapausis.md`

**Occurrences:** 5

Rest, intermission, cessation. From anapauo (G373). 5 occurrences: Mat 11:29 (find rest for souls), Mat 12:43 (unclean spirit seeking rest), Luk 11:24 (same), Rev 4:8 (living creatures rest not day and night), Rev 14:11 (beast-worshippers have no rest day nor night). The Rev 4:8 and 14:11 constructions are grammatically IDENTICAL — same word, same tense (present active indicative), same temporal genitives (hemeras kai nuktos).

**Semantic categories:** spiritual rest offered by Christ, restless seeking, uninterrupted worship activity, absence of rest for beast-worshippers

**Key verses:** Rev 14:11, Rev 4:8, Mat 11:29, Rev 14:13

**Relevance:** The 'no rest day nor night' in Rev 14:11 is structurally identical to Rev 4:8 where living creatures 'rest not day and night' — describing uninterrupted activity quality, not guaranteed eternal duration. Peterson's argument that 'annihilation would be a kind of rest' is answered by this: the phrase describes quality of experience during the process, not ontological eternality.

### G2586 — kapnos

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G2586-kapnos.md`

**Occurrences:** 13

Smoke. 13 tokens, 91% in Revelation. Used for incense smoke ascending before God (Rev 8:4), abyss smoke (Rev 9:2-3), and judgment smoke (Rev 14:11, 19:3). Smoke is testimony — visible evidence that transformation has occurred. Fire is the agent; smoke is the witness; ashes are the residue.

**Semantic categories:** prophetic sign, incense smoke ascending before God, abyss smoke, judgment smoke as testimony

**Key verses:** Rev 14:11, Rev 19:3, Rev 8:4, Rev 9:2

**Relevance:** The smoke ascending 'for ever and ever' in Rev 14:11 parallels the incense smoke ascending before God in Rev 8:4 — both are testimony before God's presence. The smoke-imagery-hebrew-mind prior study established: smoke is witness of completed transformation, not ongoing burning.

### G166 — aionios

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G166-aionios.md`

**Occurrences:** 71

Eternal, everlasting, age-long. From aion (G165). 71 tokens across 69 verses. Predominantly modifies positive theological realities (eternal life 43x). When modifying negative/judgment terms, follows the Hebrews pattern: aionios + result-noun = permanent result of completed act. Eternal redemption (Heb 9:12), eternal judgment (Heb 6:2), eternal salvation (Heb 5:9) — all describe permanent results, not ongoing processes.

**Semantic categories:** eternal life/salvation (positive), eternal judgment/destruction (result-permanence), God's eternal nature

**Key verses:** Mat 25:46, Jud 1:7, 2Th 1:9, Heb 9:12, Heb 6:2

**Relevance:** Resolves the Matt 25:46 parallel: if eternal life means life that never ends (because the subject IS alive), eternal punishment means punishment whose RESULT never ends (the subject is punished, result permanent). Jude 7 — Sodom suffered 'eternal fire' yet is now ashes.

### G165 — aion

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G165-aion.md`

**Occurrences:** 120

Age, eon, perpetuity. 95 unique verses, 120 tokens across 22 books. The doubled formula eis tous aionas ton aionon ('unto the ages of the ages') occurs in Revelation for doxological (God's existence/reign), saints' reign, and judgment contexts. In Revelation: 9 doxological, 1 saints' reign, 3 judgment (14:11, 19:3, 20:10). Doxological uses describe eternal realities because subjects are eternal. Judgment uses describe permanent consequences for non-eternal subjects.

**Semantic categories:** temporal age/era, eternal duration formula, worlds/creation

**Key verses:** Rev 14:11, Rev 19:3, Rev 20:10, Heb 1:2

**Relevance:** The formula 'eis aionas aionon' in Rev 14:11 does not automatically require conscious eternality. The for-ever-and-ever-revelation prior study catalogued all 13 Revelation occurrences and found the 3 judgment uses describe permanent consequences for non-eternal subjects.

### G1799 — enopion

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G1799-enopion.md`

**Occurrences:** 97

In the presence of, before, in the sight of. 97 occurrences, 36 in Revelation (highest concentration). Used for divine presence/worship (throne room), divine evaluation (moral standing), judicial contexts, and acts performed before witnesses. Carries formal judicial weight — 'in the face of,' implying accountability and formal proceedings.

**Semantic categories:** divine presence/worship, divine evaluation, judicial witness, formal proceedings

**Key verses:** Rev 14:10, Rev 8:4, Rev 3:5, Rev 20:12

**Relevance:** In Rev 14:10, torment occurs 'enopion the holy angels and enopion the Lamb.' This is judicial witness language, not spectator entertainment. Parallels Rev 8:4 where incense ascends enopion God — both describe things presented before God's judicial presence.

### G622 — apollymi

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G622-apollymi.md`

**Occurrences:** 102

To destroy fully, to perish, to lose. 86 unique verses, 102 tokens across 15 books. Active: destroy, ruin. Middle/Passive: perish, be lost. The primary destruction verb across genres. Antonym: sozo (G4982, to save). John 3:16 binary: apollymi (perish) vs. zoe aionios (eternal life). Used in didactic teaching by Jesus, Paul, and Peter for the fate of the wicked.

**Semantic categories:** violent destruction, religious perish/save antonym, loss of possession, cessation of existence

**Key verses:** Joh 3:16, Mat 10:28, Luk 13:3, 2Pe 3:9

**Relevance:** The dominant NT vocabulary for the fate of the wicked in DIDACTIC passages (Gospels, Epistles). Contrasts with basanizo/basanismos which appears only in apocalyptic (Revelation). The genre distribution is a structural argument: Jesus and Paul consistently use destruction/death vocabulary; torment vocabulary is confined to Revelation's apocalyptic imagery.

### G2288 — thanatos

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G2288-thanatos.md`

**Occurrences:** 119

Death. 119 tokens across 15 books. The fundamental word for death — physical, spiritual, and eschatological. Rom 6:23: 'wages of sin is death.' Rev 20:14: lake of fire = 'second death.' Rev 21:4: 'no more death.' Death is the LAST enemy destroyed (1 Cor 15:26). The 'second death' is Revelation's own definition of the lake of fire.

**Semantic categories:** physical death, spiritual death, second death (eschatological), death personified

**Key verses:** Rom 6:23, Rev 20:14, Rev 21:4, 1Co 15:26

**Relevance:** The defining term for human wicked at the lake of fire is 'second death' — death vocabulary, not torment vocabulary. If death is the last enemy destroyed (1 Cor 15:26), then death ceases to exist, and those in the 'second death' cease to exist.

### G4442 — pyr

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G4442-pyr.md`

**Occurrences:** 73

Fire. 73 unique verses, 25 in Revelation (34%). Primary word for fire, literal and figurative. Used for purification, divine presence, judgment, and destruction. God IS a consuming fire (Deu 4:24, Heb 12:29). Fire CONSUMES — the verb katakaio (burn up completely) and katesthio (devour) accompany it in judgment contexts.

**Semantic categories:** literal fire, divine nature/presence, purification, eschatological judgment/destruction

**Key verses:** Rev 14:10, Rev 20:9, Rev 20:14, Mat 3:10, Heb 12:29

**Relevance:** Fire in Rev 14:10 is the judgment agent. Rev 20:9 uses katephagen (devoured) for human wicked — fire as consuming agent, not preserving medium. God IS fire (Deu 4:24, Heb 12:29) — fire characterizes divine nature, not a punishment chamber.

### G2719 — katesthio

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G2719-katesthio.md`

**Occurrences:** 20

To devour, eat down completely. 20 occurrences. In Rev 20:9, katephagen (aorist: devoured) describes fire consuming HUMAN followers of Satan. The kata- prefix intensifies — eat down completely. Devouring fire finishes its work; the objects are consumed.

**Semantic categories:** literal eating/consumption, fire consuming/destruction, figurative devouring

**Key verses:** Rev 20:9, Rev 12:4, Gal 5:15

**Relevance:** The verb for what fire does to HUMANS in Rev 20:9. Fire DEVOURED (katephagen) them — consuming fire that finishes its work. Contrasts with basanisthesetai (tormented) used for the devil/beast/false prophet in 20:10. Different verbs for different subjects.

### H6227 — ashan

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H6227-ashan.md`

**Occurrences:** 25

Smoke (noun). From H6225. 25 occurrences across OT. 7 in Isaiah, 4 in Psalms. Used for theophanic smoke (Sinai), judgment smoke (Sodom, Edom), transience imagery, and divine anger. The Hebrew behind Greek kapnos (G2586). Isa 34:10 uses ashan for Edom's smoke ascending forever.

**Semantic categories:** theophanic smoke, judgment smoke, transience imagery, divine anger imagery

**Key verses:** Isa 34:10, Gen 19:28, Psa 37:20, Psa 68:2, Exo 19:18

**Relevance:** The Hebrew smoke vocabulary behind the smoke-ascending-forever formula. Isa 34:10 uses ashan for the smoke of Edom ascending le-olam. Maps to Greek kapnos via LXX.
