    Bible Study: Rev 18:4 'come out of her my people.' God's people inside Babylon. OT come-out tradition (Abraham, Exodus, literal Babylon). 2 Cor 6:14-18. Hina-me purpose clause. John 10:16. Fall-then-call sequence.¶

    Question¶

    Rev 18:4 'come out of her my people.' God's people inside Babylon. OT come-out tradition (Abraham, Exodus, literal Babylon). 2 Cor 6:14-18. Hina-me purpose clause. John 10:16. Fall-then-call sequence.

    Prior Research Summary¶

    Thirteen prior studies provide extensive foundation. 3am-10 established Babylon as apostate religious system using the zanah/porneia chain (H2181/G4202). 3am-11 identified the wine as false teaching creating participatory communion (1 Cor 10:16-21). 3am-12 demonstrated the doubled epesen epesen as prophetic aorist, the egeneto verb of transition showing progressive moral deterioration, and mapped twelve verbal parallels between Jer 50-51 and Rev 17-18. The prior rev-18-babylon-fall-come-out study covered the aorist imperative exelthate (G1831), the possessive ho laos mou, the synkoinoneo word (G4790), and the anti-Jubilee indictment. This study must go deeper on the come-out theology with fresh research, particularly the OT come-out chain, the hina-me purpose clause grammar, the John 10:16 fold/flock distinction, and the 2 Cor 6:14-18 composite citation.

    Primary Verses (28 — full analysis required)¶

    Crux text and immediate context: Rev 18:1-8 — the expanded fall announcement and come-out call with surrounding judgment - Rev 18:1-3 — Expanded fall announcement immediately preceding the come-out call — egeneto verb, demon-dwelling imagery - Rev 18:4-5 — Crux text — 'come out of her my people' with dual hina-me purpose clauses and sins reaching heaven - Rev 18:6-8 — Judgment specification following the come-out call — 'double unto her double' and 'in one hour' timing - Rev 18:20 — Vindication of holy ones, apostles, prophets — God avenges them against Babylon - Rev 18:24 — Blood of prophets and saints found in her — ultimate reason for the come-out call

    OT come-out chain — prophetic commands: The Jeremiah/Isaiah/Zechariah commands to leave literal Babylon - Isa 48:20 — OT come-out command — 'go ye forth of Babylon, flee ye from the Chaldeans' - Isa 52:11 — OT come-out command — 'depart ye, depart ye, go ye out from thence, touch no unclean thing' - Jer 50:8 — OT come-out command — 'remove out of the midst of Babylon, go forth out of the land of the Chaldeans' - Jer 51:6 — OT come-out command — 'flee out of the midst of Babylon, deliver every man his soul' - Jer 51:45 — Closest verbal parallel to Rev 18:4 — 'my people, go ye out of the midst of her' - Zech 2:6-7 — Post-exilic come-out call — 'flee from the land of the north' with covenant people still dwelling in Babylon

    OT come-out types — narrative patterns: Abraham, Lot, Korah, Exodus — narrative demonstrations of the separation principle - Gen 12:1 — Abraham's original come-out call — 'get thee out of thy country' from Ur/Chaldea = proto-Babylon - Gen 19:12-17 — Lot's come-out from Sodom — urgency, angels compelling departure, proximity = shared destruction - Num 16:26 — Korah rebellion — 'depart from the tents of these wicked men, touch nothing of theirs' lest shared destruction

    Hosea reversal pattern: Lo-ammi / ammi reversal applied in NT to explain who 'my people' are - Hos 1:9-10 — Lo-ammi / ammi reversal — 'not my people' becomes 'my people' — illuminates who ho laos mou are - Hos 2:23 — Reversal fulfilled — 'I will say to them which were not my people, Thou art my people' - Rom 9:25-26 — Paul quotes Hosea reversal — applies to Gentile inclusion, people called from outside covenant boundaries - 1 Pet 2:9-10 — Peter quotes Hosea — 'which in time past were not a people, but are now the people of God'

    John 10 — shepherd and flock: The fold/flock distinction and other sheep theology - John 10:1-5 — True shepherd enters by the door; sheep hear his voice — framework for the come-out call as shepherd's voice - John 10:16 — Other sheep not of this fold — aule vs poimne distinction, God's flock transcends institutional folds - John 10:27-28 — My sheep hear my voice — the come-out call requires recognition of the shepherd's voice

    Participation/fellowship cluster: Three synkoinoneo occurrences plus koinonia two-table framework - Eph 5:11 — First of three synkoinoneo/darkness parallels — 'have no fellowship with the unfruitful works of darkness' - Phil 4:14 — Second synkoinoneo occurrence — positive use (sharing in distress), illuminates the verb's participatory meaning - 1 Cor 10:16-21 — Two-cup framework — cup of the Lord vs cup of demons, table participation requiring departure

    Fall-then-call sequence: The sequence from first angel through fall announcement to come-out call - Rev 18:1-3 — Expanded fall announcement immediately preceding the come-out call — egeneto verb, demon-dwelling imagery

    Transfer/deliverance language: NT language of being transferred from one kingdom to another - Col 1:13-14 — Transfer language — 'delivered us from the power of darkness, translated us into the kingdom of his dear Son'

    Additional Primary Verses: - Rev 14:8 — Original fall announcement — doubled epesen, establishes the fall-then-call sequence - Rev 18:2 — Repeated fall announcement with additional corruption detail — demon habitation language - 2 Cor 6:14-18 — NT composite citation of Isa 52:11 with heterozygeo, five antitheses, and chiastic OT catena

    Focus Areas¶

    1. The dual hina-me purpose clause in Rev 18:4: READ the grammar reference entry at $GRAMMAR_REF/greek/purpose-clause-hina-me-aorist-subjunctive.md — it explicitly covers the Rev 18:4 double clause. Parse Rev 18:4 with greek_parser.py --verse 'Rev 18:4' to confirm the dual hina-me structure. The first clause uses synkoinonesete (G4790, aorist subjunctive of synkoinoneo — 'lest you share in her sins') and the second uses labete (aorist subjunctive of lambano — 'lest you receive her plagues'). Meyer identifies 'an explicit antithesis between tais hamartiais and ton plegon' — sharing sins leads to sharing plagues. The aorist subjunctive in both clauses views the action as a single potential event, not ongoing — they haven't done it yet, but could. Also read the mood-aorist-imperative grammar reference for the exelthate command form. The Bratcher & Hatton UBS Handbook confirms the dual purpose: (1) moral separation and (2) judgment avoidance. Cross-reference Rev 15:1 and 16:1 for the plagues (plegon) that the second clause references. Verses: Rev 18:4, Rev 18:5, Rev 15:1, Rev 16:1, Eph 5:11, Phil 4:14 Strong's: G4790, G2983, G4127, G1831

    2. The OT come-out tradition: vocabulary and pattern chain: READ the word study at $WORD_STUDIES/H3318-yatsa.md for the full semantic range of yatsa. The OT come-out chain runs: Gen 12:1 (Abraham from Ur/Chaldea — 'get thee out'), Gen 19:12-17 (Lot from Sodom — angels compelling departure), Exo 12:31-33 (Israel from Egypt — 'rise up, get you forth'), Num 16:26 (departure from Korah — 'depart from the tents'), Isa 48:20 ('go ye forth of Babylon'), Isa 52:11 ('depart ye, depart ye'), Jer 50:8 ('remove out of the midst of Babylon'), Jer 51:6 ('flee out of the midst'), Jer 51:45 ('my people, go ye out of the midst of her'), Zech 2:6-7 ('flee from the land of the north'). Run hebrew_parser.py on Isa 52:11 and Jer 51:45 to trace the Hebrew verbs. The LXX of Isa 52:11 uses exerchomai (G1831), the same root as exelthate in Rev 18:4. Retrieve full chapter context for Jer 51 and Isa 52 to see the come-out commands in their prophetic setting. Note the pattern: each come-out call is preceded by an announcement of judgment and followed by judgment execution — the same pattern Rev 14:8 → 18:4 → 18:8 follows. Verses: Gen 12:1, Gen 19:12-17, Exo 12:31-33, Num 16:26, Isa 48:20, Isa 52:11, Jer 50:8, Jer 51:6, Jer 51:45, Zech 2:6-7 Strong's: H3318, G1831, H5127, H6440

    3. Ho laos mou — God's people inside Babylon: identity and ecclesiology: READ the word studies at $WORD_STUDIES/G2992-laós.md and $WORD_STUDIES/H5971-am.md for the full semantic range of 'people' in both Testaments. The phrase ho laos mou is a covenant designation — God calls them HIS people before they leave Babylon, not after. Parse Rev 18:4 to confirm the grammatical structure of the vocative address. Trace the Hosea reversal: Hos 1:9 (lo-ammi, 'not my people'), Hos 2:23 ('I will say to them which were not my people, Thou art my people'), Rom 9:25-26 (Paul applies to Gentile inclusion), 1 Pet 2:9-10 (Peter applies to the new covenant community). Does this pattern illuminate who the ho laos mou are in Rev 18:4? Gill distinguishes 'elect ones till now uncalled' from those 'secretly called but not publicly professing.' JFB affirms 'in every apostate or world-conforming church there are some of God's invisible and true Church.' Cross-reference with the ekklesia study at $WORD_STUDIES/G1577-ekklesia.md for the relationship between visible institution and invisible community. Verses: Rev 18:4, Hos 1:9-10, Hos 2:23, Rom 9:25-26, 1 Pet 2:9-10, Rev 17:14 Strong's: G2992, H5971, G1577, G2564

    4. John 10:16 — the aule/poimne distinction and God's flock beyond institutional folds: Parse John 10:16 with greek_parser.py --verse 'John 10:16' to confirm the aule/poimne distinction. READ the word study at $WORD_STUDIES/G4166-poimen.md for shepherd semantics. Web research unanimously confirms the KJV 'one fold' mistranslates poimne (flock) — Ellicott says 'It is not unity of fold which is regarded as the future of the Church, but unity of flock.' Bengel notes 'He says other sheep, not another sheep-fold.' The asyndetic construction 'mia poimne, heis poimen' (one flock, one shepherd) emphasizes identity without requiring institutional merger. The dei (G1163, divine necessity) in 'them also I MUST bring' indicates a divine mandate — READ $WORD_STUDIES/G1163-dei.md. Compare with Ezek 34:11-16 where God himself seeks scattered sheep. The fold/flock distinction explains how God can have ho laos mou in Babylon — they belong to his flock (poimne) even though they are in the wrong fold (aule). Run cross_testament_parallels_v2.py --hybrid-nt 'John 10:16' to find OT connections. Verses: John 10:16, John 10:1-5, John 10:27-28, Ezek 34:11-16, Matt 18:12-13 Strong's: G4167, G833, G4166, G4263, G1163

    5. 2 Corinthians 6:14-18 — the composite OT catena and NT application of the come-out tradition: Parse 2 Cor 6:14-18 with greek_parser.py --verse '2 Cor 6:14' through 6:18. Lee Magness identifies a chiastically arranged catena: A (v.16b) Lev 26:12/Ezek 37:27 — divine dwelling and covenant formula; B (v.17a) Isa 52:11 — 'come out and be separate'; B' (v.17b) 'quit touching the unclean'; A' (v.18) 2 Sam 7:14 — Father-child covenant language (altered: singular to plural, 'son' to 'sons and daughters'). The opening word heterozygeo (2 Cor 6:14) is a NT hapax — READ $WORD_STUDIES/G2087-heteros.md for the heteros root. The verb derives from LXX Lev 19:19 — Paul frames the entire separation argument as a species-mixing violation. Verify each OT citation by retrieving the source texts. The five antitheses (righteousness/unrighteousness, light/darkness, Christ/Belial, believer/infidel, temple/idols) establish that the issue is ontological incompatibility, not mere preference. The closing formula 'says the Lord Almighty' (pantokrator) occurs only here and in Revelation in the NT — investigate the link. Verses: 2 Cor 6:14, 2 Cor 6:15, 2 Cor 6:16, 2 Cor 6:17, 2 Cor 6:18, Lev 26:12, Ezek 37:27, Isa 52:11, 2 Sam 7:14, Lev 19:19 Strong's: G2086, G2087, G3352, G4790, G169

    6. The fall-then-call sequence: Rev 14:8 → Rev 18:2 → Rev 18:4: Retrieve Rev 14:6-8 and Rev 18:1-5 with full chapter context. The sequence is: first angel's message (14:6-7, gospel call) → second angel's announcement (14:8, Babylon is fallen) → fourth angel's expanded announcement (18:1-3, with egeneto showing progressive deterioration and demon-dwelling imagery added) → come-out call (18:4, with hina-me purpose clauses) → judgment execution (18:8, 'in one hour'). The fall announcement makes the come-out call both POSSIBLE (people now understand the system's true character) and NECESSARY (judgment is imminent). READ $WORD_STUDIES/G4098-pipto.md for the pipto/epesen analysis already established in 3am-12. The egeneto (G1096) at 18:2 uses a verb of becoming/transition — READ $WORD_STUDIES/G1096-ginomai.md. Bohr claims Babylon falls BECAUSE she rejected the first angel's message — verify from the hoti clause in Rev 14:8 and the sequential placement. The time gap between 14:8 and 18:4 allows for progressive deterioration before the urgent come-out call. Verses: Rev 14:6-8, Rev 18:1-3, Rev 18:4, Rev 18:8, Isa 21:9 Strong's: G4098, G1096, G1831, G5456

    7. Synkoinoneo (G4790) — the three-occurrence cluster and active co-participation: Synkoinoneo (G4790) appears only three times in the NT: Eph 5:11 ('have no fellowship with the unfruitful works of darkness'), Phil 4:14 ('ye did communicate with my affliction' — positive sharing), and Rev 18:4 ('that ye be not partakers of her sins'). The syn- prefix intensifies koinoneo (G2841) — READ the library entry at $WORD_STUDIES/G2841-koinoneo.md. Parse all three verses with greek_parser.py. Bill Mounce notes the verb means 'to share in company with, be connected with, be a joint partaker.' The compound implies not mere awareness or proximity but active co-participation. Eph 5:11 provides the closest parallel — 'unfruitful works of darkness' maps to Babylon's sins. Phil 4:14 shows the same verb can describe positive sharing, confirming it means real participation in the referenced experience. The koinonia word family (G2842) is the broader framework — retrieve the context from 1 Cor 10:16-21 where two tables are set and participation at one excludes the other.     8. The Sodom-Korah-Egypt triad: OT types of judgment-separation: The triad establishes a consistent pattern: (1) Gen 19:12-17 — Lot is commanded to leave Sodom before fire falls. 2 Pet 2:6-7 interprets this: God 'delivered just Lot, vexed with the filthy conversation of the wicked.' The urgency is physical — 'Escape for thy life; look not behind thee.' (2) Num 16:26 — Moses commands Israel to 'depart from the tents of these wicked men, and touch nothing of theirs, lest ye be consumed in all their sins.' The verb is sur (H5493) — depart, turn away. The warning is explicitly about shared destruction through proximity. Num 16:41-50 shows that even after the separation, a plague killed 14,700 of those who had complained. (3) Exo 8:22-23 — God puts a 'division' (peduth, H6304, distinction/separation) between his people and the Egyptians during the plagues. READ $WORD_STUDIES/G4127-plege.md for the plague vocabulary that connects the Egyptian plagues to Rev 15-16. The GotQuestions source notes: 'In times of judgment, God separates His people from those being judged.' The come-out call of Rev 18:4 is the eschatological version of this pattern. Verses: Gen 19:12-17, Num 16:26, Exo 8:22-23, 2 Pet 2:6-7, Num 16:41-50, Exo 12:13 Strong's: H3318, H5493, H6304, G4127

    9. The speaker identity and the voice from heaven: Rev 18:4 introduces the speaker as 'allon phonen ek tou ouranou' — another voice from heaven. READ $WORD_STUDIES/G5456-phone.md for the voice vocabulary in Revelation. The 'allon' (another, of the same kind) distinguishes this voice from the angel of 18:1 but still locates it in heaven. The possessive 'ho laos MOU' (MY people) is a covenant designation — in the OT, only God uses this phrase in the possessive first person. Bratcher & Hatton note: 'The words my people clearly identify the speaker as God himself (cross-ref 16:1).' But in Revelation, angelic messengers regularly speak on God's behalf. Parse Rev 18:4 to check whether the grammar clarifies the speaker. Compare with Rev 21:3 where 'a great voice out of heaven' uses similar covenant language. The John 10:16 connection is important — if the shepherd's voice calls the sheep, and the come-out call is the shepherd's voice, then the speaker is Christ calling his scattered flock. Read the nominative-for-vocative grammar reference at $GRAMMAR_REF/greek/nominative-for-vocative.md — this may be relevant if ho laos mou uses nominative for vocative address. Verses: Rev 18:4, Rev 16:1, Rev 21:3, Rev 14:13, John 10:3-4, John 10:27 Strong's: G5456, G2992, G243, G3772

    10. Sins reaching unto heaven — the Gen 18:20-21 / Rev 18:5 parallel: Rev 18:5 uses ekollethesan (from kollao, G2853, to glue/join) — her sins are 'glued together reaching unto heaven.' This parallels Gen 18:20-21 where the 'cry of Sodom and Gomorrah' reaches God, triggering the judgment sequence. Parse Rev 18:5 with greek_parser.py --verse 'Rev 18:5'. The verb kollao means to cement or join — the image is sins piled on sins, bonded together into a structure reaching heaven. This echoes the Tower of Babel (Gen 11:4 — a structure reaching heaven in rebellion), creating an inclusio with the original Babylon narrative. Also compare Ezra 9:6 ('our iniquities are increased over our head, and our trespass is grown up unto the heavens') and Jer 51:9 ('her judgment reacheth unto heaven'). The reaching-heaven motif establishes a judicial point of no return: once this threshold is crossed, judgment is inevitable and God's people must be removed. The mnemoneuo at 18:5 ('God hath remembered her iniquities') echoes Rev 16:19 ('great Babylon came in remembrance before God'). Verses: Rev 18:5, Gen 18:20-21, Gen 11:4, Ezra 9:6, Jer 51:9, Rev 16:19 Strong's: G2853, G3421, H6817, H1361
