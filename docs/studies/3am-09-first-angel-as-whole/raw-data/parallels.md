# Cross-Testament Parallels

- Rev 14:7 → Eccl 12:13-14 (ot, structural + vocabulary — fear God (yare/phobeo) + commandments (mitsvah/entole) + judgment (mishpat/krisis). The OT structural twin.)
- Rev 14:7 → Acts 17:22-31 (nt, structural + vocabulary — Creator (poieo in both), universal scope, judgment (krino/krisis), worship correction. The apostolic precedent.)
- Rev 14:6-7 → Psa 96:1-13 (ot, structural + vocabulary — new song, basar/euangelizo, glory (kabod/doxa), creation (asah/poieo), judgment (shaphat/krisis). Every element present.)
- Rev 14:7 → Exo 20:11 (ot, vocabulary — four terms in identical sequence: made (asah/poieo), heaven (shamayim/ouranos), earth (erets/ge), sea (yam/thalassa). The most certain OT allusion in Revelation.)
- Rev 14:7 → Rom 1:21-23 (nt, antiparallel — Rom 1:21 'glorified him NOT as God' is the exact failure Rev 14:7 'give him glory' reverses. The glory-exchange (eikon, same root as Rev 13's image) is the antiparallel of Creator-worship.)
- Rev 14:7 → Rev 16:9 (nt, verbal echo — same three lexemes (didomi + autos + doxa). Command in 14:7 becomes refused purpose in 16:9.)
- Rev 14:7 → Rom 4:20 (nt, vocabulary — dous doxan to Theo (giving glory to God) = same construction as dote auto doxan in Rev 14:7. Abraham's faith IS glory-giving.)
- Rev 14:7 → Deut 10:12-13 (ot, pattern — first iteration of farewell-address pattern: fear + commandments + serve.)
- Rev 14:7 → Josh 24:14 (ot, pattern — second iteration of farewell-address pattern: fear + serve + remove idols.)
- Rev 14:7 → 1 Sam 12:24 (ot, pattern — third iteration of farewell-address pattern: fear + serve + remember God's acts.)
- Rev 14:7 → Rom 2:16 (nt, thematic — Paul labels judgment as 'according to my gospel' (kata to euangelion mou). Judgment IS gospel content.)
- Rev 14:7 → Luke 3:17-18 (nt, thematic — Luke labels John's judgment preaching as euangelizeto. Judgment preaching IS gospel.)
- Rev 14:7 → Dan 7:9-10 (ot, thematic — the throne-room judgment scene the first angel announces. Aramaic dina connects via LXX to krisis.)
- Rev 14:7 → Dan 7:22 (ot, thematic — judgment given FOR the saints (benefactive le). Judgment vindicates the faithful.)
- Rev 14:7 → 1 Thess 1:9-10 (nt, structural — compressed gospel containing all first angel elements in one sentence: turned from idols + serve living God + wait for Son + delivered from wrath.)
- Rev 14:7 → John 1:1-3 (nt, christological — Christ as Creator. 'Him that made' in Rev 14:7 implicitly invokes Christ as the agent of creation.)
- Rev 14:7 → Col 1:15-17 (nt, christological — 'by him were all things created.' Christ IS the Creator invoked by Rev 14:7.)
- Rev 14:7 → Acts 14:15 (nt, vocabulary + structural — 'turn from vanities unto the living God, which made (poieo) heaven, and earth, and the sea.' Same creation formula, same verb, same elements.)
- Psa 96:1 → Rev 14:3 (nt, vocabulary — shiyr chadash (new song) connects to Rev 14:3's 'new song' through the LXX ode kaine. The worship tradition framing the first angel's message.)
- Psa 96:2 → Rev 14:6 (nt, vocabulary — basseru (Piel of basar, 'proclaim good news') is the Hebrew root of euangelizo. The psalm literally says 'proclaim good news of his salvation.')
- Rev 14:12 → Rev 12:17 (nt, inclusio — both use entolas tou theou. Commandment-keeping brackets the three angels' messages.)
- Rev 14:12 → Gen 15:6 (ot, pattern — Abraham believed (faith FIRST), then obeyed (Gen 26:5, obedience as FRUIT). Rev 14:12 presents the same pattern under eschatological pressure.)
- Rev 14:7 → Josh 7:19 (ot, vocabulary + thematic — 'give glory to the LORD God of Israel and make confession.' Hebraic expression for truth-telling before God's judgment seat.)
- Rev 14:7 → Jer 13:16 (ot, vocabulary + thematic — 'Give glory to the LORD your God, before he cause darkness.' Glory-giving before judgment — same urgency as Rev 14:7.)
- Rev 14:7 → Psa 98:1-9 (ot, structural — parallel enthronement psalm with new song (v.1) and judgment (v.9). Confirms the worship pattern.)
- Rev 14:7 → Psa 33:1-9 (ot, structural — new song (v.3) + creation by word (v.6) + fear of the LORD (v.8). Multiple first angel elements converge.)
- Rev 14:7 → Isa 42:10-12 (ot, structural — new song (v.10) + universal scope + 'give glory unto the LORD' (v.12). Direct parallel.)
