8. **Literal vs. symbolic angels — what does the apocalyptic genre require?:**
The question is NOT whether angelos CAN mean human messenger (it can — G32 covers both celestial and human messengers). The question is whether the apocalyptic genre's symbolic world requires symbolic figures. Run search_strongs.py --verses G32 to map ALL angelos occurrences in Revelation — there are approximately 67. Categorize them: (a) clearly celestial (Rev 7:1, 8:2, etc.), (b) clearly symbolic/human (Rev 2:1 angel of the church?), (c) ambiguous. The strongest structural argument: the beasts of Rev 13 are universally understood as symbolic of earthly powers/systems, not literal animals. If the antagonists are symbolic, consistency suggests the protagonists (angels) are as well. EGW explicitly states the three angels 'symbolize the work of those who proclaim' the messages (6T 17.4). The gospel content of the first angel's message echoes Great Commission language given to human agents (Mat 28:19-20, Mark 16:15). However, the internal vision-world consistently presents angels as celestial figures who act.
