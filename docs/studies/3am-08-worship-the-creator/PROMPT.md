1. **The verbal overlap between Rev 14:7 and Exo 20:11 through the LXX:**
This is the decisive textual battleground. Run greek_parser.py --verse on Rev 14:7 to parse every word. Then retrieve the LXX text of Exo 20:11 and compare term by term. The web research has already identified four shared terms in sequence: poieo (made), ouranos (heaven), ge (earth), thalassa (sea). Both use the same verb root — Rev 14:7 has poiesanti (aorist active participle, dative) while Exo 20:11 LXX has epoiese (aorist active indicative). The critical question is the fourth element: Exo 20:11 concludes with 'panta ta en autois' (all that is in them) but Rev 14:7 substitutes 'pegas hydaton' (springs/fountains of waters). READ $WORD_STUDIES/G4160-poieo.md and $WORD_STUDIES/H6213-asah.md to understand the poieo/asah bridge — Exo 20:11 MT uses asah (H6213), which the LXX renders with poieo (G4160), and Rev 14:7 picks up the LXX verb, not the MT verb. Also READ $WORD_STUDIES/G4077-pege.md for the 'fountains' lexical data. Compare with Psa 146:6, Acts 4:24, Acts 14:15 — all use 'panta ta en autois.' Rev 14:7 alone departs from this pattern.

6. **Addressing the 'generic creation theology' objection:**
The objection deserves honest engagement. Genuinely generic creation texts exist — Acts 17:24 ('God that made the world and all things therein') uses poieo but in a different syntactic construction. Rev 4:11 ('thou hast created all things') uses ktizo, not poieo. Rom 1:20 ('from the creation of the world') uses ktisis. What makes Rev 14:7 specific rather than generic? Three cumulative arguments: (1) VERBAL — the four-term sequence (poieo + ouranos + ge + thalassa) matches Exo 20:11 LXX precisely, and the verb choice (poieo, not ktizo) aligns with the LXX Sabbath commandment; (2) THEMATIC — the passage combines creation worship with judgment and commandments, matching the Ecc 12:13-14 template; (3) STRUCTURAL — the three angels' messages are bookended by creation language (14:7) and commandments language (14:12), with the ark of the covenant (containing the Decalogue) revealed at 11:19. No single argument is conclusive; the cumulative case is. READ $WORD_STUDIES/G4352-proskyneo.md for worship vocabulary. The study should acknowledge the inferential step honestly while showing the cumulative evidence makes the inference strong.
**Verses:** Rev 14:7, Acts 17:24, Rev 4:11, Rom 1:20, Rev 14:12, Rev 11:19
**Strong's:** G4160, G2936, G4352, G1785
