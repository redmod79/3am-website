# Reference Gathering: "Give Glory to Him" (Rev 14:7b)

## Question
What does dote auto doxan ("give glory to him") mean as a gospel imperative? How is glory (doxa/kabod) given to God — through confession (Josh 7:19), character acknowledgment (Isa 42:12), conduct (Matt 5:16, 1 Pet 2:12), fruit-bearing (John 15:8), bodily stewardship (1 Cor 6:20, 10:31), beholding-transformation (2 Cor 3:18), or something else? What is the relationship between giving glory and the judgment announcement that immediately follows? How does Romans 1:21 ("they glorified him not as God") describe the exact failure the first angel addresses? Why does Rev 16:9 record that under the plagues they "repented not to give him glory" — a precise verbal echo of 14:7? How does John 17:4 ("I have glorified thee on the earth: I have finished the work") establish Christ as the model of giving glory?

## Web Research Findings

The web research agent (Phase 0) produced extensive findings documented in `00-web-research.md`. Key leads organized by research directive:

### Doxa/Kabod Lexical Bridge
- **Leona Glidden Running (Ministry, 1963):** Of 25 Hebrew/Aramaic words translated by doxa in the LXX, kabod is the critical bridge from classical to biblical meanings. The LXX translators infused doxa (classically "opinion," "reputation") with kabod's meanings: brightness, splendor, majesty, character. Doxa appears 167 times in NT. Running distinguishes objective manifestations (Shekinah-type visible glory), character expression (God's attributes in action), and reciprocal honor (human responsibility to honor God's character).
- **Kabod Etymology (multiple sources):** K-B-D root originally means "weight" or "heaviness." In Exodus 33:18-19, Moses asks to see God's glory; God responds by proclaiming His name and making "all my goodness pass before you" — equating glory with character/goodness.
- **Status:** Leads to verify against Scripture

### The "Give Glory" = Confession Idiom
- **Tactical Christianity:** Hebrew yada carries dual meaning: "to praise/give glory" AND "to confess an offence." Joshua 7:19 demonstrates: "give glory to the LORD... Tell me what you have done." Greek homologeo similarly functions both positively (confessing faith) and negatively (confessing faults). Confession and glory-giving are unified acts — acknowledging God is right.
- **Status:** Lead to verify against Scripture

### Commentators on Revelation 14:7
- **Barnes:** "To give glory to him is to acknowledge him as the only true God; to set up his pure worship in the heart; and to praise him."
- **Gill:** "Glory is to be given to him, on account of the perfections of his nature, and the works of his hands."
- **Expositor's Greek Testament:** "Since he who has created has the right to judge his creatures, as well as to receive their worship" — directly linking creation, judgment, and worship.
- **Status:** Leads to verify against Scripture

### Romans 1:18-25 as Inverse of Rev 14:7
- **Simon Turpin (AiG, 2018):** Paul employs Genesis 1-3 to expose humanity's threefold exchange: creation knowledge suppressed (Rom 1:18-21), idolatry replacing worship (Rom 1:22-25), sexual disorder following (Rom 1:26-28). Paul intentionally echoes Genesis 1:26-27 LXX language in Rom 1:23, 26-27.
- **Status:** Lead to verify against Scripture

### John 17:4 — Christ as Model of Glorification
- **BibleHub commentators:** Bengel: "I have consummated [finished] — Hereby is explained the expression, I have glorified Thee." Work-completion IS glorification. Meyer: the aorist edoxasa treats the entire redemptive mission as one accomplished act. Cambridge Bible: "This is the way in which God is glorified, the completion of the work of revelation."
- **Status:** Lead to verify against Scripture

### 2 Corinthians 3:18 — Beholding-Transformation
- **BibleHub commentators:** Katoptrizomai debate: "beholding as in a mirror" (Meyer, most moderns) vs. "reflecting as a mirror" (Chrysostom). Metamorphoo — same verb as Christ's transfiguration. "From glory to glory" = progressive transformation. Gill: the Spirit removes the veil so we can behold glory.
- **Status:** Lead to verify against Scripture

### 1 Corinthians 6:20 — Bodily Glorification
- **Precept Austin:** Doxazo aorist imperative (doxasate) — commanding immediate, urgent action. The agorazo purchase-price argument: slave redemption grounds the obligation to glorify God bodily. Connections to 1 Cor 10:31 (universal scope), Romans 12:1 (living sacrifice).
- **Status:** Lead to verify against Scripture
