3. **Daniel 7:22 — judgment given TO/FOR the saints:**
Run hebrew_parser.py --verse on Dan 7:22 to parse the Aramaic: dina yehib leqaddishe elyonin. The preposition le (ל) can mean 'to,' 'for,' 'in favor of,' or 'into the possession of.' Multiple scholarly sources confirm the primary meaning is 'in favor of' — a verdict rendered FOR the saints' benefit. Pfandl (BRI) argues the judgment's primary function is 'vindication and confirmation of salvation.' Compare with Dan 7:27 where the kingdom is given TO the people of the saints — same le preposition, clearly benefactive. Also run hebrew_parser.py on Dan 7:26-27 to see the parallel structure: the court sits (dina yittib), dominion is removed from the horn, and given to the saints. The three Dan 7 judgment references (vv.10, 22, 26) form a narrative arc: investigation (v.10), favorable verdict (v.22), execution/transfer (vv.26-27). READ $WORD_STUDIES/A3488-ythib.md for the Aramaic 'sat' verb. This three-phase structure is critical for the study's argument that judgment has distinct phases, with the pre-advent phase being investigative/vindicatory.

4. **The prophetic aorist of elthen — accomplished fact or future certainty?:**
READ $GRAMMAR_REF/greek/tense-aorist-prophetic.md — this canonical entry covers prophetic aorist in four categories: apocalyptic proclamation (Rev 14:8, 18:2 epesen doubled), proleptic doxology (Rev 19:6, 11:17), soteriological prolepsis (Rom 8:30), and Enochic tradition (Jude 14 elthen). READ $WORD_STUDIES/G2064-erchomai.md for the full lexical profile of elthen's root verb. Run greek_parser.py --verse on Rev 14:7 to confirm V-2AAI-3S morphology. The Precept Austin commentary identifies elthen as a 'prophetic aorist' — presenting the judgment as certain and imminent, 'as if it is already underway.'
