2. **The pistis Iesou genitive: subjective, objective, or both?:**
READ `$GRAMMAR_REF/greek/subjective-objective-genitive.md` for the canonical entry on genitive types — this should provide the framework for evaluating the construction. READ `$WORD_STUDIES/G4102-pistis.md` for the full pistis word study including its genitive constructions. Run greek_parser.py --verse on Rev 14:12 to confirm the genitive case of Iesou. 3. **The Abrahamic pattern: faith first, obedience after:**
The Abrahamic faith-then-obedience pattern is the key historical-grammatical evidence that commandment-keeping and faith are sequential, not competing. Gen 15:6 records Abraham's faith counted as righteousness. Gen 26:5 records God's assessment after Abraham's death: 'obeyed my voice, and kept my charge (mishmereth), my commandments (mitsvot), my statutes (chuqqot), and my laws (torot).' READ `$WORD_STUDIES/H4687-mitsvah.md`, `$WORD_STUDIES/H2706-choq.md`, `$WORD_STUDIES/H8451-towrah.md`, and `$WORD_STUDIES/H8104-shamar.md` for the Hebrew vocabulary. Paul cites Gen 15:6 in Rom 4:3 and notes the temporal ordering in Rom 4:10-11 — faith was counted BEFORE circumcision. James 2:22-23 shows faith 'working with' works and 'completed by' works, then cites Gen 15:6. Howard (2025) observes this pattern 'carries through the Old Testament' and compares faith to an apple tree that produces apples by its very nature. Run cross_testament_parallels_v2.py on Gen 15:6 for both OT and NT parallels.

8. **Paul's me genoito denials: the wall against antinomianism:**

The pattern shows that the apostle to the Gentiles — the champion of justification by faith — was simultaneously the most emphatic defender of the law's continuing validity. This is the same dual affirmation Rev 14:12 makes: commandments AND faith, not commandments OR faith.
**Verses:** Rom 3:31, Rom 6:1-2, Rom 6:15, Gal 2:17, Gal 3:21
**Strong's:** G3551, G4102, G5485
