    Word Studies¶

    Question¶

    What does hode he hupomone ton hagion estin ('here is the patience of the saints') in Rev 14:12 mean? How does hupomone (G5281, patient endurance/constancy) function as the summary response to the three messages? What is the relationship between hupomone in 14:12 and the hupomone inclusio that opens at 13:10 ('here is the patience and the faith of the saints')? How does the Romans 5:3-5 chain (tribulation -> patience -> experience -> hope) explain how endurance is produced? How does Hebrews 10:36 connect? How does James 5:7-11 use Job and the prophets as endurance models? What does Revelation 12:11 describe about the mechanism of endurance? Is this passive resignation or active, hopeful perseverance?

    G5281 — hupomone¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G5281-hupomone.md

    Occurrences: 32

    Feminine noun from G5278 (hupomeno: to stay under, remain, endure). Cheerful or hopeful endurance, constancy. The word pictures bearing a weight from below — active, steadfast endurance rather than passive resignation. Etymology: hupo (under) + meno (remain). Five semantic categories: (1) endurance through tribulation, (2) patient persistence toward God's will, (3) eschatological waiting, (4) virtue in character lists, (5) communal/apostolic testimony. 7 occurrences in Revelation (highest per-100-verses concentration at 1.7). Key collocations: pistis (faith, 9 co-occ), thlipsis (tribulation, 4), elpis (hope, 3), katergazomai (produces, 3). Hebrew source: miqveh (H4723, hope/expectation) with PMI 9.98 — the highest correlation, confirming endurance's hope-shaped character.

    Semantic categories: endurance through tribulation, patient persistence toward God's will, eschatological waiting, virtue in character lists, communal/apostolic testimony

    Key verses: Rev 14:12, Rev 13:10, Rev 1:9, Rom 5:3-4, Heb 10:36, Heb 12:1, Jas 5:11

    Relevance: Central term of the study. Rev 14:12 declares 'here is the hupomone of the saints' as the summary response to the three angels' messages. The word's five semantic categories all converge in the eschatological patience declaration.

    G5278 — hupomeno¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G5278-hupŏmĕnō.md

    Occurrences: 18

    Verb from hupo (under) + meno (remain). To stay under (behind), remain; figuratively to undergo, bear trials, have fortitude, persevere. Four semantic categories: (1) steadfast endurance under trial (dominant), (2) patient suffering of wrong, (3) literal remaining behind/staying (Luke 2:43, Acts 17:14), (4) Christ's exemplary endurance (Heb 12:2). Key collocations: telos (end, 3x — 'endure to the end'), sozo (saved, 3x — endurance linked to salvation), stauros (cross, 1x — Christ endured the cross).

    Semantic categories: steadfast endurance under trial, patient suffering of wrong, literal remaining behind, Christ's exemplary endurance

    Key verses: Matt 10:22, Matt 24:13, Heb 12:2, Jas 1:12, 2 Tim 2:12, 1 Pet 2:20

    Relevance: The verbal form of hupomone. Key for verifying the 'remaining' meaning (Bohr's claim) — Luke 2:43 and Acts 17:14 confirm a literal 'remaining/staying behind' sense. The dominant figurative usage is steadfast endurance under trial, with endurance linked to salvation (Matt 10:22, 24:13).

    G3528 — nikao¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G3528-nikao.md

    Occurrences: 28

    Verb: to subdue, conquer, overcome, prevail. From nike (victory). 17 of 28 NT uses in Revelation (61%). Five semantic categories: (1) eschatological victory (believers overcoming), (2) cosmic victory (Christ overcoming), (3) moral victory (overcoming evil), (4) military/hostile conquest, (5) legal/forensic vindication. Overwhelmingly Johannine: 86% of all uses in John + 1 John + Revelation. The nikao-hupomone relationship is central: endurance is the sustained posture, overcoming is the achieved outcome.

    Semantic categories: eschatological victory, cosmic victory, moral victory, military conquest, legal vindication

    Key verses: Rev 2:7, Rev 3:21, Rev 12:11, Rev 15:2, Rev 21:7, John 16:33, 1 John 5:4

    Relevance: Complementary vocabulary to hupomone. Rev 13:10 has hupomone + pistis; Rev 14:12 has hupomone + entolas + pistis; Rev 15:2 has nikao over the beast. The progression is endurance → overcoming → victory.

    G4102 — pistis¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G4102-pistis.md

    Occurrences: 244

    Feminine noun: persuasion, credence, moral conviction, reliance upon Christ for salvation, constancy, the system of religious truth. Seven semantic categories: (1) active trust in God, (2) justifying faith, (3) the body of belief ('the faith'), (4) faithfulness/fidelity, (5) exemplary faith (Heb 11), (6) assurance/proof, (7) spiritual gift. Paired with hupomone in Rev 13:10, 14:12, and throughout endurance theology.

    Semantic categories: active trust, justifying faith, body of belief, faithfulness, exemplary faith, assurance, spiritual gift

    Key verses: Rev 14:12, Rev 13:10, Rom 5:1, Heb 10:38, Heb 11:1, Gal 2:16

    Relevance: Paired with hupomone in the key inclusio texts (Rev 13:10 'patience and faith'; Rev 14:12 'faith of Jesus'). The 'faith of Jesus' (pistin Iesou) in 14:12 is either Jesus's own faithfulness (subjective genitive) or faith directed toward Jesus (objective genitive). Also 'the victory that overcometh the world, even our faith' (1 John 5:4).

    G2347 — thlipsis¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G2347-thlipsis.md

    Occurrences: 45

    Feminine noun from thlibo (to press, squeeze). Pressure, affliction, tribulation, trouble. Core image is pressing or squeezing — external force bearing down. Five semantic categories: (1) eschatological tribulation, (2) persecution from faith, (3) apostolic hardship, (4) tribulation as spiritual catalyst, (5) divine retributive tribulation. 4 co-occurrences with hupomone: tribulation as both test and occasion for patience.

    Semantic categories: eschatological tribulation, faith persecution, apostolic hardship, spiritual catalyst, divine retribution

    Key verses: Rom 5:3, Rev 1:9, John 16:33, Matt 24:21, 2 Thess 1:4

    Relevance: First link in the Rom 5:3-5 chain: tribulation PRODUCES patience. The context that occasions hupomone — without thlipsis, hupomone has no reference. Rev 1:9 binds thlipsis-basileia-hupomone as an inseparable triad.

    G1680 — elpis¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G1680-elpis.md

    Occurrences: 54

    Feminine noun: expectation, hope, confidence. Seven semantic categories: (1) eschatological hope, (2) theological virtue (faith-hope-love triad), (3) resurrection hope, (4) present confidence/anchor, (5) Christ as content of hope, (6) hopelessness, (7) secular hope. Absent from Revelation entirely (noun form). Primary LXX bridge: H8615 tiqvah (20 co-occ, PMI 23.08).

    Semantic categories: eschatological hope, theological virtue, resurrection hope, present confidence, Christ as hope, hopelessness, secular hope

    Key verses: Rom 5:4-5, 1 Thess 1:3, Tit 2:13, Heb 6:18, 1 Cor 13:13

    Relevance: Final link in the Rom 5:3-5 chain: proven character produces HOPE. Hope is the forward anchor that sustains endurance. The absence of elpis from Revelation is significant — Revelation uses hupomone (7x) rather than elpis, suggesting that in apocalyptic literature, hope is expressed through endurance rather than as an abstract concept.

    G1382 — dokime¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G1382-dokimḗ.md

    Occurrences: 7

    Noun: test, trustiness, proven character, experience, proof. Three semantic categories: (1) proven character through endurance (Rom 5:4), (2) demonstrated proof of genuineness (2 Cor 2:9, 9:13, 13:3), (3) trial as testing experience (2 Cor 8:2). Key position in the Rom 5:3-5 chain: patience produces dokime (proven character), and dokime produces hope. Related to dokimazo (G1381, to test/examine) and dokimion (G1383, the testing instrument).

    Semantic categories: proven character through endurance, demonstrated proof of genuineness, trial as testing experience

    Key verses: Rom 5:4, 2 Cor 2:9, 2 Cor 8:2, 2 Cor 9:13, 2 Cor 13:3, Phil 2:22

    Relevance: The middle link in the Rom 5:3-5 chain that has often been overlooked. Patience does not produce hope directly — it produces proven character (dokime) first, and proven character produces hope. Dokime is the evidence that one has been tested and passed — the quality that gives hope its substance.

    G3115 — makrothumia¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G3115-makrothumia.md

    Occurrences: 14

    Feminine noun from makros (long) + thumos (passion/wrath). Literally 'long-passion' or 'long-tempered' — slow to anger, forbearing. Three semantic categories: (1) divine attribute — God's restraint toward sinners (Rom 2:4, 2 Pet 3:15), (2) Christian virtue — patience with people (Gal 5:22, Col 1:11), (3) apostolic character (2 Tim 3:10). This is the fruit of the Spirit in Gal 5:22, NOT hupomone. The distinction: makrothumia = patience with persons; hupomone = patience with circumstances.

    Semantic categories: divine restraint, patience with persons, apostolic character, exemplary endurance

    Key verses: Gal 5:22, Col 1:11, Jas 5:7, Jas 5:10, 2 Tim 3:10, Rom 2:4

    Relevance: The distinguishing term that clarifies what hupomone IS by showing what it is NOT. Jas 5:7-10 uses makrothumia/makrothumeo for the farmer and prophets, then switches to hupomone/hupomeno for Job (5:11). Gal 5:22 lists makrothumia (not hupomone) as a fruit of the Spirit. Col 1:11 pairs both terms as complementary.

    G5602 — hode¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G5602-hōde.md

    Occurrences: 60

    Adverb: in this same spot, here, hither. Five semantic categories: (1) spatial location — present position (most common, Synoptics), (2) directional — summons to come ('come hither'), (3) apocalyptic pointer — theological declaration (Rev 13:10, 13:18, 14:12, 17:9), (4) false presence — warning against deception (Matt 24:23), (5) insufficiency/availability of resources. Key collocations: hupomone (3x — Rev 13:10, 14:12), sophia (2x — Rev 13:18, 17:9).

    Semantic categories: spatial location, directional summons, apocalyptic theological pointer, false presence warning, resource availability

    Key verses: Rev 13:10, Rev 13:18, Rev 14:12, Rev 17:9

    Relevance: The structural formula word. The four hode declarations in Revelation form two parallel pairs: endurance pair (13:10, 14:12) calling for hupomone, and wisdom pair (13:18, 17:9) calling for sophia/nous. The 13:10/14:12 pair creates an inclusio bracketing the third angel's message.

    G5083 — tereo¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G5083-tereo.md

    Occurrences: 75

    Verb: to keep, guard, observe, watch over. In Rev 14:12, the present active participle terountes characterizes the saints by their ONGOING keeping of the commandments. Also in Rev 3:10 (eteresas — 'thou hast kept the word of my patience') and Rev 12:17 (ton terounton — 'which keep the commandments'). The verb implies active guarding and preservation, not mere observance.

    Semantic categories: commandment observance, guarding/preserving, watching over

    Key verses: Rev 14:12, Rev 3:10, Rev 12:17, 1 John 5:3

    Relevance: The verb connecting patience to commandment-keeping in Rev 14:12. The present active participle terountes describes the saints' identity: they are characterized by ongoing, habitual keeping of God's commandments. This is descriptive (what they do) not prescriptive (what they must do to earn patience).

    G1785 — entole¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G1785-entole.md

    Occurrences: 71

    Feminine noun: commandment, order, injunction. In Rev 14:12, 'the commandments of God' (tas entolas tou theou) identifies what the patient saints keep. The plural 'commandments' (entolas) points to a body of divine commands, connecting to the Decalogue and the broader biblical law tradition.

    Semantic categories: divine commandment, moral law, authoritative instruction

    Key verses: Rev 14:12, Rev 12:17, 1 John 5:2-3, John 14:15

    Relevance: The commandment-keeping that characterizes the patient saints in Rev 14:12. The pairing 'commandments of God AND faith of Jesus' establishes a dual identity: obedience to divine law and trust in/faithfulness of Jesus Christ.

    G2716 — katergazomai¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G2716-katergazomai.md

    Occurrences: 26

    Verb: to work out, accomplish, produce, bring about. The engine of the endurance production chain in Rom 5:3: 'tribulation katergazetai (works out/produces) patience.' Also in Jas 1:3: 'the trying of your faith katergazetai patience.' Present middle/passive indicative — the production is ongoing. The kata- prefix intensifies: this is thorough, complete working-out.

    Semantic categories: producing through effort, accomplishing completely, working out thoroughly

    Key verses: Rom 5:3, Jas 1:3, Rom 7:8, 2 Cor 4:17

    Relevance: The verb that explains HOW endurance is produced. Tribulation does not merely accompany patience — it actively produces it through the katergazomai process. This is the mechanical link in the chain.

    G3141 — martyria¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G3141-martyria.md

    Occurrences: 37

    Feminine noun: testimony, witness, evidence, record. From martys (witness). 79% of occurrences in Johannine literature (John + 1 John + 3 John + Revelation). Five semantic categories: (1) legal/judicial testimony, (2) Johannine self-testimony debate, (3) apostolic/divine witness, (4) testimony of Jesus Christ in Revelation, (5) character reference/community report. In Revelation, 'the testimony of Jesus' (he martyria Iesou) becomes a quasi-technical phrase marking the faithful.

    Semantic categories: legal testimony, self-testimony, apostolic witness, testimony of Jesus in Revelation, character reference

    Key verses: Rev 12:11, Rev 1:2, Rev 6:9, Rev 12:17, Rev 19:10

    Relevance: The 'word of their testimony' (dia ton logon tes martyrias auton) in Rev 12:11 is the second means of overcoming. This connects to Rev 14:12's 'faith of Jesus' — testimony/witness as the active expression of faith. Rev 19:10 identifies 'the testimony of Jesus' as 'the spirit of prophecy.'

    G3144 — martys¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G3144-martys.md

    Occurrences: 34

    Masculine noun: witness, one who testifies, (later) martyr. The root of martyria. In Revelation, the witness-bearing that leads to death (Antipas in Rev 2:13 is called 'my faithful martyr/witness'). The semantic development from 'witness' to 'martyr' reflects the experience of the early church where testimony often led to death.

    Semantic categories: eyewitness, faithful witness, martyr

    Key verses: Rev 2:13, Acts 1:8, Heb 12:1

    Relevance: The person behind the testimony. The 'cloud of witnesses' (Heb 12:1) surrounds the race of patience. Antipas as 'faithful martyr' (Rev 2:13) exemplifies the connection between testimony and death that Rev 12:11 articulates.

    G5590 — psyche¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G5590-psyche.md

    Occurrences: 104

    Feminine noun: breath, life, soul, self. In Rev 12:11: 'they loved not their psyche unto death' — they did not cling to their own lives when death was the cost of faithfulness. In Luke 21:19: 'in your patience possess ye your psychas' — patience acquires/gains souls.

    Semantic categories: physical life, inner self/soul, person

    Key verses: Rev 12:11, Luke 21:19, Matt 16:25-26

    Relevance: The 'lives/souls' at stake in endurance. Rev 12:11's 'loved not their psyche unto death' is the third means of overcoming — willingness to die. Luke 21:19's 'in patience possess your psychas' makes patience the means of spiritual self-possession.

    G129 — haima¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G129-haima.md

    Occurrences: 99

    Neuter noun: blood. In Rev 12:11: 'by the blood of the Lamb' (dia to haima tou arniou) — the primary, objective basis of victory. Listed FIRST among the three means of overcoming.

    Semantic categories: sacrificial blood, blood of Christ, life force

    Key verses: Rev 12:11, Heb 9:12, 1 Pet 1:19, Eph 1:7

    Relevance: The foundation of the overcoming mechanism. Blood of the Lamb is the objective basis — believers' testimony and willingness to die are responses to what Christ has already accomplished through his blood.

    G5245 — hupernikao¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G5245-hupernikao.md

    Occurrences: 1

    Verb: to more than conquer, gain decisive surpassing victory. Hapax legomenon (Rom 8:37 only). Compound of huper (beyond) + nikao (conquer). Not merely surviving but overwhelmingly conquering WITH SURPLUS. The prefix huper- transforms ordinary victory into something exceeding all proportion. Victory comes 'through him that loved us' (dia tou agapesantos hemas) — grounded in Christ's love, not human effort.

    Semantic categories: superabundant victory through Christ's love

    Key verses: Rom 8:37

    Relevance: The intensified form of nikao that Paul uses at the climax of Romans 8. Where nikao asks 'did you win?', hupernikao says 'the opposition never stood a chance.' The victory is IN the midst of suffering (v.35-36), not after it.

    G3529 — nike¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G3529-nike.md

    Occurrences: 1

    Feminine noun: victory. Hapax legomenon (1 John 5:4 only). 'This is the victory (nike) that overcometh the world, even our faith.' The abstract noun appears only once, identifying faith as the victory itself, not merely a means to victory.

    Semantic categories: victory, conquest

    Key verses: 1 John 5:4

    Relevance: Faith IS the victory — not an instrument of victory but the victory itself. This closes the circle: faith enables endurance (hupomone), endurance achieves overcoming (nikao), and the victory (nike) is faith itself.

    G3056 — logos¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G3056-logos.md

    Occurrences: 329

    Masculine noun: word, speech, statement, account, reason. In Rev 12:11: 'the word (logon) of their testimony' — the content of the believers' confession/witness. In Rev 1:9 and 3:10: 'the word of God' and 'the word of my patience.'

    Semantic categories: spoken word, divine word, account/reason

    Key verses: Rev 12:11, Rev 1:9, Rev 3:10, John 1:1

    Relevance: The 'word of their testimony' in Rev 12:11 — what overcomers actually SAY/confess. Their testimony (martyria) has content (logos). This connects verbal confession to endurance: patience is not silent suffering but vocal witness.

    G4352 — proskyneo¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G4352-proskyneo.md

    Occurrences: 65

    Verb: to worship, prostrate oneself, do reverence. The worship at stake in Rev 14:9-12 — the beast demands proskyneo (worship) and the saints refuse. Their refusal IS the patience declared in 14:12.

    Semantic categories: worship, reverence, prostration

    Key verses: Rev 14:9, Rev 14:11, Rev 13:4, Rev 13:12

    Relevance: The worship binary that defines the crisis: proskyneo the beast OR endure (hupomone). The 3am-14 study traced the four-stage proskyneo escalation through Rev 13. Rev 14:12's patience is the CHARACTER of those who refuse proskyneo to the beast.

    G3986 — peirasmos¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G3986-peirasmos.md

    Occurrences: 21

    Masculine noun: trial, temptation, testing. In Rev 3:10: 'the hour of temptation/testing' (tes horas tou peirasmou) from which Philadelphia is kept. In Jas 1:2,12: the testing that worketh patience. The word covers both malicious temptation and divinely permitted testing.

    Semantic categories: trial, temptation, testing

    Key verses: Rev 3:10, Jas 1:2, Jas 1:12, 1 Pet 1:6

    Relevance: The testing context in which endurance operates. Rev 3:10's 'hour of temptation which shall come upon all the world' is the eschatological trial that requires hupomone.

    G1342 — dikaios¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G1342-dikaios.md

    Occurrences: 81

    Adjective: just, righteous, upright. In Heb 10:38: 'the just shall live by faith' — quoted from Hab 2:4. The one who lives by faith is the one who does not draw back — the patient endurer.

    Semantic categories: just, righteous, upright

    Key verses: Heb 10:38, Hab 2:4, Rom 1:17

    Relevance: The character of the endurer: righteous, living by faith. Heb 10:38's 'the just shall live by faith' immediately follows 10:36's call for patience, linking righteousness-faith-patience as an inseparable triad.

    G40 — hagios¶

    Occurrences: 229

    Adjective used as noun: holy, sacred, saint. In Rev 14:12 and 13:10: 'the patience of the SAINTS (ton hagion)' — the 'holy ones' whose patience is declared. The genitive ton hagion could be subjective (patience the saints possess) or possessive (patience belonging to the saints as a community mark).

    Semantic categories: holy ones, saints, set apart

    Key verses: Rev 14:12, Rev 13:10, Dan 7:18, Dan 7:27

    Relevance: Identifies WHO has the patience: the hagioi, the holy ones, the saints. The term connects to Dan 7:18,27 where 'the saints of the most High' receive the kingdom — linking Revelation's saints to Daniel's eschatological people.
