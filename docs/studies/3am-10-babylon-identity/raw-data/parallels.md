# Cross-Testament Parallels

- Rev 17:1 → Rev 21:9 (nt, structural bracket — identical angel-introduction formula (0.763 hybrid score, highest in dataset). Same angel, same invitation words, opposite content (harlot judgment vs. bride display).)
- Rev 17:3 → Rev 21:10 (nt, structural bracket — apophero (G667) + en Pneumati (G4151) occurs ONLY at these two verses in the NT. Contrasting destinations: wilderness (eremos) vs. great high mountain (oros mega).)
- Rev 14:8 → Isa 21:9 (ot, vocabulary + structural — doubled 'is fallen, is fallen' formula. Hebrew nephla nephla → Greek epesen epesen. Direct OT source for the Babylon-fall announcement.)
- Rev 14:8 → Jer 51:7-8 (ot, vocabulary + thematic — golden cup, wine, nations drunk, Babylon fallen. The primary OT template for Rev 14:8's composite imagery.)
- Rev 17:4 → Exod 28:5-6 (ot, vocabulary — priestly garment vocabulary: gold, purple, scarlet, precious stones in both. Harlot matches priestly garments MINUS blue (Beale's claim verified).)
- Rev 18:4 → Jer 51:6 (ot, vocabulary + thematic — 'Come out of her, my people' (Rev 18:4) echoes 'Flee out of the midst of Babylon, and deliver every man his soul' (Jer 51:6). Direct come-out command.)
- Rev 17:5 → 2 Thess 2:7 (nt, vocabulary — MYSTERION in both. The 'mystery of lawlessness already at work' (2 Thess 2:7) is the same concealed corruption labeled MYSTERY BABYLON (Rev 17:5).)
- Rev 17:16 → Ezek 16:37-41 (ot, structural + vocabulary — stripped naked, burned with fire, destroyed by former allies. Ezek 16's judgment on harlot Jerusalem is the direct template for Rev 17:16's judgment on the harlot Babylon.)
- Rev 17:5 → Exod 28:36 (ot, structural contrast — forehead inscription. High priest: 'HOLINESS TO THE LORD' on forehead (Exod 28:36). Harlot: 'MYSTERY BABYLON THE GREAT, MOTHER OF HARLOTS' on forehead (Rev 17:5). Deliberate priestly parody.)
- Rev 18:21 → Jer 51:63-64 (ot, vocabulary + action — millstone cast into water, Babylon never to rise. Jeremiah's symbolic act (binding a stone to the scroll and casting into Euphrates) = Rev 18:21 (mighty angel casts millstone into sea).)
- Jer 51:7 → Rev 17:4 (nt, vocabulary — golden cup (kos-zahab / poterion chrysoun), nations drunk, Babylon as cup-bearer. The most direct OT→NT vocabulary transfer in the Babylon imagery.)
- Rev 19:10 → Rev 22:8-9 (nt, structural bracket — angel-worship refusal frames both harlot section (17:1-19:10) and bride section (21:9-22:9). Confirms structural parallelism.)
