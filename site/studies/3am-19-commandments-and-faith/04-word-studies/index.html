    Word Studies¶

    Question¶

    What does Revelation 14:12's description of the saints — 'they that keep the commandments of God, and the faith of Jesus' — mean? Which commandments does tas entolas tou theou refer to? Is pistis Iesou subjective or objective genitive? How does the Abrahamic pattern work (faith first, obedience after)? How does Paul's 'obedience of faith' connect? Address the legalism objection: Rev 14:12 DESCRIBES the saints (present active participle terountes), not PRESCRIBES conditions.

    G1785 — entole¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G1785-entole.md

    Occurrences: 71

    Commandment, precept. 65 unique verses (71 tokens). Rendered 'commandment(s)' in 93.8% of verses. Johannine concentration is the defining feature (34% of uses). Key semantic categories: (1) Decalogue/Mosaic Law, (2) Christ's new commandment of love, (3) Father's commandment to Christ, (4) Keeping commandments as knowing/loving God, (5) Commandment/sin/death (Pauline), (6) Human traditions vs. God's commandment, (7) Priestly/Levitical commandment. Collocates strongly with theos (14x, 'commandments of God') and tereo (14x, 'keep commandments'). NT vocabulary partition: entole = moral commands, dogma = abolished, entalma = human precepts.

    Semantic categories: Decalogue/Mosaic Law, Christ's new commandment, Father's commandment to Christ, keeping commandments as knowing God, sin exploiting the commandment, human traditions vs. God's commandment, priestly/Levitical commandment

    Key verses: Rev 14:12, Rev 12:17, 1 John 5:3, Rom 7:8-12, Eph 6:2, Luke 23:56, 1 Cor 7:19, John 14:15

    Relevance: Core word in Rev 14:12. The entole = Decalogue pattern (43/43 unqualified instances) and the three decisive test cases (Rom 7:7-12, Eph 6:2, Luke 23:56) establish that tas entolas tou theou = the Decalogue.

    G4102 — pistis¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G4102-pistis.md

    Occurrences: 244

    Faith, faithfulness, belief. 244 occurrences across 222 KJV translation instances. Overwhelmingly Pauline — Romans, Galatians, and Pastorals dominate. Semantic categories: (1) active trust/confidence in God, (2) justifying faith (means of righteousness), (3) the body of belief ('the faith'), (4) faithfulness/fidelity, (5) exemplary faith (Hebrews 11), (6) assurance/proof, (7) spiritual gift. Collocates strongly with theos (52x), Iesous (40x), Christos (36x), agape (24x). The pistis Christou genitive construction is the most debated subjective/objective genitive in NT scholarship.

    Semantic categories: active trust in God, justifying faith, body of belief, faithfulness/fidelity, exemplary faith, assurance, spiritual gift

    Key verses: Rev 14:12, Rom 3:22, Gal 2:16, Eph 2:8, Heb 11:1, James 2:22

    Relevance: Core word in Rev 14:12 — 'the faith of Jesus.' The genitive construction (pistis + personal name in genitive) is structurally ambiguous between subjective (Jesus' faithfulness) and objective (faith in Jesus). The grammar reference entry on subjective/objective genitive provides the analytical framework.

    G5083 — tereo¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G5083-tereo.md

    Occurrences: 75

    Keep, guard, observe. 75 occurrences. Dominant usage: faithful, active compliance with a divine charge — keeping commandments, words, and law. Strong Johannine concentration (John 18, Revelation 11, 1 John 8). Collocates with entole (14x, 'keep commandments') and logos (15x, 'keep my word'). Distinct from phylasso (preventing escape, defensive containment) — tereo = watchful, attentive, active surveillance and fidelity. Hebrew sources: natsar H5341 (8x, PMI 7.27) and shamar H8104 (10x, PMI 4.78).

    Semantic categories: keeping commandments/words, physical custody, reserving for judgment, protective keeping, maintaining a condition

    Key verses: Rev 14:12, Rev 12:17, John 14:15, 1 John 2:3, 1 John 5:3

    Relevance: Core verb in Rev 14:12 — 'they that KEEP (terountes) the commandments.' The present active participle form (terountes) characterizes the saints by ongoing habitual activity — descriptive, not prescriptive.

    G3551 — nomos¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G3551-nomos.md

    Occurrences: 197

    Law. 197 occurrences. Heavily concentrated in Paul (Romans 74, Galatians 32). ABSENT from Revelation entirely. Semantic categories: (1) Mosaic Law/Torah, (2) OT Scriptures broadly, (3) principle/operative rule, (4) law of Christ/royal law/law of liberty, (5) natural/moral law, (6) law of commandments/ordinances. The absence of nomos from Revelation is significant: John uses entole (G1785) exclusively for the commandment vocabulary in Revelation. The Tinker/Answering Adventism claim that entole ≠ Decalogue because nomos = the Law fails when tested against Paul's usage (Rom 7:7-12, Eph 6:2).

    Semantic categories: Mosaic Law/Torah, OT Scriptures broadly, principle/operative rule, law of Christ, natural/moral law, ceremonial ordinances

    Key verses: Rom 3:31, Rom 7:7-12, Rom 8:4, Rom 13:10, Gal 3:21

    Relevance: The nomos/entole distinction is central to the study. Nomos is ABSENT from Revelation; John uses entole exclusively. The critic argument that entole ≠ Decalogue because nomos = the Law is refuted by Paul's own usage where he calls Decalogue commands entole.

    G5218 — hupakoe¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G5218-hupakoe.md

    Occurrences: 15

    Obedience, attentive hearkening. 14 unique verses (15 tokens). Etymologically hupo + akouo = hearing as a subordinate. Concentrated in Paul (Romans 6, 2 Corinthians 3, Philemon 1). Semantic categories: (1) obedience to gospel/faith, (2) Christ's obedience as redemptive counterpart, (3) obedience vs. disobedience, (4) practical submission, (5) sanctification through obedience to truth. The hupakoen pisteos construction (Rom 1:5, 16:26) frames all of Romans — a deliberate Pauline coinage expressing both 'the obedience which IS faith' and 'the obedience which springs FROM faith.'

    Semantic categories: obedience to gospel/faith, Christ's redemptive obedience, obedience vs. disobedience, practical submission, sanctification through obedience

    Key verses: Rom 1:5, Rom 16:26, Rom 5:19, Rom 6:16, Heb 5:8

    Relevance: The hupakoe pisteos inclusio (Rom 1:5/16:26) is the conceptual bridge between Rev 14:12's twin marks. Garlington argues this is unique to pre-Christian Greek literature — a deliberate Pauline coinage showing faith and obedience are inseparable.

    G26 — agape¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G26-agape.md

    Occurrences: 116

    Love. 116 tokens in 106 unique verses across 24 books. Overwhelmingly Pauline and Johannine. Semantic categories: (1) God's love toward humanity, (2) God's essential nature as love, (3) Christ's love, (4) mutual love among believers, (5) supreme virtue/greatest gift, (6) love as obedience/walking in commandments, (7) love in virtue lists. Critical for the faith-love-law chain: Gal 5:6 (faith works by love) → 1 John 5:3 (love = keeping commandments) → Rom 13:10 (love fulfills the law).

    Semantic categories: God's love toward humanity, God as love, Christ's love, mutual love, supreme virtue, love as obedience, love in virtue lists

    Key verses: 1 John 5:3, 1 John 4:8, Rom 13:10, Gal 5:6, John 14:15

    Relevance: Bridge between faith and commandments in the faith-love-law chain. Agape is the medium through which faith operates (Gal 5:6) and the content of commandment-keeping (1 John 5:3).

    G5485 — charis¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G5485-charis.md

    Occurrences: 156

    Grace, favor, thanks. 147 unique verses (156 tokens). Dominantly Pauline (~80 of 147 verses). Semantic categories: (1) epistolary greeting, (2) divine saving grace/unmerited favor, (3) ministry grace for service, (4) social favor, (5) gratitude/thanks, (6) epistolary benediction. Key for legalism objection: Titus 2:11-12 shows grace as PEDAGOGICAL — grace teaches denial of ungodliness and godly living. Grace produces, not prevents, obedience.

    Semantic categories: epistolary greeting, divine saving grace, ministry grace, social favor, gratitude, epistolary benediction

    Key verses: Eph 2:8, Titus 2:11-12, Rom 3:24, Rom 6:15

    Relevance: Addresses the legalism charge. Grace is not opposed to obedience — it produces it. Titus 2:11-12 (grace teaches godly living) and Rom 6:15 (grace does not permit sin) together answer the claim that commandment-keeping = legalism.

    G4100 — pisteuo¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G4100-pisteuo.md

    Occurrences: 264

    Believe, trust, entrust. 220 unique verses (264 tokens). John dominates with 86/220 verses (39%). Present active participle (ho pisteuon, 'the one believing') accounts for 22% of forms — ongoing faith. Semantic categories: (1) saving faith in Christ, (2) belief in God's word/promises, (3) intellectual acknowledgment, (4) ongoing trust and reliance, (5) refusal to believe, (6) entrusting. Antonym: apeitheo G544 (disbelieve/disobey) — John 3:36 sets them as direct antonyms.

    Semantic categories: saving faith in Christ, belief in God's word, intellectual acknowledgment, ongoing trust, refusal to believe, entrusting

    Key verses: John 3:16, John 3:36, Rom 4:3, Acts 16:31, Eph 2:8

    Relevance: Verb form of pistis. The pisteuo/apeitheo antonym pair in John 3:36 shows the biblical languages do not separate belief from obedience.

    G2041 — ergon¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G2041-ergon.md

    Occurrences: 176

    Work, deed, action. 161 unique verses (176 tokens). Heaviest in Pastoral Epistles, James, and Revelation. Two critical vocabulary categories: (1) erga nomou ('works of the law') — works excluded as CAUSE of justification (Rom 3:20,28; Gal 2:16; 3:2,5,10); (2) erga agatha ('good works') — works affirmed as PURPOSE/FRUIT (Eph 2:10; Titus 2:14; 3:1,8). The distinction is visible only in Greek — English 'works' collapses both categories.

    Semantic categories: works of God/Christ, works of the law (excluded as cause), good works of believers (affirmed as fruit), evil works, work/labor

    Key verses: Eph 2:8-10, Gal 2:16, James 2:17, Rev 14:12-13, Titus 2:14

    Relevance: The erga nomou vs. erga agatha distinction is critical for answering the legalism charge. Rev 14:12's commandment-keeping falls in the fruit/purpose category (erga agatha), not the cause category (erga nomou).

    G4103 — pistos¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G4103-pistos.md

    Occurrences: 67

    Faithful, trustworthy, believing. 62 unique verses (67 tokens). Two senses: active (one who believes/trusts) and passive (one who is trustworthy/reliable). Applied to God/Christ as divine title ('God is faithful,' 'Faithful and True'). The 'faithful sayings' formula in Pastorals. In Revelation, Jesus is called 'Faithful and True' (Rev 19:11) — supporting the subjective genitive reading of pistis Iesou as Jesus' own faithfulness.

    Semantic categories: faithful character quality, God/Christ as faithful, faithful sayings formula, believers as 'the faithful'

    Key verses: Rev 19:11, Rev 2:13, 1 Cor 1:9, 1 John 1:9, 1 Tim 1:15

    Relevance: Adjective from pistis. Jesus is called pistos in Revelation (19:11, 'Faithful and True') — supporting the reading of pistis Iesou in 14:12 as Jesus' faithfulness.

    G5219 — hupakouo¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G5219-hupakouo.md

    Occurrences: 23

    Obey, heed. Verb form of hupakoe (G5218). Heb 11:8 uses it for Abraham: pistei Abraam kaloumenos hupekousen — 'By faith Abraham, being called, obeyed.' Faith is the instrument of obedience (dative of means).

    Semantic categories: obedience to God/Christ, obedience to authorities, obedience of faith

    Key verses: Heb 11:8, Rom 6:17, Phil 2:12

    Relevance: Verb form of hupakoe. Heb 11:8 unifies faith and obedience in a single clause: 'by faith Abraham obeyed.'

    G544 — apeitheo¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G544-apeitheo.md

    Occurrences: 17

    Disbelieve, disobey. The word carries BOTH meanings — the biblical languages do not separate unbelief from disobedience. In John 3:36, pisteuo and apeitheo are set as direct antonyms: 'he that believeth (ho pisteuon) the Son... he that believeth not (ho apeithon) the Son.' The KJV translates apeithon as 'believeth not,' but the word fundamentally means 'disobey.'

    Semantic categories: disbelief as disobedience, rejection of gospel, stubborn non-compliance

    Key verses: John 3:36, Rom 2:8, 1 Pet 2:7-8, 1 Pet 3:1

    Relevance: Shows the belief-obedience unity in biblical thought. The antonym of pisteuo (believe) is apeitheo (disobey) — not merely 'disagree' or 'doubt.'

    G4160 — poieo¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G4160-poieo.md

    Occurrences: 597

    Make, do. Generic creation/action verb. In Rev 14:7, deliberately chosen over ktizo (G2936) for the creation formula — matching Exo 20:11 LXX where poieo renders asah (H6213). Rev 22:14 uses poieo for 'do his commandments' (poiountes tas entolas autou). The poieo/ktizo distinction in Revelation: generic creation language uses ktizo (Rev 4:11, 10:6); only Rev 14:7 uses poieo — the verb mapping to Exo 20:11 LXX.

    Semantic categories: create/make, do/perform, produce/cause

    Key verses: Rev 14:7, Rev 22:14, Exo 20:11 LXX

    Relevance: The deliberate choice of poieo over ktizo in Rev 14:7 links the first angel's message to the fourth commandment's creation rationale.

    G5602 — hode¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G5602-hōde.md

    Occurrences: 60

    Here, in this place. Functions as apocalyptic pointer in Revelation — 'HERE it is, look.' The hode inclusio brackets the beast crisis: Rev 13:10 ('Here is the patience and the faith') opens; Rev 14:12 ('Here is the patience of the saints: here are they that keep...') closes. The hode + estin construction is declarative, not imperative — it DESCRIBES, does not PRESCRIBE.

    Semantic categories: spatial reference, apocalyptic pointer, declarative identification

    Key verses: Rev 13:10, Rev 14:12, Rev 13:18, Rev 17:9

    Relevance: The hode + estin construction confirms Rev 14:12 as declarative description of the saints, not prescriptive command.

    G1378 — dogma¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G1378-dogma.md

    Occurrences: 5

    Decree, ordinance. NT vocabulary for abolished ceremonial regulations (Eph 2:15, Col 2:14). Distinguished from entole: dogma = ceremonial decrees that have been removed; entole = moral commandments that continue. The NT cessation vocabulary (dogma, cheirographon, skia) consistently refers to ceremonial regulations; the continuation vocabulary (entole, nomos unqualified) refers to moral law.

    Semantic categories: imperial decree, abolished ceremonial regulations, religious ordinance

    Key verses: Eph 2:15, Col 2:14, Luke 2:1, Acts 17:7

    Relevance: The entole/dogma distinction: NT uses dogma for abolished ceremonial regulations and entole for continuing moral commands. No overlap.

    G4151 — pneuma¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G4151-pneuma.md

    Occurrences: 385

    Spirit, wind, breath. The Spirit-enablement mechanism: Ezek 36:27 (God causes obedience through Spirit), Rom 8:4 (law fulfilled in Spirit-walkers), Gal 5:22 (fruit of the Spirit). The Spirit is the power source for the obedience described in Rev 14:12 — eliminating the legalism charge.

    Semantic categories: Holy Spirit, human spirit, wind/breath, spiritual realm

    Key verses: Ezek 36:27, Rom 8:4, Gal 5:22, 1 John 3:24

    Relevance: Spirit-enablement eliminates the legalism charge. If God causes obedience through the Spirit, it cannot be self-powered legalism.

    G3982 — peitho¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G3982-peitho.md

    Occurrences: 62

    Persuade, trust, obey. Root verb behind pistis (G4102). Semantic range spans from persuade to trust to obey — showing the conceptual continuity between belief and obedience in the pistis word family.

    Semantic categories: persuade, trust/rely, obey, be confident

    Key verses: Heb 13:17, Gal 5:7, Acts 5:36-37

    Relevance: Root of pistis. The semantic range of peitho (persuade → trust → obey) shows that pistis-family words inherently include an obedience dimension.

    H539 — aman¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H539-aman.md

    Occurrences: 133

    Believe, trust, be firm. Hebrew root behind Gen 15:6 (he'emin = 'he believed'). The Hiphil form conveys putting trust in, relying upon. LXX renders with pisteuo (G4100).

    Semantic categories: believe/trust, be firm/established, be faithful

    Key verses: Gen 15:6, Exo 14:31, Isa 7:9

    Relevance: Abraham's belief in Gen 15:6 — the foundational OT faith text that establishes the faith-first pattern.

    H8104 — shamar¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H8104-shamar.md

    Occurrences: 511

    Keep, guard, watch over, observe. Hebrew verb governing the four obedience terms in Gen 26:5 (shamar mishmarti mitsvotai chuqqotai torotai). LXX renders with both tereo and phylasso. Cognate of mishmereth (H4931).

    Semantic categories: keep/guard, observe commandments, watch over, preserve

    Key verses: Gen 26:5, Deut 6:17, Ps 119:2

    Relevance: The governing verb in Gen 26:5 — Abraham 'kept' (shamar) God's comprehensive charge. Connects to tereo in Rev 14:12 through LXX bridge.

    H4687 — mitsvah¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H4687-mitsvah.md

    Occurrences: 190

    Commandment. Hebrew equivalent of entole (G1785). One of four obedience terms in Gen 26:5 (mitsvotai = 'my commandments'). LXX renders with entole — establishing the direct translation bridge from OT 'commandments' to NT entole.

    Semantic categories: divine commandment, instruction, charge

    Key verses: Gen 26:5, Deut 6:1, Ps 119:96

    Relevance: Hebrew behind 'commandments' in Gen 26:5. The mitsvah → entole LXX bridge connects Abraham's obedience vocabulary to Rev 14:12's entolas tou theou.

    H4931 — mishmereth¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H4931-mishmereth.md

    Occurrences: 78

    Charge, guard, watch, duty. 78 occurrences. Cognate noun of shamar (H8104). Dominant usage: Levitical/priestly duty (Numbers 3-4 accounts for nearly half of all occurrences). Also used for guarding/watchkeeping, divine command/covenant obligation, and safekeeping/custody. In Gen 26:5, mishmereth represents the broadest, most comprehensive obedience term — the total body of obligations entrusted by God. Collocates strongly with shamar (30x, 'keep the charge' — cognate verb+noun intensifying the obligation), mitsvah (4x), towrah (3x), and chuqqah (3x).

    Semantic categories: Levitical/priestly duty, guarding/watchkeeping, divine command/covenant obligation, safekeeping/custody

    Key verses: Gen 26:5, Num 3:7, Num 18:3-5, Deut 11:1, Zech 3:7

    Relevance: In Gen 26:5, mishmereth is the broadest obedience term — Abraham kept God's total 'charge.' The comprehensive vocabulary (mishmereth + mitsvot + chuqqot + torot) shows Abraham's obedience was exhaustive.
