## Question
How do the 14 major critical arguments against the three angels' messages fare when tested against biblical evidence? Take each objection and respond using evidence from the prior 19 studies.

## Prior Research Summary
This is study 20 of 21 in the 3AM series. All 19 prior studies are complete (3am-00 through 3am-19). The reference agent gathered findings from every prior study mapped to each of the 14 objections, plus external corpus leads from EGW, Secrets Unsealed, and YouTube critics (Answering Adventism). Web research identified the strongest formulations of each objection from three critical traditions: former-Adventist (Ratzlaff, Tinker), evangelical/Reformed (Packer, Ortlund, GotQuestions), and academic/internal (Ford, deSilva, Spectrum). The strongest objections are: entole vs. nomos (Obj 4/6), Ford's Glacier View arguments (Obj 8), Sabbath silence (Obj 6), ECT arguments (Obj 7), and Gal 1:8-9 application (Obj 2). The weakest are: no gospel (Obj 1), literal angels (Obj 11), and 19th-century invention (Obj 14).

The critical question is whether Paul's gospel is limited to 1 Cor 15:3-4 or broader — check Rom 1:1-4, Acts 17:22-31, Gal 3:8 for Paul's OWN variant summaries. Run greek_parser.py --verse on Rev 14:6-7 to confirm the legon participle embeds v.7 as gospel CONTENT. Compare Acts 17:22-31 element-by-element with Rev 14:6-7 (Creator God, universal scope, judgment, moral reorientation). 2. **Objection 3: Do the apostles teach these elements as gospel?:**
Three independent NT texts explicitly label the combined message — including judgment — as gospel: Luke 3:18 (euengelizeto for John's judgment preaching), Rom 2:16 ('according to my gospel'), Rev 14:6-7 (judgment IS the everlasting gospel content). Paul at Athens (Acts 17:22-31) contains the tightest correspondence with Rev 14:6-7. Run cross_testament_parallels_v2.py --hybrid-nt on Rev 14:7 to find NT parallels. READ $WORD_STUDIES/WG-gospel.md for comprehensive gospel vocabulary. Check whether Carson (Reformed, non-Adventist) acknowledged Acts 17's creation-judgment framework as 'the necessary framework without which his proclamation of Jesus makes no sense.' Prior study 3am-03 documented six major NT preaching episodes with the three-part structure.
**Verses:** Acts 17:22-31, Luke 3:18, Rom 2:16, Matt 3:2-12, Acts 7:1-53, Rom 1:18-25, 1 Pet 4:17
**Strong's:** G2098, G2097, G2920, G4160, G4352

3. **Objection 4: Does Rev 14:12 teach legalism?:**
READ $WORD_STUDIES/G1785-entole.md and $WORD_STUDIES/G3551-nomos.md and TR-mitsvah-entole.md and TR-towrah-nomos.md for the commandment/law vocabulary analysis. The grammar is decisive: terountes is a present active PARTICIPLE describing the saints, not an imperative prescribing conditions. V.7 uses imperatives (commands); v.12 uses participle (description). **Verses:** Rev 14:12, Rom 7:7-12, Eph 6:2, Luke 23:56, Gal 5:6, 1 John 5:3, Rom 13:10, Rom 8:4, Rom 3:31, Ezek 36:27, Phil 2:13, Gen 15:6, Gen 26:5, Rev 22:14
**Strong's:** G1785, G3551, G4102, G5083, G26

4. **Objection 5: Does 'fear God' conflict with 'perfect love casts out fear'?:**
