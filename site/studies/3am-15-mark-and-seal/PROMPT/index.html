    Question¶

    What is the charagma (mark, G5480) and what is the sphragis (seal, G4973)? How do they relate to the Deuteronomy 6:6-8 / Exodus 13:9 hand-and-forehead loyalty pattern? How does the Ezekiel 9:4 tav (mark on the forehead of those who sigh and cry) prefigure the seal of God? What is the relationship between God's seal and the Sabbath — does Exodus 31:13,17 ("a sign between me and the children of Israel") and Ezekiel 20:12,20 ("I gave them my sabbaths, to be a sign between me and them") establish the Sabbath as the seal's content? How does a seal function — identifying who seals (God's name), what authority they have (Creator), and over what domain (heaven and earth) — and which commandment contains all three elements? How does the Spirit-seal connection (Eph 1:13, 4:30) relate to the Sabbath-seal claim? What is the composite argument for the seal being connected to Sabbath observance?

    Prior Research Summary¶

    Seven prior studies provide substantial groundwork: 3am-14 established charagma never appears without proskyneo/eikon context, God seals forehead ONLY vs beast marks forehead OR hand, Sabbath is the only commandment called owth four times, the charakter/charagma etymology, and the Spirit as sealing AGENT (dative of instrument in Eph 1:13). mark-of-beast-vs-seal-of-god established the Deut 6:6-8 hand-forehead pattern, Ezek 9:4 as OT template with five structural links to Rev 7, and the owth vocabulary chain. gsp-11 established Rom 4:11 as interpretive key (circumcision = both semeion and sphragis), the owth-to-semeion LXX bridge (94% consistency), sabbatismos as hapax in Heb 4:9, and Rev 14:7 embedding Sabbath within the gospel. 3am-08 confirmed the four shared terms between Rev 14:7 and Exo 20:11 LXX, the diagnostic poieo/ktizo distinction, and the fourth commandment as containing three seal elements. Web research provided Deissmann's charagma background, the Talmudic tav-as-divine-seal tradition (Shabbat 55a), and critic arguments (Life Assurance Ministries sign/seal vocabulary separation, evangelical Spirit-seal exclusivity). External corpus (EGW, Bohr, Smith, Haskell) provided claims requiring biblical verification.

    Primary Verses (30 — full analysis required)¶

    Revelation seal/mark passages: All Revelation texts with seal or mark vocabulary - Rev 7:2-3 — Angel with seal of the living God, sealing on foreheads of servants - Rev 9:4 — Locusts cannot hurt those with seal of God on foreheads — protective function - Rev 13:16-17 — Beast mark on right hand OR forehead — economic coercion, compliance option - Rev 14:1 — 144,000 with Father's name written on foreheads — name = seal content - Rev 14:9-11 — Third angel warning against receiving mark — 'no rest' (anapausis) irony - Rev 14:12 — Commandment-keepers identified as the sealed — faith of Jesus + commandments of God - Rev 20:4 — Those who did not receive mark on forehead or hand — final vindication - Rev 22:4 — His name shall be in their foreheads — eschatological completion of sealing

    Sabbath-as-sign texts: The four explicit owth designations plus creation institution - Exo 31:13 — Sabbath as sign (owth) between God and Israel — 'that ye may know that I am the LORD that doth sanctify you' - Exo 31:16-17 — Sabbath as perpetual covenant (berit olam) and sign forever (owth le-olam) — creation rationale - Ezek 20:12 — Sabbath as sign — 'I gave them my sabbaths, to be a sign between me and them, that they might know that I am the LORD that sanctify them' - Ezek 20:20 — Sabbath as sign — 'hallow my sabbaths; and they shall be a sign between me and you, that ye may know that I am the LORD your God'

    Hand-and-forehead loyalty pattern: OT sign-on-hand and frontlets-between-eyes texts - Deut 6:6-8 — Heart FIRST, then hand and forehead — the OT loyalty-sign pattern that Revelation inverts for the beast - Exo 13:9 — Passover as sign on hand and memorial between eyes — earliest hand-forehead pattern - Exo 13:16 — Firstborn redemption as sign on hand and frontlets between eyes — parallel to 13:9 - Deut 11:18 — Words as sign on hand and frontlets between eyes — repeats the loyalty-binding pattern

    Ezekiel 8-9 seal/mark prefiguration: Sun worship (greatest abomination) vs. tav on faithful - Ezek 8:16 — Sun worship at the temple — the greatest abomination contrasting with the tav in Ezek 9:4 - Ezek 9:4-6 — Tav mark on foreheads of those who sigh and cry — OT template for Rev 7 sealing

    Spirit-seal passages: NT texts on sealing by the Holy Spirit - Eph 1:13-14 — Sealed with Holy Spirit of promise — the Spirit-seal text, key grammatical question (dative of instrument vs. appositional) - Eph 4:30 — Sealed unto the day of redemption by the Holy Spirit — Spirit as sealing agent - 2 Cor 1:22 — God sealed us and gave the earnest (arrabon) of the Spirit — threefold: sealed, anointed, earnest

    Fourth commandment as seal content: The commandment with name, title, and domain + its NT allusion - Exo 20:8-11 — Fourth commandment — the only commandment with name (LORD), title (Creator), domain (heaven/earth/sea) - Rev 14:7 — First angel — 'worship him that made heaven, earth, sea' echoes fourth commandment

    Sign/seal overlap — circumcision test case: Romans 4:11 and its OT background - Rom 4:11 — Circumcision as sign (semeion) AND seal (sphragis) of faith-righteousness — the test case for sign/seal overlap - Gen 17:11 — Circumcision as owth (sign) of the covenant — parallel to Sabbath as owth

    Sabbath rest theology: Sabbatismos and no-rest irony - Heb 4:9 — Sabbatismos (G4520) remaining for the people of God — Sabbath-rest as ongoing obligation

    Additional Primary Verses: - Isa 8:16 — 'Seal the law among my disciples' — seal attached to the law, EGW's key proof-text - Acts 17:29 — Charagma in non-Revelation context — idolatry connection (graven art of man) - Heb 1:3 — Christ as charakter (G5481) of God's person — etymological root shared with charagma - 2 Tim 2:19 — 'The foundation of God standeth sure, having this seal: The Lord knoweth them that are his' — seal as ownership identification

    Focus Areas¶

    1. Charagma (G5480) — etymology, usage, and Greco-Roman background: READ $WORD_STUDIES/G5480-charagma.md for the canonical entry. Charagma appears 9x in the NT (8 in Revelation + Acts 17:29). The Deissmann background (Light from the Ancient East, pp. 276-277) establishes it as the technical term for imperial stamps, slave brands, and religious tattoos. Run greek_parser.py --verse on Rev 13:16, Rev 14:9, Rev 14:11, Rev 19:20, Rev 20:4, and Acts 17:29 to examine each grammatical context. The charakter/charagma etymology is critical — both from root charasso. READ $WORD_STUDIES/G5481-charaktēr.md for the charakter entry. Christ as charakter of God (Heb 1:3) vs. beast's charagma creates a theological antithesis. Also check G4742 stigma (Gal 6:17) — Paul's marks of Jesus vs. beast's mark. The visible/invisible distinction (charagma = visible physical mark, sphragis allows invisible interpretation) is a web research claim that needs lexical verification. Verses: Rev 13:16, Rev 14:9, Rev 14:11, Rev 19:20, Rev 20:4, Acts 17:29, Heb 1:3, Gal 6:17 Strong's: G5480, G5481, G4742

    2. Sphragis (G4973) and Sphragizo (G4972) — seal function and theology: READ $WORD_STUDIES/G4973-sphragis.md and $WORD_STUDIES/G4972-sphragizo.md for the canonical entries. Sphragis appears 16x in the NT, sphragizo appears 15x. Classify each occurrence by function: (1) authentication/authority (Jhn 6:27, Rom 4:11, 1 Cor 9:2), (2) concealment (Rev 5:1, Dan 12:9 LXX), (3) protective ownership (Rev 7:2-3, 9:4, Eph 1:13, 4:30), (4) divine ratification (2 Tim 2:19, 2 Cor 1:22). Run greek_parser.py --verse on Rev 7:2-3, Eph 1:13, Eph 4:30, 2 Cor 1:22, 2 Tim 2:19, Jhn 6:27, Jhn 3:33, Rom 4:11, Rom 15:28. The EST 8:8 and 1 Ki 21:8 OT parallels show a royal seal functions with name + authority + irrevocability. Also READ $WORD_STUDIES/H2856-chatham.md and $WORD_STUDIES/H2368-chowtham.md for the Hebrew seal vocabulary. Check the grammar reference entry on seal-command syntax: $GRAMMAR_REF/hebrew/seal-command-syntax-daniel.md for chatham usage in Daniel. Verses: Rev 7:2-3, Rev 9:4, Rev 14:1, Eph 1:13-14, Eph 4:30, 2 Cor 1:22, 2 Tim 2:19, Jhn 6:27, Jhn 3:33, Rom 4:11, Rom 15:28, Est 8:8, 1 Ki 21:8, Dan 6:17 Strong's: G4973, G4972, H2856, H2368

    3. The Deut 6:6-8 / Exo 13:9 hand-and-forehead loyalty pattern: READ $WORD_STUDIES/H2903-ṭôwphâphâh.md for totaphoth (frontlets) and $WORD_STUDIES/H226-owth.md for owth (sign). The pattern appears in Exo 13:9 (sign on hand, memorial between eyes), Exo 13:16 (sign on hand, frontlets between eyes), Deut 6:6-8 (words on heart FIRST, then bind on hand and frontlets between eyes), and Deut 11:18 (words in heart and soul, bind on hand and frontlets between eyes). Run hebrew_parser.py --verse on each of these passages to confirm the vocabulary. The critical theological point is the SEQUENCE: heart first (internal conviction), then hand and forehead (external identification). God's seal is forehead ONLY (Rev 7:3, 9:4, 14:1, 22:4 — no exceptions) while the beast accepts hand OR forehead (Rev 13:16, 14:9, 20:4). READ $WORD_STUDIES/G3359-metopon.md for the Greek metopon (forehead) and $WORD_STUDIES/H4696-mêtsach.md for the Hebrew metsach (forehead). Run cross_testament_parallels_v2.py --hybrid-ot on Deut 6:8 to find the OT-NT parallel chain. Verses: Deut 6:6-8, Exo 13:9, Exo 13:16, Deut 11:18, Rev 7:3, Rev 9:4, Rev 13:16, Rev 14:1, Rev 20:4, Rev 22:4 Strong's: H2903, H226, G3359, H4696

    4. Ezekiel 9:4 tav mark and Ezekiel 8:16 sun worship — the OT seal/mark prefiguration: Run hebrew_parser.py --verse on Ezek 9:4, Ezek 9:5-6, and Ezek 8:16. The word 'mark' in Ezek 9:4 is tav (H8420) — the letter name, not a generic mark. The verb is tavah (H8427) 'to mark out.' The Talmudic tradition (Shabbat 55a) says the tav is 'the last letter of the seal of the Holy One' because God's seal is emet (truth = alef-mem-tav). The paleo-Hebrew tav resembled an X or cross. Five structural elements link Ezek 9 to Rev 7: (1) marking on foreheads, (2) judgment on unmarked, (3) begins at sanctuary, (4) universal scope, (5) marking precedes destruction. The moral criterion is crucial — those who 'sigh and cry' (H584 anach + H602 naaq) over abominations — this is heart orientation, not mere ritual compliance. Ezek 8:16 places sun worship as THE greatest abomination immediately before the tav marking. Bohr's Ezek 8:16/9:4 contrast (sun worship vs. tav on faithful) needs verification as a genuine textual sequence. Run cross_testament_parallels_v2.py --hybrid-nt on Ezek 9:4 to find NT connections. Verses: Ezek 9:4-6, Ezek 8:16, Rev 7:2-3 Strong's: H8420, H8427

    5. Sabbath as sign (owth) — Exo 31:13,17 and Ezek 20:12,20: READ $WORD_STUDIES/H226-owth.md for the canonical owth entry and $WORD_STUDIES/H7676-shabbath.md for shabbath. Run hebrew_parser.py --verse on Exo 31:13, Exo 31:17, Ezek 20:12, and Ezek 20:20. In each, verify the exact Hebrew: owth (sign) + beyn (between) + God and His people. Exo 31:16 adds berit olam (perpetual covenant) and 31:17 adds owth le-olam (sign forever). The critic challenge is whether owth is used for OTHER commandments — check circumcision (Gen 17:11 uses owth), rainbow (Gen 9:12-13 uses owth), blood on doorpost (Exo 12:13 uses owth). The question is not whether owth is used elsewhere but whether any other COMMANDMENT of the Decalogue is explicitly called owth.     Additional Research Instructions¶

    Critical Research Directives¶

    1. The Sign/Seal Linguistic Question¶

    This is the MOST important exegetical issue. Life Assurance Ministries claims owth (sign) and chowtham (seal) are never interchanged in translation. Verify this at the TRANSLATION level (check LXX patterns for H226 and H2368). Then verify at the FUNCTIONAL level: Rom 4:11 uses BOTH semeion and sphragis for circumcision, which in Hebrew is called owth (Gen 17:11). If one owth (circumcision) = both semeion and sphragis, the strict sign/seal separation is broken at the conceptual level even if maintained at the translation level.

    2. Spirit-Seal Grammar¶

    The dative case analysis in Eph 1:13 is crucial. The phrase 'esphragisthete to pneumati' could be: (a) instrumental dative — 'sealed BY/WITH the Spirit' (Spirit = agent), or (b) dative of content/apposition — 'sealed, namely the Spirit' (Spirit = seal). Wallace's Greek Grammar Beyond the Basics is the standard reference. The 2 Cor 1:22 threefold structure (sealed + anointed + given earnest) may help disambiguate — if three distinct actions are named, the Spirit as arrabon is distinct from the sealing.

    3. Isaiah 8:16 Context¶

    EGW's use of Isa 8:16 ('seal the law among my disciples') as a proof-text needs careful contextual analysis. What is the immediate context? Is 'seal the law' about the Sabbath specifically, or about Isaiah's testimony generally? Parse the Hebrew.

    4. The Name Argument¶

    Smith claims 'the Father regards his name as his seal' and 'the commandment of the law which contains God's name is therefore the seal.' But 'the LORD thy God' appears in MULTIPLE commandments (Exo 20:2, 5, 7, 10, 12). The fourth commandment's distinction is not merely having God's name but having name + creative title + universal domain TOGETHER. Be precise about what the fourth commandment uniquely has.

    5. Counter-Text Engagement¶

    Address Col 2:16 and Rom 14:5-6 honestly. These are the strongest critic texts. The prior study (law-27) found that Col 2:16's sabbata is contested (ceremonial sabbaths vs. weekly Sabbath) and Rom 14 addresses 'doubtful disputations' without using sabbaton. But the research agent should examine these texts freshly, not just cite prior findings.

    6. The Composite Argument Assessment¶

    After verifying each individual link, the research agent should assess the cumulative strength of the chain. Note which links are strongest (e.g., Sabbath = owth four times is textually indisputable) and which are weakest (e.g., sign can function as seal is based primarily on one verse, Rom 4:11). An honest assessment strengthens the study's credibility.
