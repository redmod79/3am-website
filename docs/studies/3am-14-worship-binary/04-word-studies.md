# Word Studies

## Question
How does the proskyneo (worship) verb chain through Revelation 13-14 establish the worship binary as THE defining issue of the end-time crisis? Trace every occurrence of proskyneo in Rev 13-14: spontaneous (13:4), universal (13:8), coerced by the earth beast (13:12), death-enforced (13:15), then the Creator alternative (14:7) and the warning against beast worship (14:9,11). How does the Daniel 3 structural parallel (image + worship command + death decree + faithful refusal) function as the OT type for Rev 13's image and mark? How do the first and second commandments (Exo 20:3-5) establish that worship belongs to God alone? Address the objection that the mark is 'purely spiritual' and not about specific commandments -- show that the text itself links the mark to a specific worship system. What is the significance of seal on forehead only (willing allegiance) vs. mark on forehead OR hand (conviction or compliance)?

### G4352 — proskyneo

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G4352-proskyneo.md`

**Occurrences:** 65

The central worship verb. Root from pros (toward) + probable derivative of kuon (dog, meaning to kiss). Dominant meaning: to prostrate oneself in homage, worship. LXX translates Hebrew shachah (H7812) with proskyneo extensively. KJV renders almost exclusively as 'worship/worshipped.' Concentrated in Revelation (22 unique verses per library entry, 41% of all NT occurrences). Functions as a Leitwort throughout Revelation. Semantic range: prostration before Jesus (healing/petition), recognition of divine sonship, true worship discourse (John 4), exclusive worship command (Matt 4:10). In Revelation specifically: worship of God/Lamb (4:10, 5:14, 7:11, 11:16, 15:4, 19:4), worship refused by angels (19:10, 22:8-9), worship of the beast/dragon (13:4,8,12,15), Creator worship (14:7), beast worship warning (14:9,11).

**Semantic categories:** prostration before Jesus, recognition of divine sonship, true worship in spirit and truth, exclusive worship command, heavenly worship of God/Lamb, false worship of beast/dragon

**Key verses:** Rev 13:4, Rev 13:8, Rev 13:12, Rev 14:7, Rev 14:9, Rev 14:11, Matt 4:10, John 4:24

**Relevance:** THE central word of this study. The proskyneo verb chain through Rev 13-14 establishes the four-stage worship escalation (spontaneous -> universal -> coerced -> death-enforced) followed by the Creator alternative (14:7) and the warning (14:9,11). Every use in the Revelation worship-binary echoes the Exo 20:5 prohibition.

### G5480 — charagma

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G5480-charagma.md`

**Occurrences:** 9

Mark/brand/stamp. Root charasso (to scratch/engrave). 9 NT occurrences: 8 in Revelation (beast's mark) + 1 in Acts 17:29 (graven art). Semantic categories: engraved artwork (Acts 17:29), mark compulsorily imposed (Rev 13:16-17), mark linked to worship (Rev 14:9,11), mark as basis for judgment (Rev 16:2, 19:20, 20:4), victory over the mark (Rev 15:2). The mark NEVER appears in the NT without proskyneo or eikon in the same context. Shares root charasso with charakter (G5481). Key collocations: proskyneo (56% co-occurrence), eikon (frequent), metopon (forehead).

**Semantic categories:** engraved artwork, compulsory mark of allegiance, worship-linked brand, basis for divine judgment, object of victory

**Key verses:** Rev 13:16, Rev 13:17, Rev 14:9, Rev 14:11, Rev 16:2, Rev 19:20, Rev 20:4, Acts 17:29, Rev 15:2

**Relevance:** The mark is the counterpart to the seal. Critical finding: charagma NEVER appears without proskyneo or eikon in the same passage — the mark is grammatically inseparable from worship. Shares root with charakter (Heb 1:3) creating the theological irony: Christ bears God's character; beast-followers bear the beast's brand.

### G4973 — sphragis

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G4973-sphragis.md`

**Occurrences:** 16

Seal/signet. A stamp impressed as a mark of privacy, genuineness, ownership. 16 NT occurrences across Revelation (13), Romans (1), 1 Corinthians (1), 2 Timothy (1). Semantic categories: authentication/certification (Rom 4:11, 1 Cor 9:2), divine concealment/authorization (Rev 5:1-9), sequential judgment unveiling (Rev 6:1-8:1), divine ownership and eschatological protection (Rev 7:2, 9:4). Direct antithesis of charagma: sphragis implies protective ownership via signet impression; charagma implies subjugation via branding.

**Semantic categories:** authentication and certification, divine concealment and authorization, sequential judgment unveiling, divine ownership and protection

**Key verses:** Rev 7:2, Rev 9:4, Rom 4:11, Eph 1:13, 2 Tim 2:19

**Relevance:** The seal contrasts with the mark. Sphragis = divine authorization and protection; charagma = imposed control and subjugation. Both appear on the forehead, making them direct antitheses. The seal authenticates prior allegiance; the mark may be imposed externally.

### G1504 — eikon

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G1504-eikon.md`

**Occurrences:** 23

Image/likeness/representation. 23 NT occurrences. Revelation dominates (10 of 23, 43%). Semantic categories: imperial coin inscription (Matt 22:20), Christ as image of God (Col 1:15, 2 Cor 4:4), humanity bearing/restored to God's image (Rom 8:29, Col 3:10), shadow vs. true image (Heb 10:1), image of the beast (Rev 13:14-15, 14:9,11, 15:2, 16:2, 19:20, 20:4). Hebrew source: tselem (H6754/A6755) with PMI 8.98-9.36. The Daniel 3 golden image (tselem) maps directly to Revelation's image (eikon) of the beast through the LXX.

**Semantic categories:** imperial coin inscription, Christ as image of God, humanity bearing God's image, image of the beast

**Key verses:** Rev 13:14, Rev 13:15, Rev 14:9, Col 1:15, Heb 1:3

**Relevance:** Links Daniel 3's tselem to Revelation's beast-image. The LXX vocabulary chain (tselem -> eikon) confirms the Daniel 3/Rev 13 structural parallel. Image of the beast (10 occurrences in Revelation) always appears with worship language.

### G4160 — poieo

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G4160-poieo.md`

**Occurrences:** 597

Make/do. The broadest Greek action verb. 518+ unique verses in NT. Critical for this study: Rev 14:7 uses poiesanti ('him that MADE') while Rev 4:11 and 10:6 use ktizo. The deliberate choice of poieo maps Rev 14:7 to Exo 20:11 LXX where poieo translates asah (H6213). Also critical: Rev 13:12 and 13:16 use poiei in the causative poiei+hina construction binding worship (v.12) and mark (v.16) to the same coercive program.

**Semantic categories:** general action, physical making/creating, causation/coercion, commandment-keeping

**Key verses:** Rev 14:7, Rev 13:12, Rev 13:16, Rev 4:11, Exo 20:11

**Relevance:** The verb choice in Rev 14:7 (poieo not ktizo) is the strongest evidence linking the Creator worship call to the Sabbath commandment (Exo 20:11). Also the key verb in the poiei+hina construction that binds worship and mark in Rev 13.

### G5481 — charakter

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G5481-charaktēr.md`

**Occurrences:** 1

Express image/exact representation. Hapax legomenon in NT (Heb 1:3 only). Root charasso shared with charagma (G5480). Christ is the charakter (exact stamp-die impression) of God's person. Theological contrast: Christ bears God's character (charakter); beast-followers bear the beast's brand (charagma). Same root, opposite valence.

**Semantic categories:** exact divine representation, theological contrast with charagma

**Key verses:** Heb 1:3

**Relevance:** The charakter/charagma etymological connection is theologically significant. Christ as God's charakter vs. beast-followers bearing the beast's charagma — divine character vs. bestial brand.

### G1785 — entole

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G1785-entole.md`

**Occurrences:** 71

Commandment/precept. 65 unique verses (71 tokens). Johannine concentration (34% of uses). In Revelation: Rev 12:17 (commandments of God), Rev 14:12 (commandments of God), Rev 22:14. These three Revelation uses bracket the worship conflict: the dragon targets commandment-keepers (12:17), the saints are identified as commandment-keepers (14:12), and commandment-keeping grants access to the tree of life (22:14).

**Semantic categories:** Decalogue/Mosaic Law, Christ's new commandment of love, end-time remnant identification

**Key verses:** Rev 12:17, Rev 14:12, Rev 22:14, Matt 22:36, John 13:34

**Relevance:** The commandment frame for the worship binary. Rev 12:17 and 14:12 use entolas tou theou ('commandments of God') to identify the faithful remnant, bracketing the entire Rev 13 worship-coercion narrative.

### G3359 — metopon

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G3359-metopon.md`

**Occurrences:** 8

Forehead. From meta (between) + ops (face). 8 NT occurrences, ALL in Revelation. Semantic categories: location of God's seal/name (Rev 7:3, 14:1, 22:4), location of beast's mark (Rev 13:16, 14:9), location of Babylon's identity (Rev 17:5), criterion of protection/judgment (Rev 9:4, 20:4). Critical pattern: God's seal appears on forehead ONLY; beast's mark appears on forehead OR hand.

**Semantic categories:** location of God's seal, location of beast's mark, location of Babylon's identity, criterion of judgment

**Key verses:** Rev 7:3, Rev 13:16, Rev 14:1, Rev 14:9, Rev 17:5, Rev 22:4

**Relevance:** The forehead-only vs. forehead-or-hand distinction is critical. Every divine seal/name passage is forehead-only (7:3, 9:4, 14:1, 22:4). Every mark passage includes 'or hand' option (13:16, 14:9, 20:4). God requires mind/conviction; the beast accepts compliance.

### H7812 — shachah

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H7812-shachah.md`

**Occurrences:** 194

Bow down/worship/prostrate. 166 unique verses (194 tokens) across 25 books. Hishtaphel stem dominates (99%). KJV: worship (46%), bowed (29%). The LXX translates shachah with proskyneo — creating the direct linguistic link between OT and NT worship vocabulary. Exo 20:5 and Deut 5:9 use shachah in the commandment prohibition; the LXX renders this as proskyneo. Therefore every proskyneo in Revelation's worship chain echoes the Sinai prohibition.

**Semantic categories:** worship of God, bowing before royalty/humans, idol worship (condemned), commandment prohibition

**Key verses:** Exo 20:5, Exo 34:8, Deut 5:9, Isa 44:15, Isa 44:17

**Relevance:** The Hebrew worship verb that maps to proskyneo through the LXX. The shachah -> proskyneo translation relationship means Revelation's worship chain directly echoes the Sinai commandment.

### H226 — owth

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H226-owth.md`

**Occurrences:** 85

Sign/token/mark. 77 unique verses (85 tokens) across 14 books. Semantic categories: covenant sign/token (Gen 9:12, 17:11, Exo 31:13,17, Ezek 20:12,20), miraculous signs (Exo 7:3), confirmatory/prophetic sign (Isa 7:14), memorial sign. The Sabbath is called owth 4 times: Exo 31:13, 31:17, Ezek 20:12, 20:20. No other commandment receives this designation.

**Semantic categories:** covenant sign, miraculous signs, prophetic sign, memorial sign

**Key verses:** Exo 31:13, Exo 31:17, Ezek 20:12, Ezek 20:20, Gen 9:12, Gen 17:11

**Relevance:** The Sabbath is uniquely called owth (sign) four times. No other commandment receives this designation. This connects the Sabbath to the seal/mark discussion — if God's seal requires a sign of allegiance, the Sabbath is the only commandment explicitly designated as such.

### G372 — anapausis

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G372-anapausis.md`

**Occurrences:** 5

Rest/cessation/intermission. 5 NT occurrences in 3 books (Matthew, Luke, Revelation). Semantic categories: spiritual rest offered by Christ (Matt 11:29), rest sought by demons (Matt 12:43, Luke 11:24), ceaseless worship (Rev 4:8 'they rest not'), absence of rest as punishment (Rev 14:11 'no rest day nor night'). The Rev 4:8/14:11 contrast: ceaseless worship of God vs. ceaseless torment of beast-worshippers. Both use 'ouk echousin anapausin hemeras kai nuktos.'

**Semantic categories:** spiritual rest in Christ, ceaseless heavenly worship, absence of rest as punishment

**Key verses:** Rev 14:11, Rev 4:8, Matt 11:29

**Relevance:** The 'no rest day nor night' (Rev 14:11) for beast-worshippers ironically contrasts with the sabbatismos (Heb 4:9) remaining for God's people. Same construction in Rev 4:8 (ceaseless praise) and 14:11 (ceaseless torment) — literary inversion.

### G3709 — orge

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G3709-orge.md`

**Occurrences:** 36

Wrath/anger. Distinguished from thumos: orge is settled indignation while thumos is passionate outburst. In Rev 14:10, both appear: 'the wine of the wrath (thumos) of God poured out without mixture into the cup of his indignation (orge).' The double vocabulary intensifies the warning.

**Semantic categories:** divine settled wrath, eschatological judgment

**Key verses:** Rev 14:10, Rev 6:16, Rev 6:17, Rev 11:18, Rev 19:15

**Relevance:** The orge + thumos combination in Rev 14:10 represents the most intense wrath-language in the NT, underscoring the severity of the beast-worship warning.

**Key verses:** Matt 4:10, Luke 4:8, Rom 12:1, Heb 9:14

### H5456 — sagad

**Occurrences:** 4

Hebrew: to prostrate oneself in homage, fall down. Primitive root. 4 occurrences (BLB count): Isa 44:15, 44:17, 44:19, 46:6. The Hebrew cognate of Aramaic sgid (A5457, used in Daniel 3). Only appears in Isaiah's anti-idol satire. Isa 44:15,17 is the only OT passage where sagad co-occurs with shachah (H7812), linking the three worship verbs: sagad + shachah + palal (pray). This connects Isaiah's idol-worship polemic to Daniel 3's worship vocabulary and through it to Revelation's proskyneo chain.

**Semantic categories:** prostrate worship of idols, Isaiah's anti-idol satire

**Key verses:** Isa 44:15, Isa 44:17, Isa 44:19, Isa 46:6

**Relevance:** The Hebrew cognate of Daniel 3's Aramaic sgid. Its co-occurrence with shachah in Isa 44:15,17 links the three worship-vocabulary families (sagad/sgid, shachah/proskyneo, palal) across Isaiah, Daniel, and Revelation.
