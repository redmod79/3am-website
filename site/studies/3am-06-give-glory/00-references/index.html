    Reference Gathering: "Give Glory to Him" (Rev 14:7b)¶

    Question¶

    What does dote auto doxan ("give glory to him") mean as a gospel imperative? How is glory (doxa/kabod) given to God — through confession (Josh 7:19), character acknowledgment (Isa 42:12), conduct (Matt 5:16, 1 Pet 2:12), fruit-bearing (John 15:8), bodily stewardship (1 Cor 6:20, 10:31), beholding-transformation (2 Cor 3:18), or something else? What is the relationship between giving glory and the judgment announcement that immediately follows? How does Romans 1:21 ("they glorified him not as God") describe the exact failure the first angel addresses? Why does Rev 16:9 record that under the plagues they "repented not to give him glory" — a precise verbal echo of 14:7? How does John 17:4 ("I have glorified thee on the earth: I have finished the work") establish Christ as the model of giving glory?

    Study Plan Context¶

    This is Study 6 (3am-06) of the 22-study Three Angels' Messages series. It belongs to Arc 1: The First Angel — Creator, Judge, Worshipped (Studies 04-09). The study plan describes it as examining "give glory to him" (dote auto doxan) as the second imperative of the everlasting gospel, exploring how glory (doxa/kabod) is given to God through confession, character, conduct, fruit-bearing, bodily stewardship, and beholding-transformation. It connects giving glory to the judgment announcement and shows the verbal echo between Rev 14:7 and Rev 16:9. It examines Christ as the model of giving glory (John 17:4).

    Depends on: 3am-05 (Fear God) Criticism addressed: None directly — builds the positive case Series function: Unpacks the second imperative and connects glory-giving to both gospel response and judgment.

    Completed prior 3AM studies: - 3am-00-series-methodology - 3am-01-literary-context-rev-14 - 3am-02-three-angels-in-ot - 3am-03-three-angels-in-nt - 3am-04-euangelion-aionion - 3am-05-fear-god

    From Study Plan (Series Studies)¶

    3am-00-series-methodology: - Question: What is the purpose of this 22-study series on the Three Angels' Messages? - Mapped Rev 14:7c "dote auto doxan" to Study 06 — glory given through confession (Josh 7:19), character acknowledgment (Isa 42:12), conduct (Matt 5:16), bodily stewardship (1 Cor 6:20, 10:31), beholding-transformation (2 Cor 3:18) - Identified the three aorist imperatives (phobethete, dote, proskynesate) as one reality viewed from three angles: fear = internal orientation, glory = relational acknowledgment, worship = behavioral expression - Noted that the legon participle connecting v.7 to v.6 means these commands ARE the gospel content

    3am-01-literary-context-rev-14: - Question: What is the literary structure of Revelation 12-14? - Established the competing-messages framework: where Rev 13 demands beast worship, Rev 14:7 calls for Creator worship - Documented the hupomone inclusio (13:10 to 14:12) bracketing the three angels as a single literary unit - Identified the "no rest day nor night" (14:11, anapausin) as a lexical inversion of Rev 4:8 — ceaseless false worship produces ceaseless unrest - Confirmed the three angels are symbolic of human proclaimers carrying divine messages

    3am-02-three-angels-in-ot: - Question: Is the three-part pattern repeated throughout the OT? - Documented six OT episodes (Noah, Sodom, Egypt, Carmel, Josiah, Jeremiah) all containing the three-part structure - Established the covenant lawsuit (riyb, H7378) as the formal legal structure underlying the pattern — riyb translates to krisis (G2920) in the LXX, the word used in Rev 14:7 - Documented the "give glory" idiom in the covenant-lawsuit tradition (Josh 7:19 as a judicial oath formula) - Traced the "come out" vocabulary chain (yatsa → exerchomai) from Gen 19 through Jer 51:45 to Rev 18:4

    3am-03-three-angels-in-nt: - Question: Does the NT repeat the same three-part pattern? - Documented five NT episodes (John the Baptist, Jesus, Stephen, Paul at Athens, Paul in Romans) - Identified the Romans 1:18-32 degenerative chain: knew God → glorified him not → became fools → changed the glory of God into images → God gave them up — the glory exchange in Rom 1:23 as the exact antiparallel of Rev 14:7 - Noted Rom 3:18 ("there is no fear of God before their eyes") inverts the first angel's opening command - Established the anticipation-vs-announcement distinction: NT preachers used mellei (about to judge); the first angel uses elthen (IS come)

    3am-04-euangelion-aionion: - Question: What does "everlasting gospel" mean? - Confirmed euangelion aionion as qualitative — emphasizing the eternal character of the gospel while referring to THE gospel - Traced the basar → euangelizo → euangelion vocabulary chain from OT through LXX to NT - Established that "give glory" (dote doxan) connects to the OT covenant-lawsuit tradition (Josh 7:19) - Paul calls the gospel "the glorious gospel of Christ" (2 Cor 4:4), using the same doxa vocabulary - Confirmed Rev 14:12 describes gospel RESULTS (commandments + faith), not additions

    3am-05-fear-god: - Question: What does "fear God" mean as the first imperative? - Established the three Hebrew fear words: yare (reverence), eimah (terror), pachad (dread) — all collapse into phobos in Greek - Resolved the 1 John 4:18 tension: the fear love casts out is kolasin-fear (dread of punishment), not the reverential awe of Rev 14:7 - Confirmed the three aorist imperatives (fear, give glory, worship) as one reality viewed from three angles - Identified Ecclesiastes 12:13-14 as the OT template: fear God → keep commandments → judgment — the first angel restates this universally - Established fearing God as the foundation for refusing to fear the beast (Matt 10:28; Dan 3:16-18)

    Web Research Findings¶

    The web research agent (Phase 0) produced extensive findings documented in 00-web-research.md. Key leads organized by research directive:

    Doxa/Kabod Lexical Bridge¶

    • Leona Glidden Running (Ministry, 1963): Of 25 Hebrew/Aramaic words translated by doxa in the LXX, kabod is the critical bridge from classical to biblical meanings. The LXX translators infused doxa (classically "opinion," "reputation") with kabod's meanings: brightness, splendor, majesty, character. Doxa appears 167 times in NT. Running distinguishes objective manifestations (Shekinah-type visible glory), character expression (God's attributes in action), and reciprocal honor (human responsibility to honor God's character).
    • Kabod Etymology (multiple sources): K-B-D root originally means "weight" or "heaviness." In Exodus 33:18-19, Moses asks to see God's glory; God responds by proclaiming His name and making "all my goodness pass before you" — equating glory with character/goodness.
    • Status: Leads to verify against Scripture

    The "Give Glory" = Confession Idiom¶

    • Tactical Christianity: Hebrew yada carries dual meaning: "to praise/give glory" AND "to confess an offence." Joshua 7:19 demonstrates: "give glory to the LORD... Tell me what you have done." Greek homologeo similarly functions both positively (confessing faith) and negatively (confessing faults). Confession and glory-giving are unified acts — acknowledging God is right.
    • Status: Lead to verify against Scripture

    Commentators on Revelation 14:7¶

    • Barnes: "To give glory to him is to acknowledge him as the only true God; to set up his pure worship in the heart; and to praise him."
    • Gill: "Glory is to be given to him, on account of the perfections of his nature, and the works of his hands."
    • Expositor's Greek Testament: "Since he who has created has the right to judge his creatures, as well as to receive their worship" — directly linking creation, judgment, and worship.
    • Status: Leads to verify against Scripture

    Romans 1:18-25 as Inverse of Rev 14:7¶

    • Simon Turpin (AiG, 2018): Paul employs Genesis 1-3 to expose humanity's threefold exchange: creation knowledge suppressed (Rom 1:18-21), idolatry replacing worship (Rom 1:22-25), sexual disorder following (Rom 1:26-28). Paul intentionally echoes Genesis 1:26-27 LXX language in Rom 1:23, 26-27.
    • Status: Lead to verify against Scripture

    John 17:4 — Christ as Model of Glorification¶

    • BibleHub commentators: Bengel: "I have consummated [finished] — Hereby is explained the expression, I have glorified Thee." Work-completion IS glorification. Meyer: the aorist edoxasa treats the entire redemptive mission as one accomplished act. Cambridge Bible: "This is the way in which God is glorified, the completion of the work of revelation."
    • Status: Lead to verify against Scripture

    2 Corinthians 3:18 — Beholding-Transformation¶

    • BibleHub commentators: Katoptrizomai debate: "beholding as in a mirror" (Meyer, most moderns) vs. "reflecting as a mirror" (Chrysostom). Metamorphoo — same verb as Christ's transfiguration. "From glory to glory" = progressive transformation. Gill: the Spirit removes the veil so we can behold glory.
    • Status: Lead to verify against Scripture

    1 Corinthians 6:20 — Bodily Glorification¶

    • Precept Austin: Doxazo aorist imperative (doxasate) — commanding immediate, urgent action. The agorazo purchase-price argument: slave redemption grounds the obligation to glorify God bodily. Connections to 1 Cor 10:31 (universal scope), Romans 12:1 (living sacrifice).
    • Status: Lead to verify against Scripture

    Key leads for the scoping agent: 1. The kabod/doxa lexical bridge is foundational — kabod = "weight/substance"; Exod 33:18-19 equates glory with goodness/character. This must be verified through primary texts, not assumed. 2. The "give glory" idiom has a specific CONFESSIONAL dimension (Josh 7:19 → John 9:24 → Rev 14:7) that functions as a judicial oath formula — must trace this construction (ten kabod la-YHWH / dos doxan to theo / dote auto doxan) through its attestations. 3. Romans 1:21 (edoxasan, aorist of doxazo — "they glorified not") is the exact verbal inversion of Rev 14:7 (dote doxan — "give glory"). The Rom 1:18-25 downward spiral (suppress truth → refuse to glorify → exchange glory → idolatry → wrath) maps as a precise inverse of Rev 14:7 (fear God → give glory → worship Creator). This must be verified at the Greek level. 4. Rev 16:9 ("repented not to give him glory") uses dounai auto doxan — the same construction as Rev 14:7's dote auto doxan. This verbal echo creates a structural inclusio around the seal-plague narrative. Must verify exact Greek phrasing. 5. John 17:4's edoxasa/eteliosa pairing establishes completed-work as a mode of glorification — Bengel's claim that "I have finished" EXPLAINS "I have glorified" needs Greek verification. 6. 2 Cor 3:18's beholding-transformation mechanism — the Spirit changes believers "from glory to glory" by sustained contemplation of God's character (EGW: "from character to character"). Must verify katoptrizomai debate and metamorphoumetha parsing. 7. The multiple DIMENSIONS of glory-giving (confession, character, conduct, fruit-bearing, bodily stewardship, beholding-transformation, completed work) need to be systematically verified and organized — this is not one thing but a multifaceted response.
