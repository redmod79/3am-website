1. **The kabod/doxa semantic bridge and the meaning of 'glory':**
This is the foundational linguistic investigation. READ `$WORD_STUDIES/H3519-kabowd.md` and `$WORD_STUDIES/G1391-doxa.md` for the canonical semantic ranges. READ `$WORD_STUDIES/TR-kabod-doxa.md` for the translation bridge showing how the LXX infused doxa (classically 'opinion/reputation') with kabod's meanings ('weight, substance, brightness, character'). Running (Ministry 1963) claims kabod denotes 'the very character or noblest part of man' and when applied to God, 'His character and attributes.' Verify this against Exodus 33:18-19 where Moses asks to see God's glory and God responds by proclaiming His goodness and name, then Exod 34:5-7 where the actual content of the glory proclamation is character attributes. Parse Isa 6:3 ('the whole earth is full of his glory') and Hab 2:14 ('filled with the knowledge of the glory of the LORD'). Run greek_parser.py --verse on Rev 14:7 to establish the exact morphology of dote auto doxan. The 2 Cor 4:17 phrase 'eternal weight of glory' (baros doxes) may preserve the kabod='weight' etymology in Greek dress.

2. **The 'give glory' confession idiom (Josh 7:19 → John 9:24 → Rev 14:7):**
The 'give glory' idiom in Joshua 7:19 functions as a judicial oath formula: Achan is commanded to 'give glory to the LORD' and 'make confession' — the two are equated. READ `$WORD_STUDIES/H3034-yadah.md` for yadah's dual meaning of 'praise/confess.' READ `$WORD_STUDIES/TR-yadah-exomologeo.md` for how this transfers to Greek. The Hebrew construction ten kabod la-YHWH appears also in Mal 2:2 (latheth kabod) with a curse attached for failure.
