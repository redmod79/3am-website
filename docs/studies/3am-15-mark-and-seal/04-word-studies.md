# Word Studies

## Question
What is the charagma (mark, G5480) and what is the sphragis (seal, G4973)? How do they relate to the Deuteronomy 6:6-8 / Exodus 13:9 hand-and-forehead loyalty pattern? How does the Ezekiel 9:4 tav (mark on the forehead of those who sigh and cry) prefigure the seal of God? What is the relationship between God's seal and the Sabbath — does Exodus 31:13,17 and Ezekiel 20:12,20 establish the Sabbath as the seal's content? How does a seal function — identifying who seals (God's name), what authority they have (Creator), and over what domain (heaven and earth) — and which commandment contains all three elements? How does the Spirit-seal connection (Eph 1:13, 4:30) relate to the Sabbath-seal claim? What is the composite argument for the seal being connected to Sabbath observance?

### G5480 — charagma

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G5480-charagma.md`

**Occurrences:** 9

Mark/brand/stamp. 9 NT occurrences (8 in Revelation + Acts 17:29). From charasso (to engrave). All Revelation uses refer to the beast's mark on hand or forehead. Acts 17:29 = graven artwork (idolatry context). Deissmann: technical term for imperial stamps, slave brands, military tattoos. Shares root with charakter (G5481).

**Semantic categories:** engraved artwork, compulsory mark, mark linked to worship, basis for judgment, victory over the mark

**Key verses:** Rev 13:16, Rev 13:17, Rev 14:9, Rev 14:11, Rev 16:2, Rev 19:20, Rev 20:4, Rev 15:2, Acts 17:29

**Relevance:** Core term — the 'mark' of the beast. Always paired with proskyneo (worship) in Revelation. Visible, physical marking contrasted with sphragis which allows invisible interpretation.

### G4973 — sphragis

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G4973-sphragis.md`

**Occurrences:** 16

Seal/signet. 16 NT occurrences (13 in Revelation). Functions: authentication (Rom 4:11, 1 Cor 9:2), concealment (Rev 5:1), protective ownership (Rev 7:2, 9:4), divine ratification (2 Tim 2:19). LXX maps from chowtham (H2368) with PMI score of 10.71.

**Semantic categories:** authentication and certification, divine concealment and authorization, sequential judgment unveiling, divine ownership and eschatological protection

**Key verses:** Rev 7:2, Rev 9:4, Rom 4:11, 1 Cor 9:2, 2 Tim 2:19, Rev 5:1

**Relevance:** Core term — the 'seal' of God. Direct antithesis of charagma. Both placed on forehead, but sphragis = divine ownership/protection vs. charagma = bestial subjugation.

### G4972 — sphragizo

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G4972-sphragizo.md`

**Occurrences:** 28

To seal. 15 distinct verse locations (28 tokens). Functions: physical sealing (Mat 27:66), divine authentication (Jhn 6:27), spiritual sealing by Spirit (Eph 1:13, 4:30, 2 Cor 1:22), eschatological marking (Rev 7:3-8). Passive voice appears exclusively in divine sealing contexts.

**Semantic categories:** physical sealing, divine authentication, spiritual sealing by Spirit, eschatological sealing, concealing revelation

**Key verses:** Eph 1:13, Eph 4:30, 2 Cor 1:22, Rev 7:3, Rev 7:4, Jhn 6:27, Mat 27:66

**Relevance:** Verb form of sealing. The aorist passive in Eph 1:13/4:30 with dative of Spirit is the key grammatical question for whether the Spirit IS the seal or APPLIES the seal.

### G5481 — charakter

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G5481-charaktēr.md`

**Occurrences:** 1

Exact representation/express image. Hapax in NT (Heb 1:3). From charasso (same root as charagma). Christ as charakter of God's hupostasis = exact point-for-point correspondence. Stronger than eikon (derived likeness).

**Semantic categories:** exact representation of divine being

**Key verses:** Heb 1:3

**Relevance:** Shares root charasso with charagma (G5480). Creates theological antithesis: Christ as true charakter (divine imprint) vs. beast's charagma (counterfeit brand).

### H226 — owth

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H226-owth.md`

**Occurrences:** 85

Sign/token/mark. 77 unique verses across 14 books. Semantic categories: covenant sign (Gen 9:12, 17:11, Exo 31:13,17, Ezek 20:12,20), miraculous signs (Exo plagues), confirmatory signs, memorial signs (Exo 13:9,16, Deut 6:8). LXX renders as semeion (G4592) with 94% consistency (PMI score 33.43).

**Semantic categories:** covenant sign/token, miraculous signs, confirmatory/prophetic sign, memorial/reminder sign, celestial signs, judgment/warning sign

**Key verses:** Exo 31:13, Exo 31:17, Ezek 20:12, Ezek 20:20, Gen 17:11, Exo 13:9, Exo 13:16, Deut 6:8

**Relevance:** The word used for the Sabbath as sign (4x). Also used for circumcision (Gen 17:11). Paul calls circumcision-as-owth both semeion AND sphragis (Rom 4:11), bridging sign and seal.

### G4592 — semeion

**Occurrences:** 77

Sign/miracle. The dominant LXX rendering of owth (H226) with 94% consistency. Used alongside sphragis in Rom 4:11 for circumcision, demonstrating that 'sign' and 'seal' can describe the same reality.

**Semantic categories:** miraculous sign, authenticating sign, prophetic sign

**Key verses:** Rom 4:11, Jhn 2:11, Mat 12:38

**Relevance:** Critical for the sign/seal overlap question. Paul uses semeion AND sphragis for circumcision in Rom 4:11 — both words for the same owth, breaking the strict sign/seal separation.

### G3359 — metopon

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G3359-metopon.md`

**Occurrences:** 8

Forehead. 8 NT occurrences, ALL in Revelation. From meta + ops (the face). God's seal location (Rev 7:3, 14:1, 22:4) vs. beast's mark location (Rev 13:16, 14:9) vs. Babylon's name (Rev 17:5). LXX maps from metsach (H4696) with PMI score of 21.99.

**Semantic categories:** location of God's seal/name, location of beast's mark, location of Babylon's identity, criterion of protection/judgment

**Key verses:** Rev 7:3, Rev 9:4, Rev 13:16, Rev 14:1, Rev 14:9, Rev 17:5, Rev 20:4, Rev 22:4

**Relevance:** Exclusively Revelation. God seals forehead ONLY (4 occurrences: 7:3, 9:4, 14:1, 22:4). Beast marks forehead OR hand (3 occurrences: 13:16, 14:9, 20:4). The asymmetry is theologically significant: conviction vs. compliance.

### G4520 — sabbatismos

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G4520-sabbatismos.md`

**Occurrences:** 1

Sabbath-keeping. Hapax legomenon in Heb 4:9. Verbal noun from sabbatizo (to keep the Sabbath). Deliberately distinguished from katapausis (used 8x in Heb 3-4). apoleipetai (present passive indicative) = 'is currently remaining.' Gallos: always means literal Sabbath-keeping in extant literature.

**Semantic categories:** Sabbath-rest as distinct state of repose

**Key verses:** Heb 4:9

**Relevance:** Connects to the 'no rest' irony: beast-worshippers have 'no anapausis' (Rev 14:11) while sabbatismos 'remains' for God's people (Heb 4:9).

### G4151 — pneuma

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G4151-pneuma.md`

**Occurrences:** 385

Spirit/breath/wind. 385 NT occurrences. In sealing contexts (Eph 1:13, 4:30, 2 Cor 1:22), the Spirit appears in the dative case as the means/agent of sealing.

**Semantic categories:** Holy Spirit, human spirit, unclean spirits, wind/breath

**Key verses:** Eph 1:13, Eph 4:30, 2 Cor 1:22

**Relevance:** The sealing agent in the Spirit-seal passages. The grammatical question is whether the dative is instrumental (Spirit = agent who applies) or appositional (Spirit = the seal itself).

### G728 — arrabon

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G728-arrabon.md`

**Occurrences:** 3

Earnest/pledge/down-payment. 3 NT occurrences (2 Cor 1:22, 2 Cor 5:5, Eph 1:14). From Hebrew H6162. The Spirit IS the arrabon in every occurrence. Paired with sphragizo in 2 Cor 1:22 — sealing and earnest are distinguished as two complementary actions.

**Semantic categories:** Spirit as God's pledge of completed redemption

**Key verses:** 2 Cor 1:22, 2 Cor 5:5, Eph 1:14

**Relevance:** In 2 Cor 1:22, sealed (sphragizo) AND given earnest (arrabon) are listed as distinct actions. If the Spirit is the arrabon and the sealing is a separate action, the Spirit is the sealing AGENT, not the seal's CONTENT.

### G4160 — poieo

**Occurrences:** 597

To make/do. Diagnostic verb in Rev 14:7 matching Exo 20:11 LXX. John used ktizo (G2936) for creation in Rev 4:11 and 10:6 but switched to poieo in 14:7 — the one verb matching Exo 20:11 LXX (where asah maps to poieo). This departure from his own pattern signals intentional Sabbath-commandment allusion.

**Semantic categories:** creation activity, general doing/making

**Key verses:** Rev 14:7, Exo 20:11

**Relevance:** The poieo/ktizo switch is the strongest single piece of evidence for Rev 14:7 as a deliberate allusion to the Sabbath commandment.

### H8420 — tav

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H8420-tâv.md`

**Occurrences:** 3

Mark/signature. 3 OT occurrences (Ezek 9:4, Ezek 9:6, Job 31:35). The letter-name used as 'mark' in Ezek 9:4. In rabbinic tradition (Shabbat 55a), the tav is 'the last letter of the seal of the Holy One' because God's seal is emet (truth, alef-mem-tav). Paleo-Hebrew tav resembled X or +.

**Semantic categories:** identifying mark or sign, signature or attestation

**Key verses:** Ezek 9:4, Ezek 9:6, Job 31:35

**Relevance:** The specific mark placed on foreheads in Ezek 9:4 — direct OT background for Revelation's seal. The verb-noun cognate pair (tavah + tav) in Ezek 9:4 emphasizes the marking action.

### H8427 — tavah

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H8427-tavah.md`

To mark out/scratch/imprint. 2 OT occurrences (1 Sam 21:13, Ezek 9:4). In Ezek 9:4, God commands 'set a mark' (hitavita tav) — Hiphil perfect 2ms of tavah + the noun tav. Root of the marking action.

**Semantic categories:** physical marking/scratching, purposeful imprinting

**Key verses:** Ezek 9:4, 1 Sam 21:13

**Relevance:** The verb of marking in Ezek 9:4. The Hiphil stem indicates causative action: 'cause a tav to be placed.' Cognate with tav (H8420).

### G4742 — stigma

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G4742-stigma.md`

**Occurrences:** 1

Mark/brand. Hapax in NT (Gal 6:17). From stizo (to prick). Paul bears the stigmata (marks/scars) of the Lord Jesus. In Greco-Roman world: slave brands, military tattoos, temple devotee marks. Distinct from charagma: stigma = ownership scar from suffering; charagma = engraved/stamped mark of imposed authority.

**Semantic categories:** physical branding/marking, counter-mark to circumcision

**Key verses:** Gal 6:17

**Relevance:** Contrast with charagma. Paul's stigma = voluntary suffering-marks of belonging to Christ. Beast's charagma = imposed brand of allegiance.

### H6942 — qadash

To be holy/sanctify. Appears in the Sabbath-sign formula: 'that ye may know that I am the LORD who sanctifies you' (meqaddishkhem, Piel participle in Exo 31:13 and Ezek 20:12). The Sabbath sign-function is tied to God's sanctifying identity.

**Semantic categories:** sanctification, consecration

**Key verses:** Exo 31:13, Ezek 20:12, Exo 20:8, Exo 20:11

**Relevance:** The sanctification formula in the Sabbath-sign texts connects the Sabbath to God's identity as sanctifier.

### G1849 — exousia

**Occurrences:** 103

Authority/power/right. Used in Revelation for the beast's authority and in seal theology for the Creator's authority over heaven and earth. The fourth commandment identifies God's authority as Creator (he who made heaven and earth), which is the authority-element of a royal seal.

**Semantic categories:** delegated authority, inherent right, jurisdiction

**Key verses:** Rev 13:12, Rev 13:4, Mat 28:18

**Relevance:** Seal authority element: a royal seal identifies the authority of the one who seals. The fourth commandment identifies God's authority as Creator with universal jurisdiction.

### G2696 — katasphragizo

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G2696-katasphragizo.md`

**Occurrences:** 1

To seal completely. Hapax in NT (Rev 5:1). Intensive compound: kata- (intensifying) + sphragizo. The scroll sealed with seven seals. Perfect passive participle (katesphragismenon) = completed, permanent state of being sealed.

**Semantic categories:** intensive/thorough sealing

**Key verses:** Rev 5:1

**Relevance:** Intensive form shows the Revelation sealing vocabulary is nuanced — simple sealing (sphragizo) vs. complete sealing (katasphragizo).
