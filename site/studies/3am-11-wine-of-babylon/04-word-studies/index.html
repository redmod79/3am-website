    Word Studies¶

    Question¶

    What is 'the wine of the wrath of her fornication' (Rev 14:8)? What specific categories of false teaching constitute Babylon's wine? How does the intoxication metaphor work — Jeremiah 51:7, Psalm 75:8, the polarity flip in Rev 14:8-10? What are the bdelygmata (abominations, G946) that fill Babylon's cup (Rev 17:4) — trace bdelygma back to OT towebah (H8441) and shiqquwts (H8251) to identify the categories (idolatry, occult practices, turning from God's law, sun-worship as in Ezek 8:16). What does pharmakeia (G5331, Rev 18:23) add — not just intellectual error but cognitive poison? Does the text identify specific false doctrines, or does it establish categories that can be applied? How does 'all nations have drunk' (Rev 14:8) indicate universal reach?

    G3631 — oinos¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G3631-oinos.md

    Occurrences: 33

    Primary NT wine word. 33 tokens in 25 verses across 9 books. 8 occurrences in Revelation (highest raw count), concentrated in chs. 14-19. Semantic categories: literal beverage, new wine/wineskins, miraculous provision, abstinence/temperance, church office qualification, Babylon's wine/God's wrath. The same word is used for both positive (Cana, Lord's Supper) and negative (Babylon's wine, drunkenness) — no fermented/unfermented binary in the vocabulary itself.

    Semantic categories: literal beverage, parabolic, miraculous provision, temperance, Babylon's wine of fornication, God's wrath

    Key verses: Rev 14:8, Rev 14:10, Rev 16:19, Rev 18:3, Rev 18:13, Eph 5:18, John 2:9

    Relevance: Central metaphor word. Wine symbolizes both true and false doctrine because it is a substance that enters and transforms. Same word for both polarities demolishes Bohr's fermented/unfermented binary.

    G2372 — thumos¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G2372-thumos.md

    Occurrences: 18

    Passion/boiling fury. 18 occurrences, 10 in Revelation (56%). Distinguished from orge as passionate eruption vs. settled judicial anger. In Revelation: divine eschatological fury (14:10,19; 15:1,7; 16:1,19; 19:15), Babylon's corrupting passion (14:8; 18:3), satanic rage (12:12). The thumos chain is the structural skeleton of Revelation's judgment narrative.

    Semantic categories: divine eschatological fury, corrupting passion (Babylon), satanic rage, human mob fury, vice-list anger

    Key verses: Rev 14:8, Rev 14:10, Rev 14:19, Rev 15:1, Rev 16:19, Rev 19:15, Rev 12:12

    Relevance: The connecting term in the genitive chain. Thumos bridges Babylon's wine (14:8) and God's judgment wine (14:10). The polarity flip is encoded in the shift of thumos from Babylon's source to God's source.

    G3709 — orge¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G3709-orge.md

    Occurrences: 36

    Settled judicial wrath. 36 occurrences. Concentrated in Romans (12) and Revelation (6). Distinguished from thumos: orge = deliberate, judicial; thumos = passionate, eruptive. In Revelation, orge appears only in the divine wrath formula (6:16-17; 11:18; 14:10; 16:19; 19:15) — never applied to Babylon.

    Semantic categories: divine eschatological wrath, divine judicial wrath, wrath and the law, human anger (vice lists)

    Key verses: Rev 14:10, Rev 16:19, Rev 19:15, Rom 1:18, Rom 2:5

    Relevance: Absent from Babylon's genitive chain but present in God's — the terminal genitive tes orges autou (16:19) replaces tes porneias autes (14:8). The addition of orge to thumos in God's cup signals judicial authority behind the passionate intensity.

    G946 — bdelygma¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G946-bdelygma.md

    Occurrences: 6

    Abomination/detestation. 6 in NT across 4 books. Root = bdeo (to stink). LXX translates both towebah (H8441) and shiqquwts (H8251), inheriting the full OT range: idolatry, sexual transgression, sorcery, commercial dishonesty, ethical violations, hypocritical worship, dietary prohibitions. In Revelation: harlot's cup contents (17:4), harlot's title (17:5), exclusion from New Jerusalem (21:27).

    Semantic categories: abomination of desolation, idolatrous cup contents, divine ethical reversal, eschatological exclusion

    Key verses: Rev 17:4, Rev 17:5, Rev 21:27, Mat 24:15, Luke 16:15

    Relevance: The LXX compression is the key: bdelygma collapses the entire OT abomination vocabulary into one term. Rev 17:4's 'full of bdelygmata' means the cup contains the full range of what God calls abominable — not just idols but all eight towebah categories.

    H8441 — towebah¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H8441-towebah.md

    Occurrences: 127

    Broadest OT abomination term. 112 unique verses, 127 tokens across 14 books. Concentrated in Ezekiel (41), Proverbs (20), Deuteronomy (16). Eight categories: (1) idolatry/foreign gods, (2) sexual transgressions, (3) sorcery/divination/necromancy, (4) commercial dishonesty, (5) ethical/character violations, (6) hypocritical worship, (7) dietary prohibitions, (8) violations of divine distinctions. Covers the full range of what God calls abominable.

    Semantic categories: cultural taboo, idolatry, sexual transgression, dietary/cultic, commercial dishonesty, character/disposition, covenant apostasy, child sacrifice

    Key verses: Deu 7:25, Deu 12:31, Deu 18:9-12, Pro 6:16-19, Ezek 8:6-17

    Relevance: The OT source for bdelygma. The eight categories define what fills Babylon's cup. Each category maps to a type of false teaching that Babylon promotes.

    H8251 — shiqquwts¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H8251-shiqquwts.md

    Occurrences: 29

    Narrower abomination term, exclusively idolatrous. 29 occurrences concentrated in prophetic literature (Ezekiel 8, Jeremiah 5, Daniel 3). Used as title for foreign deities (1 Ki 11:5,7; 2 Ki 23:13), for idolatrous objects defiling sacred space (Jer 7:30; 32:34), and for the eschatological 'abomination of desolation' (Dan 9:27; 11:31; 12:11).

    Semantic categories: title for foreign deities, idolatrous objects defiling sacred space, abomination of desolation, divine disgust metaphor

    Key verses: Dan 9:27, Dan 11:31, Dan 12:11, 1 Ki 11:5, Jer 7:30

    Relevance: Narrower than towebah — exclusively focused on idolatrous sacred-space violation. Contributes the 'defilement of holy space' dimension to bdelygma. When foreign worship enters God's sanctuary, it is shiqquwts.

    G5331 — pharmakeia¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G5331-pharmakeia.md

    Occurrences: 3

    Sorcery/drug-deception. 3 in NT (Gal 5:20, Rev 9:21, Rev 18:23). Root = pharmakon (drug/spell-giving potion). Louw-Nida classifies under Religious Activities (53.10), not medicine. In Galatians: listed immediately after eidololatreia (idolatry). In Revelation: the mechanism by which Babylon deceives all nations (18:23, dative instrumental). LXX uses pharmakeia in Isa 47:9,12 for historical Babylon's sorcery — direct allusion.

    Semantic categories: vice/work of flesh, apocalyptic deception, impenitence

    Key verses: Rev 18:23, Gal 5:20, Rev 9:21

    Relevance: Adds the cognitive-impairment dimension to Babylon's wine. Not just wrong ideas but ideas that disable the capacity to think clearly. The drug/sorcery etymology preserves the substance-based alteration metaphor.

    G4202 — porneia¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G4202-porneia.md

    Occurrences: 25

    Fornication/spiritual adultery. 25 unique verses, 26 tokens across 12 books. Highest in Revelation (7, 1.7/100v). Semantic range: divorce exception (Mat), vice lists, sexual ethics, Jerusalem Council decree, spiritual adultery of Babylon. Root from pernemi (to sell) — commercial exchange dimension.

    Semantic categories: divorce exception, vice list, sexual ethics, Jerusalem Council, spiritual adultery, Babylon's unfaithfulness

    Key verses: Rev 14:8, Rev 17:2, Rev 17:4, Rev 18:3, 1 Cor 6:18, Acts 15:20

    Relevance: Terminal genitive in Babylon's chain. porneia requires prior covenant relationship (Walvoord's principle, confirmed in 3am-10). This means the wine = teachings that break the covenant. The genitive 'tes porneias autes' means the wine's contents ARE covenant-breaking teachings.

    G194 — akratos¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G194-akratos.md

    Occurrences: 1

    Hapax legomenon. Unmixed/undiluted wine. Alpha-privative + kerannymi (to mix). In Rev 14:10, qualifies the wine of God's wrath as 'without mixture' — judgment at full strength. Greco-Roman wine was always diluted with water; drinking it akratos was considered barbaric. Applied to divine wrath: mercy-dilution withdrawn.

    Semantic categories: undiluted wine of judgment

    Key verses: Rev 14:10

    Relevance: The reversal marker. Babylon's wine was mixed (seductive, palatable). God's judgment wine is unmixed — no mercy-dilution. When God returns the cup, He removes the tempering element.

    H3196 — yayin¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H3196-yayin.md

    Occurrences: 151

    Primary Hebrew wine word. 134 unique verses, 151 tokens across 31 books. From root meaning to effervesce/ferment. Used for drink offerings (Exo 29:40), Nazirite prohibition (Num 6:3), Melchizedek's gift (Gen 14:18), figurative drunkenness/judgment (Jer 51:7; Psa 75:8), and literal intoxication warnings (Pro 23:29-32). Same word for positive and negative uses.

    Semantic categories: cultic drink offering, Nazirite prohibition, covenant celebration, figurative judgment, intoxication warning, agricultural product

    Key verses: Jer 51:7, Psa 75:8, Hos 4:11, Gen 14:18, Isa 28:7

    Relevance: Hebrew equivalent of oinos. Same word (yayin) serves for both the positive covenant cup and the negative judgment cup — same as oinos in Revelation. This demolishes the fermented=bad/unfermented=good binary.

    H2534 — chemah¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H2534-chemah.md

    Occurrences: 133

    Fury/wrath. 117 unique verses, 133 tokens across 20 books. From yacham (to be hot). Concentrated in Ezekiel (30), Jeremiah (16), Isaiah (12). Maps to thumos (G2372) via LXX bridge. Used for divine wrath poured out, the cup/wine of fury (Isa 51:17; Jer 25:15), burning heat metaphor, and human anger.

    Semantic categories: divine wrath poured out, cup of fury, burning heat, serpent venom, human anger

    Key verses: Isa 51:17, Isa 51:22, Jer 25:15, Isa 63:6

    Relevance: Hebrew equivalent of thumos. chemah-to-thumos LXX bridge (H2534→G2372) carries the cup-of-wrath vocabulary from OT into NT. Jer 25:15's 'wine cup of this fury' uses chemah — same word mapped to thumos in Rev 14:8.

    H3563 — kowc¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H3563-kowc.md

    Occurrences: 38

    Cup. 29 unique verses, 38 tokens across 13 books. Semantic range: literal vessel (Gen 40), architectural form (1 Ki 7:26), cup as portion/destiny (Psa 16:5; 23:5; 116:13), cup of divine wrath/judgment (Psa 75:8; Isa 51:17; Jer 25:15; Jer 51:7). Highest density in prophets (Jeremiah 7x) where the cup-of-wrath imagery dominates.

    Semantic categories: literal vessel, architectural form, portion/destiny, divine wrath/judgment

    Key verses: Psa 75:8, Isa 51:17, Jer 25:15, Jer 51:7, Psa 116:13, Psa 23:5

    Relevance: OT cup vocabulary. The cup is a transferable judicial object — it can be passed from hand to hand. This establishes the framework for the polarity flip in Revelation.

    G4221 — poterion¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G4221-poterion.md

    Occurrences: 33

    Cup. 30 unique verses, 33 tokens across 6 books. NT equivalent of kowc. Three settings: (1) Jesus teaching/Passion (Gospels), (2) Lord's Supper (1 Cor), (3) apocalyptic judgment (Revelation). 1 Corinthians has highest density (6x) due to Lord's Supper discourse. 1 Cor 10:21 establishes the two-cup mutual exclusion: cup of Lord vs. cup of demons.

    Semantic categories: literal vessel, ritual cleansing object, cup of suffering, Lord's Supper, two-cup mutual exclusion, apocalyptic judgment

    Key verses: 1 Cor 10:16, 1 Cor 10:21, Rev 14:10, Rev 16:19, Rev 17:4, Mat 26:39

    Relevance: NT cup vocabulary. The two-cup framework (1 Cor 10) provides the theological structure: drinking from one cup = communion with its source. Babylon's cup creates communion with the false system, just as the Lord's cup creates communion with Christ.

    H3948 — leqach¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H3948-leqach.md

    Occurrences: 9

    Doctrine/teaching. 9 unique verses, 10 tokens across 4 books. From laqach (H3947, to take/receive). Concentrated in Proverbs (6). Semantic categories: teaching as substance given/received, learning as capacity increased, persuasion/fair speech (negative, Pro 7:21), wisdom communicated through lips.

    Semantic categories: teaching as transferable substance, learning capacity, persuasion/fair speech, wisdom on lips

    Key verses: Deu 32:2, Pro 4:2, Pro 7:21, Isa 29:24

    Relevance: CRUCIAL root connection: laqach (to take) → leqach (doctrine = what is taken in). Wine 'takes' the heart (Hos 4:11, laqach); doctrine IS 'what is taken in' (leqach). This etymological link makes wine the natural symbol for doctrine — both are entering substances that transform.

    G3182 — methysko¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G3182-methysko.md

    Occurrences: 3

    To intoxicate (causative/inchoative). 3 in NT (Luke 12:45, Eph 5:18, 1 Thess 5:7). Distinguished from methyo (G3184, stative — to be drunk): methysko describes the PROCESS of getting drunk or of MAKING someone drunk. The -sko suffix marks causative/inchoative stem.

    Semantic categories: ethical warning, Spirit-filling contrast, eschatological exhortation

    Key verses: Eph 5:18, Luke 12:45, 1 Thess 5:7

    Relevance: The transitive/causative dimension is key: Babylon MAKES nations drunk (methysko-type action). The intoxication is not self-induced — it is administered.

    G3525 — nepho¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G3525-nepho.md

    Occurrences: 6

    To be sober/watchful. 6 unique verses, 7 tokens across 3 books (1 Peter 3, 1 Thessalonians 2, 2 Timothy 1). Louw-Nida dual domain: self-control (88.011) + thought (30.001). Figurative sense dominates: mental/moral sobriety, clear-headed alertness for the Lord's return.

    Semantic categories: eschatological watchfulness, hope-anchored mental preparation, prayer-linked sobriety

    Key verses: 1 Thess 5:6, 1 Thess 5:8, 1 Pet 4:7, 1 Pet 1:13, 1 Pet 5:8

    Relevance: The antidote to Babylon's wine. If Babylon intoxicates, the remedy is sobriety (nepho). All occurrences are eschatological — sobriety is demanded specifically in the end-time context when Babylon's wine is most potent.

    H7937 — shakar¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H7937-shakar.md

    Occurrences: 19

    To be/make drunk. 19 unique verses, 21 tokens across 11 books. Verb stems: Qal (9, become drunk), Piel (4, make drunk), Hiphil (4, cause to be drunk), Hithpael (1, act drunken). Concentrated in prophets (11/19 = 58%). Jeremiah carries 5 — all in judgment oracles against nations.

    Semantic categories: literal intoxication, prophetic judgment imagery, celebratory satiation, figurative drunkenness

    Key verses: Jer 51:7, Jer 25:27, Isa 63:6, Gen 9:21

    Relevance: Hebrew intoxication verb. Jer 51:7 uses Piel participle (meshakkeret) — Babylon is the agent MAKING others drunk. The causative stem matches Revelation's pepotiken — Babylon actively intoxicates.

    H8263 — sheqets¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H8263-sheqets.md

    Occurrences: 11

    Detestable thing. 11 in OT across 3 books. Almost exclusively Leviticus (9/11 = 82%). Applied to creatures in dietary/purity laws — aquatic creatures without fins/scales, prohibited birds, swarming things. The concrete classification label vs. towebah's moral category.

    Semantic categories: aquatic creatures, prohibited birds, swarming things

    Key verses: Lev 11:10-12, Lev 11:13, Ezek 8:10

    Relevance: Part of the four-term abomination system. Contributes the creature-classification dimension. Ezek 8:10's 'abominable beasts portrayed on the wall' uses sheqets imagery — connecting dietary/creature categories to idolatrous worship.

    H6292 — piggul¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H6292-piggul.md

    Occurrences: 4

    Polluted/expired sacrifice. 4 in OT (Lev 7:18; 19:7; Isa 65:4; Ezek 4:14). From root meaning 'to stink.' Technical sacrificial term for flesh kept beyond permitted time. The narrowest abomination term — entirely confined to priestly/ritual contexts.

    Semantic categories: expired sacrifice, ritual putrefaction

    Key verses: Lev 7:18, Lev 19:7, Isa 65:4, Ezek 4:14

    Relevance: Narrowest of the four abomination terms. Represents corrupted worship — offerings that should have been sacred but have become putrid through violation of God's prescribed timing. Maps to Babylon's corruption of legitimate religious forms.

    G2767 — kerannymi¶

    Occurrences: 3

    To mix/fill wine. 3 occurrences in NT. Appears in Rev 14:10 (kekerasmenou, Perf Pass Ptcp GSM — the wine 'having been mixed/prepared'), Rev 18:6 (twice — ekerasan 'she mixed' and kerasate 'mix for her double'). The word akratos (G194) is the alpha-privative of this verb: a-kratos = un-mixed. The paradox in Rev 14:10: the cup is both kekerasmenou (prepared/mixed) and akratos (undiluted).

    Semantic categories: wine preparation, judgment cup filling, lex talionis

    Key verses: Rev 14:10, Rev 18:6

    Relevance: The verb behind the cup-filling imagery. Rev 18:6 closes the transfer cycle: 'in the cup which she hath filled (ekerasan), fill (kerasate) to her double.' Same verb, same action, reversed direction.

    G366 — ananepho¶

    Occurrences: 1

    To sober up again. 1 occurrence in NT (2 Tim 2:26). Compound: ana- (again) + nepho (to be sober). KJV: 'they may recover themselves.' The prefix ana- implies returning to a prior state of sobriety. If the remedy for doctrinal captivity is 'sobering up again,' then the captivity itself was intoxication.

    Semantic categories: recovery from doctrinal captivity

    Key verses: 2 Tim 2:26

    Relevance: Directly supports the intoxication metaphor for false doctrine. Paul uses wine/sobriety vocabulary to describe escape from the devil's snare — confirming that doctrinal deception operates like intoxication.

    G4222 — potizo¶

    Occurrences: 23

    To give to drink. 23 occurrences in NT. Rev 14:8 uses pepotiken (Perf Act Ind 3S) — Babylon 'has made to drink.' The perfect tense indicates completed action with ongoing results: the nations have been given the wine and remain under its influence.

    Semantic categories: literal giving drink, agricultural watering, figurative intoxication, Spirit-baptism

    Key verses: Rev 14:8, 1 Cor 3:2, 1 Cor 12:13, Mat 25:35

    Relevance: The verb in Rev 14:8's crux phrase. pepotiken = she HAS MADE to drink (perfect tense — completed with ongoing results). Babylon actively administers the wine; the nations are the recipients.

    G169 — akathartos¶

    Occurrences: 30

    Unclean/impure. 30 occurrences in NT. In Rev 17:4: ta akatharta tes porneias autes — 'the unclean things of her fornication' — ACCUSATIVE case, contrasting with bdelygmaton in GENITIVE. Used throughout NT for unclean spirits (majority of uses) and ritual/moral impurity.

    Semantic categories: unclean spirits, ritual impurity, moral impurity, concrete cup contents

    Key verses: Rev 17:4, Rev 18:2, Rev 16:13, Mat 10:1

    Relevance: Paired with bdelygma in Rev 17:4. Alford's observation: bdelygmata (genitive, abstract categories) vs. akatharta (accusative, concrete impurities). The case shift distinguishes what the abominations ARE (categories) from what they PRODUCE (specific unclean things).
