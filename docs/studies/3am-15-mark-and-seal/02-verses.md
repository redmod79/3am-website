# Verses

## Question
What is the charagma (mark, G5480) and what is the sphragis (seal, G4973)? How do they relate to the Deuteronomy 6:6-8 / Exodus 13:9 hand-and-forehead loyalty pattern? How does the Ezekiel 9:4 tav (mark on the forehead of those who sigh and cry) prefigure the seal of God? What is the relationship between God's seal and the Sabbath — does Exodus 31:13,17 and Ezekiel 20:12,20 establish the Sabbath as the seal's content? How does a seal function — identifying who seals (God's name), what authority they have (Creator), and over what domain (heaven and earth) — and which commandment contains all three elements? How does the Spirit-seal connection (Eph 1:13, 4:30) relate to the Sabbath-seal claim? What is the composite argument for the seal being connected to Sabbath observance?

### Angel with seal of the living God — sealing on foreheads of servants

> And I saw another angel ascending from the east, having the seal of the living God: and he cried with a loud voice to the four angels, to whom it was given to hurt the earth and the sea, Saying, Hurt not the earth, neither the sea, nor the trees, till we have sealed the servants of our God in their foreheads.

**Context:** Between the sixth and seventh seals. Four angels hold back the four winds of destruction. A fifth angel ascends from the east with the seal of the living God (sphragida theou zontos) and commands the delay of judgment until God's servants are sealed on their foreheads. This is the direct NT counterpart to Ezekiel 9:4.

**Notes:** Key terms: sphragis (G4973, seal) + sphragizo (G4972, to seal) + metopon (G3359, forehead). The seal is described as belonging to 'the living God' (theou zontos). Sealing is exclusively on foreheads — no hand option. The angel ascends from the east (sunrise direction), possibly contrasting with the sun worship of Ezek 8:16.

Three-part identification: mark OR name OR number.

### 144,000 with Father's name on foreheads

> And I looked, and, lo, a Lamb stood on the mount Sion, and with him an hundred forty and four thousand, having his Father's name written in their foreheads.

**Context:** Following the beast-mark passage of Rev 13. The 144,000 stand with the Lamb on Mount Zion. They have the Father's name (onoma) written on their foreheads — the content of God's seal is his NAME.

**Notes:** The Father's name (to onoma tou patros autou) is the content of the seal on the foreheads. This identifies what sphragis theou zontos (Rev 7:2) actually signifies — the name of God. The question then becomes: which commandment contains God's full name with identifying title and domain?

### Sabbath as sign (owth) between God and Israel — sanctification formula

> Speak thou also unto the children of Israel, saying, Verily my sabbaths ye shall keep: for it is a sign between me and you throughout your generations; that ye may know that I am the LORD that doth sanctify you.

**Context:** Immediately after the tabernacle construction instructions. God commands Moses to tell Israel that the Sabbath is a sign (owth) between God and them. The purpose clause: 'that ye may know that I am the LORD that doth sanctify you.'

**Notes:** Hebrew confirmed: owth (H226, sign) + beyniy ubeyneykhem (between me and you) + ledorotheykhem (throughout your generations) + ladaat ki ani YHWH meqaddishkhem (to know that I am YHWH who sanctifies you). The Sabbath is explicitly called owth — sign. The sanctification formula (meqaddishkhem, Piel participle of qadash) connects the Sabbath to God's sanctifying identity.

> Wherefore the children of Israel shall keep the sabbath, to observe the sabbath throughout their generations, for a perpetual covenant. **Context:** Continuation of the Sabbath-sign passage. The Sabbath is called a perpetual covenant (berit olam) and a sign forever (owth le-olam). Creation rationale: 'in six days the LORD made heaven and earth.'

**Notes:** Hebrew confirmed: berit olam (perpetual covenant) in v.16 + owth hi le-olam (sign she/it forever) in v.17. The creation rationale uses asah (make, H6213) YHWH + shamayim (heavens) + erets (earth) — the same vocabulary echoed in Rev 14:7 (poieo + ouranos + ge). The Sabbath's sign-status is explicitly tied to creation identity.

### Heart FIRST, then hand and forehead — the OT loyalty-sign pattern

> And these words, which I command thee this day, shall be in thine heart: And thou shalt teach them diligently unto thy children, and shalt talk of them when thou sittest in thine house, and when thou walkest by the way, and when thou liest down, and when thou risest up. And thou shalt bind them for a sign upon thine hand, and they shall be as frontlets between thine eyes.

**Context:** Following the Shema (Deut 6:4-5). The commands are first internalized in the heart (v.6), then externalized as a sign on the hand (owth al yadekha) and frontlets between the eyes (totaphoth beyn eynekha).

**Notes:** Hebrew confirmed: owth (H226, sign) + al yadekha (upon your hand) + totaphoth (H2903, frontlets) + beyn eynekha (between your eyes = forehead). CRITICAL: the sequence is heart FIRST (v.6), then hand and forehead (v.8). God requires internalization before externalization. Revelation inverts this for the beast: the beast accepts hand OR forehead compliance without requiring heart conviction.

**Context:** Instructions regarding the Feast of Unleavened Bread. The observance is a sign (owth) on the hand and a memorial (zikkaron) between the eyes.

**Notes:** Earliest hand-forehead pattern in Scripture. owth (sign) + al yadkha (upon your hand) + zikkaron (memorial) + beyn eynekha (between your eyes). This establishes the loyalty-pattern that Deut 6:8, Deut 11:18, and Exo 13:16 all repeat. Revelation's forehead-and-hand imagery draws from this OT template.

### Sealed with Holy Spirit of promise — Spirit-seal text

> In whom ye also trusted, after that ye heard the word of truth, the gospel of your salvation: in whom also after that ye believed, ye were sealed with that holy Spirit of promise, Which is the earnest of our inheritance until the redemption of the purchased possession, unto the praise of his glory.

**Context:** Paul's doxological opening of Ephesians. After hearing and believing, believers were sealed (esphragisthete, aorist passive) with (to pneumati to hagio, dative) the Holy Spirit of promise.

**Notes:** CRITICAL GRAMMATICAL QUESTION: esphragisthete (V-API-2P, aorist passive indicative 2nd person plural of sphragizo) + to Pneumati (T-DSN + N-DSN, dative singular neuter) + tes epangelias (T-GSF + N-GSF, genitive of description) + to Hagio (T-DSN + A-DSN, dative agreeing with Pneumati). The dative to Pneumati to Hagio can be read as: (a) INSTRUMENTAL dative — 'sealed BY/WITH the Spirit' (Spirit = agent who applies the seal), or (b) CONTENT/APPOSITIONAL dative — 'sealed, namely the Spirit' (Spirit = the seal itself). The sequence akousantes...pisteusantes...esphragisthete (hearing → believing → being sealed) shows sealing follows faith. Verse 14 identifies the Spirit as the arrabon (earnest/down-payment) of the inheritance — a distinct metaphor from sealing, suggesting the Spirit functions as both seal-agent and earnest-payment.

### Sealed unto the day of redemption by the Holy Spirit

> And grieve not the holy Spirit of God, whereby ye are sealed unto the day of redemption.

**Context:** Practical exhortation section. Paul warns against grieving the Holy Spirit by whom believers are sealed.

**Notes:** en ho esphragisthete (in/by whom you were sealed) — the relative pronoun ho (dative neuter singular) refers back to to Pneuma to Hagion tou Theou (the Holy Spirit of God). The preposition en + dative = instrument/means ('by which'). Temporal marker: eis hemeran apolutroseos (unto the day of redemption) — the seal holds until consummation. This supports the Spirit as sealing AGENT rather than the seal itself.

### God sealed us and gave the earnest of the Spirit — threefold distinction

> Who hath also sealed us, and given the earnest of the Spirit in our hearts.

**Context:** Paul affirms God's faithfulness. Three actions are attributed to God in vv.21-22: (1) established/stablished us (bebaiōn), (2) anointed us (chrisas), (3) sealed us (sphragisamenos) and gave the earnest (arrabon) of the Spirit.

**Notes:** sphragisamenos (aorist middle participle of sphragizo) + dous (aorist active participle of didomi, 'having given') + ton arrabona tou Pneumatos (the earnest of the Spirit). The kai...kai (both...and) structure distinguishes sealing FROM giving the earnest. The earnest (arrabon) is the Spirit given in our hearts. If sealing and the Spirit-as-earnest are listed as distinct actions, then the seal is not simply the Spirit — the Spirit is the agent/means, and the arrabon is a separate metaphor for the Spirit's guaranteeing function.

### Fourth commandment — the only commandment with name, title, and domain

> Remember the sabbath day, to keep it holy. Six days shalt thou labour, and do all thy work: But the seventh day is the sabbath of the LORD thy God: in it thou shalt not do any work, thou, nor thy son, nor thy daughter, thy manservant, nor thy maidservant, nor thy cattle, nor thy stranger that is within thy gates: For in six days the LORD made heaven and earth, the sea, and all that in them is, and rested the seventh day: wherefore the LORD blessed the sabbath day, and hallowed it.

**Context:** The fourth commandment in the Decalogue. Contains three seal elements: (1) NAME — 'the LORD thy God' (YHWH Elohekha), (2) TITLE — 'who made' (asah, Creator), (3) DOMAIN — 'heaven and earth, the sea, and all that in them is.'

**Notes:** Hebrew confirmed: YHWH (name) + asah (Qal perfect 3ms, 'made' = Creator title) + ha-shamayim (the heavens) + ve-et-ha-arets (and the earth) + et-ha-yam (the sea) + ve-et-kol-asher-bam (and all that in them). The verb asah (H6213) maps to poieo (G4160) in the LXX — the same verb used in Rev 14:7. No other commandment contains all three elements: (1) Commandments 1-3 reference God but don't identify his creative power with universal domain; (2) Commandments 5-10 don't identify the lawgiver at all. The 'LORD thy God' (YHWH Elohekha) appears in commandments 1, 2, 3, 4, and 5, but ONLY the fourth adds what YHWH DID (made heaven and earth) and what his jurisdiction COVERS (heaven, earth, sea). Smith's claim that 'the fourth commandment uniquely contains God's name' is imprecise — it uniquely contains name + creative title + universal domain TOGETHER.

### Circumcision as sign (semeion) AND seal (sphragis) — test case for sign/seal overlap

> And he received the sign of circumcision, a seal of the righteousness of the faith which he had yet being uncircumcised: that he might be the father of all them that believe, though they be not circumcised; that righteousness might be imputed unto them also:

**Context:** Paul's argument about Abraham's justification by faith. Abraham received circumcision as both semeion (sign) and sphragis (seal) of his existing faith-righteousness.

**Notes:** Greek confirmed: semeion (G4592, N-ASN, sign) + elaben (received) + peritomes (of circumcision, genitive) + sphragida (G4973, N-ASF, seal) + tes dikaiosynes (of the righteousness) + tes pisteos (of the faith). Paul uses BOTH semeion AND sphragis for the same thing: circumcision. In Hebrew, circumcision is called owth (Gen 17:11). Therefore: if circumcision as owth = both semeion and sphragis, the strict sign/seal vocabulary separation claimed by Life Assurance Ministries collapses at the FUNCTIONAL level. The translation-level claim (owth is never translated sphragis) may be technically correct, but Paul demonstrates that the same reality can be BOTH sign and seal.

### Circumcision as owth (sign) of the covenant

> And ye shall circumcise the flesh of your foreskin; and it shall be a token of the covenant betwixt me and you.

**Context:** God institutes circumcision as the covenant sign with Abraham. The word for 'token' is owth (H226) — the same word used for the Sabbath in Exo 31:13,17 and Ezek 20:12,20.

**Notes:** The owth vocabulary is shared between circumcision (Gen 17:11) and Sabbath (Exo 31:13,17; Ezek 20:12,20). Paul takes this circumcision-as-owth and calls it both semeion and sphragis in Rom 4:11. ### Sabbatismos remaining for the people of God — Sabbath rest as ongoing obligation

> There remaineth therefore a rest to the people of God.

**Context:** Climax of the Heb 3-4 rest argument. The author switches from katapausis (used 8x in Heb 3-4) to sabbatismos (hapax) at the theological climax.

- τῷ (G2992) — N-DSM — Dat Sg M (for the people)
- τοῦ (G2316) — N-GSM — Gen Sg M (of God)

**Notes:** Greek confirmed: apoleipetai (V-PPI-3S, present passive indicative, 'is [currently] remaining/left behind') + sabbatismos (G4520, N-NSM, sabbath-keeping). The present tense of apoleipetai means 'is currently remaining' — not past, not future, but ongoing. sabbatismos is a verbal noun from sabbatizo ('to keep the Sabbath'), not a synonym for katapausis. The author deliberately switched vocabulary at the climax to invoke the Sabbath institution specifically. Gallos (Andrews University): sabbatismos always means literal Sabbath-keeping in extant literature.

### Ark of the testament seen in temple

> And the temple of God was opened in heaven, and there was seen in his temple the ark of his testament: and there were lightnings, and voices, and thunderings, and an earthquake, and great hail.

**Context:** Seventh trumpet. The ark containing the Ten Commandments is revealed.

**Notes:** Inclusio bracket with commandment-keepers (12:17, 14:12).

---

### Remnant keep commandments of God

> And the dragon was wroth with the woman, and went to make war with the remnant of her seed, which keep the commandments of God, and have the testimony of Jesus Christ.

**Context:** Dragon's war against the remnant identified by commandment-keeping.

**Notes:** Same dual identification as Rev 14:12.

---

### First three commandments - none contain all three seal elements

> Thou shalt have no other gods before me. Thou shalt not make unto thee any graven image, or any likeness of any thing that is in heaven above, or that is in the earth beneath, or that is in the water under the earth: Thou shalt not bow down thyself to them, nor serve them: for I the LORD thy God am a jealous God, visiting the iniquity of the fathers upon the children unto the third and fourth generation of them that hate me; And shewing mercy unto thousands of them that love me, and keep my commandments. Thou shalt not take the name of the LORD thy God in vain; for the LORD will not hold him guiltless that taketh his name in vain.

**Context:** First three commandments reference God but lack creation-identification with universal domain.

**Notes:** Contain 'the LORD thy God' but no creation title or universal domain.

---

### Last six commandments - none contain all three seal elements

> Honour thy father and thy mother: that thy days may be long upon the land which the LORD thy God giveth thee. Thou shalt not kill. Thou shalt not commit adultery. Thou shalt not steal. Thou shalt not bear false witness against thy neighbour. Thou shalt not covet thy neighbour's house, thou shalt not covet thy neighbour's wife, nor his manservant, nor his maidservant, nor his ox, nor his ass, nor any thing that is thy neighbour's.

**Context:** Last six commandments. Only the fifth mentions God at all.

**Notes:** Commandments 6-10 contain no divine identification. None contain name + title + domain.

### New covenant law in hearts and minds

> But this shall be the covenant that I will make with the house of Israel; After those days, saith the LORD, I will put my law in their inward parts, and write it in their hearts; and will be their God, and they shall be my people.

**Context:** Jeremiah's new covenant prophecy. Source for Heb 10:16.

**Notes:** Law written in hearts/minds supports forehead = mind interpretation.

### God the Father sealed the Son

> Labour not for the meat which perisheth, but for that meat which endureth unto everlasting life, which the Son of man shall give unto you: for him hath God the Father sealed.

**Context:** Jesus declares God the Father has sealed (esphragisen) him.

**Notes:** Sphragizo for divine authentication of Christ's mission.

---

### Royal seal - irrevocability

> Write ye also for the Jews, as it liketh you, in the king's name, and seal it with the king's ring: for the writing which is written in the king's name, and sealed with the king's ring, may no man reverse.

**Context:** What is sealed with the king's ring cannot be reversed.

**Notes:** Seal function: king's name + seal = irrevocable authority.

---

### Jezebel sealed letters with Ahab's seal

> So she wrote letters in Ahab's name, and sealed them with his seal, and sent the letters unto the elders and to the nobles that were in his city, dwelling with Naboth.

**Context:** Seal as authority transfer.

**Notes:** The signet carries the identity and authority of its owner.

---

### Stone sealed with king's signet

> And a stone was brought, and laid upon the mouth of the den; and the king sealed it with his own signet, and with the signet of his lords; that the purpose might not be changed concerning Daniel.

**Context:** Lions' den. Seal ensures the purpose cannot be changed.

**Notes:** Seal as authentication and irrevocability.

### Pharaoh's ring given to Joseph

> And Pharaoh took off his ring from his hand, and put it upon Joseph's hand, and arrayed him in vestures of fine linen, and put a gold chain about his neck;

**Context:** Pharaoh delegates authority to Joseph via his signet ring.

**Notes:** Signet ring transfers full authorization.

### Tomb sealed

> So they went, and made the sepulchre sure, sealing the stone, and setting a watch.

**Context:** Physical sealing of Jesus' tomb.

**Notes:** Physical sealing as securing against tampering. Same verb sphragizo.

### Book sealed with seven seals

> And I saw in the right hand of him that sat on the throne a book written within and on the backside, sealed with seven seals.

**Context:** The scroll sealed with seven seals. Only the Lamb is worthy to open.

**Notes:** Uses katasphragizo (G2696) - intensive sealing. Seven seals = thoroughness.

### Paul bears stigmata of the Lord Jesus

> From henceforth let no man trouble me: for I bear in my body the marks of the Lord Jesus.

**Context:** Paul's persecution scars as marks (stigma G4742) of belonging to Christ.

**Notes:** Stigma not charagma. Voluntary suffering-scars vs imposed brands.

### God blessed and sanctified the seventh day

> And on the seventh day God ended his work which he had made; and he rested on the seventh day from all his work which he had made. And God blessed the seventh day, and sanctified it: because that in it he had rested from all his work which God created and made.

**Context:** Creation account. Pre-Sinai Sabbath institution.

**Notes:** Three divine acts: rested (shabat), blessed (barak), sanctified (qadash). Pre-Israel institution.

---

### Sabbath commandment with redemption rationale

> Keep the sabbath day to sanctify it, as the LORD thy God hath commanded thee. Six days thou shalt labour, and do all thy work: But the seventh day is the sabbath of the LORD thy God: in it thou shalt not do any work, thou, nor thy son, nor thy daughter, nor thy manservant, nor thy maidservant, nor thine ox, nor thine ass, nor any of thy cattle, nor thy stranger that is within thy gates; that thy manservant and thy maidservant may rest as well as thou. And remember that thou wast a servant in the land of Egypt, and that the LORD thy God brought thee out thence through a mighty hand and by a stretched out arm: therefore the LORD thy God commanded thee to keep the sabbath day.

**Context:** Deuteronomy version adds redemption rationale to the creation rationale of Exo 20:11.

**Notes:** Complementary to Exo 20:8-11 creation rationale.

### God made known his holy sabbath

> Thou camest down also upon mount Sinai, and spakest with them from heaven, and gavest them right judgments, and true laws, good statutes and commandments: And madest known unto them thy holy sabbath, and commandedst them precepts, statutes, and laws, by the hand of Moses thy servant:

**Context:** Levites' prayer. The Sabbath reveals God's identity.

**Notes:** The Sabbath is 'made known' - it reveals God's character.

### Sabbath-keeping by foreigners and eunuchs

> Blessed is the man that doeth this, and the son of man that layeth hold on it; that keepeth the sabbath from polluting it, and keepeth his hand from doing any evil. For thus saith the LORD unto the eunuchs that keep my sabbaths, and choose the things that please me, and take hold of my covenant; Even unto them will I give in mine house and within my walls a place and a name better than of sons and of daughters: I will give them an everlasting name, that shall not be cut off. Also the sons of the stranger, that join themselves to the LORD, to serve him, and to love the name of the LORD, to be his servants, every one that keepeth the sabbath from polluting it, and taketh hold of my covenant; Even them will I bring to my holy mountain, and make them joyful in my house of prayer.

**Context:** Isaiah extends Sabbath-keeping beyond ethnic Israel to foreigners and eunuchs.

**Notes:** Universal scope of Sabbath beyond Israel.

### All flesh worship from Sabbath to Sabbath

> And it shall come to pass, that from one new moon to another, and from one sabbath to another, shall all flesh come to worship before me, saith the LORD.

**Context:** New creation prophecy. Eschatological Sabbath observance.

**Notes:** Sabbath worship continues in the new creation by all flesh.

---

### Counter-text on sabbath days

> Let no man therefore judge you in meat, or in drink, or in respect of an holyday, or of the new moon, or of the sabbath days:

**Context:** Paul warns against being judged on calendrical observances.

**Notes:** Contested: ceremonial sabbaths (annual/monthly/weekly descending pattern) vs weekly Sabbath.

---

### Counter-text on day-esteeming

> One man esteemeth one day above another: another esteemeth every day alike. Let every man be fully persuaded in his own mind. He that regardeth the day, regardeth it unto the Lord; and he that regardeth not the day, to the Lord he doth not regard it.

**Context:** Paul addresses 'doubtful disputations' about days and food.

**Notes:** Does not use sabbaton. Addresses personal scruples, not Decalogue commands.

### Israel polluted God's sabbaths

> But the house of Israel rebelled against me in the wilderness: they walked not in my statutes, and they despised my judgments... and my sabbaths they greatly polluted. Because they despised my judgments, and walked not in my statutes, but polluted my sabbaths: for their heart went after their idols. Notwithstanding the children rebelled against me... they polluted my sabbaths. Because they had not executed my judgments, but had despised my statutes, and had polluted my sabbaths, and their eyes were after their fathers' idols.

**Context:** Four times in Ezek 20, Israel charged with polluting sabbaths.

**Notes:** The sign rejected leads to judgment. Parallels the four owth designations.

### Set me as a seal upon thine heart

> Set me as a seal upon thine heart, as a seal upon thine arm: for love is strong as death; jealousy is cruel as the grave: the coals thereof are coals of fire, which hath a most vehement flame.

**Context:** Song of Solomon. Seal (chowtham) on heart and arm.

**Notes:** Seal on heart and arm parallels forehead and hand. Intimate belonging.

### Zerubbabel as signet ring

> In that day, saith the LORD of hosts, will I take thee, O Zerubbabel, my servant, the son of Shealtiel, saith the LORD, and will make thee as a signet: for I have chosen thee, saith the LORD of hosts.

**Context:** God makes Zerubbabel his chowtham, reversing the Coniah judgment.

**Notes:** Restoration of the signet-ring relationship.

### Coniah as signet on God's right hand - removal

> As I live, saith the LORD, though Coniah the son of Jehoiakim king of Judah were the signet upon my right hand, yet would I pluck thee thence;

**Context:** God removes Coniah like tearing off a signet ring.

**Notes:** Reverse of Hag 2:23. Breaking the seal-bearer relationship.
