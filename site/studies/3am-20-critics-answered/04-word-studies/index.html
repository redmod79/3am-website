    Word Studies¶

    Question¶

    How do the 14 major critical arguments against the three angels' messages fare when tested against biblical evidence? Take each objection and respond using evidence from the prior 19 studies.

    G2098 — euangelion¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G2098-euangelion.md

    Occurrences: 77

    Good message/gospel. Neuter noun from eu (good) + angello (announce). 77 NT occurrences across 17 books. Rendered 'gospel' in 94.8% of verses. Overwhelmingly Pauline (Romans 11, Philippians 10, Galatians 7). Semantic categories: gospel of the kingdom, gospel of Christ, apostolic trust, standard of conduct, universal scope. Rev 14:6 is the ONLY Revelation occurrence.

    Semantic categories: gospel of the kingdom, gospel of Christ, apostolic trust, standard of conduct, universal scope

    Key verses: Matt 4:23, Rom 1:16, Gal 1:6, Rev 14:6

    Relevance: Core word for Objections 1-3. The everlasting gospel (euangelion aionion) of Rev 14:6 uses the standard NT gospel word, not a different term.

    G2097 — euangelizo¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G2097-euangelizo.md

    Occurrences: 61

    Verb form of euangelion — to announce good news, to preach the gospel. Used for gospel proclamation. Luke 3:18 uses euengelizeto for John's judgment preaching.

    Semantic categories: gospel proclamation, good news announcement

    Key verses: Luke 3:18, Rev 14:6, Gal 3:8

    Relevance: Verb form confirming judgment preaching IS gospel. Luke uses it for John the Baptist's fire-judgment warnings.

    G166 — aionios¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G166-aionios.md

    Occurrences: 71

    Adjective from aion (G165). 71 tokens in 69 verses across 19 books. Rendered 'eternal' (46.4%) and 'everlasting' (30.4%). Dominant usage: 'eternal life' (~43/69 verses). Also applied to punishment, fire, redemption, Spirit, covenant. The adjective is NT-only but LXX uses it for H5769 olam. 2 Tim 1:9 'before eternal times' proves aionios cannot mean merely 'age-long.'

    Semantic categories: eternal life, eternal judgment/punishment, eternal attributes of God, eternal covenant

    Key verses: John 3:16, Matt 25:46, 2 Tim 1:9, Rev 14:6

    Relevance: Central to Objections 1, 7, 12. DeSilva's 'age-long' translation breaks at 2 Tim 1:9 and Matt 25:46.

    G5399 — phobeo¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G5399-phobeo.md

    Occurrences: 110

    Verb from phobos (G5401). 90 unique verses, 110 tokens across 14 books. Nearly all forms middle/passive (deponent). Present vs. aorist imperative pattern: 'Fear not' = present imperative (me phobou, stop fearing); Rev 14:7 phobethete = aorist passive imperative (decisive call to action). Three Louw-Nida domains: Fear/Terror (025.022), Worship/Reverence (053.007), Honor/Status (087.002). Same word used for terror AND reverence — context determines sense.

    Semantic categories: terror/alarm, reverential awe, worship-related fear

    Key verses: Rev 14:7, Matt 10:28, Luke 12:5, Acts 10:2

    Relevance: Core to Objection 5. The phobeo word field spans terror to reverence. Rev 14:7's aorist imperative commands decisive reverential action.

    G5401 — phobos¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G5401-phobos.md

    Occurrences: 47

    Noun from phobeo. Fear/terror/reverence. In 1 John 4:18, the phobos that love casts out is qualified by kolasis (punishment-fear). In 1 Pet 1:17, believers live 'in phobos' — reverential awe coexisting with Father relationship.

    Semantic categories: terror, reverence, punishment-fear

    Key verses: 1 John 4:18, 1 Pet 1:17, Rom 3:18

    Relevance: Core to Objection 5. The noun phobos is ambiguous without context; 1 John 4:18 specifies kolasis-fear.

    G2851 — kolasis¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G2851-kolasis.md

    Occurrences: 2

    Punishment/correction. Appears only 2x in NT (Matt 25:46, 1 John 4:18). Both in judgment contexts. Root idea: pruning/corrective discipline (from kolazo). In 1 John 4:18, kolasis qualifies the fear that love casts out — punishment-dread, not reverential awe.

    Semantic categories: punishment, corrective discipline, judgment-context fear

    Key verses: 1 John 4:18, Matt 25:46

    Relevance: Core to Objection 5. The kolasis qualifier narrows 1 John 4:18's 'fear' to punishment-dread specifically.

    G1785 — entole¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G1785-entole.md

    Occurrences: 71

    Commandment/injunction. Feminine noun from entellomai (G1781). 65 unique verses, 71 tokens. Rendered 'commandment(s)' in 93.8% of verses. Johannine concentration: John + 1 John + 2 John = 22 verses (34%). Semantic categories: Decalogue/Mosaic law, Christ's new commandment, Father's commandment to Christ. Three NT test cases proving entole refers to Decalogue: Rom 7:7-12 (tenth commandment, 5x), Eph 6:2 (fifth commandment), Luke 23:56 (Sabbath).

    Semantic categories: Decalogue/Mosaic law, Christ's love commandment, Father's commandment to Christ, general divine injunction

    Key verses: Rev 14:12, Rom 7:7, Eph 6:2, Luke 23:56

    Relevance: Core to Objections 4, 6. Demolishes the claim that entole cannot mean Decalogue. Nomos never appears in Revelation.

    G3551 — nomos¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G3551-nomos.md

    Occurrences: 197

    Law/Torah. Masculine noun. The standard word for the Mosaic law as a body. NEVER appears in Revelation. The entole/nomos distinction is crucial: Revelation uses entole exclusively for commandments, not nomos.

    Semantic categories: Mosaic law, Torah, legal principle, natural law

    Key verses: Rom 3:31, Rom 7:7, Gal 3:24

    Relevance: Core to Objection 4. Nomos absence from Revelation makes the Tinker/Answering Adventism claim (entole = Jesus' teachings, nomos = Law) irrelevant — Revelation's only vocabulary is entole.

    G4102 — pistis¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G4102-pistis.md

    Occurrences: 244

    Faith/trust/faithfulness. Feminine noun from peitho (G3982). 244 NT occurrences across 21 books. Rendered 'faith' in 68.9%. Overwhelmingly Pauline (Romans 40, Galatians 22, Hebrews 32). Semantic categories: active trust in God, body of Christian belief, faithfulness/reliability. The pistin Iesou (Rev 14:12) genitive is debated: subjective (Jesus' faithfulness), objective (faith in Jesus), or plenary (both).

    Semantic categories: active trust, body of belief, faithfulness, conviction

    Key verses: Rom 3:22, Gal 2:16, Rev 14:12

    Relevance: Core to Objection 4. The pistis Iesou genitive in Rev 14:12 — plenary reading supported by grammar-reference/greek/subjective-objective-genitive.md.

    G4160 — poieo¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G4160-poieo.md

    Occurrences: 597

    To make/do. The verb used in Rev 14:7 (poiesanti) for creation, mapping to Hebrew asah (H6213) in Exo 20:11. Every other creation text in Revelation uses ktizo (G2936). The verb switch is the diagnostic allusion to the fourth commandment.

    Semantic categories: creation, manufacture, action, performance

    Key verses: Rev 14:7, Acts 4:24, Acts 14:15

    Relevance: Core to Objection 6. The poieo/ktizo verb switch is the strongest evidence for the Sabbath allusion.

    G4520 — sabbatismos¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G4520-sabbatismos.md

    Occurrences: 1

    Sabbath-keeping/Sabbath rest. NT hapax legomenon (Heb 4:9 only). Deliberately chosen over katapausis (G2663) used throughout Heb 4. Denotes Sabbath-observance in every extant Greek source. Even anti-Sabbatarian Lincoln concedes the word's Sabbath-keeping meaning.

    Semantic categories: Sabbath observance, eschatological rest

    Key verses: Heb 4:9

    Relevance: Core to Objection 6. The NT hapax for Sabbath-keeping.

    G2920 — krisis¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G2920-krisis.md

    Occurrences: 47

    Judgment/judicial evaluation. Feminine noun from krino (G2919). 47 NT occurrences across 13 books. Rendered 'judgment' in 55%. Concentrated in Matthew (12) and John (11). Semantic categories: day of judgment (eschatological), judicial process, divine evaluation. Distinguished from katakrima (G2631, condemnation/adverse verdict). Rev 14:7 uses krisis — evaluation, not condemnation.

    Semantic categories: day of judgment, judicial process, divine evaluation, tribunal

    Key verses: Rev 14:7, John 5:24, Matt 12:36, Heb 9:27

    Relevance: Core to Objection 8. The krisis/katakrima distinction is decisive: believers face evaluation but not condemnation.

    G2631 — katakrima¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G2631-katakrima.md

    Occurrences: 3

    Condemnation/adverse verdict with execution. Neuter noun. Only 3 NT occurrences: Rom 5:16, 5:18, 8:1. All in Romans. Kata- (against) + krima (judgment) = judgment AGAINST someone. Rom 8:1 negates katakrima for those in Christ Jesus — no condemnation, not no evaluation.

    Semantic categories: condemnation, adverse verdict, judicial execution

    Key verses: Rom 8:1, Rom 5:16, Rom 5:18

    Relevance: Core to Objection 8. Rom 8:1 negates katakrima (condemnation), NOT krisis (evaluation).

    G165 — aion¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G165-aion.md

    Occurrences: 120

    Age/perpetuity/forever. Masculine noun. The eis aionas aionon formula (into ages of ages) appears 21x in NT. Applied to God's existence, praise, and to the torment formula in Revelation.

    Semantic categories: age/era, perpetuity, forever formula

    Key verses: Rev 14:11, Rev 4:9, Rev 19:3

    Relevance: Core to Objection 7. The eis aionas aionon formula in Rev 14:11 matches Rev 19:3 (Babylon's cessation) and Rev 4:8 (living creatures' praise).

    H5769 — owlam¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H5769-owlam.md

    Occurrences: 432

    Forever/eternity/perpetuity. Hebrew temporal term. Used in Isa 34:10 for Edom's 'forever' destruction that was clearly temporal. The OT prophetic convention: owlam describes permanence of result, not necessarily infinite duration of process.

    Semantic categories: perpetuity, eternity, long duration, ancient time

    Key verses: Isa 34:10, Exo 31:17, Dan 7:18

    Relevance: Core to Objection 7. The OT 'forever' language for temporal destructions establishes the convention Rev 14:11 draws on.

    H226 — owth¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H226-owth.md

    Occurrences: 85

    Sign/token/mark. Hebrew noun. The Sabbath is called owth four times (Exo 31:13, 31:17, Ezek 20:12, 20:20). No other Decalogue commandment receives this designation. Rom 4:11 bridges owth/semeion to sphragis (seal).

    Semantic categories: sign, token, covenant mark, wonder

    Key verses: Exo 31:13, Exo 31:17, Ezek 20:12

    Relevance: Core to Objection 10. Sabbath as uniquely designated owth among the Ten Commandments.

    G4972 — sphragizo¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G4972-sphragizo.md

    Occurrences: 28

    To seal/stamp. Verb form of sphragis (G4973). Used for the sealing of believers by the Holy Spirit (Eph 1:13, 4:30) and the sealing of God's servants (Rev 7:3-4).

    Semantic categories: divine sealing, authentication, protection

    Key verses: Eph 1:13, Eph 4:30, Rev 7:3

    Relevance: Core to Objection 10. The Spirit is the sealing AGENT; the seal has content.

    G4973 — sphragis¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G4973-sphragis.md

    Occurrences: 16

    Seal/signet/stamp. Feminine noun. 16 NT occurrences. Overwhelmingly Revelation (13/16 = 81%). Semantic categories: authentication/certification (Rom 4:11, 1 Cor 9:2), divine concealment (Rev 5:1-2), judgment unveiling (Rev 6), divine ownership/protection (Rev 7:2, 9:4). Rom 4:11 is critical — Paul calls circumcision BOTH semeion AND sphragis, breaking the vocabulary barrier.

    Semantic categories: authentication, divine concealment, judgment unveiling, divine ownership

    Key verses: Rom 4:11, Rev 7:2, 2 Tim 2:19

    Relevance: Core to Objection 10. The sign/seal vocabulary bridge via Rom 4:11.

    G5480 — charagma¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G5480-charagma.md

    Occurrences: 9

    Mark/stamp/engraving. Neuter noun from charasso (to scratch/engrave). 9 NT occurrences: 8 in Revelation, 1 in Acts 17:29. Semantic categories: engraved artwork (Acts 17:29), compulsory mark (Rev 13:16-17), mark linked to worship (Rev 14:9, 11), basis for judgment (Rev 16:2, 19:20). The mark is paired with proskyneo (worship) in every Revelation occurrence — receiving the mark IS an act of false worship.

    Semantic categories: engraved artwork, compulsory badge, mark of allegiance, basis for judgment

    Key verses: Rev 13:16, Rev 14:9, Rev 14:11

    Relevance: Core to Objection 10. The charagma is the direct antithesis of the sphragis — both placed on foreheads, but one denotes divine ownership and the other bestial allegiance.

    H2181 — zanah¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H2181-zanah.md

    Occurrences: 107

    To commit harlotry/be unfaithful. Hebrew verb. 107 OT occurrences. In the prophets, used almost exclusively for entities that were ONCE faithful (Israel, Judah, Jerusalem). The harlotry imagery requires prior covenant relationship with YHWH.

    Semantic categories: covenant unfaithfulness, prostitution, spiritual adultery

    Key verses: Ezek 16:15, Hos 2:5, Jer 3:1

    Relevance: Core to Objection 13. The zanah vocabulary requires prior covenant — Rome never had one.

    G4202 — porneia¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G4202-porneia.md

    Occurrences: 26

    Fornication/harlotry. Greek noun. The LXX equivalent of Hebrew zanah in prophetic contexts. Used in Revelation for Babylon's fornication with the kings of the earth.

    Semantic categories: sexual immorality, covenant unfaithfulness, spiritual adultery

    Key verses: Rev 14:8, Rev 17:2, Rev 18:3

    Relevance: Core to Objection 13. The porneia of Babylon = covenant unfaithfulness, not generic immorality.

    H1101 — balal¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H1101-balal.md

    Occurrences: 45

    To mix/confuse. Hebrew verb. Etymology behind 'Babel/Babylon.' The essential nature of Babylon is MIXTURE — blending truth with error, sacred with profane.

    Semantic categories: mixture, confusion, blending

    Key verses: Gen 11:9

    Relevance: Core to Objection 13. Babylon's essence is mixture of truth and error — fits a corrupted religious system.

    G4352 — proskyneo¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G4352-proskyneo.md

    Occurrences: 65

    To worship/bow down. Verb. The central worship verb in Rev 14:7 (proskynēsate, aorist active imperative). Commands worship of the Creator vs. beast (Rev 14:9). The charagma is linked to proskyneo in every Revelation occurrence.

    Semantic categories: worship, prostration, adoration

    Key verses: Rev 14:7, Rev 14:9, Rev 4:10

    Relevance: Core to Objections 6, 14. The worship command in Rev 14:7 echoes the creation language of Exo 20:11.

    H1319 — basar¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H1319-basar.md

    Occurrences: 27

    To bear tidings/announce good news. Hebrew verb. Maps to LXX euangelizo in 18/21 occurrences (PMI 10.0). The angel of Rev 14:6 stands at the end of the basar-euangelizo chain — the vocabulary establishes continuity with OT good-news proclamation.

    Semantic categories: good news announcement, gospel proclamation

    Key verses: Isa 52:7, Isa 61:1

    Relevance: Core to Objection 1. The basar-euangelizo chain proves the gospel is not a NT innovation.

    G5281 — hupomone¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G5281-hupomone.md

    Occurrences: 32

    Patience/endurance/steadfast perseverance. Feminine noun. Used in both Rev 13:10 and 14:12, creating a literary bracket (hupomone inclusio) that binds the beast narrative to the angel messages as a single unit.

    Semantic categories: endurance, steadfastness, patient perseverance

    Key verses: Rev 13:10, Rev 14:12

    Relevance: Core to Objections 4, 11. The hupomone bracket makes Rev 13-14 a single literary unit.

    H3372 — yare¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H3372-yare.md

    Occurrences: 414

    To fear/revere. Hebrew verb. 414 OT occurrences. Used for both terror (Gen 28:17) and reverence (Ecc 12:13). LXX collapses into phobeo. Exo 20:20 uses yare twice in one verse with opposite meanings: 'Fear not' (stop terror) AND 'that his fear may be before your faces' (maintain reverence).

    Semantic categories: terror, reverence, awe, worship-fear

    Key verses: Exo 20:20, Ecc 12:13, Ps 130:4

    Relevance: Core to Objection 5. The Hebrew root behind phobeo in Rev 14:7.

    H7676 — shabbath¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H7676-shabbath.md

    Occurrences: 109

    Sabbath/rest. Hebrew noun. The seventh-day rest. Called owth (sign) four times. Absent from Revelation by name — but the creation formula in Rev 14:7 alludes to the fourth commandment via the poieo/asah verb match.

    Semantic categories: seventh-day rest, covenant sign, creation memorial

    Key verses: Exo 20:8-11, Exo 31:13, Heb 4:9

    Relevance: Core to Objection 6. The Sabbath is NOT named in Revelation — honest acknowledgment required.

    G4077 — pege¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G4077-pege.md

    Occurrences: 12

    Fountain/spring/source. Feminine noun. Rev 14:7 substitutes 'fountains of waters' (pegas hydaton) where every other Exo 20:11 echo uses 'all that in them is.' This divergence marks Rev 14:7's allusion as both dependent on Exo 20:11 and deliberately modified.

    Semantic categories: water source, fountain, spring

    Key verses: Rev 14:7, Rev 8:10, Rev 16:4

    Relevance: Core to Objection 6. The 'fountains of waters' divergence — Rev 14:7's unique fourth element.

    G968 — bema¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G968-bema.md

    Occurrences: 13

    Judgment seat/tribunal/platform. Neuter noun. Used in 2 Cor 5:10 for the judgment seat of Christ where all believers appear. An evaluative tribunal, not a condemnation court.

    Semantic categories: judicial platform, tribunal

    Key verses: 2 Cor 5:10, Rom 14:10

    Relevance: Supporting Objection 8. Believers face the bema — evaluation, not condemnation.

    G5083 — tereo¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G5083-tereo.md

    Occurrences: 75

    To keep/guard/observe. Verb. 75 NT occurrences. Johannine concentration (John 18, 1 John 8, Revelation 11). Used in Rev 14:12 as terountes (present active participle) — 'those keeping.' The participle is descriptive (who the saints are), not prescriptive (conditions for salvation).

    Semantic categories: guard/protect, observe/keep commandments, preserve/maintain

    Key verses: Rev 14:12, John 14:15, 1 John 5:3

    Relevance: Core to Objection 4. The participle terountes describes rather than prescribes.

    G26 — agape¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G26-agape.md

    Occurrences: 116

    Love. Feminine noun. The teleia agape (perfect/complete love) in 1 John 4:18 casts out punishment-fear. The faith-love-law chain: Gal 5:6 (faith works by love) → 1 John 5:3 (love IS keeping commandments).

    Semantic categories: divine love, self-giving love, love between believers

    Key verses: 1 John 4:18, 1 John 5:3, Gal 5:6

    Relevance: Core to Objections 4, 5. Love connects faith to commandment-keeping.
