# Reference Gathering: "Come Out of Her, My People" (Rev 18:4)

## Question
What does Revelation 18:4 mean when God calls "my people" (ho laos mou) out of Babylon? How can God have people — HIS people — inside an apostate system? What is the biblical theology of separation from corrupt religion? Trace the OT "come out" tradition: Abraham from Ur (Gen 12:1), Israel from Egypt (Exo 12), Jews from literal Babylon (Isa 48:20, 52:11, Jer 50:8, 51:6,45). How does 2 Corinthians 6:14-18 (quoting Isa 52:11) apply? What does the hina-me ("so that you do not") purpose clause reveal — they haven't yet participated in the final sins? How does John 10:16 ("other sheep I have, which are not of this fold: them also I must bring") guarantee that God reaches sincere seekers in wrong systems? What is the relationship between the second angel's message and this call — does the "come out" call flow from the announcement of Babylon's fall?

## Web Research Findings

The web research agent (Phase 0) produced extensive findings in 00-web-research.md. Key leads for this study:

### Greek Grammar Leads
- **Exelthate** (Rev 18:4): Aorist Imperative Active, 2nd Person Plural — a single decisive command matching Isa 52:12 LXX
- **Dual hina-me purpose clauses**: (1) hina me synkoinonesete tais hamartiais autes (don't share her sins); (2) kai ek ton plegon autes hina me labete (don't receive her plagues). Meyer identifies "an explicit antithesis between tais hamartiais and ton plegon"
- **Synkoinoneo** (G4790): Only 3 NT occurrences — Eph 5:11 (don't participate in darkness), Phil 4:14 (sharing in distress), Rev 18:4 (don't share sins). Implies active co-participation, not mere proximity
- **Aule vs. poimne** (John 10:16): Commentators unanimously note the KJV "one fold" mistranslates "one flock" (poimne) — the fold/flock distinction means God's flock transcends institutional folds

### 2 Corinthians 6:14-18 Structure
- Lee Magness identifies a chiastically arranged catena of four OT citations:
  - A (v.16b): Lev 26:12 / Ezek 37:27 — divine dwelling and covenant formula
  - B (v.17a): Isa 52:11 — "come out and be separate"
  - B' (v.17b): "quit touching the unclean"
  - A' (v.18): 2 Sam 7:14 — Father-child covenant language (altered: singular → plural, "son" → "sons and daughters")
- **Heterozygeo** (v.14): "to be yoked with one of a different kind" — from LXX Leviticus 19:19

### OT "Come Out" Tradition
- Abraham from Ur (Gen 12:1): Chaldea functions as a synonym for Babylon; Calvin: "a type of us leaving everything behind"
- Lot from Sodom (Gen 19:12-13): The yatsa (H3318) verb in Hiphil — same root that translates to exerchomai (G1831) in Rev 18:4
- Israel from Egypt: "Let my people go" formula
- Jews from literal Babylon: Isa 48:20, 52:11; Jer 50:8, 51:6, 51:45; Zech 2:6-7
- Korah rebellion (Num 16:26): Moses' command to "move back from the tents of these wicked men" — proximity = shared destruction
- Col 1:13-14 transfer language: "transferred from the domain of darkness to the kingdom of the Son"

### Ecclesiological Leads
- **Gill**: Two categories — "elect ones till now uncalled" and "secretly called but not publicly professing"
- **Jamieson-Fausset-Brown**: "In every apostate or world-conforming church there are some of God's invisible and true Church"
- **Ellicott on John 10:16**: "It is not unity of fold which is regarded as the future of the Church, but unity of flock. There will be many folds, in many nations, in many ages"
- **Fall-then-call sequence**: The fall announcement makes the come-out call both POSSIBLE (now people know) and NECESSARY (judgment follows)

### Hosea Reversal Pattern (web-suggested lead)
- Does ho laos mou (Rev 18:4) connect to Hosea's "not my people" / "my people" reversal (Hos 1:9; 2:23; Rom 9:25; 1 Pet 2:10)? People called out of Babylon may include former "not-my-people" who have been reclaimed
