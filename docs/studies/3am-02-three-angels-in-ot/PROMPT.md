## Question
Is the three-part pattern of the three angels' messages — (1) gospel call to worship the true God, (2) exposure of a false system, (3) warning of judgment — repeated throughout the Old Testament? Examine: Noah before the flood, Abraham and Sodom, Moses and Egypt, Elijah at Carmel, Josiah's reform, Jeremiah's ministry. Do these share the same structure as Rev 14:6-12?

## Prior Research Summary
3am-00 defined the three-part pattern as (1) gospel/worship call, (2) exposure of false system, (3) judgment warning, and stated that studies 02 and 03 demonstrate this pattern runs through OT and NT respectively. 3am-01 established the literary structure of Rev 12-14 and the competing-messages framework. hist-15 established that God's universal pattern is to warn before judging (Ezek 33:3-5, Joel 2:1, Amos 3:7). flood-judgment-severity confirmed Noah as 'preacher of righteousness' (2 Pet 2:5) and the moral reasons for the flood. biblical-worship-patterns provided the worship framework (Abel vs Cain, Nadab & Abihu, golden calf, Saul). Web research identified the covenant lawsuit (rib) tradition as a formal legal framework underlying prophetic warnings, the 'come out' tradition tracing from Jer 50-51 through Isa 48:20, 52:11 to Rev 18:4, and the Exodus plague-deity targeting pattern (Exo 12:12). Bohr identifies Elijah as restoring 'true worship to the creator God' and traces the 'come out of Babylon' pattern from Jeremiah through Revelation. EGW explicitly parallels Noah's warning, the Egyptian plagues against gods, Lot's extraction from Sodom, and Elijah's Carmel confrontation with end-time messages.

5. **Elijah at Carmel: choose you this day whom ye will serve:**
The Carmel confrontation presents the most concentrated instance of the three-part pattern — all three elements occur on the same day, in the same location. (1) Gospel/worship call — 1 Ki 18:21 'How long halt ye (pasach, H6452) between two opinions? if the LORD be God, follow him; but if Baal, then follow him.' This is a worship binary exactly paralleling the first angel's call. READ TR-shachah-proskyneo and WG-worship for the worship concept. The drought itself was a theological challenge to Baal, the storm god. (2) Exposure — 1 Ki 18:25-29 records the prolonged, humiliating failure of Baal's prophets. Elijah's mockery (18:27 'Cry aloud: for he is a god; either he is talking, or he is pursuing, or he is in a journey, or peradventure he sleepeth') systematically exposes the false system. READ H1168 (Baal) if available, or note as missing. (3) Judgment — 1 Ki 18:36-39, fire from heaven consumes the sacrifice and the people confess 'The LORD, he is the God' (H3068, H430). Then 18:40 — execution of false prophets per Deut 13:13-15. READ H3068-yhwh and H430-elohiym. Run hebrew_parser.py on 1 Ki 18:21 and 18:39.
**Verses:** 1 Ki 16:31-33, 1 Ki 18:17-21, 1 Ki 18:25-29, 1 Ki 18:36-39, 1 Ki 18:40

6. **Josiah's reform: the book of the law rediscovered:**
Josiah's reform is the most bittersweet instance of the pattern — it shows the three-part message delivered and received at the highest level, yet corporate judgment remains fixed. (1) Gospel/covenant call — 2 Ki 22:8-13, the discovery of the book of the law (towrah, H8451) produces genuine repentance in Josiah. 2 Chr 34:3 says Josiah began seeking God from age 16. 2 Ki 23:3 records covenant renewal before the LORD. READ H8451-towrah and TR-towrah-nomos. (2) Exposure of false system — 2 Ki 23:4-20 is the most detailed account of idolatry destruction in the Bible: removal of Baal vessels, deposing idolatrous priests, burning Asherah, defiling Topheth, removing horses dedicated to the sun, destroying high places. READ H6456-pciyl (graven image) and WG-worship. (3) Judgment warning — 2 Ki 22:15-17, Huldah's prophecy: 'my wrath shall be kindled against this place, and shall not be quenched.' Despite Josiah's personal exemption (22:18-20), corporate judgment remains. This mirrors how Rev 14's messages save individuals ('patience of the saints') while corporate Babylon still falls. Run hebrew_parser.py on 2 Ki 22:16-17.
