    Question¶

    What does dote auto doxan ("give glory to him") mean as a gospel imperative? How is glory (doxa/kabod) given to God — through confession (Josh 7:19), character acknowledgment (Isa 42:12), conduct (Matt 5:16, 1 Pet 2:12), fruit-bearing (John 15:8), bodily stewardship (1 Cor 6:20, 10:31), beholding-transformation (2 Cor 3:18), or something else? What is the relationship between giving glory and the judgment announcement that immediately follows? How does Romans 1:21 ("they glorified him not as God") describe the exact failure the first angel addresses? Why does Rev 16:9 record that under the plagues they "repented not to give him glory" — a precise verbal echo of 14:7? How does John 17:4 ("I have glorified thee on the earth: I have finished the work") establish Christ as the model of giving glory?

    Prior Research Summary¶

    Six prior 3AM series studies (00-05) completed. Study 3am-05 established the three aorist imperatives (fear, give glory, worship) as one reality from three angles. Study 3am-02 documented the covenant-lawsuit tradition and 'give glory' idiom in Josh 7:19. Study 3am-03 identified the Romans 1:18-32 degenerative chain as the antiparallel of Rev 14:7. Study 3am-04 confirmed the basar-euangelizo vocabulary chain and 'give glory' as covenant-lawsuit language. A prior standalone study (rev-14-7-fear-glory-worship) covered similar ground at shallower depth. External corpus findings from EGW define 'give glory' as revealing God's character (3AM 55.3), interpret 'glory to glory' as 'character to character' (2 Cor 3:18), and connect bodily glorification to health reform. Bohr distinguishes types of glory and notes the Rev 14:7/16:9 echo. Web research yielded scholarly leads on the kabod/doxa bridge (Running 1963), the confession idiom (Tactical Christianity), commentator analysis of Rev 14:7 and John 17:4, and the Rom 1:18-25 inverse framework.

    Primary Verses (31 — full analysis required)¶

    Primary glory-giving passages: The core texts defining what it means to give glory to God - Rev 14:6-7 — Primary text — the three imperatives including 'dote auto doxan'; the glory-judgment-worship triad - Rev 16:9 — Precise verbal echo of 14:7 — 'repented not to give him glory' (dounai auto doxan); plagues-as-answer to rejected gospel call - John 17:1-5 — Christ as model of glorification — 'I have glorified thee on the earth: I have finished the work'; edoxasa/eteliosa pairing; glorification = completed work - 1 Cor 6:19-20 — Bodily stewardship as glorification — 'glorify God in your body'; body as temple (naos); doxasate aorist imperative; purchase-price argument (egorasthete) - 1 Cor 10:31 — Universal scope of glorification — 'Whether therefore ye eat, or drink, or whatsoever ye do, do all to the glory of God'; extends glory-giving to all life activity - 2 Cor 3:18 — Beholding-transformation mechanism — 'beholding as in a glass the glory of the Lord, are changed into the same image from glory to glory'; katoptrizomai/metamorphoumetha - Matt 5:16 — Conduct-based glorification — 'Let your light so shine before men, that they may see your good works, and glorify your Father'; human observers glorify God through believer conduct - John 15:8 — Fruit-bearing as glorification — 'Herein is my Father glorified, that ye bear much fruit'; fruit = evidence of abiding in Christ

    The kabod/doxa equation passages: Passages where glory is equated with character, presence, or divine attributes - Exod 33:18-19 — OT equation of glory with character — Moses requests to see God's glory; God responds by proclaiming goodness and name; glory = revealed character - Exod 34:5-7 — The actual content of God's glory — 'The LORD, The LORD God, merciful and gracious, longsuffering'; character attributes proclaimed as glory response - Isa 6:1-3 — The seraphic declaration — 'the whole earth is full of his glory'; kabod fills creation; the recognition template - John 1:14 — Incarnate glory — 'we beheld his glory, the glory as of the only begotten of the Father, full of grace and truth'; doxa made visible in Christ - 2 Cor 4:6 — The glory of God in the face of Jesus Christ — creation-light parallel; God shines in hearts to give the light of the knowledge of His glory - Hab 2:14 — Eschatological promise — 'the earth shall be filled with the knowledge of the glory of the LORD, as the waters cover the sea'; parallels Isa 6:3 and Rev 18:1

    Romans 1 inverse framework: The degenerative chain that inverts Rev 14:7 - Rom 1:18-23 — The exact inverse of Rev 14:7 — 'they glorified him not as God' (edoxasan); the glory-exchange spiral: knew God → refused to glorify → exchanged glory → idolatry → wrath - Rom 1:24-25 — Continuation of the glory-exchange: God gave them up; 'changed the truth of God into a lie, and worshipped and served the creature' — direct antiparallel of 'worship him that made' - Rom 3:23 — Universal failure to glorify — 'all have sinned, and come short of the glory of God'; husteroumenoi tes doxes = deficit of glory

    Confession idiom chain: The 'give glory' = confession trajectory - Josh 7:19 — The confession idiom — 'give glory to the LORD God of Israel, and make confession unto him; and tell me now what thou hast done'; ten kabod la-YHWH as judicial oath formula - John 9:24 — NT parallel of the confession idiom — 'Give God the praise (dos doxan to theo)' in a judicial setting; Pharisees demanding truthful testimony - Mal 2:2 — Curse for not glorifying — 'If ye will not hear, and if ye will not lay it to heart, to give glory unto my name'; latheth kabod — same construction as Rev 14:7

    Negative examples: Judgment for failing to glorify God - Dan 5:23 — Negative example — Belshazzar 'hast not glorified' the God in whose hand is his breath; listed in Nave's as punishment for not glorifying God - Acts 12:23 — Herod struck dead for not giving God glory — 'because he gave not God the glory'; immediate divine judgment for glory-theft

    Transformation/image passages: The beholding-transformation mechanism and image-renewal - 2 Cor 3:18 — Beholding-transformation mechanism — 'beholding as in a glass the glory of the Lord, are changed into the same image from glory to glory'; katoptrizomai/metamorphoumetha

    Conduct and fruit-bearing: Visible works and fruit that produce glorification - Matt 5:16 — Conduct-based glorification — 'Let your light so shine before men, that they may see your good works, and glorify your Father'; human observers glorify God through believer conduct - 1 Pet 2:12 — Parallel to Matt 5:16 — 'they may by your good works, which they shall behold, glorify God in the day of visitation'; kala erga producing doxazo - Phil 1:11 — Fruits of righteousness through Christ unto God's glory — 'Being filled with the fruits of righteousness, which are by Jesus Christ, unto the glory and praise of God'

    Eschatological glory passages: Future and cosmic glory themes - Rev 18:1 — Earth lightened with glory — the fourth angel's message; the practical outworking of glory-giving on a cosmic scale - Rev 15:4 — Eschatological glorification — 'Who shall not fear thee, O Lord, and glorify thy name?'; post-judgment glorification; combines fear + glorify - Rev 4:11 — Heavenly pattern of glory-giving — 'Thou art worthy, O Lord, to receive glory and honour and power: for thou hast created all things'; creation grounds glory - Hab 2:14 — Eschatological promise — 'the earth shall be filled with the knowledge of the glory of the LORD, as the waters cover the sea'; parallels Isa 6:3 and Rev 18:1

    Additional Primary Verses: - Isa 42:12 — Character acknowledgment as glorification — 'Let them give glory unto the LORD, and declare his praise in the islands'; commanded in Nave's under GLORIFYING GOD - Rom 4:20 — Abraham's faith as glorification — 'He staggered not at the promise of God through unbelief; but was strong in faith, giving glory to God'; listed in Nave's GLORIFYING GOD as exemplified - Rom 12:1 — Living sacrifice as bodily glorification — 'present your bodies a living sacrifice, holy, acceptable unto God, which is your reasonable service'; bridge between worship and bodily stewardship - Eccl 12:13-14 — OT template — 'Fear God, and keep his commandments... For God shall bring every work into judgment'; the first angel restates this universally; glory implicit in the fear-commandment-judgment triad

    Focus Areas¶

    1. The kabod/doxa semantic bridge and the meaning of 'glory': This is the foundational linguistic investigation. READ $WORD_STUDIES/H3519-kabowd.md and $WORD_STUDIES/G1391-doxa.md for the canonical semantic ranges. READ $WORD_STUDIES/TR-kabod-doxa.md for the translation bridge showing how the LXX infused doxa (classically 'opinion/reputation') with kabod's meanings ('weight, substance, brightness, character'). Running (Ministry 1963) claims kabod denotes 'the very character or noblest part of man' and when applied to God, 'His character and attributes.' Verify this against Exodus 33:18-19 where Moses asks to see God's glory and God responds by proclaiming His goodness and name, then Exod 34:5-7 where the actual content of the glory proclamation is character attributes. Parse Isa 6:3 ('the whole earth is full of his glory') and Hab 2:14 ('filled with the knowledge of the glory of the LORD'). Run greek_parser.py --verse on Rev 14:7 to establish the exact morphology of dote auto doxan. The 2 Cor 4:17 phrase 'eternal weight of glory' (baros doxes) may preserve the kabod='weight' etymology in Greek dress. Verses: Exod 33:18-19, Exod 34:5-7, Isa 6:1-3, Hab 2:14, Rev 14:6-7, 2 Cor 4:6, 2 Cor 4:17, John 1:14, Heb 1:3 Strong's: H3519, H3513, G1391, G1392

    2. The 'give glory' confession idiom (Josh 7:19 → John 9:24 → Rev 14:7): The 'give glory' idiom in Joshua 7:19 functions as a judicial oath formula: Achan is commanded to 'give glory to the LORD' and 'make confession' — the two are equated. READ $WORD_STUDIES/H3034-yadah.md for yadah's dual meaning of 'praise/confess.' READ $WORD_STUDIES/TR-yadah-exomologeo.md for how this transfers to Greek. The Hebrew construction ten kabod la-YHWH appears also in Mal 2:2 (latheth kabod) with a curse attached for failure. In John 9:24 the Pharisees use the identical Greek formula dos doxan to theo when demanding truthful testimony from the healed blind man. READ $WORD_STUDIES/G3670-homologeo.md and $WORD_STUDIES/G1843-exomologeo.md for the confession vocabulary. READ $WORD_STUDIES/WG-confess.md. Run greek_parser.py --verse on Josh 7:19 (LXX), John 9:24, and Rev 14:7. The research agent should determine whether the first angel's command carries this confessional force — acknowledging that God is right and humanity is wrong. Verses: Josh 7:19, John 9:24, Rev 14:7, Mal 2:2, Phil 2:11, 1 John 1:9 Strong's: H3034, G3670, G1843, H5414, G1325

    3. Romans 1:18-25 as the precise inverse of the first angel's message: Prior study 3am-03 identified Romans 1:18-32 as a structural antiparallel to the first angel's message. This focus area must verify that claim at the Greek level. Parse Rom 1:21 to confirm edoxasan (V-AAI-3P of doxazo G1392) and compare it morphologically with dote (V-2AAM-2P of didomi G1325) + doxan (N-ASF of doxa G1391) in Rev 14:7. Turpin (AiG 2018) argues Paul employs Genesis 1-3 language in Romans 1:23, 26-27 to show the glory-exchange is a reversal of creation order. READ $WORD_STUDIES/G1392-doxazo.md for the verb's full range. Map the five-step chain: (1) knew God through creation (Rom 1:19-20, cf. Rev 14:7 'worship him that made'), (2) glorified him not (Rom 1:21, cf. Rev 14:7 'give him glory'), (3) became fools/darkened (Rom 1:21-22), (4) exchanged glory of incorruptible God for images (Rom 1:23, cf. Rev 13 beast-worship), (5) God gave them up (Rom 1:24-28, cf. Rev 14:9-11 plagues). The research agent should retrieve full chapter context for Romans 1 and compare the vocabulary with Revelation 14. Verses: Rom 1:18-23, Rom 1:24-25, Rom 3:23, Rev 14:6-7, Rev 13:14-15 Strong's: G1392, G1391, G236, G1504

    4. The Rev 14:7 / Rev 16:9 verbal echo and the seal-plague inclusio: Bohr (RST ch.7) notes the positive/negative echo: the righteous give glory (Rev 14:7), the wicked refuse to give glory (Rev 16:9). But this needs Greek-level verification. Parse Rev 14:7 to identify the exact construction: dote (2nd aorist active imperative of didomi) auto (dative personal pronoun) doxan (accusative of doxa). Then parse Rev 16:9 to verify dounai (aorist active infinitive of didomi) auto doxan uses the same lexemes. READ $WORD_STUDIES/G1325-didomi.md for the verb's morphology. The switch from imperative (14:7) to purpose infinitive (16:9 — 'repented not SO AS TO give') is theologically significant: the imperative offered becomes the infinitive refused. Also examine Rev 11:13 where earthquake survivors 'gave glory to the God of heaven' using edokan doxan — a positive response using the same vocabulary. The research agent should map all Revelation occurrences of doxan + didomi to establish the pattern. Verses: Rev 14:7, Rev 16:9, Rev 11:13, Rev 9:20-21, Rev 15:4, Rev 4:11 Strong's: G1325, G1391, G3340, G3341

    5. Christ as the model of giving glory (John 17:4): Bengel's claim that 'I have finished' EXPLAINS 'I have glorified' (eteliosa as epexegetical to edoxasa) makes John 17:4 the definitive statement: glorification = work-completion. READ $WORD_STUDIES/G5048-teleioo.md and $WORD_STUDIES/G5055-teleo.md for the perfection/completion vocabulary. Parse John 17:4 to verify both verbs are aorist (edoxasa V-AAI-1S, eteliosa V-AAI-1S), spoken from the vantage of completed work — Meyer notes the aorist treats the entire redemptive mission as one accomplished act. Cross-reference with John 4:34 ('My meat is to do the will of him that sent me, and to finish his work') where the same teleioo appears. Examine John 12:28 ('Father, glorify thy name') where the Father responds 'I have both glorified it, and will glorify it again.' Also John 13:31-32 where the betrayal triggers 'Now is the Son of man glorified.' The research agent should trace the glorification theme through John's Gospel to show that Christ's entire life is a single act of glory-giving through obedient work-completion. This establishes the model believers are called to follow in Rev 14:7. Verses: John 17:1-5, John 4:34, John 12:28, John 13:31-32, John 17:22, John 21:19 Strong's: G1392, G5048, G5055, G2041

    6. The beholding-transformation mechanism (2 Cor 3:18): The katoptrizomai debate (beholding-as-in-mirror vs. reflecting-as-mirror) is significant. Meyer argues the middle voice never means 'reflecting' in Greek usage, favoring 'beholding.' READ $WORD_STUDIES/G1504-eikon.md for the image vocabulary. Parse the verse: katoptrizomenoi (present middle participle — ongoing action) + metamorphoumetha (present passive indicative — we are being transformed) + apo doxes eis doxan (from glory to glory = progressive transformation). EGW interprets 'from glory to glory' as 'from character to character' (8LtMs Lt 23a 1893; 6MR 342.2; 10LtMs Ms 64 1895). The research agent should verify whether the Greek supports reading doxa as 'character' in this context by checking other Pauline uses of doxa. The metamorphoo verb (G3339) is the same word used for Christ's transfiguration (Matt 17:2), suggesting the transformation is real and radical, not metaphorical. Bohr poses the paradox: 'How can we give glory to God when we don't have any glory to give?' — the beholding-transformation mechanism resolves this: glory is received by contemplation and then reflected in character, making glory-giving a participatory process rather than a unilateral offering. Verses: 2 Cor 3:18, Rom 8:29, Col 3:10, 1 John 3:1-3, Matt 17:2, Eph 4:24, Jas 1:23-24 Strong's: G2734, G3339, G1504, G3345

    7. Conduct-based glorification: good works producing doxa (Matt 5:16, 1 Pet 2:12): In Matt 5:16, Jesus commands believers to let their light shine so that observers 'may see your good works (kala erga) and glorify (doxasosin) your Father which is in heaven.' READ $WORD_STUDIES/G2570-kalos.md for kalos (beautiful/noble, not just agathos/morally good) — the works are aesthetically compelling, attracting glory. Parse both Matt 5:16 and 1 Pet 2:12 where Peter uses nearly identical language: 'by your good works, which they shall behold, glorify God in the day of visitation.' The verb 'behold' (epopteuontes in 1 Pet 2:12) suggests sustained observation, not a glance. Compare with Gal 1:24 where Paul's conversion caused others to glorify God 'in me' — the preposition en suggests Paul became the arena where God's glory was visible. READ $WORD_STUDIES/G2041-ergon.md for the 'works' vocabulary. The Nave's entry for GLORIFYING GOD lists 'The holy example of the saints may lead others to' glorify God (Matt 5:16, 1 Pet 2:12) as a distinct subcategory. This mode makes glory-giving communal, not individual. Verses: Matt 5:16, 1 Pet 2:12, Gal 1:24, 2 Cor 9:13, 2 Th 1:12, Phil 2:15 Strong's: G2570, G2041, G1392, G5316

    8. Bodily stewardship as glorification (1 Cor 6:19-20, 10:31, Rom 12:1): Precept Austin notes doxasate is an aorist imperative — commanding immediate, decisive action, not gradual adjustment. READ $WORD_STUDIES/G4983-soma.md for soma (body) and $WORD_STUDIES/G3485-naos.md for naos (inner temple, not hieron/temple precinct). The purchase-price argument (egorasthete, aorist passive of agorazo — 'you were bought with a price') establishes that the body's ownership transferred from Sin to Christ, grounding the obligation to glorify God bodily. READ $WORD_STUDIES/G1805-exagorazo.md for the redemption vocabulary. EGW connects 1 Cor 6:19-20 directly to health reform (18LtMs Lt 30, 1903). Bohr (3AM Lesson #4 p.33) also connects health to glory-giving capacity. The immediate context in 1 Cor 6 is sexual purity (porneia), but 1 Cor 10:31 universalizes: 'Whether therefore ye eat, or drink, or whatsoever ye do, do all to the glory of God.' Romans 12:1 extends this: 'present your bodies a living sacrifice, holy, acceptable unto God, which is your reasonable service (logiken latreian).' The research agent should parse all three passages and determine whether the scope legitimately extends beyond sexual purity to all bodily stewardship. Verses: 1 Cor 6:19-20, 1 Cor 10:31, Rom 12:1, 1 Cor 3:16-17, 1 Th 5:23 Strong's: G1392, G4983, G3485, G59, G3056

    9. Fruit-bearing as glorification (John 15:8, Phil 1:11): In John 15:8, fruit-bearing IS glorification. READ $WORD_STUDIES/G2590-karpos.md for the karpos vocabulary. Parse the verse: 'en touto edoxasthe ho pater mou' — 'in this my Father is glorified.' The aorist passive edoxasthe may be gnomic (timeless truth) or proleptic (anticipating the result). The fruit metaphor requires abiding in the vine (menete en emoi, v.4 — present imperative for ongoing action). Fruit is not self-generated but Christ-generated: 'without me ye can do nothing' (v.5). This parallels the beholding-transformation mechanism — the branch does not produce fruit by effort but by connection. Phil 1:11 specifies: 'fruits of righteousness, which are BY Jesus Christ, unto the glory and praise of God' — the dia Iesou Christou makes Christ the source. The Nave's entry lists 'Bringing forth fruits of righteousness' (John 15:8, Phil 1:11) under GLORIFYING GOD 'Accomplished by.' Retrieve full John 15:1-8 context. Verses: John 15:1-8, Phil 1:11, Gal 5:22-23, Col 1:10, Matt 13:23 Strong's: G2590, G1392, G3306

    10. The glory-judgment connection: why 'the hour of his judgment is come' follows 'give glory': The Expositor's Greek Testament claims 'he who has created has the right to judge his creatures, as well as to receive their worship' — linking Creator-right-to-judge as the logic connecting glory and judgment. Parse Rev 14:7 to identify the hoti (G3754) clause: is it causal ('give glory BECAUSE judgment has come') or content-declarative ('the everlasting gospel, WHICH IS THAT the hour of judgment has come')? READ $WORD_STUDIES/G2920-krisis.md — prior study gsp-07 established that krisis (judicial process) differs from katakrima (condemnation). READ $WORD_STUDIES/TR-riyb-krisis.md for the covenant-lawsuit background where riyb (H7378) translates to krisis. Prior study 3am-02 established the covenant-lawsuit pattern underlying all three angels. Eccl 12:13-14 provides the OT template: fear God → keep commandments → judgment comes. The first angel restates this universally. Acts 17:30-31 shows Paul using the same logic: God 'commandeth all men every where to repent: Because he hath appointed a day, in the which he will judge the world.' The research agent should parse the hoti clause and retrieve full context for Rev 14:6-7 and the Ecclesiastes passage. Verses: Rev 14:7, Eccl 12:13-14, Acts 17:30-31, Rom 2:16, Psa 96:13, Psa 50:3-6, Dan 7:9-10 Strong's: G2920, G3754, G2064, H7378, H4941

    11. Negative examples: the consequences of refusing to glorify God: Nave's GLORIFYING GOD lists specific punishments: Dan 5:23,30 (Belshazzar praised gods of gold/silver but 'the God in whose hand thy breath is... hast thou not glorified' — judgment that night), Mal 2:2 ('if ye will not lay it to heart, to give glory unto my name... I will curse your blessings'), Acts 12:23 (Herod 'gave not God the glory: and he was eaten of worms'). Rom 1:21 describes the general pattern: 'they glorified him not as God... their foolish heart was darkened.' Verses: Dan 5:23, Acts 12:23, Mal 2:2, Rom 1:21, Rev 16:9, Lev 10:3, Num 20:12 Strong's: G1392, H3513, G3340

    12. Abraham's faith as glory-giving (Rom 4:20) and the faith-glory connection: Romans 4:20 uses the exact 'give glory' construction found in Revelation 14:7: dous doxan to theo. Parse the verse: endynamothe (aorist passive — 'was strengthened') te pistei (instrumental dative — 'by faith') dous (aorist active participle of didomi — 'giving') doxan (accusative — 'glory') to theo (dative — 'to God'). READ $WORD_STUDIES/G1325-didomi.md. The participle dous is circumstantial, describing HOW Abraham glorified God — by trusting the promise despite physical impossibility. This means faith = glory-giving. Not wavering (me diakritheis) at the promise through unbelief, but being fully persuaded (plerophoretheis) that God was able to perform. This links directly to Heb 11:6 ('without faith it is impossible to please him') and to the first angel's connection of faith with glory. Nave's GLORIFYING GOD lists 'Relying on his promises' (Rom 4:20) as one way glorification is 'Accomplished by.' The research agent should trace this faith-glory connection through Romans 4. Verses: Rom 4:20, Heb 11:6, Rom 4:18-22, Rev 14:7, Rev 14:12 Strong's: G1325, G1391, G4102, G4100
