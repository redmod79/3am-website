    Reference Gathering: "Worship Him That Made" — The Creation Formula and the Sabbath Connection¶

    Question¶

    Why does the first angel call for worship of the Creator using the specific formula "him that made heaven, and earth, and the sea, and the fountains of waters"? Is this a direct quotation of Exodus 20:11? Verbal comparison through the LXX. Compare with other creation-worship texts. Sabbath connection via Rev 14:12. Creation-to-new-creation bookend. Hebrews 4:9 sabbatismos.

    Study Plan Context¶

    Study 8 of the 3AM series (Arc 1: The First Angel — Creator, Judge, Worshipped). This study makes the textual case for the Sabbath connection from within the 3AM text itself. Addresses criticism #6: "The Sabbath connection is just inference."

    Depends on: 3am-07 (Hour of Judgment) Series function: Makes the textual case for the Sabbath connection from within the 3AM text itself.

    Prior completed 3AM studies: 3am-00 through 3am-07 (Series Methodology, Literary Context, OT Pattern, NT Pattern, Everlasting Gospel, Fear God, Give Glory, Hour of Judgment).

    3am-00-series-methodology: - Mapped Rev 14:7e ("worship him that made heaven, and earth, and the sea, and the fountains of waters") to study 3am-08 - Identified creation formula as echoing Exo 20:11 through LXX — the highest-confidence pair (asah/poieo, 2022 co-occurrences) - Noted "fountains of waters" (pegas hydaton, G4077) as the fourth element beyond Exo 20:11's three; Gen 7:11 as possible second allusion - Confirmed 3am-08 must build the Sabbath case "from textual evidence, not assertion, and honestly acknowledge the inferential step" - The methodology study already identified three creation domains shared with Exo 20:11 and the fourth element (fountains) as a complication requiring explanation

    3am-01-literary-context-rev-14: - Rev 11:19 opens the cosmic conflict unit by revealing the ark of the covenant — containing the Ten Commandments — activating a commandment theme running through 12:17 to 14:12 - The covenant-law thread: diatheke (11:19) → entole (12:17) → entole (14:12) - Creation language in 14:7 connects back to the covenant law contained in the ark revealed at 11:19 - The three imperatives (fear, give glory, worship Creator) counter the beast's worship demands point-by-point - Identified the first angel's creation formula as "the strongest linguistic link between the first angel's message and the Ten Commandments"

    3am-02-three-angels-in-ot: - The Exodus episode (Episode 3) provides the clearest three-part pattern: "Let my people go, that they may serve me" with the worship verb abad (H5647) - The covenant lawsuit tradition (riyb → krisis) provides the formal legal structure underlying the three-part prophetic pattern - Elijah at Carmel presents the worship binary in its most concentrated form: YHWH or Baal, no middle ground — the same binary the first angel presents

    3am-03-three-angels-in-nt: - Paul at Athens (Acts 17:22-31) is the closest NT parallel to Rev 14:7: Creator God who made the world (17:24), universal scope (17:26,30), judgment (17:31) — all matching the first angel - Both Acts 17:24 and Rev 14:7 use poieo (G4160) for God's creative act and identify God as Lord of heaven and earth - Ecclesiastes 12:13-14 structural template confirmed: fear God + commandments + judgment = the first angel's message

    3am-04-euangelion-aionion: - Confirmed Rev 14:7's creation language echoes the Sabbath commandment's creation rationale - The legon participle connecting v.7 to v.6 means the creation-worship command IS part of the gospel, not an appendix - 1 Cor 7:19: Paul dismisses circumcision while affirming "the keeping of the commandments of God" (teresis entolon Theou) — same root as Rev 14:12's terountes - Rev 14:12 describes the gospel's fruit, not additional requirements

    3am-05-fear-god: - Ecclesiastes 12:13-14 structural parallel confirmed: fear God, keep commandments, judgment — the exact structure of the first angel's message - Four OT farewell addresses give the same triad (Moses, Joshua, Samuel, Solomon); the first angel's message is the fifth, addressed to all nations - The fear-of-God tradition functions as the foundation for the entire first angel's message

    3am-06-give-glory: - The creation formula in Rev 14:7 echoes Exo 20:11 and directly precedes the judgment announcement — "The Creator IS the Judge" - Romans 1:18-25 inverse framework: "glorified him not as God" → "changed the glory of the incorruptible God into an image" → "worshipped and served the creature rather than the Creator" — Rev 14:7 reverses this exact chain

    3am-07-hour-of-judgment: - The judgment announcement (hoti elthen he hora tes kriseos) grounds the worship call: Fear God BECAUSE judgment has come - Daniel 7:22: judgment given FOR the saints — the judgment vindicates, does not threaten - The krisis/katakrima distinction: believers face evaluation (krisis, G2920), not condemnation (katakrima, G2631) - Vindication declaration progression: Rev 14:7 → 15:3-4 → 16:7 → 19:1-2

    From Semantic Search (Additional)¶

    cmd-05-fourth-commandment-sabbath: (score: 0.675) - Clause-by-clause analysis of Exo 20:8-11; the creation rationale (E5) cites "for in six days the LORD made heaven and earth, the sea, and all that in them is" - The ger (H1616) inclusion extends the commandment to non-Israelites within the Decalogue text itself - Pre-Sinai manna narrative (Exo 16:4-30) presents the Sabbath as operative before Sinai - Hebrews 4:9 sabbatismos (G4520): a NT hapax meaning "Sabbath-keeping," deliberately chosen over katapausis (used 8x in context) - The -ismos suffix denotes action/practice — sabbatismos = the act of Sabbath-keeping - E/N tally across 7 law-series studies: 219 Continues, 0 Abolished for the Sabbath

    law-28-revelation-commandments: (score: 0.544) - "Commandments of God" (entolas tou theou) in Rev 12:17 and 14:12 uses entole (G1785), which refers to Decalogue content in 43/43 identifiable NT instances without a qualifier - The creation-worship language of Rev 14:7 echoes the Fourth Commandment's rationale (Exo 20:11) — classified as E4 (Explicit, Continues) - Vocabulary partition: entole for God's moral commands, dogma for what was abolished, entalma for human precepts - Nomos (G3551) does not appear in Revelation; the book uses entole exclusively for God's commands

    gsp-11-sabbath-and-gospel: (score: 0.596) - The Sabbath is a covenant sign (owth), not a salvation condition — it ENACTS the gospel rather than adding to it - Hebrews 4:10 draws the explicit parallel: believers cease from works "as God did from his" (hosper comparison) - The everlasting gospel quotes the Sabbath commandment's creation rationale (Rev 14:7 = Exo 20:11) — embedding the Sabbath within the gospel itself - The weekly Sabbath enacts the believer's rest in God's finished work — "a weekly declaration that 'it is finished'"

    ot-final-controversy-worship: (score: 0.559) - Daniel 3's five-fold pattern (manufactured image, universal worship command, death penalty, faithful refusal, divine vindication) parallels Rev 13-14 - The Sabbath is "the only one of the Ten Commandments explicitly called an owth (sign)" — Exo 31:13, 31:17, Eze 20:12, 20:20 - The sgid (Aramaic) → proskyneo (Greek, LXX) → proskyneo (Rev 13-14) linguistic chain is unbroken - Worship tests escalate from individual (Cain/Abel) through national (golden calf, Carmel) to international (Daniel 3), making Revelation's global worship crisis the natural culmination

    sabbath-shadow-or-memorial: (score: 0.560) - The Sabbath is a memorial (zakar, H2142 — looking backward to creation), not a shadow (skia — looking forward to fulfillment) - The Sabbath fails all five criteria for being a biblical shadow: points backward (not forward), is the original (not a copy), remains after the cross (Heb 4:9), belongs to the Decalogue (not the ceremonial code), is "perpetual covenant" (Exo 31:16) - Sabbatismos (G4520) in Heb 4:9 means "Sabbath-keeping" in all extant Greek literature except two figurative uses by Origen (Gallos doctoral dissertation, Andrews, 2011) - Leviticus 23:37-38 explicitly separates weekly from ceremonial sabbaths using the fourfold separator millibad (H905)

    Web Research Findings¶

    The 00-web-research.md file contains extensive scholarly findings. Key leads for the scoping agent:

    Verbal Comparison Data (LXX)¶

    • Rev 14:7 and Exo 20:11 share four terms in the same order: poieo (made), ouranos (heaven), ge (earth), thalassa (sea)
    • Rev 14:7 uses poieo (poiesanti) — the same verb root as Exo 20:11 LXX (epoiese), NOT ktizo (used in Rev 10:6)
    • Mueller (BRI): The poieo/ktizo distinction is significant — Rev 10:6 uses ktizo for creation, Rev 14:7 deliberately uses poieo, aligning with Gen 1-2 LXX and Exo 20:11 LXX
    • All parallel creation-formula texts (Psa 146:6, Acts 4:24, Acts 14:15) conclude with "panta ta en autois" (all that is in them), faithfully reproducing Exo 20:11. Rev 14:7 ALONE substitutes "pegas hydaton" (springs of waters) — a deliberate departure
    • Baldwin: pegas appears in LXX Gen 7:11 ("fountains of the deep"), suggesting an implicit Flood allusion alongside the Creation allusion

    Paulien's Allusion Methodology¶

    • Three-pronged approach: (1) verbal parallels (shared terminology), (2) thematic parallels (salvation, judgment, creation mapped to First Table of Decalogue), (3) structural analysis (Ten Commandments frame Rev 12-14)
    • Claims: "there is no direct allusion to the Old Testament in Revelation that is more certain than the allusion to the fourth commandment in Rev. 14:7"
    • Structural argument: Rev 13 beasts counterfeit all four worship commandments (1st: 13:4,8; 2nd: 13:15; 3rd: 13:6; 4th: seal/mark contrast)

    The Sabbatismos Debate (Heb 4:9)¶

    • Lincoln (anti-Sabbatarian scholar): concedes every extant use of sabbatismos in Greek literature denotes Sabbath observance — Plutarch, Justin, Epiphanius, Apostolic Constitutions
    • Lane (WBC): translates "Sabbath celebration" — sabbatismos conveys "the special aspect of festivity and joy, expressed in the adoration and praise of God"
    • GCI counter-argument: cites SDA Bible Commentary against itself — "If the conclusion is that Sabbathkeeping remains, there would have been no point in so labored an effort to persuade the Jews to do what they were already doing"
    • Page (Ministry Magazine, mediating position): sabbatismos is "the God-given symbol and sample of the rest of grace"

    The Entole vs. Nomos Objection (Tinker)¶

    • Claims John uses nomos for Mosaic Law and entole for Jesus's teachings — severing Rev 14:12 from Decalogue theology
    • Counter: Luke 23:56 applies entole to the Sabbath commandment ("rested the sabbath day according to the commandment [entole]"); Heb 7:15-16 also uses entole for Mosaic law
    • Law-28 prior study already established: entole without qualifier = Decalogue content in 43/43 NT instances

    Creation-to-New-Creation Bookend¶

    • Paulien cautions against over-reading Isa 66:22-23 — New Moon problem and Isa 66:24 (dead bodies) complicates a literal new-earth reading
    • Answering Adventism objection: Rev 21:23 says there is no sun/moon — but the study should check whether the text says NO sun/moon or merely no NEED for them as light
    • Sequeira: both creative work (Gen 2) and redemptive work (cross) followed six-day completion + seventh-day rest pattern

    Summary for Scoping Agent¶

    • 8 prior 3AM studies read, all contributing relevant findings (especially 3am-00's methodology and 3am-01's literary context)
    • 6 additional studies from semantic search with directly relevant findings (cmd-05, law-28, gsp-11, ot-final-controversy-worship, sabbath-shadow-or-memorial, jesus-is-my-sabbath)

    Key leads: 1. The LXX verbal comparison between Rev 14:7 and Exo 20:11 is the central piece of evidence — four shared terms (poieo, ouranos, ge, thalassa) in the same order, with the deliberate verb choice of poieo over ktizo distinguishing Rev 14:7 from Rev 10:6. The "pegas hydaton" substitution for "panta ta en autois" is the key departure requiring explanation. 2. The Rev 14:7-to-14:12 inclusio — creation formula echoing the fourth commandment bookended by "commandments of God" — is the structural argument that elevates the verbal parallel from coincidence to intentional allusion. 3. The sabbatismos debate in Heb 4:9 is linguistically clear (every extant use = Sabbath observance, per Lincoln's own anti-Sabbatarian concession) but theologically contested. The GCI counter-argument about persuading Jews to do what they already did deserves engagement. 4. The creation-to-new-creation bookend (Gen 2:2-3 → Isa 66:22-23) is complicated by the New Moon problem and Paulien's own caution — the study should note this honestly.
