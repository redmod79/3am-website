    Word Studies¶

    Question¶

    What is the literary structure of Revelation 12-14 and how does the placement of the three angels' messages within this macro-structure determine their meaning? What comes before (Rev 12's cosmic conflict, Rev 13's two beasts), what comes after (Rev 14:13-20's harvest), and how does the Lamb-on-Zion unit (14:1-5) frame the messages? How does Rev 11:19 (the ark of the covenant revealed) open the entire sequence? Are the three angels literal angels or symbolic movements — what does the apocalyptic genre require?

    G32 — aggelos¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G32-aggelos.md

    Occurrences: 186

    From aggello (to bring tidings); a messenger, especially an angel. 186 NT occurrences. Predominantly supernatural heavenly beings (approx. 175 of 186). 76 occurrences in Revelation alone (most of any book). Human messenger usage: 7 occurrences (John the Baptist in Matt 11:10, Mark 1:2, Luke 7:27; human scouts in Luke 7:24, 9:52; Rahab's spies in James 2:25). The seven 'angels' of the seven churches (Rev 1:20-3:14) are a contested category — they could be literal guardian angels, human pastors/bishops, or personified spirits. This is the key interpretive crux for the literal vs. symbolic question in Rev 14.

    Semantic categories: supernatural heavenly beings, human messengers/envoys, angels of the seven churches (disputed), fallen angelic beings, judgment-executing angels

    Key verses: Rev 14:6, Rev 14:8, Rev 14:9, Rev 12:7, Rev 1:20, Rev 2:1, Matt 11:10, James 2:25

    Relevance: Core to the literal vs. symbolic angels question. The word's semantic range includes both celestial beings and human messengers. In Revelation, angels are predominantly celestial actors (trumpets, bowls, harvest). But the church 'angels' of Rev 1:20-3:14 may be human leaders, establishing a precedent for symbolic angelos usage within Revelation itself.

    G1404 — drakon¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G1404-drakon.md

    Occurrences: 13

    Dragon — 13 tokens in 12 unique verses, exclusively in Revelation. Identified with Satan, the Devil, and the ancient serpent (Rev 12:9; 20:2). Appears with seven heads, ten horns, seven crowns (12:3). The dragon is the source of the beast's power (13:2) and the channel of deception through unclean spirits (16:13).

    Semantic categories: identified with Satan/Devil/serpent, heavenly adversary, persecutor of the woman and remnant, source of beast's power, channel of deception

    Key verses: Rev 12:3, Rev 12:7-9, Rev 12:17, Rev 13:2, Rev 13:4, Rev 16:13, Rev 20:2

    Relevance: The dragon is the primary antagonist of Rev 12 and the power behind the beasts of Rev 13. The fourfold identification chain in 12:9 removes ambiguity. The dragon's pursuit of the woman's remnant (12:17) is the immediate context for the three angels' counter-proclamation (14:6-12).

    G2342 — therion¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G2342-therion.md

    Occurrences: 46

    Wild beast — 46 NT occurrences, 38 in Revelation (84%). Composite Daniel 7 entity (Rev 13:1-2). Object of false worship (Rev 14:9). The beast is the structural antagonist that the three angels' messages counter.

    Semantic categories: literal wild animal, apocalyptic political/religious power, object of false worship

    Key verses: Rev 13:1-2, Rev 13:11, Rev 14:9, Rev 14:11, Rev 19:20

    Relevance: The beast is the target of the third angel's warning (14:9-11). Universally understood as symbolic of earthly powers — if the antagonists (beasts) are symbolic, consistency suggests the protagonists (angels) are symbolic too.

    G721 — arnion¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G721-arnion.md

    Occurrences: 30

    Lamb — 30 tokens, 27 in Revelation. Christological title: enthroned (5:6), slain (5:12), worshiped (5:13), warrior-judge (6:16, 17:14), bridegroom (19:7). The Lamb of 14:1 stands on Mount Zion with the 144,000, providing the contrast-frame to the beast.

    Semantic categories: enthroned Lamb, slain Lamb, object of worship, warrior-judge, bridegroom

    Key verses: Rev 5:6, Rev 14:1, Rev 14:4, Rev 14:10, Rev 13:11

    Relevance: The Lamb (14:1) is the structural counterpart to the beast (13:1). The earth beast has horns 'like a lamb' (13:11) but speaks 'as a dragon' — a near-homophone contrast. The three angels' messages emerge from the Lamb's victory position.

    G4592 — semeion¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G4592-semeion.md

    Occurrences: 77

    Sign — 77 tokens in 69 verses, 7 in Revelation. A pointer that signifies deeper meaning. In Revelation: heavenly/apocalyptic sign (12:1, 12:3, 15:1) and deceptive signs of the beast (13:13-14, 16:14, 19:20).

    Semantic categories: authenticating signs of Jesus, demanded sign, eschatological sign, deceptive signs, heavenly/apocalyptic sign

    Key verses: Rev 12:1, Rev 12:3, Rev 15:1, Rev 13:13-14

    Relevance: The semeion inclusio (12:1, 12:3, 15:1) brackets the cosmic conflict unit. These three 'sign' markers define the literary boundaries of the section containing the three angels' messages.

    G5480 — charagma¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G5480-charagma.md

    Occurrences: 9

    Mark — 9 verses, 8 in Revelation. An engraved mark/stamp imposed as badge of servitude. The mark of the beast (13:16-17) is contrasted with the seal/name of God on the 144,000 foreheads (14:1).

    Semantic categories: engraved artwork, mark compulsorily imposed, mark linked to worship, mark as basis for judgment, victory over the mark

    Key verses: Rev 13:16-17, Rev 14:9, Rev 14:11, Rev 15:2, Rev 19:20

    Relevance: The mark is the specific thing the third angel warns against receiving. It contrasts with the Father's name on the 144,000's foreheads — two competing loyalty markers.

    G4352 — proskyneo¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G4352-proskyneo.md

    Occurrences: 65

    Worship — to prostrate, do homage. The central verb of the competing-messages framework. Beast worship (Rev 13:4,8,12,15) vs. Creator worship (Rev 14:7). The third angel warns against worshiping (proskyneo) the beast (14:9,11).

    Semantic categories: prostration before God/Christ, beast worship, Creator worship call

    Key verses: Rev 13:4, Rev 13:8, Rev 13:12, Rev 13:15, Rev 14:7, Rev 14:9, Rev 14:11

    Relevance: The pivot word of Rev 12-14. The entire conflict is about WHO is worshiped. proskyneo appears on both sides: beast-worship (Rev 13) and Creator-worship (Rev 14:7).

    G2098 — euangelion¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G2098-euangelion.md

    Occurrences: 77

    Gospel — 77 tokens, 74 unique verses. A good message. The only occurrence in Revelation is Rev 14:6 — 'everlasting gospel' (euangelion aionion). Shares the aggel- root with aggelos, creating a wordplay: the messenger good-messages.

    Semantic categories: the gospel of the kingdom, Paul's gospel, everlasting gospel

    Key verses: Rev 14:6, Matt 4:23, Mark 16:15, Rom 1:16

    Relevance: Rev 14:6 is the ONLY use of euangelion in Revelation — making it structurally emphatic. The 'everlasting gospel' (euangelion aionion) is the first angel's defining content.

    G166 — aionios¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G166-aionios.md

    Occurrences: 71

    Eternal/everlasting — modifies euangelion in Rev 14:6 ('everlasting gospel'). The gospel is described as aionios — not a new message but one with eternal standing.

    Semantic categories: eternal life, everlasting punishment, eternal gospel

    Key verses: Rev 14:6, John 3:16, Matt 25:46

    Relevance: The modifier that makes 14:6's gospel transcendent — not a time-bound message but an eternal proclamation.

    G5281 — hupomone¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G5281-hupomone.md

    Occurrences: 32

    Patience/endurance — 32 tokens in 31 verses. Active, steadfast endurance rather than passive resignation. Forms the inclusio bracketing the three angels' messages: Rev 13:10 and Rev 14:12 both use 'here is the hupomone of the saints.'

    Semantic categories: patient endurance under trial, perseverance in faith

    Key verses: Rev 13:10, Rev 14:12, Rom 5:3-4, Jam 1:3-4

    Relevance: The hupomone inclusio (13:10 + 14:12) brackets the three angels' messages, marking them as the content that requires and sustains patient endurance.

    G1785 — entole¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G1785-entole.md

    Occurrences: 71

    Commandment — 71 tokens in 65 verses. An authoritative prescription. Appears in the thematic arc: ark of the covenant/testament (11:19) -> remnant keeps commandments (12:17) -> saints keep commandments (14:12).

    Semantic categories: divine commandments, love commandments, covenant law

    Key verses: Rev 12:17, Rev 14:12, Exo 20:1-17

    Relevance: The commandment theme threads from 11:19 (ark containing commandments) through 12:17 (remnant keeps commandments) to 14:12 (saints keep commandments). This is the covenant-law chain.

    G4102 — pistis¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G4102-pistis.md

    Occurrences: 244

    Faith — appears in both hupomone formulas: 13:10 ('the faith of the saints') and 14:12 ('the faith of Jesus'). The genitive Iesou can be objective (faith IN Jesus) or subjective (faith OF Jesus / Jesus' faithfulness).

    Semantic categories: saving faith, faithfulness, the faith (doctrinal content)

    Key verses: Rev 13:10, Rev 14:12

    Relevance: Paired with hupomone in the inclusio formulas. The shift from 'faith of the saints' (13:10) to 'faith of Jesus' (14:12) may indicate the source of endurance.

    G536 — aparche¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G536-aparche.md

    Occurrences: 8

    Firstfruits — the 144,000 are aparche to God and to the Lamb (14:4). As firstfruits, they are the representative sample guaranteeing the full harvest (14:14-16).

    Semantic categories: firstfruits offering, representative sample, eschatological guarantee

    Key verses: Rev 14:4

    Relevance: Connects the 144,000 (14:1-5) to the grain harvest (14:14-16): firstfruits guarantee the coming full harvest.

    G1504 — eikon¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G1504-eikon.md

    Occurrences: 23

    Image — the image of the beast (13:14-15). Parallels Dan 3's golden image worship. The third angel warns against worshiping the beast AND his image (14:9,11).

    Semantic categories: image of the beast, idol worship

    Key verses: Rev 13:14-15, Rev 14:9, Rev 14:11, Rev 15:2

    Relevance: The image (eikon) is part of the beast-worship system that the third angel opposes.

    G2372 — thumos¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G2372-thumos.md

    Occurrences: 18

    Wrath/fury — the thumos chain through Revelation: 14:8 (wine of thumos of Babylon's fornication), 14:10 (wine of thumos of God), 14:19 (winepress of thumos of God), 15:1, 16:1, 19:15. 10 of 18 NT occurrences are in Revelation.

    Semantic categories: divine wrath, passionate anger, winepress imagery

    Key verses: Rev 14:8, Rev 14:10, Rev 14:19, Rev 15:1, Rev 19:15

    Relevance: The wrath-chain is a sustained trajectory through the second angel's message (14:8), the third angel's message (14:10), the grape harvest (14:19), and the bowl plagues (15:1, 16:1). thumos links all these sections.

    G2325 — therizo¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G2325-therizo.md

    Occurrences: 24

    Reap/harvest — the grain harvest verb in Rev 14:15-16. Distinct from trygao (G5166, gather grapes) in 14:18-19. therizo = positive grain ingathering; trygao = negative grape judgment.

    Semantic categories: grain harvest, eschatological reaping

    Key verses: Rev 14:15, Rev 14:16, Matt 13:39, Joel 3:13

    Relevance: The therizo/trygao distinction is the lexical key to the dual-harvest eschatology of Rev 14:14-20.

    G1242 — diatheke¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G1242-diatheke.md

    Occurrences: 33

    Covenant/testament — the ark of his testament (diatheke) in Rev 11:19. The covenant standard (Ten Commandments) contained in the ark connects to the commandment-keeping theme of 12:17 and 14:12.

    Semantic categories: old covenant, new covenant, ark of the covenant

    Key verses: Rev 11:19, Heb 8:6, Heb 9:4

    Relevance: The diatheke in Rev 11:19 opens the covenant-commandment thread that runs through the entire cosmic conflict unit to 14:12.

    G3485 — naos¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G3485-naos.md

    Occurrences: 46

    Temple/sanctuary — the inner sanctuary where deity dwells, distinct from hieron (the temple complex). 16 occurrences in Revelation. The naos is opened in 11:19 and again in 15:5. The harvest angels come out of the naos (14:15,17).

    Semantic categories: inner sanctuary, heavenly temple, believers as temple

    Key verses: Rev 11:19, Rev 14:15, Rev 14:17, Rev 15:5, Rev 15:8

    Relevance: The naos provides the structural frame: opened at 11:19 (beginning of unit) and 15:5 (end of unit). The harvest is authorized from the naos.

    G897 — Babylon¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G897-babylon.md

    Occurrences: 12

    Babylon — the second angel declares 'Babylon is fallen, is fallen' (14:8). Babylon makes all nations drink the wine of her fornication. OT background in Jer 51:7-8, Isa 21:9.

    Semantic categories: OT Babylon empire, eschatological Babylon system

    Key verses: Rev 14:8, Rev 16:19, Rev 17:5, Rev 18:2

    Relevance: The target of the second angel's message. Babylon is a system, not a literal city — supporting the symbolic reading of the entire Rev 12-14 section.

    G3789 — ophis¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G3789-ophis.md

    Occurrences: 14

    Serpent — 13 NT verses. Explicitly equated with the dragon in Rev 12:9 and 20:2 ('that old serpent'). Connects the Revelation dragon to the Eden serpent of Gen 3.

    Semantic categories: Eden serpent, literal snakes, dragon identification

    Key verses: Rev 12:9, Rev 12:14-15, Rev 20:2, Gen 3:1

    Relevance: Part of the fourfold identification chain in 12:9: dragon = old serpent = Devil = Satan.

    G1849 — exousia¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G1849-exousia.md

    Occurrences: 103

    Authority/power — the beast receives exousia from the dragon (13:2). The edothe pattern gives the beast bounded authority (13:5,7). The angel from the altar has exousia over fire (14:18).

    Semantic categories: delegated authority, divine authority, beast authority

    Key verses: Rev 13:2, Rev 13:5, Rev 13:7, Rev 14:18

    Relevance: The authority chain: God permits (edothe) -> dragon delegates (13:2) -> beast exercises (13:5-7). All authority is ultimately bounded by God's sovereign grant.

    G5399 — phobeo¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G5399-phobeo.md

    Occurrences: 110

    Fear — the first angel commands 'Fear God' (phobethete ton Theon, 14:7). Aorist passive imperative — a decisive call to reverence the Creator rather than fearing the beast's threats.

    Semantic categories: reverence for God, fear of judgment, fear of persecution

    Key verses: Rev 14:7, Rev 15:4

    Relevance: The opening command of the first angel's message — sets up the competing-fear dynamic: fear God (14:7) vs. fear the beast (13:4's implied 'who is able to make war with him?').

    G1391 — doxa¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G1391-doxa.md

    Occurrences: 168

    Glory — 'give glory to him' (dote auto doxan, 14:7). Part of the first angel's threefold command: fear God + give glory + worship the Creator.

    Semantic categories: divine glory, glory given to God

    Key verses: Rev 14:7, Rev 15:4, Rev 4:11

    Relevance: The second command of the first angel's message. Giving glory to God rather than to the beast.

    G2919 — krino¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G2919-krino.md

    Occurrences: 132

    Judge — 'the hour of his judgment (krisis, G2920) is come' (14:7). The judgment hour is presented as having arrived (elthen, aorist).

    Semantic categories: divine judgment, judicial process

    Key verses: Rev 14:7, Rev 20:12

    Relevance: The judgment announcement is the urgency behind the first angel's message — the hour has come, demanding an immediate response.

    G4160 — poieo¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G4160-poieo.md

    Occurrences: 597

    Make/do — 'worship him that made (poiesanti) heaven, and earth, and the sea' (14:7). The Creator-worship call uses poieo (aorist participle) to identify God as the one who made all things.

    Semantic categories: creative activity, general action

    Key verses: Rev 14:7, Rev 13:5

    Relevance: The Creator identity in Rev 14:7 uses the same verb (poieo) with the same creation domains (heaven, earth, sea) as Exo 20:11, linking the first angel's message to the Sabbath commandment.

    G4098 — pipto¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G4098-pipto.md

    Occurrences: 93

    Fall — 'Babylon is fallen, is fallen' (epesen epesen, 14:8). Doubled aorist for emphatic, prophetic announcement.

    Semantic categories: literal falling, prophetic fall of powers

    Key verses: Rev 14:8, Rev 18:2

    Relevance: The doubled epesen in the second angel's message echoes Isa 21:9 and anticipates Rev 18:2.

    G3709 — orge¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G3709-orge.md

    Occurrences: 36

    Wrath/anger — 'the cup of his indignation (orge)' (14:10). Distinguished from thumos (fiery passion): orge is settled judicial wrath.

    Semantic categories: divine judicial wrath, human anger

    Key verses: Rev 14:10, Rev 6:16-17

    Relevance: Paired with thumos in Rev 14:10 — both words for wrath appear together, intensifying the judgment warning.

    G2784 — kerysso¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G2784-kerysso.md

    Occurrences: 63

    Preach/proclaim — the standard verb for gospel proclamation (Mark 16:15 'preach the gospel'). Not used directly in Rev 14:6 (which uses euangelizo), but the concept of proclamation underlies the angel's mission.

    Semantic categories: herald proclamation, gospel preaching

    Key verses: Mark 16:15, Matt 24:14

    Relevance: The Great Commission uses kerysso for human preaching. Rev 14:6 uses the cognate euangelizo. Both describe universal gospel proclamation.

    G5166 — trygao¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G5166-trygao.md

    Occurrences: 3

    Gather grapes — 3 NT occurrences (Luke 6:44, Rev 14:18-19). The grape harvest verb, distinct from therizo (grain harvest). In Rev 14:18-19, trygao describes the angel gathering the vine of the earth for the winepress of God's wrath.

    Semantic categories: grape harvest, judgment harvest

    Key verses: Rev 14:18, Rev 14:19, Luke 6:44

    Relevance: The therizo/trygao lexical distinction is a key indicator that Rev 14:14-16 (grain) and 14:17-20 (grape) are morally distinct harvests.

    G1407 — drepanon¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G1407-drepanon.md

    Occurrences: 8

    Sickle — 8 occurrences (Mark 4:29, Rev 14:14-19). 100% translated 'sickle.' Used for both grain harvest (14:14-16) and grape harvest (14:17-19). The same tool is used for both operations, but the verbs and agents differ.

    Semantic categories: grain harvest tool, grape harvest tool

    Key verses: Rev 14:14, Rev 14:15, Rev 14:17, Rev 14:18, Rev 14:19, Mark 4:29

    Relevance: The sickle (drepanon) appears in Joel 3:13 LXX and in both Rev 14 harvests, connecting the OT source to both NT applications.

    G3025 — lenos¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G3025-lenos.md

    Occurrences: 5

    Winepress — 5 occurrences (Matt 21:33, Rev 14:19-20, Rev 19:15). The great winepress of the wrath (thumos) of God (14:19). OT background: Isa 63:2-3 (divine warrior treading), Joel 3:13 (press is full), Lam 1:15 (God trod the daughter of Judah as in a winepress).

    Semantic categories: agricultural winepress, eschatological judgment winepress

    Key verses: Rev 14:19, Rev 14:20, Rev 19:15

    Relevance: The winepress is the culmination of the grape harvest — the thumos chain reaches its endpoint. The winepress of God's wrath is the final consequence for those who reject the three angels' messages.

    G2316 — theos¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G2316-theos.md

    Occurrences: 1343

    God — appears throughout: the temple of God (11:19), commandments of God (12:17, 14:12), wrath of God (14:10,19), the Lamb and God paired (14:4). The competing worship claim centers on who is theos.

    Semantic categories: God the Creator, God the Judge

    Key verses: Rev 11:19, Rev 14:7, Rev 14:10, Rev 14:12, Rev 14:19

    Relevance: The God whose temple is opened (11:19), whose commandments define the remnant (12:17, 14:12), and whose judgment the first angel announces (14:7).

    G1484 — ethnos¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G1484-ethnos.md

    Occurrences: 164

    G3173 — megas¶

    H5715 — eduwth¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H5715-eduwth.md

    Occurrences: 66

    Testimony — the tablets of the law placed in the ark (Exo 25:16,21). The 'ark of the testimony' = the ark containing the Ten Commandments.

    Semantic categories: law tablets, covenant testimony

    Key verses: Exo 25:16, Exo 25:21, Exo 31:18

    Relevance: The testimony (eduwth) in the ark becomes the diatheke (testament/covenant) in Rev 11:19's 'ark of his testament.'
