### From Semantic Search

**rev-14-7-fear-glory-worship** (score: 0.625)
- Question: What does it mean to fear God, give glory to Him, and worship Him in Rev 14:7?
- **Directly relevant — this is the most comprehensive prior study on the fear-of-God theme**
- Phobeo/yare: LXX bridge of 241 co-occurrences connects Greek command to entire OT "fear of the LORD" tradition
- Yirah (H3374): "fear of the LORD" is settled disposition — foundation of wisdom (Prov 1:7), moral compass (Prov 8:13; 16:6), source of life (Prov 14:26-27), Messianic endowment (Isa 11:2-3)
- LXX sometimes rendered yirah as eusebeia ("godliness") — ancient translators understood fear as life-orientation, not emotion
- Matt 10:28 / Luke 12:5: Jesus distinguishes fear of God from fear of humans — fear of God relativizes all other fears, including the beast
- 1 John 4:18 resolution: phobos with kolasis = slavish terror of condemnation; Rev 14:7 = reverential awe. 1 Pet 1:17 shows both coexisting ("Father" + "pass time in fear"). Same John wrote both — he saw no contradiction
- Eccl 12:13: fear of God frames the entire pursuit of wisdom from beginning (Prov 1:7) to end (Eccl 12:13)
- Proverbs 8:13: "The fear of the LORD is to hate evil" — active moral opposition
- Deut 10:12-13: fear → walk → love → serve → keep — fear comes FIRST in the sequence
- Cornelius (Acts 10:2, 35): practical example of what fearing God looks like — devout living, generosity, prayer, righteousness
- Key limitation: study covered all three imperatives broadly; did not go deep on the OT yare semantic range, the Hebrew fear-word distinctions (yirah vs. eimah vs. pachad), the LXX bridge mechanics, or the beast/fear contrast in detail

## Web Research Findings

The web research agent (Phase 0) identified significant scholarly leads:

**Moskala (JATS, 2019)** — Four definitions of fearing God: (1) making decisions in respect to God's will; (2) fearing to grieve Him; (3) loving and obeying the Lord; (4) cultivating awareness of God's presence. Claims phobethete ton theon in its imperative form "occurs only once in the entire Bible." Identifies a Hebrew wordplay between ra'ah (to see) and yare (to fear). Claims "the concept of love in the notion of fear is no longer present in modern languages" but survives in biblical Hebrew.

**Precept Austin (commentator compilation)** — Vincent: ou (not me) indicates fear "has no existence" in love — categorical denial. Distinguishes slavish fear from filial reverence. Kolasis (G2851) appears only twice in NT (Matt 25:46 and 1 John 4:18). Bengel outlines spiritual progression: no love/no fear → fear without love → fear and love → love without fear.

**Hebrew word distinctions** — YIR'AH (reverence/awe) vs. EIMAH (H367, terror) vs. PACHAD (H6343, dread). Three different Hebrew words for different types of fear.

**LXX bridge claim** — Phobos translates yirah in the LXX. This cross-linguistic bridge explains why NT writers use the same Greek word for two different responses (terror and reverence).

**Aorist imperative in Rev 14:7** — Reading Acts article suggests "proleptic aorist or aorist of assurance" for the judgment announcement. The urgency of phobethete as aorist passive imperative needs verification.
