## Prior Studies

### From Series Dependencies

**3am-18-patience-of-the-saints:**
- Question: What does hode he hupomone ton hagion estin mean in Rev 14:12?
- Key finding 1: Hode (G5602) functions as apocalyptic pointer -- "HERE it is, look." Estin (present active indicative) is declarative, not imperative. Rev 14:12 DESCRIBES patience as a characteristic, not PRESCRIBES it as a duty.
- Key finding 2: The hode inclusio brackets the beast crisis -- Rev 13:10 ("Here is the patience AND the faith of the saints") opens; Rev 14:12 closes. The content of 14:12 DEFINES what 13:10 introduced: "patience and faith" = "commandment-keeping and faith of Jesus."
- Key finding 3: Hoi terountes (G5083, present active participle, nominative plural masculine) characterizes the saints by ongoing habitual activity: keeping commandments. Present tense = settled way of life, not one-time decision.
- Key finding 4: Hupomone is active, defiant, hope-filled endurance (NOT passive resignation). Etymologically hupo + meno = "staying under" a burden. Every passage shows forward movement and refusal to surrender.
- Key finding 5: Rev 12:11's three-fold mechanism (blood of the Lamb FIRST, word of testimony, willingness to die) makes the order clear: the blood is the objective basis, endurance is the response.
- Key finding 6: The nikao-hupomone relationship -- endurance and overcoming are two aspects of the same reality. The saints who endure in 14:12 are the overcomers of 15:2.
- Open question: The content of "the commandments of God" and "the faith of Jesus" is left for 3am-19 to address.

**3am-08-worship-the-creator:**
- Question: Why does the first angel use the specific creation formula "him that made heaven, and earth, and the sea"?
- Key finding 1: Rev 14:7 echoes Exo 20:11 through the LXX -- four shared terms in sequence: poieo (made), ouranos (heaven), ge (earth), thalassa (sea). The verb poieo is deliberately chosen over ktizo, aligning with the Sabbath commandment's creation rationale.
- Key finding 2: The poieo/ktizo distinction is decisive: generic creation language in Revelation uses ktizo (Rev 4:11, 10:6); only Rev 14:7 uses poieo -- the verb mapping to Exo 20:11 LXX.
- Key finding 3: The commandment inclusio -- Rev 11:19 (ark of the covenant revealed) -> 12:17 ("keep the commandments of God") -> 14:7 (creation formula echoing 4th commandment) -> 14:12 ("keep the commandments of God and the faith of Jesus").
- Key finding 4: Entole (G1785) is used without qualifier in Rev 12:17 and 14:12. Prior law-28 study established unqualified entole = Decalogue content in 43 of 43 identifiable NT instances. Nomos (G3551) never appears in Revelation.
- Key finding 5: Luke 23:56 applies entole directly to the Sabbath commandment -- "rested the sabbath day according to the commandment (kata ten entolen)." This collapses the Tinker/Answering Adventism entole vs. nomos distinction claim.
- Key finding 6: The Ecclesiastes 12:13-14 template -- "Fear God + keep commandments + judgment" -- maps directly onto Rev 14:7,12.

### From Semantic Search (Additional)

**law-28-revelation-commandments:** (score: 0.712)
- Question: "What commandments are in Revelation and what do they mean? Does 'commandments of God' (entolas tou theou) refer to the Decalogue?"
- Key finding 1: Entole (G1785) unqualified = moral/Decalogue content in 43/43 identifiable NT instances. Definite article (tas) marks a specific, known body of commands.
- Key finding 2: NT vocabulary partition -- entole = moral commands, dogma = abolished, entalma = human precepts. No overlap.
- Key finding 3: 1 Cor 7:19 uses identical phrase "entolas tou theou" while explicitly excluding circumcision (ceremonial).
- Key finding 4: Rev 22:15's excluded sins (murder, idolatry, lying) correspond to specific Decalogue violations.
- Key finding 5: The dual identification formula -- "commandments of God" NEVER stands alone in Revelation; always paired with testimony/faith of Jesus (Rev 12:17, 14:12, 22:14).
- Key finding 6: Pistis (G4102) in Rev 14:12 -- whether subjective or objective genitive, it pairs with commandment-keeping. The dual formula means saints are neither legalists (commandments without faith) nor antinomians (faith without commandments).
- I-B Resolution: "Commandments of God = generic instruction (not Decalogue)" resolved Strong AGAINST -- three Plain items (E8/1 Cor 7:19, E16/Rom 7:12, N097/43-of-43 pattern) determine the reading.

**gsp-06-obedience-gospel-fruit:** (score: 0.719)
- Question: "How does obedience relate to salvation? Is calling people to obey God 'legalism' or 'gospel fruit'?"
- Key finding 1: The faith-love-law chain verified in Greek: faith works by love (Gal 5:6) -> love = keeping commandments (1 Jhn 5:3) -> love fulfills the law (Rom 13:10) -> law fulfilled in Spirit-walkers (Rom 8:4) -> faith establishes the law (Rom 3:31).
- Key finding 2: 1 Cor 7:19 parallel to Gal 5:6 -- same sentence frame with two fill-ins: "faith working by love" = "keeping the commandments of God." Paul treats them as equivalent.
- Key finding 3: The hupakoe pisteos inclusio (Rom 1:5, 16:26) frames all of Romans. The genitive pisteos is deliberately multi-layered: "obedience which IS faith" AND "obedience which springs FROM faith."
- Key finding 4: Apeitheo (G544) means both "disbelieve" and "disobey" -- the biblical languages do not separate these concepts. John 3:36 sets pisteuo vs. apeitheo as direct antonyms.
- Key finding 5: The Spirit-enablement mechanism eliminates the legalism charge: Eze 36:27 (God causes obedience through Spirit), Rom 8:4 (divine passive -- God fulfills law's requirement IN believers), Php 2:13 (God works both willing and doing).
- Key finding 6: Works excluded as CAUSE (Eph 2:8-9, erga nomou), affirmed as PURPOSE (Eph 2:10, erga agatha). The vocabulary distinction is visible only in Greek.

**cmd-15-faith-grace-and-obedience:** (score: 0.631)
- Question: "What does the Bible say about the relationship between faith, grace, and commandment-keeping?"
- Key finding 1: 99 explicit statements, 9 necessary implications, 8 I-A inferences -- all pointing the same direction: faith and obedience are inseparable.
- Key finding 2: Paul's five me genoito denials form a wall against antinomian readings: Rom 3:31, Rom 6:1-2, Rom 6:15, Gal 2:17, Gal 3:21.
- Key finding 3: Abraham demonstrates the faith-then-obedience pattern: Gen 15:6 (faith counted as righteousness) THEN Gen 26:5 (Abraham kept commandments, statutes, laws) -- three NT authors cite both.
- Key finding 4: Grace is both forensic (declaring righteous) and pedagogical (Tit 2:11-12 -- grace TEACHES denial of ungodliness). Grace produces, not prevents, good works.
- Key finding 5: Rev 14:12 serves as eschatological confirmation: end-time saints identified by BOTH commandments AND faith, held together without subordinating either.

**gsp-04-law-and-gospel:** (score: 0.612)
- Question: "Does the gospel abolish God's law, or establish it?"
- Key finding 1: Telos (G5056) in Rom 10:4 means "goal" not "termination" -- 90% purpose/goal vs. 10% cessation in NT usage. The 1 Tim 1:5 parallel is decisive.
- Key finding 2: The NT cessation/continuation vocabulary pattern holds without exception: cessation vocabulary (dogma, cheirographon, skia) always = ceremonial; continuation vocabulary (entole, nomos unqualified) always = moral.
- Key finding 3: The new covenant (Jer 31:33, Heb 8:10) writes the SAME law on hearts -- same content, new surface, new power source. The verb kathab connects Exo 31:18, Jer 31:33, and 2 Cor 3:3.
- Key finding 4: Paul's ennomos Christou (1 Cor 9:21) -- "in-lawed to Christ" -- the en- prefix directly opposes a- prefix (anomos). Paul is within the law's framework through Christ.

**rev-13-14-commandments:** (score: 0.633)
- Question: "In Revelation 13 and 14, are any commandments being broken, kept, or told to keep?"
- Key finding 1: The beast system systematically violates at least 6 Decalogue commandments: 1st (false worship), 2nd (image worship), 3rd (blasphemy), 4th (counterfeit seal/mark), 6th (death decree), 9th (deception).
- Key finding 2: The first angel's three aorist imperatives (phobethete, dote, proskynegate) are commandment-keeping instructions, not optional suggestions.
- Key finding 3: Proskyneo (G4352) appears 8 times in Rev 13-14 -- the entire two-chapter drama is a contest over the direction of worship. Same verb describes both the sin (beast worship) and the obedience (Creator worship).
- Key finding 4: The hupomone inclusio (13:10 + 14:12) makes commandment-keeping the essential CONTENT of Christian endurance during the beast's reign.
- Key finding 5: The "faith of Jesus" (ten pistin Iesou) is genuinely grammatically ambiguous. The study notes a "plenary genitive" may encompass both objective (faith directed toward Jesus) and subjective (faith modeled after His faithfulness).

## Web Research Findings

The web research agent (Phase 0) produced extensive findings in `00-web-research.md`. Key highlights:

### Pistis Iesou Debate
- **Tonstad (2005, St. Andrews PhD):** Argues subjective genitive within Revelation's theodicy framework -- Jesus' faithfulness vindicates God's character in cosmic conflict. Rev 14:12 is his thesis centerpiece.
- **Easter (2010, Currents in Biblical Research):** Comprehensive survey concluding grammar alone cannot resolve the debate. "Attempts at reading Paul grammatically and syntactically without a wider theological lens have fallen flat."
- **Hays (1983):** Redundancy argument -- if pistis Christou = "faith in Christ," then Rom 3:22 becomes redundant with "to all who believe."
- **Most older commentators (Gill, JFB, Vincent):** Default to objective genitive ("faith in Jesus").
- **Both-and gaining traction:** Jesus' own faithfulness as the ground; believer's faith in Him as the response.

### Entole/Nomos Distinction (Counter-argument)
- **Tinker (Proclamation! Magazine, 2019):** Charges entole in John = Jesus's teachings only; nomos = the Law. Therefore "commandments of God" cannot mean the Decalogue.
- **Answering Adventism website:** Same argument -- "word association fallacy."
- **REBUTTAL (Advent Defense League, verified):** Rom 7:7-11 (Paul quotes 10th commandment and calls it entole), Eph 6:2 (5th commandment called entole), Luke 23:56 (Sabbath called entole). The Rev 11:19 ark imagery further clinches it.

### Abrahamic Faith-Obedience Pattern
- **Howard (Tyndale House, 2025):** Abraham's faith (Gen 15:6) preceded obedience chronologically. James 2:22 -- "faith was active along with his works, and faith was completed by his works." Gen 26:5 uses comprehensive obedience vocabulary: mishmeret, mitsvot, chuqqot, torot.

### Hupakoe Pisteos (Obedience of Faith)
- **Garlington (WTJ):** Unique Pauline coinage. Deliberately ambiguous -- denotes simultaneously "the obedience which is faith" AND "the obedience which is the product of faith." The phrase bridges "present justification by faith alone and future judgment according to works."
- **Wilkin (Grace Evangelical Society):** Counter-position -- appositional genitive; faith and obedience are equivalent, limiting "obedience" to obeying the command to believe.

### Legalism Objection
- Multiple scholars across theological traditions agree on the formal distinction between legalism (earning salvation) and obedience (fruit of salvation).
- The grammatical observation that terountes (present active participle) is descriptive, not imperative, supports the descriptive reading of Rev 14:12.
