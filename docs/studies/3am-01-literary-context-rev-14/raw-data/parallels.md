# Cross-Testament Parallels

- Rev 11:19 → Rev 16:18 (nt, vocabulary (earthquake, lightning, thunder, great))
- Rev 11:19 → Rev 8:5 (nt, vocabulary + structural (same theophany phenomena; altar-censer scene))
- Rev 11:19 → Rev 15:5 (nt, structural (temple opened in heaven; bookend pair))
- Rev 11:19 → Exo 19:16-19 (ot, vocabulary + theological (Sinai theophany parallel — thunders, lightnings, earthquake))
- Rev 11:19 → 1 Chr 16:6 (ot, vocabulary (ark, covenant))
- Rev 12:7 → Rev 13:4 (nt, vocabulary + structural (dragon, war — beast-worship response to dragon's power))
- Rev 12:7 → Rev 12:17 (nt, structural (dragon's war continues from heaven to remnant))
- Rev 12:7 → Dan 12:1 (ot, vocabulary + theological (Michael as warrior-champion))
- Rev 13:5 → Rev 13:7 (nt, vocabulary (edothe divine passive, authority))
- Rev 13:5 → Dan 7:8 (ot, verbatim quotation (stoma laloun megala))
- Rev 13:5 → Dan 7:25 (ot, vocabulary (time duration — 42 months = time, times, half a time))
- Rev 14:6 → Rev 18:1 (nt, vocabulary + structural (angel + another + saw — scene markers))
- Rev 14:6 → Rev 8:13 (nt, vocabulary (angel/eagle, fly, mid-heaven, dwell))
- Rev 14:6 → Luke 1:19 (nt, vocabulary (angel, tidings — Gabriel brings good tidings))
- Rev 14:6 → Mat 28:19 (nt, theological (universal gospel commission to all nations))
- Rev 14:14 → Dan 7:13 (ot, vocabulary + structural (cloud, Son of Man, like — direct OT allusion))
- Rev 14:14 → Rev 10:1 (nt, vocabulary (cloud, head, like))
- Rev 14:14 → Rev 1:13 (nt, vocabulary (like, Son of Man figure))
- Rev 14:19 → Rev 14:18 (nt, vocabulary (angel, sickle, gather — grape harvest sequence))
- Rev 14:19 → Joel 3:13 (ot, vocabulary + structural (sickle, harvest, winepress — OT bifurcation source))
- Rev 14:19 → Isa 63:2-3 (ot, vocabulary + theological (winepress treading, divine warrior, blood/garments))
- Rev 14:8 → Rev 18:2 (nt, vocabulary (Babylon, fallen, great))
- Rev 14:8 → Jer 51:7-8 (ot, verbatim quotation (nations drunk on wine, Babylon fallen))
- Rev 14:8 → Isa 21:9 (ot, vocabulary (Babylon is fallen, is fallen — doubled formula))
