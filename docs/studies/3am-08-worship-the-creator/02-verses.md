# Verses

## Question
Why does the first angel call for worship of the Creator using the specific formula 'him that made heaven, and earth, and the sea, and the fountains of waters'? Is this a direct quotation of Exodus 20:11? Verbal comparison through LXX — analyze poieo/asah bridge. Compare with other creation-worship texts (Neh 9:6, Acts 4:24, Acts 14:15, Psa 146:6). In a passage concluding with 'the commandments of God' (Rev 14:12), how does the creation formula link to the Sabbath commandment? Address the objection that this is generic creation theology. Creation-to-new-creation bookend (Gen 2:2-3 to Isa 66:22-23). Hebrews 4:9 sabbatismos.

### The First Angel's Message — Creation Formula and the Call to Worship

> And I saw another angel fly in the midst of heaven, having the everlasting gospel to preach unto them that dwell on the earth, and to every nation, and kindred, and tongue, and people, Saying with a loud voice, Fear God, and give glory to him; for the hour of his judgment is come: and worship him that made heaven, and earth, and the sea, and the fountains of waters.

**Context:** The first of three angels delivers the everlasting gospel. The three imperatives (fear, give glory, worship) form the message content. The creation formula 'him that made heaven, and earth, and the sea, and the fountains of waters' echoes Exo 20:11 through the LXX. This verse uses poieo (G4160), not ktizo (G2936).

**Notes:** The dative articular aorist active participle 'to poiesanti' (V-AAP-DSM) identifies the One worshipped by His creative act. The four objects (ton ouranon, ten gen, thalassan, pegas hydaton) share three of four elements with Exo 20:11 LXX. The fourth element diverges: 'pegas hydaton' replaces 'panta ta en autois.' All three imperatives are aorist — decisive, punctiliar commands. The legon participle (V-PAP-NSM) connecting v.7 to v.6 makes the worship command the CONTENT of the everlasting gospel.

### The Fourth Commandment — Creation Rationale for the Sabbath

> Remember the sabbath day, to keep it holy. Six days shalt thou labour, and do all thy work: But the seventh day [is] the sabbath of the LORD thy God: [in it] thou shalt not do any work, thou, nor thy son, nor thy daughter, thy manservant, nor thy maidservant, nor thy cattle, nor thy stranger that [is] within thy gates: For [in] six days the LORD made heaven and earth, the sea, and all that in them [is], and rested the seventh day: wherefore the LORD blessed the sabbath day, and hallowed it.

**Context:** The fourth of the Ten Commandments. The creation rationale (v.11) grounds Sabbath observance in God's creative act. The Hebrew uses asah (H6213) — 'the LORD made (asah) heaven and earth.' The LXX renders asah as poieo (epoiese), the same verb root Rev 14:7 uses (poiesanti). This is the asah/poieo bridge that links the two texts.

**Notes:** Exo 20:11 LXX: epoiese (aorist active indicative of poieo) Kyrios ton ouranon kai ten gen kai ten thalassan kai panta ta en autois. Compare Rev 14:7: poiesanti (aorist active participle of poieo) ton ouranon kai ten gen kai thalassan kai pegas hydaton. Four shared terms in sequence: poieo, ouranon, gen, thalassan. The verb choice is the decisive datum — the LXX renders H6213 asah as G4160 poieo, and Rev 14:7 picks up the LXX verb, not bara/ktizo.

### Creation Formula Parallel — 'Made Heaven, Earth, Sea, and All Therein'

> Happy [is he] that [hath] the God of Jacob for his help, whose hope [is] in the LORD his God: Which made heaven, and earth, the sea, and all that therein [is]: which keepeth truth for ever:

**Context:** A creation-worship psalm using the exact formula of Exo 20:11. The LXX uses poiesanta ton ouranon kai ten gen ten thalassan kai panta ta en autois — concluding with 'panta ta en autois' (all that is in them), not 'pegas hydaton.' This confirms that the standard creation formula includes 'all in them,' making Rev 14:7's substitution conspicuous.

**Notes:** One of three parallel texts (with Acts 4:24 and Acts 14:15) that faithfully reproduce the Exo 20:11 formula including 'panta ta en autois.' Rev 14:7 alone departs from this pattern.

### Creation Formula in Early Church Prayer — Follows Exo 20:11 Exactly

> And when they heard that, they lifted up their voice to God with one accord, and said, Lord, thou [art] God, which hast made heaven, and earth, and the sea, and all that in them is:

**Context:** The early church prays to the Creator using the Exo 20:11 formula. The Greek follows the LXX exactly: ton poiesanta ton ouranon kai ten gen kai ten thalassan kai panta ta en autois. This is the most exact NT reproduction of the Sabbath commandment's creation rationale.

**Notes:** Confirms the standard NT creation formula uses 'panta ta en autois.' Acts 4:24 and 14:15 both use this completion phrase; Rev 14:7 alone substitutes 'pegas hydaton.'

### Paul and Barnabas at Lystra — Same Creation Formula

> And saying, Sirs, why do ye these things? We also are men of like passions with you, and preach unto you that ye should turn from these vanities unto the living God, which made heaven, and earth, and the sea, and all things that are therein:

**Context:** Paul redirects worship from himself to the living God who made heaven, earth, the sea, and all things therein. Uses the same Exo 20:11 creation formula as Acts 4:24.

**Notes:** Third parallel text confirming the standard formula. The pattern is consistent: Psa 146:6, Acts 4:24, Acts 14:15 all use 'panta ta en autois.' Rev 14:7's departure is deliberate.

### The Most Expansive Creation-Worship Formula

> Thou, [even] thou, [art] LORD alone; thou hast made heaven, the heaven of heavens, with all their host, the earth, and all [things] that [are] therein, the seas, and all that [is] therein, and thou preservest them all; and the host of heaven worshippeth thee.

**Context:** Levitical prayer expanding the creation formula to include the heaven of heavens and their host. The most detailed version of the formula in the OT, connecting creation to worship explicitly.

**Notes:** The most expansive OT version of the creation formula. Uses asah (made) with heaven, earth, seas, and all therein. Adds 'thou preservest them all' — sustaining power alongside creative power.

### Paul at Athens — Closest NT Parallel to Rev 14:7

> God that made the world and all things therein, seeing that he is Lord of heaven and earth, dwelleth not in temples made with hands; Neither is worshipped with men's hands, as though he needed any thing, seeing he giveth to all life, and breath, and all things; And hath made of one blood all nations of men for to dwell on all the face of the earth, and hath determined the times before appointed, and the bounds of their habitation; That they should seek the Lord, if haply they might feel after him, and find him, though he be not far from every one of us: For in him we live, and move, and have our being; as certain also of your own poets have said, For we are also his offspring. Forasmuch then as we are the offspring of God, we ought not to think that the Godhead is like unto gold, or silver, or stone, graven by art and man's device. And the times of this ignorance God winked at; but now commandeth all men every where to repent: Because he hath appointed a day, in the which he will judge the world in righteousness by [that] man whom he hath ordained; [whereof] he hath given assurance unto all [men], in that he hath raised him from the dead.

**Context:** Paul at the Areopagus combines Creator God (v.24), universal scope (v.26,30), and appointed judgment day (v.31) — the exact triad of the first angel's message. Uses poieo (G4160) for God's creative act (v.24). The closest NT parallel to Rev 14:7's combination of creation, worship, and judgment.

**Notes:** Both Acts 17:24 and Rev 14:7 use poieo for God's creative act and identify God as Lord of heaven and earth. The judgment announcement (v.31: 'appointed a day to judge') parallels Rev 14:7 ('the hour of his judgment is come'). This is the NT sermon most structurally parallel to the first angel's message.

### The Creation Sabbath — God Rested, Blessed, and Sanctified the Seventh Day

> Thus the heavens and the earth were finished, and all the host of them. And on the seventh day God ended his work which he had made; and he rested on the seventh day from all his work which he had made. And God blessed the seventh day, and sanctified it: because that in it he had rested from all his work which God created and made.

**Context:** The institution of the Sabbath at creation, before sin, before Israel, before the ceremonial law. Three divine acts on the seventh day: shabath (ceased/rested, H7673 Qal.Wayq.3ms), barak (blessed, Piel.Wayq.3ms), and qadash (sanctified, Piel.Wayq.3ms). Gen 2:3 uniquely pairs bara (create) and asah (make) in one verse.

**Notes:** The Hebrew parsing confirms: wayyishbot (Qal wayyiqtol of shabath) = 'and he ceased'; wayebarek (Piel wayyiqtol of barak) = 'and he blessed'; wayeqaddesh (Piel wayyiqtol of qadash) = 'and he sanctified.' The three-fold action sequence establishes the seventh day's unique status at creation itself.

### Sabbath as Owth (Sign) — Perpetual Covenant

> Speak thou also unto the children of Israel, saying, Verily my sabbaths ye shall keep: for it [is] a sign between me and you throughout your generations; that [ye] may know that I [am] the LORD that doth sanctify you. Ye shall keep the sabbath therefore; for it [is] holy unto you: every one that defileth it shall surely be put to death: for whosoever doeth [any] work therein, that soul shall be cut off from among his people. Six days may work be done; but in the seventh [is] the sabbath of rest, holy to the LORD: whosoever doeth [any] work in the sabbath day, he shall surely be put to death. Wherefore the children of Israel shall keep the sabbath, to observe the sabbath throughout their generations, [for] a perpetual covenant. It [is] a sign between me and the children of Israel for ever: for [in] six days the LORD made heaven and earth, and on the seventh day he rested, and was refreshed.

**Context:** The Sabbath explicitly designated as owth (sign, H226) in v.13 and v.17 — the only commandment to receive this designation. The reason given in v.17 echoes the creation rationale of Exo 20:11: 'for in six days the LORD made (asah) heaven and earth.' Called a 'perpetual covenant' (berith olam) in v.16.

**Notes:** Hebrew parsing of Exo 31:17 confirms: owth (sign, Noun.s.Abs) + ki clause (because) + asah (Verb.Qal.Perf.3ms = made) YHWH + et-hashamayim ve'et-ha'arets (the heavens and the earth). The creation rationale is identical to Exo 20:11. The Sabbath is the ONLY commandment explicitly called owth in the OT.

### Alternative Sabbath Rationale — Deliverance, Not Creation

> Keep the sabbath day to sanctify it, as the LORD thy God hath commanded thee. Six days thou shalt labour, and do all thy work: But the seventh day [is] the sabbath of the LORD thy God: [in it] thou shalt not do any work, thou, nor thy son, nor thy daughter, nor thy manservant, nor thy maidservant, nor thine ox, nor thine ass, nor any of thy cattle, nor thy stranger that [is] within thy gates; that thy manservant and thy maidservant may rest as well as thou. And remember that thou wast a servant in the land of Egypt, and [that] the LORD thy God brought thee out thence through a mighty hand and by a stretched out arm: therefore the LORD thy God commanded thee to keep the sabbath day.

**Context:** Deuteronomy's version of the fourth commandment replaces the creation rationale with a deliverance rationale. The Sabbath here commemorates the Exodus. Rev 14:7 echoes the creation rationale (Exo 20:11), not the deliverance rationale (Deut 5:15), which is significant for identifying the specific allusion.

**Notes:** The use of zakar (remember, H2142) in both Exo 20:8 and Deut 5:15 frames the Sabbath as a memorial institution pointing backward, not a shadow pointing forward.

### The Ark of the Covenant Revealed — Activating the Commandment Theme

> And the temple of God was opened in heaven, and there was seen in his temple the ark of his testament: and there were lightnings, and voices, and thunderings, and an earthquake, and great hail.

**Context:** The heavenly temple opens, revealing the ark of the covenant (containing the Ten Commandments). This activates the commandment theme that runs through Rev 12:17 to 14:12, establishing the Decalogue as the backdrop for the three angels' messages.

**Notes:** The covenant-law thread: diatheke (11:19) → entole (12:17) → entole (14:12). The ark revelation at 11:19 begins the cosmic conflict unit; the commandments mentioned at 12:17 and 14:12 reference the law contained in that ark.

### The Remnant Keeps the Commandments of God

> And the dragon was wroth with the woman, and went to make war with the remnant of her seed, which keep the commandments of God, and have the testimony of Jesus Christ.

**Context:** The remnant is identified by two characteristics: keeping the commandments of God (entolas tou theou) and having the testimony of Jesus. This is the first occurrence of 'commandments of God' after the ark revelation at 11:19.

**Notes:** Greek parsing confirms: ton terounton (V-PAP-GPM, present active participle genitive plural of tereo = the ones keeping/guarding) tas entolas (N-APF, accusative plural of entole = the commandments) tou theou (N-GSM, genitive singular of theos = of God). The present participle indicates ongoing, continuous keeping. The parallel with Rev 14:12 is structural: both use tereo + entolas + tou theou.

### The Inclusio — Commandments of God and Faith of Jesus

> Here is the patience of the saints: here [are] they that keep the commandments of God, and the faith of Jesus.

**Context:** The concluding verse of the three angels' messages. Forms an inclusio with Rev 14:7: the creation formula (echoing the fourth commandment) is bookended by explicit mention of 'the commandments of God.' The two-part structure (commandments of God AND faith of Jesus) distinguishes divine commands from Jesus-specific content.

**Notes:** Greek parsing confirms: hoi terountes (V-PAP-NPM, present active participle nominative plural of tereo = the ones keeping) tas entolas (N-APF, accusative plural of entole = the commandments) tou theou (N-GSM) kai ten pistin (N-ASF, accusative singular of pistis = the faith) Iesou (N-GSM = of Jesus). The kai linking 'commandments of God' with 'faith of Jesus' uses two distinct accusative objects, showing these are two different categories of loyalty.

### The Sabbatismos Argument — A Sabbath-Rest Remains

> Let us therefore fear, lest, a promise being left [us] of entering into his rest, any of you should seem to come short of it. For unto us was the gospel preached, as well as unto them: but the word preached did not profit them, not being mixed with faith in them that heard [it]. For we which have believed do enter into rest, as he said, As I have sworn in my wrath, if they shall enter into my rest: although the works were finished from the foundation of the world. For he spake in a certain place of the seventh [day] on this wise, And God did rest the seventh day from all his works. And in this [place] again, If they shall enter into my rest. Seeing therefore it remaineth that some must enter therein, and they to whom it was first preached entered not in because of unbelief: Again, he limiteth a certain day, saying in David, To day, after so long a time; as it is said, To day if ye will hear his voice, harden not your hearts. For if Jesus had given them rest, then would he not afterward have spoken of another day. There remaineth therefore a rest to the people of God. For he that is entered into his rest, he also hath ceased from his own works, as God [did] from his. Let us labour therefore to enter into that rest, lest any man fall after the same example of unbelief.

**Context:** The full argument flow: creation rest (v.4, Gen 2:2), Promised Land rest (vv.6-8), Davidic 'today' (v.7, Psa 95:11), and the remaining sabbatismos (v.9). The author uses katapausis (G2663) eight times but shifts to sabbatismos (G4520) in v.9 — a deliberate intensification, not stylistic variation. V.10 draws the hosper comparison: believers cease from works 'as God did from his.'

**Notes:** Heb 4:9 parsing: ara (particle, 'therefore') apoleipetai (V-PPI-3S, present passive indicative of apoleipo = remains/is left behind) sabbatismos (N-NSM, nominative singular of sabbatismos = Sabbath-keeping) to lao (N-DSM, dative singular = to the people) tou theou (N-GSM = of God). The -ismos suffix denotes the ACT of doing something (cf. baptismos). Lincoln concedes every extant use of sabbatismos denotes Sabbath observance. Heb 4:10: hosper (ADV, 'just as') comparison — the believer's rest is explicitly patterned on God's seventh-day rest.

### Sabbath as Sign — 'That They Might Know I Am the LORD'

> Moreover also I gave them my sabbaths, to be a sign between me and them, that they might know that I [am] the LORD that sanctify them.

**Context:** God designates the Sabbath as owth (sign) in the context of covenant history. The purpose clause ('that they might know') establishes the Sabbath's function as a revelatory sign identifying YHWH as the one who sanctifies.

**Notes:** Paired with Eze 20:20 which repeats the owth designation. The Sabbath is explicitly called owth in Exo 31:13, 31:17, Eze 20:12, and Eze 20:20 — four times. No other commandment receives this designation.

### Sabbath as Sign Between God and His People

> And hallow my sabbaths; and they shall be a sign between me and you, that ye may know that I [am] the LORD your God.

**Context:** Reaffirms the Sabbath as owth (sign) in the prophetic context. The sign function identifies the relationship between Creator and creature.

**Notes:** The language 'between me and you' echoes the covenant sign language of Exo 31:13,17. The four-fold owth designation (Exo 31:13, 31:17, Eze 20:12, 20:20) makes the Sabbath the most explicitly designated sign-commandment.

And there was given unto him a mouth speaking great things and blasphemies; and power was given unto him to continue forty [and] two months. And all that dwell upon the earth shall worship him, whose names are not written in the book of life of the Lamb slain from the foundation of the world.

**Context:** The beast demands exclusive worship (counterfeiting the first commandment) and blasphemes God's name (counterfeiting the third commandment). Proskyneo (G4352) dominates: the same verb used in Rev 14:7 for worshipping the Creator.

**Notes:** Rev 13:4 uses proskyneo twice for beast-worship. Rev 14:7 uses proskyneo for Creator-worship. The worship binary is explicit: worship the beast (Rev 13) or worship the Creator (Rev 14:7).

### Image of the Beast — Counterfeit of the Second Commandment

> And deceiveth them that dwell on the earth by [the means of] those miracles which he had power to do in the sight of the beast; saying to them that dwell on the earth, that they should make an image to the beast, which had the wound by a sword, and did live. **Notes:** The image-worship pattern mirrors Daniel 3's manufactured image + universal worship command + death penalty. Rev 13:15 uses eikon (G1504) for the beast's image, the same word used in the second commandment prohibition.

### Mark of the Beast — Counterfeit Seal Against the Fourth Commandment

> And he causeth all, both small and great, rich and poor, free and bond, to receive a mark in their right hand, or in their foreheads: And that no man might buy or sell, save he that had the mark, or the name of the beast, or the number of his name.

**Context:** The mark (charagma, G5480) functions as a counterfeit of the seal of God (Rev 7:3). If the Sabbath is God's sign/seal (Exo 31:13,17; Eze 20:12,20), the mark represents a counterfeit worship identity.

**Notes:** The seal/mark binary: Rev 7:3 (seal of God on foreheads) vs. Rev 13:16-17 (mark of the beast on hand/forehead). The Sabbath-as-sign tradition (Exo 31, Eze 20) informs this contrast if the first angel's creation formula points to the fourth commandment.

### Third Angel's Warning Against Beast Worship

> And the third angel followed them, saying with a loud voice, If any man worship the beast and his image, and receive [his] mark in his forehead, or in his hand, The same shall drink of the wine of the wrath of God, which is poured out without mixture into the cup of his indignation; and he shall be tormented with fire and brimstone in the presence of the holy angels, and in the presence of the Lamb: And the smoke of their torment ascendeth up for ever and ever: and they have no rest day nor night, who worship the beast and his image, and whosoever receiveth the mark of his name.

**Context:** The consequences of wrong worship. The three-part description (beast + image + mark) inverts the first angel's three-part command (fear God + give glory + worship Creator). 'No rest day nor night' contrasts with the Sabbath rest.

**Notes:** 'No rest' (ouk echousin anapausin) in 14:11 for beast-worshippers contrasts with the sabbatismos (rest) remaining for God's people (Heb 4:9). Anapausis (G372) is used, a rest-family word.

### New Heavens and New Earth — Sabbath in the New Creation

> For as the new heavens and the new earth, which I will make, shall remain before me, saith the LORD, so shall your seed and your name remain. And it shall come to pass, [that] from one new moon to another, and from one sabbath to another, shall all flesh come to worship before me, saith the LORD.

**Context:** Prophetic vision of worship in the new creation: from sabbath to sabbath, all flesh shall worship before YHWH. Creates the creation-to-new-creation bookend with Gen 2:2-3. However, the passage also mentions New Moons and v.24 (dead bodies) complicates a fully literal reading.

**Notes:** The Sabbath appears at creation (Gen 2:2-3), at Sinai (Exo 20:8-11), and in the new creation (Isa 66:22-23) — a bookend structure suggesting permanence. Paulien cautions against over-reading this passage: the New Moon problem and Isa 66:24 (gazing at dead bodies) complicate a purely literal interpretation. The study should note this honestly.

### Ktizo in Revelation — Verb Distinction with Rev 14:7

> And sware by him that liveth for ever and ever, who created heaven, and the things that therein are, and the earth, and the things that therein are, and the sea, and the things which are therein, that there should be time no longer:

**Context:** The mighty angel swears by the Creator. Uses ktizo (G2936) — 'who created (ektisen) heaven.' Rev 14:7 uses poieo (poiesanti) for the same concept. Both describe God as Creator of heaven, earth, and sea. The verb switch within the same book is significant.

**Notes:** Greek parsing confirms: ektisen (V-AAI-3S, aorist active indicative of ktizo G2936). Compare Rev 14:7 poiesanti (V-AAP-DSM, aorist active participle of poieo G4160). Rev 10:6 uses ktizo; Rev 14:7 deliberately switches to poieo. Gen 1-2 LXX and Exo 20:11 LXX use epoiesen (poieo), not ektisen. Mueller: the poieo/ktizo distinction shows Rev 14:7 deliberately echoes Exo 20:11 LXX.

### Fountains of the Great Deep — Possible Source for 'Pegas Hydaton'

> In the six hundredth year of Noah's life, in the second month, the seventeenth day of the month, the same day were all the fountains of the great deep broken up, and the windows of heaven were opened.

**Context:** The Flood narrative mentions 'fountains of the great deep.' If the LXX uses pege/pegas for 'fountains,' Rev 14:7's 'pegas hydaton' may layer a Flood judgment allusion onto the creation allusion. Baldwin identifies this as the key departure from the standard creation formula.

**Notes:** The Hebrew uses ma'yanoth (from ma'yan, H4599 — the primary Hebrew equivalent of pege G4077 per LXX mapping with PMI 8.15). Baldwin argues Rev 14:7 substitutes 'pegas hydaton' for 'panta ta en autois' to add a Flood judgment allusion alongside the creation allusion.

### The Structural Template — Fear God + Commandments + Judgment

> Let us hear the conclusion of the whole matter: Fear God, and keep his commandments: for this [is] the whole [duty] of man. For God shall bring every work into judgment, with every secret thing, whether [it be] good, or whether [it be] evil.

**Context:** Solomon's conclusion matches the first angel's message precisely: fear God (Ecc 12:13a = Rev 14:7a), keep commandments (Ecc 12:13b = Rev 14:12), judgment (Ecc 12:14 = Rev 14:7b). Four OT farewell addresses give the same triad; the first angel's message is the fifth.

**Notes:** The structural parallel is threefold: (1) fear God — yare in Ecc 12:13, phobeo in Rev 14:7; (2) keep commandments — mitsvah in Ecc 12:13, entole in Rev 14:12; (3) judgment — mishpat in Ecc 12:14, krisis in Rev 14:7. This pattern confirms the first angel's message is not a novel formulation but follows a recognizable OT template.

### Entole Applied to the Sabbath Commandment

> And they returned, and prepared spices and ointments; and rested the sabbath day according to the commandment.

**Context:** The women 'rested the sabbath day according to the commandment (kata ten entolen).' This directly applies entole (G1785) to the Sabbath commandment, collapsing Tinker's claim that John uses entole only for Jesus's teachings and nomos for the Mosaic Law.

**Notes:** Greek parsing confirms: sabbaton (N-ASN, G4521 = the Sabbath) hesychasan (V-AAI-3P = they rested) kata (PREP = according to) ten entolen (N-ASF, G1785 = the commandment). This is the single most decisive verse against the nomos/entole distinction: entole refers directly to the Sabbath commandment.

### Sabbath Days as Shadow — The Objection Text

> Let no man therefore judge you in meat, or in drink, or in respect of an holyday, or of the new moon, or of the sabbath [days]: Which are a shadow of things to come; but the body [is] of Christ.

**Context:** The primary objection text against ongoing Sabbath obligation. 'Sabbath days' (sabbaton, G4521) appears in a triad with 'holyday' (heorte, G1859) and 'new moon' (neomenia, G3561) — the annual/monthly/weekly ceremonial calendar triad from 1 Chr 23:31 and 2 Chr 31:3. These are called 'a shadow (skia, G4639) of things to come.'

**Notes:** Greek parsing of Col 2:16 confirms sabbaton (N-GPN, genitive plural) in the triad heortes (G1859) — neomenias (G3561) — sabbaton (G4521). Col 2:17 confirms skia (N-NSF, G4639 = shadow) ton mellonton (V-PAP-GPN = of things to come). The prior sabbath-shadow-or-memorial study established five criteria the weekly Sabbath fails for being a biblical shadow: it points backward (zakar), is the original, remains after the cross (Heb 4:9), belongs to the Decalogue, and is called 'perpetual covenant.' Lev 23:37-38 separates ceremonial sabbaths from 'the sabbaths of the LORD' using millibad (H905).

### No Need for Sun/Moon — The Greek Is Decisive

> And the city had no need of the sun, neither of the moon, to shine in it: for the glory of God did lighten it, and the Lamb [is] the light thereof.

**Context:** The Answering Adventism objection claims Rev 21:23 says there IS no sun or moon, making Isa 66:23's New Moon worship impossible. But the Greek text says the city 'has no NEED (ou chreian echei) of the sun... to shine in it (hina phainosin aute).' The text says no need for their light function, not that they cease to exist.

**Notes:** Greek parsing is decisive: he polis ou chreian echei (the city has no need) tou heliou (of the sun) oude (nor) tes selenes (of the moon) hina phainosin aute (in order that they might shine in it). The construction is: no need + genitive of thing not needed + hina purpose clause (to shine). The text does NOT say the sun and moon cease to exist — it says the city does not need them as light sources because God's glory illuminates it. This leaves open the possibility of New Moon and Sabbath observance in the new earth.

**Context:** The Hebrews author quotes Gen 2:2, grounding the rest argument in the creation Sabbath. The seventh day is explicitly named as the archetype for the rest that 'remains.'

### Sabbatismos — A Sabbath-Keeping Remains for the People of God

> There remaineth therefore a rest to the people of God.

**Context:** The NT hapax sabbatismos (G4520). The author shifts from katapausis (used 8x in context) to sabbatismos at the climactic verse. Every extant use of sabbatismos in Greek literature denotes Sabbath observance (Lincoln's concession). The -ismos suffix marks the act of Sabbath-keeping.

**Notes:** Parsing: ara (particle = therefore) apoleipetai (V-PPI-3S = remains) sabbatismos (N-NSM = Sabbath-keeping) to lao tou theou (to the people of God). Lincoln (anti-Sabbatarian): Plutarch, Justin, Epiphanius, Apostolic Constitutions — every extant use denotes Sabbath observance. Lane (WBC): translates 'Sabbath celebration.' The GCI counter-argument ('no point persuading Jews to do what they already did') assumes the audience was keeping the Sabbath; but the Hebrews audience may have been tempted to abandon it.

### The Hosper Comparison — As God Did From His Works

> For he that is entered into his rest, he also hath ceased from his own works, as God [did] from his.

**Context:** The explicit parallel: the believer's rest is patterned on God's seventh-day rest. The hosper (G5618, 'just as') comparison draws a direct analogy between human sabbatismos and divine Sabbath rest at creation.

**Notes:** Parsing: ho eiselthon (the one having entered) eis ten katapausin autou (into his rest) kai autos katepausen (he also ceased, V-AAI-3S of katapauo G2664) apo ton ergon autou (from his works) hosper (ADV = just as) apo ton idion (from his own) ho theos (God). The hosper draws an exact parallel: as God ceased from works, so the believer ceases.

### Ktizo for Creation in Revelation — Generic Creation Praise

> Thou art worthy, O Lord, to receive glory and honour and power: for thou hast created all things, and for thy pleasure they are and were created.

**Context:** The heavenly beings praise God as Creator using ktizo (G2936), not poieo. This is generic creation theology. Rev 14:7's switch to poieo distinguishes it from this verse and aligns it with the specific Exo 20:11 LXX vocabulary.

**Notes:** Rev 4:11 uses ektisas (aorist active of ktizo) and ektisthesan (aorist passive of ktizo). Rev 14:7 uses poiesanti (aorist active participle of poieo). The verb distinction within the same book shows deliberate vocabulary selection.

### The Millibad Separator — Weekly Sabbath Distinguished from Ceremonial

> These [are] the feasts of the LORD, which ye shall proclaim [to be] holy convocations, to offer an offering made by fire unto the LORD, a burnt offering, and a meat offering, a sacrifice, and drink offerings, every thing upon his day: Beside the sabbaths of the LORD, and beside your gifts, and beside all your vows, and beside all your freewill offerings, which ye give unto the LORD.

**Context:** After listing the ceremonial feasts/sabbaths in Lev 23:1-36, v.38 explicitly separates them from 'the sabbaths of the LORD' using the fourfold millibad (H905, 'besides/apart from'). The ceremonial calendar is 'beside' (not including) the weekly Sabbath.

**Notes:** The fourfold millibad in v.38 creates an explicit textual separation: these feasts are 'beside' (1) the sabbaths of the LORD, (2) your gifts, (3) your vows, (4) your freewill offerings. This is the OT's own distinction between ceremonial and weekly sabbaths.
