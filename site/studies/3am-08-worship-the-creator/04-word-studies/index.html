    Word Studies¶

    Question¶

    Why does the first angel call for worship of the Creator using the specific formula 'him that made heaven, and earth, and the sea, and the fountains of waters'? Is this a direct quotation of Exodus 20:11? Verbal comparison through LXX — analyze poieo/asah bridge. Compare with other creation-worship texts (Neh 9:6, Acts 4:24, Acts 14:15, Psa 146:6). In a passage concluding with 'the commandments of God' (Rev 14:12), how does the creation formula link to the Sabbath commandment? Address the objection that this is generic creation theology. Creation-to-new-creation bookend (Gen 2:2-3 to Isa 66:22-23). Hebrews 4:9 sabbatismos.

    G4160 — poieo¶

    Source: word-studies/G4160-poieo.md

    Occurrences: 597

    The broadest Greek verb for 'do/make,' functioning as the default action verb. In creation contexts, poieo is the LXX's standard rendering of Hebrew asah (H6213), with 2,022 co-occurrences and PMI score 3.15 — the highest-confidence Hebrew-to-Greek lexical alignment. Rev 14:7 uses poiesanti (aorist active participle, dative) to identify the Creator — the same verb root as Exo 20:11 LXX (epoiese). Rev 10:6 uses ktizo instead, showing the author had both verbs available and deliberately chose poieo for Rev 14:7.

    Semantic categories: general action, physical making/creating, performing signs, moral/ethical conduct, divine redemptive action

    Key verses: Rev 14:7, Acts 17:24, Matt 19:4, Heb 1:2

    Relevance: The key verb linking Rev 14:7 to Exo 20:11 LXX. The poieo/asah bridge (2,022 LXX co-occurrences) is the linguistic mechanism by which the Hebrew Sabbath commandment's creation rationale (asah) enters the Greek NT (poieo). The deliberate choice of poieo over ktizo in Rev 14:7 distinguishes this verse from generic creation theology (Rev 4:11, Rev 10:6) and aligns it specifically with the Exo 20:11 LXX tradition.

    H6213 — asah¶

    Source: word-studies/H6213-asah.md

    Occurrences: 2934

    The most semantically generic Hebrew verb of action. In creation contexts, asah denotes God's making/fashioning work, distinct from bara (create ex nihilo). Exo 20:11: 'For in six days the LORD made (asah) heaven and earth, the sea.' The LXX renders asah as poieo in 2,022 of its occurrences, establishing the bridge to Rev 14:7's poiesanti.

    Semantic categories: divine creative action, human craftsmanship, ritual observance, moral/ethical conduct, covenant action

    Key verses: Gen 1:7, Gen 1:16, Gen 1:26, Gen 2:2, Exo 20:11, Exo 31:17

    Relevance: The Hebrew verb behind Exo 20:11's creation rationale. When the LXX translators rendered asah as poieo, they established the lexical link that Rev 14:7 picks up. The asah/poieo bridge is the strongest Hebrew-to-Greek pairing in the database (PMI 3.15).

    G2936 — ktizo¶

    Source: word-studies/G2936-ktizo.md

    Occurrences: 16

    Specialized creation verb meaning 'to create/found originally.' Used 12 times in NT, overwhelmingly in Pauline epistles. In Revelation: Rev 4:11 (ektisas/ektisthesan) and Rev 10:6 (ektisen) use ktizo; Rev 14:7 switches to poieo. The LXX bridges ktizo primarily with Hebrew bara (H1254, 16 co-occurrences, PMI 8.25) — the divine creation verb.

    Semantic categories: cosmic creation, new creation in Christ, corporate unity, Creator as title

    Key verses: Rev 10:6, Rev 4:11, Col 1:16, Rom 1:25, Eph 2:10

    Relevance: The contrast verb. Rev 10:6 uses ktizo for creation; Rev 14:7 uses poieo. Since ktizo maps to bara and poieo maps to asah, the verb switch shows Rev 14:7 deliberately echoes the Exo 20:11 tradition (asah/poieo) rather than the Gen 1:1 tradition (bara/ktizo).

    H1254 — bara¶

    Source: word-studies/H1254-bara.md

    Occurrences: 59

    Hebrew creation verb meaning 'create' — exclusively divine subject. Distinguished from asah (make) and yatsar (form). The LXX renders bara primarily as ktizo (G2936). Gen 1:1 uses bara; Gen 1:7,16,26 use asah. The verb distinction in Hebrew (bara vs. asah) maps onto the Greek distinction (ktizo vs. poieo).

    Semantic categories: divine creation ex nihilo

    Key verses: Gen 1:1, Gen 1:27, Gen 2:3, Isa 40:28, Isa 45:18

    Relevance: Supports the poieo/ktizo argument by showing that the Hebrew verb distinction (bara/asah) maps consistently onto the Greek distinction (ktizo/poieo). Rev 14:7's poieo = asah = Exo 20:11 tradition.

    G4352 — proskyneo¶

    Source: word-studies/G4352-proskyneo.md

    Occurrences: 65

    The dominant worship verb in the NT, meaning to prostrate in homage. Revelation accounts for 41% of all occurrences (22 of 54 verses), making it the book's central worship term. The verb dominates the Rev 13-14 worship conflict: beast-worship (13:4,8,12,15) vs. Creator-worship (14:7). The proskynēsate in Rev 14:7 is an aorist imperative — a decisive command to worship.

    Semantic categories: prostration before Jesus, divine worship, false worship, eschatological worship conflict

    Key verses: Rev 14:7, Rev 13:4, Rev 13:8, Rev 13:15, Rev 15:4, Rev 19:10

    Relevance: The worship verb that frames the entire Rev 13-14 conflict. The same word (proskyneo) is used for both beast-worship and Creator-worship, creating a binary: whom will you worship? The aorist imperative in Rev 14:7 demands a decisive choice.

    H7812 — shachah¶

    Source: word-studies/H7812-shachah.md

    Occurrences: 194

    The primary OT worship verb meaning 'to bow down, prostrate oneself.' The LXX renders shachah as proskyneo, establishing the linguistic chain from OT worship to the NT worship conflict in Revelation.

    Semantic categories: prostration in worship, bowing before God, bowing before humans

    Key verses: Exo 20:5, Psa 95:6, Isa 66:23

    Relevance: OT background for proskyneo in Rev 14:7. The shachah → proskyneo chain connects OT worship commands to the first angel's worship imperative.

    G4520 — sabbatismos¶

    Source: word-studies/G4520-sabbatismos.md

    Occurrences: 1

    NT hapax legomenon in Heb 4:9. A verbal noun from sabbatizo (to keep the Sabbath), not a synonym for katapausis (cessation). The -ismos suffix denotes the ACT of Sabbath-keeping. Every extant use in Greek literature denotes Sabbath observance: Plutarch (De Superstitione 3), Justin (Dialogue 23:3), Epiphanius (Adversus Haereses 30:2:2), Apostolic Constitutions 2:36:2. Lincoln concedes this while arguing against ongoing Sabbath obligation.

    Semantic categories: Sabbath-rest, act of Sabbath-keeping

    Key verses: Heb 4:9

    Relevance: The deliberate shift from katapausis (used 8x) to sabbatismos at the argument's climax signals intentional Sabbath language. The lexical data is clear: sabbatismos = Sabbath observance in every extant Greek source. The GCI counter-argument ('no point persuading Jews') assumes the audience was keeping the Sabbath; but the epistle's audience may have been tempted to abandon it.

    G2663 — katapausis¶

    Source: word-studies/G2663-katapausis.md

    Occurrences: 9

    Feminine noun meaning 'rest/reposing down.' Used 8 times in Hebrews 3-4, once in Acts 7:49 (quoting Isa 66:1). The contrast term for sabbatismos: katapausis = cessation/repose; sabbatismos = Sabbath-keeping rest with institutional connotation. The KJV obscures the distinction by translating both as 'rest.'

    Semantic categories: God's dwelling, Promised Land rest, creation rest

    Key verses: Heb 4:1, Heb 4:3, Heb 4:5, Heb 4:10, Heb 4:11, Acts 7:49

    Relevance: The contrast term that makes sabbatismos significant. The author uses katapausis eight times, then deliberately switches to sabbatismos at the climactic v.9 — not stylistic variation but deliberate intensification.

    G4521 — sabbaton¶

    Source: word-studies/G4521-sabbaton.md

    Occurrences: 68

    Greek Sabbath noun, the parent word of sabbatismos. Used for both the weekly Sabbath and in the Col 2:16 triad (heorte/neomenia/sabbaton). The context determines whether weekly or ceremonial sabbaths are in view.

    Semantic categories: weekly Sabbath day, sabbath days (ceremonial), week

    Key verses: Luke 23:56, Col 2:16, Acts 13:14, Mark 2:27-28

    Relevance: The Greek Sabbath word appearing in both positive (Luke 23:56 — kata ten entolen) and contested (Col 2:16 — shadow) contexts. The Col 2:16 triad (heorte + neomenia + sabbaton) matches the OT ceremonial calendar triad of 1 Chr 23:31.

    H7676 — shabbath¶

    Source: word-studies/H7676-shabbath.md

    Occurrences: 109

    Hebrew Sabbath noun, intensive form of shabath (to cease). Appears 89 times across 15 books. Concentrated in Leviticus (law), Nehemiah (enforcement), Ezekiel (prophetic rebuke), and Exodus (founding legislation). First occurrence: Exo 16:23 (pre-Sinai, in the manna narrative).

    Semantic categories: weekly seventh-day rest, covenant sign, ceremonial sabbaths

    Key verses: Exo 20:8-10, Exo 31:13-17, Eze 20:12,20, Gen 2:2-3, Isa 66:23

    Relevance: The central Hebrew word for the institution under study. The Sabbath appears at creation (Gen 2), at Sinai (Exo 20), as sign (Exo 31, Eze 20), and in the new creation (Isa 66:23) — an arc the study traces.

    G1785 — entole¶

    Source: word-studies/G1785-entole.md

    Occurrences: 71

    Feminine noun meaning 'commandment, injunction.' Johannine concentration (34% of all uses). In Revelation: Rev 12:17 and 14:12 use 'entolas tou theou' (commandments of God). The prior law-28 study established that entole without qualifier = Decalogue content in 43/43 identifiable NT instances. Nomos (G3551) never appears in Revelation.

    Semantic categories: Decalogue/Mosaic Law, Christ's new commandment, Father's commandment to Christ, apostolic commands

    Key verses: Rev 12:17, Rev 14:12, Luke 23:56, 1 John 2:3-4, 1 Cor 7:19

    Relevance: The commandment term that bookends the three angels' messages (Rev 12:17, 14:12). Luke 23:56 applies entole directly to the Sabbath commandment, collapsing the nomos/entole distinction claimed by Tinker. Nomos never appears in Revelation — the book uses entole exclusively.

    G5083 — tereo¶

    Source: word-studies/G5083-tereo.md

    Occurrences: 75

    Verb meaning 'to keep, guard, observe.' In Revelation: 'hoi terountes tas entolas tou theou' (Rev 14:12) and 'ton terounton tas entolas tou theou' (Rev 12:17). The present participle indicates ongoing, continuous keeping. Same root as teresis in 1 Cor 7:19.

    Semantic categories: keeping commandments, guarding/watching, preserving

    Key verses: Rev 14:12, Rev 12:17, 1 Cor 7:19, John 14:15, 1 John 5:3

    Relevance: The 'keeping' verb in both Rev 12:17 and 14:12. The tereo + entole + theos formula appears in Rev 12:17, Rev 14:12, and 1 Cor 7:19 — a consistent NT pattern for commandment-keeping.

    G4077 — pege¶

    Source: word-studies/G4077-pege.md

    Occurrences: 12

    Feminine noun meaning 'spring, fountain, well.' 5 of 12 NT occurrences are in Revelation. In Rev 14:7, 'pegas hydaton' (springs of waters) replaces the expected 'panta ta en autois' (all that is in them) from the standard creation formula. In Revelation judgment contexts (8:10, 16:4), pegas appears alongside rivers as freshwater sources subject to divine judgment. In redemptive contexts (7:17, 21:6), God/the Lamb is the pege of living water.

    Semantic categories: physical water source, bodily flow, metaphor for speech/character, judgment on water sources, eschatological life-giving source

    Key verses: Rev 14:7, Rev 8:10, Rev 16:4, Rev 7:17, Rev 21:6, John 4:14

    Relevance: The key departure word. Rev 14:7's 'pegas hydaton' substitutes for the expected 'panta ta en autois' (Psa 146:6, Acts 4:24, Acts 14:15). The Hebrew equivalent ma'yan (H4599, PMI 8.15) appears in Gen 7:11 ('fountains of the great deep'), supporting Baldwin's Flood allusion theory.

    H226 — owth¶

    Source: word-studies/H226-owth.md

    Occurrences: 85

    Feminine noun meaning 'sign, token, mark.' Used for covenant signs (rainbow Gen 9:12; circumcision Gen 17:11; Sabbath Exo 31:13,17 and Eze 20:12,20), miraculous signs (Exodus plagues), and confirmatory/prophetic signs. The Sabbath is explicitly called owth four times — the only commandment to receive this designation.

    Semantic categories: covenant sign, miraculous signs, confirmatory/prophetic sign, memorial/reminder sign

    Key verses: Exo 31:13, Exo 31:17, Eze 20:12, Eze 20:20, Gen 9:12, Gen 17:11

    Relevance: The Sabbath is the ONLY commandment explicitly called owth (sign). Exo 31:13,17 and Eze 20:12,20 designate it four times. This supports the seal/mark binary in Revelation: if the Sabbath is God's sign, the mark of the beast is the counterfeit sign.

    H2142 — zakar¶

    Source: word-studies/H2142-zakar.md

    Occurrences: 280

    Primitive root meaning 'to remember, be mindful, mention.' Exo 20:8 opens the fourth commandment with zakar: 'Remember the sabbath day.' Zakar points backward — it is a memorial function, not a prophetic/shadow function. A memorial (zakar) is the opposite of a shadow (skia): shadows look forward; memorials look backward.

    Semantic categories: divine remembrance, human remembrance of God, memorial institution

    Key verses: Exo 20:8, Gen 8:1, Psa 105:8

    Relevance: The memorial function of the Sabbath. 'Remember' (zakar) in Exo 20:8 points backward to creation, not forward to fulfillment. This is the primary argument against Col 2:17 applying to the weekly Sabbath: shadows (skia) look forward; the Sabbath (zakar) looks backward.

    H7673 — shabath¶

    Source: word-studies/H7673-shabath.md

    Occurrences: 112

    Primitive root meaning 'to cease, rest, desist.' Parent verb of shabbath (the Sabbath). Gen 2:2 uses Qal wayyiqtol: wayyishbot (and he ceased/rested). Dominant Hiphil stem (56%) shows the word is used most often in prophetic judgment (God causing things to cease). The Qal covers creation and Sabbath-keeping contexts.

    Semantic categories: divine rest at creation, causing to cease (judgment), Sabbath observance

    Key verses: Gen 2:2, Gen 2:3, Exo 31:17, Exo 34:21

    Relevance: The verbal root of the Sabbath institution. Gen 2:2 wayyishbot establishes the creation pattern: God ceased from work. Heb 4:10's hosper comparison draws on this exact pattern.

    H6942 — qadash¶

    Source: word-studies/H6942-qadash.md

    Occurrences: 186

    Primitive root meaning 'to be/make holy, sanctify.' First occurrence: Gen 2:3 — 'God blessed the seventh day, and sanctified it.' Used in Piel (make holy, 41x), Hiphil (cause to be holy, 13x), and Qal (be holy, 7x). Gen 2:3 uses Piel wayyiqtol: wayeqaddesh (and he sanctified it).

    Semantic categories: sanctifying the Sabbath, consecrating priests/people, declaring holy

    Key verses: Gen 2:3, Exo 20:11, Exo 31:13

    Relevance: The sanctification verb applied to the seventh day at creation (Gen 2:3) and in the fourth commandment (Exo 20:11 'hallowed it'). Only the seventh day receives this verb at creation.

    H8104 — shamar¶

    Source: word-studies/H8104-shamar.md

    Occurrences: 511

    Verb meaning 'to keep, guard, watch, preserve.' Used for Sabbath-keeping in Exo 31:13-14: 'my sabbaths ye shall keep (shamar).' The guarding/protecting sense implies active preservation of the institution.

    Semantic categories: keeping/guarding commandments, watching, preserving

    Key verses: Exo 31:13, Exo 31:14, Deut 5:12

    Relevance: The Sabbath-keeping verb in Exo 31:13-14 and Deut 5:12. Carries the sense of guarding something precious, not merely observing a rule.

    G4639 — skia¶

    Source: word-studies/G4639-skia.md

    Occurrences: 7

    Feminine noun meaning 'shadow.' In the typological sense (Col 2:17, Heb 8:5, Heb 10:1), a shadow is an outline/adumbration of the coming reality, contrasted with soma (body/substance) and eikon (image/true form). Col 2:17 calls the ceremonial calendar observances 'a shadow of things to come.'

    Semantic categories: physical shade, shadow of death, typological shadow

    Key verses: Col 2:17, Heb 8:5, Heb 10:1

    Relevance: The key term in the Col 2:16-17 objection. Shadows (skia) look forward to fulfillment. The Sabbath uses zakar (remember) — looking backward. The sabbath-shadow-or-memorial study established five criteria the weekly Sabbath fails for being a biblical shadow.

    G2281 — thalassa¶

    Source: word-studies/G2281-thalassa.md

    Occurrences: 92

    Feminine noun meaning 'sea.' Part of the creation triad (heaven, earth, sea) in both Rev 14:7 and Rev 10:6. Revelation dominates with 27 of 92 occurrences. In Rev 21:1, 'there was no more sea' — the chaotic element is absent from the new creation.

    Semantic categories: literal body of water, creation element, domain of chaos, apocalyptic symbol

    Key verses: Rev 14:7, Rev 10:6, Acts 4:24, Rev 21:1, Rev 13:1

    Relevance: Third element in the creation formula shared between Rev 14:7 and Exo 20:11. Both use thalassa(n) in the same position. The LXX rendering of Hebrew yam (H3220).

    G3772 — ouranos¶

    Source: word-studies/G3772-ouranos.md

    Occurrences: 284

    Masculine noun meaning 'heaven/sky.' 284 NT occurrences, highest density in Revelation (54 occurrences, 13.4 per 100 verses). Part of the creation formula in Rev 14:7 and Exo 20:11 LXX: ton ouranon is an exact match between the two texts.

    Semantic categories: God's dwelling, physical sky, created order, kingdom of heaven, eschatological transformation

    Key verses: Rev 14:7, Rev 10:6, Acts 4:24, Rev 21:1, 2 Pet 3:13

    Relevance: First element in the creation formula. 'Ton ouranon' in Rev 14:7 matches 'ton ouranon' in Exo 20:11 LXX exactly. Hebrew source is shamayim (H8064).

    G1093 — ge¶

    Source: word-studies/G1093-gē.md

    Occurrences: 252

    Feminine noun meaning 'earth/land/ground.' 252 NT occurrences, highest in Revelation (82, 6.3 per 100 verses). Part of the creation formula: 'ten gen' in Rev 14:7 matches 'ten gen' in Exo 20:11 LXX exactly.

    Semantic categories: physical planet, inhabited world, specific land/region, heaven-earth contrast, eschatological earth

    Key verses: Rev 14:7, Rev 10:6, Acts 4:24, Rev 21:1

    Relevance: Second element in the creation formula. 'Ten gen' in Rev 14:7 matches Exo 20:11 LXX exactly. Hebrew source is erets (H776).

    G2920 — krisis¶

    Source: word-studies/G2920-krisis.md

    Occurrences: 48

    Feminine noun meaning 'judgment, judicial process.' Used in Rev 14:7 for 'the hour of his judgment' (he hora tes kriseos autou). The prior 3am-07 study established the krisis/katakrima distinction: krisis = evaluation, katakrima = condemnation.

    Semantic categories: divine judgment, judicial process, day of judgment

    Key verses: Rev 14:7, John 5:24, Acts 17:31

    Relevance: The judgment term in Rev 14:7. The creation formula is set within a judgment announcement: worship the Creator BECAUSE judgment has come. The Ecc 12:13-14 template pairs creation-worship with judgment.

    G5399 — phobeo¶

    Source: word-studies/G5399-phobeo.md

    Occurrences: 110

    Verb meaning 'to fear, be afraid, revere.' Rev 14:7 uses the aorist passive imperative phobethete — 'Fear God.' The prior 3am-05 study established the OT fear-of-God tradition (yare/phobos) as the foundation for the first angel's message.

    Semantic categories: reverential fear of God, physical fear/terror

    Key verses: Rev 14:7, Rev 15:4, Ecc 12:13

    Relevance: First imperative in the first angel's message. The fear/commandments/judgment triad matches the Ecc 12:13-14 template.

    G1391 — doxa¶

    Source: word-studies/G1391-doxa.md

    Occurrences: 168

    Feminine noun meaning 'glory, honor, splendor.' Rev 14:7: 'give glory to him' (dote auto doxan). The prior 3am-06 study established seven modes of glory-giving. Rev 21:23: 'the glory of God did lighten it.'

    Semantic categories: divine glory, glory-giving

    Key verses: Rev 14:7, Rev 15:4, Rev 21:23

    Relevance: Second imperative content in the first angel's message. In Rev 21:23, God's doxa replaces the need for sun/moon light, which is relevant to the Isa 66:23 question.

    G3551 — nomos¶

    Source: word-studies/G3551-nomos.md

    Occurrences: 197

    Masculine noun meaning 'law.' Critically, nomos NEVER appears in Revelation. The book uses entole exclusively for God's commands. This undermines Tinker's claim that John uses nomos for Mosaic Law and entole for Jesus's teachings.

    Semantic categories: Mosaic Law, law principle, Torah

    Key verses: Rom 3:20, Gal 3:24

    Relevance: The absent term. Nomos never appears in Revelation, falsifying the claim that John distinguishes nomos (Mosaic Law) from entole (Jesus's teachings) in his eschatological vocabulary.

    G2664 — katapauo¶

    Source: word-studies/G2664-katapauo.md

    Occurrences: 5

    Verb meaning 'to cause to rest, to cease.' Verbal form of katapausis. Used in Heb 4:10: katepausen (aorist active indicative) = 'he ceased from his works.' The hosper comparison: as God katapauo'd from His works, so the believer ceases.

    Semantic categories: divine rest, ceasing from works

    Key verses: Heb 4:4, Heb 4:8, Heb 4:10

    Relevance: The verbal form of katapausis, used in the hosper comparison (Heb 4:10) that patterns human rest on God's seventh-day rest.

    G2889 — kosmos¶

    Source: word-studies/G2889-kosmos.md

    Occurrences: 187

    Masculine noun meaning 'world, ordered system.' Acts 17:24 uses kosmos ('God that made the world') while Rev 14:7 uses the specific creation formula (heaven, earth, sea). The shift from general (kosmos) to specific (creation formula) is part of the argument that Rev 14:7 deliberately echoes Exo 20:11.

    Semantic categories: created world, world system

    Key verses: Acts 17:24, John 1:10

    Relevance: The contrast term. Generic creation texts use kosmos (Acts 17:24) or ktizo (Rev 4:11). Rev 14:7 uses the specific four-element formula with poieo, aligning with Exo 20:11 rather than generic creation language.
