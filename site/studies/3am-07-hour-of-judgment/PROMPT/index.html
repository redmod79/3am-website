    Question¶

    What does hoti elthen he hora tes kriseos autou ("for the hour of his judgment is come") mean? What is this judgment — the cross, the second coming, the investigative/pre-advent judgment, or something else? Why does the aorist elthen present it as an accomplished fact? How does krisis (G2920) differ from katakrima (G2631)? Trace krisis through the LXX to Daniel 7's dina. How does Daniel 7:22 establish this judgment is FOR believers? How can judgment be "good news"? Does the pre-advent judgment undermine assurance? Address using Daniel 12:1, 1 John 4:17, Romans 8:1,33-34, John 5:24. How does Romans 2:16 prove judgment IS gospel content?

    Prior Research Summary¶

    Seven prior 3AM studies have been completed. Study 00 mapped the phrase structure; Study 01 established literary context and the aorist elthen as marking specific arrival; Study 02 confirmed the riyb-to-krisis LXX bridge; Study 03 identified three NT texts that explicitly include judgment within gospel (Luke 3:18, Rom 2:16, Rev 14:6-7) and the anticipation-vs-announcement framework; Study 04 confirmed the legon participle makes judgment gospel content; Study 05 found 1 John 4:17's parrhesia in the day of krisis; Study 06 connected glory-giving to the judgment announcement via the hoti clause. Additional deep studies exist: gsp-07 (judgment and gospel), gsp-08 (assurance and judgment), sanc-24 (Daniel 7 court), dan-7-judgment-antitypical-doa (DOA-to-Dan-7 progression), sv-21 (three angels vocabulary chain). Web research produced 13 research directives covering scholarly landscape, lexical data, and critic engagement.

    Primary Verses (33 — full analysis required)¶

    The krisis/katakrima distinction passages: Core texts establishing the difference between judgment-as-process and condemnation-as-verdict - Rom 8:1 — ouden katakrima — no CONDEMNATION (katakrima G2631), not no judgment (krisis). The krisis/katakrima distinction pivot - Rom 5:16 — katakrima — one of only 3 NT occurrences; contrasts with charisma. Critical for katakrima definition - Rom 5:18 — Second katakrima occurrence — through one trespass, katakrima came to all; contrasts with dikaiosis - John 5:22 — The Father has committed ALL judgment (krisin) to the Son — establishes Christ as the divine judge of Rev 14:7 - John 5:24 — eis krisin ouk erchetai — 'does not come into judgment/condemnation'; must resolve against broader krisis usage in John 5 - John 5:27-29 — Authority to execute judgment because He is Son of Man — Dan 7:13 allusion; resurrection judgment - 1 John 4:17 — parresia en te hemera tes kriseos — boldness IN the day of judgment. Same krisis as Rev 14:7. Confidence, not exemption

    Daniel 7 judgment court scene: The OT source text for the heavenly judgment vocabulary and sequence - Dan 7:9-10 — The OT source of the judgment vocabulary — dina yitib, siphrin pethichu; court scene with Ancient of Days - Dan 7:13-14 — Son of Man approaches Ancient of Days (TO God, not to earth) — receives dominion after judgment - Dan 7:22 — Pivotal verse — dina yehib leqaddishe elyonin: judgment given TO/FOR the saints; vindication, not condemnation - Dan 7:26-27 — Third judgment reference — court sits, dominion removed from horn, given to saints permanently

    Judgment-as-gospel passages: Texts where judgment is explicitly presented as gospel content - Rev 14:6-7 — The crux text — contains the entire phrase under study: hoti elthen he hora tes kriseos autou - Rom 2:16 — kata to euangelion mou — God judges secrets of men ACCORDING TO my gospel. Judgment IS gospel content - Acts 17:30-31 — God commands repentance because He has APPOINTED a day to judge the world — judgment as basis for gospel call

    Assurance-in-judgment passages: Texts grounding believer confidence despite/within judgment - Rom 8:33-34 — Rhetorical challenge: who shall lay charge? who condemns? Christ intercedes — courtroom vindication language - Dan 12:1 — Everyone FOUND WRITTEN in the book is delivered — judgment as deliverance for those already inscribed - 1 Cor 3:13-15 — Fire tests each one's work — even when all works burn, person is saved; evaluative judgment with safety net

    Investigation-before-execution pattern: OT texts showing God's pattern of investigating before judging - Gen 18:20-21 — God descends to investigate Sodom BEFORE judging — the divine pattern of investigation before execution - Matt 22:11-14 — Wedding garment inspection — the king investigates guests BEFORE the feast; pre-event examination pattern - Eccl 12:14 — God will bring every work into judgment (mishpat) — comprehensive evaluation; OT judgment expectation

    Prophetic aorist parallels: Other prophetic aorists in Revelation for comparison with elthen - Rev 14:8 — Epesen epesen Babylōn — prophetic aorist parallel to elthen in 14:7; same grammatical phenomenon

    Books-of-judgment imagery: Passages about the books opened in judgment — life, deeds, remembrance - Rev 20:11-15 — Great white throne — books opened, book of life; post-millennial judgment phase for comparison with pre-advent - Mal 3:16-17 — Book of remembrance written FOR those who feared the LORD — preserves, not prosecutes; judgment as recording faithful service - Dan 12:1 — Everyone FOUND WRITTEN in the book is delivered — judgment as deliverance for those already inscribed

    Courtroom vindication type: Zechariah 3 and related courtroom-defense imagery - Zech 3:1-5 — Courtroom vindication type — Joshua the high priest accused by Satan, defended by God, filthy garments replaced - Mal 3:16-17 — Book of remembrance written FOR those who feared the LORD — preserves, not prosecutes; judgment as recording faithful service

    OT judgment expectations: Psalms and prophets on the righteous character of God's judgment - Psa 50:3-6 — God calls heaven and earth as witnesses to judge His people — OT divine judgment-of-the-faithful pattern - Psa 96:13 — He comes to judge the earth with righteousness — judgment as positive, righteous vindication - Psa 9:7-8 — The LORD has prepared His throne for judgment; He shall judge the world in righteousness — judgment as established, righteous order - 1 Pet 4:17 — Judgment begins at the house of God — pre-advent judgment concept in Peter; if it begins with us, what of the ungodly?

    Additional Primary Verses: - 1 John 4:18 — Perfect love casts out fear — kolasin fear, not reverential fear. The kolasis/phobos distinction - Rev 11:18-19 — Judgment of the dead + ark of covenant revealed — DOA/judgment theme activated before the three angels - 2 Cor 5:10 — Bema of Christ — all must appear for evaluation of deeds; judgment of believers is evaluative disclosure - Dan 8:14 — nitsdaq — the sanctuary shall be vindicated/restored/cleansed; linked to the judgment timing - Jas 2:12-13 — Speak and act as those about to be judged by the law of liberty — mercy triumphs over judgment

    Focus Areas¶

    1. The krisis/katakrima distinction: process vs. condemnation: READ $WORD_STUDIES/G2920-krisis.md, $WORD_STUDIES/G2631-katakrima.md, $WORD_STUDIES/G2917-krima.md, $WORD_STUDIES/G2919-krino.md, $WORD_STUDIES/G2633-katakrisis.md, $WORD_STUDIES/G2632-katakrino.md, and $WORD_STUDIES/G350-anakrino.md to map the entire krin- word family. The key distinction: krisis appears 48 times in the NT and denotes the process of judicial evaluation — 'a separating or sundering, a trial or contest, selection.' Katakrima appears only 3 times (all in Romans 5-8) and denotes the adverse result including both sentence AND execution — 'the adjudication of punishment.' Romans 8:1 says 'no katakrima' (not 'no krisis') for those in Christ. This means believers are exempt from condemnation but NOT from evaluation. Run greek_parser.py --verse on Rom 8:1, Rom 5:16, Rom 5:18 to confirm katakrima morphology, then on Rev 14:7, John 5:22, John 5:24, 1 John 4:17 to confirm krisis morphology. The research agent should also check G1341 dikaiokrisia (righteous judgment, $WORD_STUDIES/G1341-dikaiokrisia.md) and G1349 dike ($WORD_STUDIES/G1349-dike.md) to complete the picture of the Greek judgment vocabulary family. Verses: Rev 14:7, Rom 8:1, Rom 5:16, Rom 5:18, John 5:22, John 5:24, John 5:27-29, 1 John 4:17 Strong's: G2920, G2631, G2917, G2919, G2633, G2632, G350, G1341, G1349

    2. The Aramaic dina to LXX kriterion/krima to NT krisis vocabulary chain: READ $WORD_STUDIES/A1780-diyn.md (Aramaic dina), $WORD_STUDIES/H1779-diyn.md (Hebrew din), $WORD_STUDIES/H1777-diyn.md (Hebrew verb diyn), $WORD_STUDIES/H4941-mishpat.md, $WORD_STUDIES/H8199-shaphat.md, $WORD_STUDIES/H7378-riyb.md, and $WORD_STUDIES/H7379-riyb.md.     11. The courtroom vindication type: Zechariah 3 and Revelation's judgment imagery: Retrieve Zech 3:1-5 with full context and run hebrew_parser.py on the key verses.     The hoti Clause Function¶

    The hoti in Rev 14:7 introduces either a causal clause ('because the hour has come') or a content clause ('that the hour has come'). Both readings are significant: causal makes judgment the REASON for fearing God and giving glory; content makes judgment part of the gospel MESSAGE being proclaimed. The research agent should parse hoti and determine from context which function is primary — likely causal, supporting the three imperatives.
