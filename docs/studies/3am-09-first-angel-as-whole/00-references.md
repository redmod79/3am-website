## Prior Studies

### From Study Plan (Arc 1 Studies 04-08 — All Required Integration)

**3am-04-euangelion-aionion (Study 4: "Everlasting Gospel"):**
- Question: What does "everlasting gospel" (euangelion aionion) mean in Rev 14:6?
- Key finding: The anarthrous construction is qualitative, emphasizing the gospel's eternal character while still referring to THE well-known gospel. Not "a gospel" but "gospel of an eternal kind."
- Key finding: The basar → euangelizo → euangelion vocabulary chain is confirmed by apostolic quotation (Rom 10:15 quoting Isa 52:7), dominical application (Luke 4:18 quoting Isa 61:1), and Pauline coinage (proeuangelizomai, Gal 3:8). Rev 14:6 is the only NT verse where noun (euangelion) and verb (euangelisai) co-occur — a cognate accusative intensifying the gospel-ness.
- Key finding: The legon participle (present active, connecting v.7 to v.6) means the commands that follow ARE the gospel content — not a supplement to it.
- Key finding: Acts 17:22-31 matches Rev 14:7 element by element: Creator (17:24 = 14:7), universal scope (17:26,30 = 14:6), judgment (17:31 = 14:7), repentance (17:30 = 14:7). If Paul's Athens sermon IS gospel, then Rev 14:7's content IS gospel.
- Key finding: Galatians 1:6-9 condemns the substitution of works-righteousness for grace, not the inclusion of judgment, creation, or commandments in gospel content. Paul himself includes all three (Rom 2:16, Acts 17:24, 1 Cor 7:19).
- Key finding: The imperial euangelia thesis (DeSilva) recognizes a real counter-imperial dimension but fails when made the primary meaning. "Age-long" fails the 2 Tim 1:9 test.
- Key finding: Rev 14:12's commandments + faith of Jesus is gospel RESULT, not gospel ADDITION. The articular present participle terountes is substantival (descriptive), not imperative (prescriptive).

**3am-05-fear-god (Study 5: "Fear God"):**
- Question: What does phobethete ton theon mean as the first imperative?
- Key finding: Not servile terror but reverential awe — the same moral orientation the OT calls yirah (H3374). Exodus 20:20 distinguishes two fears in a single verse: "Fear not [terror]... that his fear [reverence] may be before your faces."
- Key finding: 1 John 4:18 ("perfect love casts out fear") does NOT contradict Rev 14:7. The fear love casts out is specifically kolasin-fear — dread of divine punishment. Kolasis (G2851) appears only 2 times in the NT, both in judgment contexts. Reverential awe is a different category.
- Key finding: Ecclesiastes 12:13-14 provides the OT template. The structural parallel — fear God + commandments + judgment — anticipates the first angel's message precisely. LXX bridges verified: yare → phobeo (241 co-occurrences), mitsvah → entole (153 co-occurrences), mishpat → krisis.
- Key finding: The farewell-address pattern (fear + obedience + judgment) recurs across Moses (Deut 10:12-13), Joshua (Josh 24:14), Samuel (1 Sam 12:24), and Solomon (Eccl 12:13-14). The first angel is the fifth iteration — addressed universally.
- Key finding: Fearing God is the foundation for refusing to fear the beast (Matt 10:28; Rev 13:15). The person who has settled whom they ultimately fear cannot be moved by a death threat.
- Key finding: Three aorist imperatives (phobethete, dote, proskynesate) form a climactic triad — not separate actions but one reality viewed from three angles: internal orientation, relational acknowledgment, behavioral expression.

**3am-06-give-glory (Study 6: "Give Glory to Him"):**
- Question: What does dote auto doxan mean as a gospel imperative?
- Key finding: Kabod (H3519) = character-substance, not merely visible radiance. When Moses asked to see God's glory (Exod 33:18), God proclaimed His character attributes (Exod 34:6-7). Glory-giving = giving God the full weight of recognition His character deserves.
- Key finding: Seven biblically attested modes of glorification identified: confession/truth-telling (Josh 7:19), character acknowledgment (Isa 42:12), visible conduct (Matt 5:16), fruit-bearing (John 15:8), bodily stewardship (1 Cor 6:20, 10:31), beholding-transformation (2 Cor 3:18), and completed work (John 17:4).
- Key finding: Romans 1:21 describes the exact failure the first angel addresses — "they glorified him not as God." The glory-exchange (Rom 1:23, using eikon G1504 — same word as Rev 13's image) is the precise antiparallel of Rev 14:7.
- Key finding: The Rev 14:7 / Rev 16:9 verbal echo uses the same three Greek lexemes (didomi + autos + doxa). The imperative (dote, command) becomes the infinitive (dounai, refused purpose). The gospel call rejected becomes the standard of judgment.
- Key finding: Christ's model (John 17:4) — "I have glorified thee: I have finished the work" — makes glory-giving concrete: faithfully completing assigned work.
- Key finding: Abraham's faith is itself glory-giving (Rom 4:20, dous doxan to theo) — same didomi + doxa + dative construction as Rev 14:7.

**3am-07-hour-of-judgment (Study 7: "The Hour of His Judgment Is Come"):**
- Question: What does hoti elthen he hora tes kriseos autou mean?
- Key finding: Krisis (G2920, 47 NT occurrences) = judicial process/evaluation. Katakrima (G2631, only 3 NT occurrences) = adverse verdict + execution. Romans 8:1 negates katakrima for believers. The angel chose the process word, not the condemnation word.
- Key finding: Daniel 7 vocabulary chain — Aramaic dina → LXX kriterion/krima → NT krisis — connects the first angel's announcement to Daniel's throne-room vision.
- Key finding: Daniel 7:22 — dina yehib leqaddishe elyonin — "judgment was given FOR the saints." The preposition le is benefactive. The judgment reverses persecution, not creates it.
- Key finding: The prophetic aorist elthen presents the judgment's arrival as accomplished certainty. The shift from mellei/mellon (about to, Acts 17:31) to elthen (IS come, Rev 14:7) marks the transition from anticipation to announcement.
- Key finding: Judgment IS gospel content — three independent texts prove this: Rom 2:16 ("according to my gospel"), Luke 3:18 (John's judgment preaching labeled euangelizo), and Rev 14:6-7 (judgment within the euangelion aionion).
- Key finding: Five independent grounds of assurance extend THROUGH judgment: God's promises (1 John 5:13), the Spirit's seal (Eph 1:13-14), Christ's intercession (Rom 8:34), books of faithful service (Mal 3:16), and Christ's completed work (Rom 5:9-10).
- Key finding: The courtroom has a righteous judge (2 Tim 4:8), a defense attorney who is Christ (1 John 2:1), a disbarred accuser (Rev 12:10), and books that include faithful service (Mal 3:16). Judgment is vindication for those in Christ.

**3am-08-worship-the-creator (Study 8: "Worship Him That Made"):**
- Question: Is the creation formula a direct reference to Exodus 20:11?
- Key finding: Four terms match Exo 20:11 LXX in the same sequence: poieo (made), ouranos (heaven), ge (earth), thalassa (sea). The fourth element diverges — "fountains of waters" instead of "all that is in them."
- Key finding: The verb poieo (G4160) is deliberately chosen over ktizo (G2936). Revelation uses ktizo in 4:11 and 10:6 for generic creation praise. Only 14:7 uses poieo — the verb that maps to asah (H6213) in the LXX of Exo 20:11. Mueller (BRI): "By using poieo instead of ktizo, the connection to Genesis 1-2 is even more clearly established, even if through Exodus 20:11."
- Key finding: The commandment inclusio (Rev 12:17 and 14:12 both using entole + theou) brackets the creation formula. In a passage concluding with "the commandments of God," the creation formula's strongest verbal link is to the commandment that memorializes creation.
- Key finding: Paulien's assessment: "There is no direct allusion to the Old Testament in Revelation that is more certain than the allusion to the fourth commandment in Rev 14:7."
- Key finding: Hebrews 4:9 — sabbatismos (G4520, hapax legomenon) means Sabbath-keeping in every extant Greek source. Lincoln (anti-Sabbatarian) concedes the lexical point.
- Key finding: The Sabbath is the only commandment explicitly called owth (sign, H226) — four times (Exo 31:13, 31:17, Eze 20:12, 20:20). The fourth commandment uniquely contains the three elements of a royal seal: name (YHWH), title (Creator), territory (heaven, earth, sea).

## Web Research Findings

The web research agent (Phase 0) gathered extensive scholarly material. Key findings are summarized here (full details in 00-web-research.md):

**Acts 17 / Revelation 14:7 Parallel:**
- Multiple scholars across traditions (Carson/Reformed, Parsons/academic, Perriman/narrative-historical, Bratt/Calvinist) recognize that Paul's Areopagus sermon follows a creation → worship correction → judgment framework.
- Carson explicitly calls this "the necessary framework without which his proclamation of Jesus makes no sense." This directly counters the "natural theology, not gospel" critique.
- Parsons identifies a chiastic structure: A (ignorance) / B (Creator) / C (humanity-God) / B' (idols exposed) / A' (judgment). Creator-worship is the structural center.
- No scholar found disputes that Acts 17 moves from Creator God to appointed judgment.

**Psalm 96 — Creation-Worship-Judgment in OT Worship Form:**
- Scholars uniformly recognize Psalm 96 as containing: (a) "new song" motif, (b) universal call to all nations, (c) creation worship (v.5 "the LORD made the heavens"), (d) glory-giving (vv.7-8), (e) judgment (v.13 "he cometh to judge the earth").
- The "new song" of Ps 96:1 connects to Revelation 14:3's "new song" — linking the psalm directly to the Rev 14 context.
- Gap identified: No major monograph develops the element-by-element Ps 96 / Rev 14:7 comparison. This study can fill that gap.

**Ecclesiastes 12:13-14 — The Structural Twin:**
- The verbal parallels between Eccl 12:13-14 and Rev 14:7,12 are striking: "Fear God" + commandment-keeping + judgment.
- Hebrew kol-ha-adam being "man's all" (not just "man's duty") suggests Eccl 12:13 defines the totality of human existence. This strengthens the parallel: what Solomon calls "the whole of man" is what the first angel proclaims as "the everlasting gospel."
- Gap identified: No major scholarly treatment develops the structural parallel in depth. Most note the thematic connection without detailed comparison.

**The "Natural Theology" Critique:**
- Answering Adventism argues the first angel's message cannot be gospel because it doesn't explicitly mention Christ. 
- Counter-responses from web sources: (a) Gentet argues creation theology IS christological because Christ is the Creator (John 1:1-5); (b) Carson shows the creation-judgment framework is essential to gospel proclamation; (c) Stefanovic insists the text itself calls the message euangelion.
- Paul at Athens also delays mentioning Christ until the climax (v.31). If Paul's speech is gospel, the same framework in Rev 14:7 cannot be dismissed as non-gospel.
