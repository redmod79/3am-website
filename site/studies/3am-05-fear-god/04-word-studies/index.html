    Word Studies¶

    Question¶

    What does phobethete ton theon ('fear God') mean as the first imperative of the everlasting gospel? How does the OT yare/phobos tradition inform this command — is it servile terror or reverent awe? How does Deuteronomy 6:13 and 10:12-13 pair fear and love? Does 'fear God' conflict with 'perfect love casts out fear' (1 John 4:18)? Parse the 1 John 4:18 text carefully — what does phobos meta kolaseos ('fear with punishment') mean, and is that the same kind of fear as Rev 14:7? How does fearing God function as the foundation for refusing to fear the beast and his threats (Rev 13:15, Matt 10:28)? How does Ecclesiastes 12:13 ('Fear God, and keep his commandments: for this is the whole duty of man') provide the OT template for the first angel's message?

    H3372 — yare¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H3372-yare.md

    Occurrences: 414

    Primary OT verb for 'fear' — covers the full spectrum from terror (Gen 3:10) to reverence (Eccl 12:13). Qal dominates (282 of 332 forms) covering both fear and awe. Niphal participle 'nora' = awesome/terrible, applied to God. Collocates with YHWH 130x (41%) forming the 'fear the LORD' covenant formula. Deuteronomy has highest raw count (35). LXX maps to phobeo (G5399) with 241 co-occurrences (PMI 5.75).

    Semantic categories: reverent awe/worship of God, natural terror/human fear, divine reassurance (fear not), the awesome/terrible (Niphal nora), ethical foundation, deterrent fear, syncretistic/divided fear

    Key verses: Eccl 12:13, Deut 10:12, Exod 20:20, Gen 22:12, Prov 1:7

    Relevance: The ROOT behind the entire yare/phobos tradition. Rev 14:7's phobethete inherits this full semantic range through the LXX bridge. The dual use in Exod 20:18-20 (stop terror / maintain reverence) is the paradigm for the filial/slavish distinction.

    H3374 — yirah¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H3374-yirah.md

    Occurrences: 45

    Feminine noun from yare — 'the fear of the LORD' as sustained disposition. Proverbs accounts for 14 of 42 uses. Dominant in wisdom tradition: beginning of knowledge (Prov 1:7), beginning of wisdom (Prov 9:10), hate evil (Prov 8:13). LXX maps to phobos (G5401) 31x; also to eusebeia ('godliness') 3x — LXX translators understood yirah as a life-orientation, not mere terror.

    Semantic categories: foundational principle of wisdom, moral compass — turning from evil, source of life, security, blessing, standard for just rule, Messianic endowment, covenant promise

    Key verses: Prov 1:7, Prov 8:13, Prov 9:10, Prov 14:26-27, Isa 11:2-3, Exod 20:20

    Relevance: The NOUN form that dominates the wisdom tradition. Its consistent association with wisdom, life, confidence, and moral orientation (not terror) demonstrates that the 'fear of the LORD' is a settled disposition of reverent awe.

    G5399 — phobeo¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G5399-phobeo.md

    Occurrences: 110

    NT verb 'to fear/revere' — the LXX equivalent of yare. 90 unique verses across 14 books. Nearly all forms middle/passive (deponent). Present imperative for ongoing fear; aorist imperative for decisive action. Rev 14:7's phobethete is aorist passive imperative — unique decisive call. Collocates with theos 15x. Spans from supernatural terror (Matt 17:6) to reverent awe (Acts 10:2) to anxious dread (Matt 25:25).

    Semantic categories: reverent awe/fear of God, terror at supernatural, angelic fear-not reassurances, Jesus' fear-not commands, fear of human authorities, anxious apprehension, appropriate caution

    Key verses: Rev 14:7, Matt 10:28, Acts 10:2, Luke 12:5, Rev 15:4

    Relevance: The VERB in Rev 14:7's command. Inherits the full range of yare through 241 LXX co-occurrences. The aorist passive imperative form is a decisive, whole-action command.

    G5401 — phobos¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G5401-phobos.md

    Occurrences: 47

    Masculine noun 'fear/terror/reverence.' 44 verses, 47 tokens across 16 books. Hebrew sources: pachad H6343 (35 co-occ) and yirah H3374 (31 co-occ) — inherits BOTH terror and reverence sides. Collocates with theos 9x ('fear of God'). In 1 John 4:18, phobos is cast out by perfect love — but context specifies this is fear-with-punishment (kolasin echei), not reverential awe.

    Semantic categories: reverential awe/fear of God, alarm at divine manifestation, fear spreading through community, fear of human authorities, servile terror/existential dread, respectful deference, eschatological terror

    Key verses: 1 John 4:18, Rom 8:15, Acts 9:31, 2 Cor 7:1, Heb 2:15

    Relevance: The NOUN in 1 John 4:18 — the specific fear being cast out. Its dual Hebrew source (pachad + yirah) explains the semantic ambiguity: context must determine whether terror or reverence is meant.

    G2851 — kolasis¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G2851-kolasis.md

    Occurrences: 2

    Feminine noun 'punishment/torment' — only 2 NT occurrences: Matt 25:46 ('everlasting punishment') and 1 John 4:18 ('fear hath torment'). From kolazō (to curtail/punish). Distinguished from timōria (retributive penalty) — kolasis has judicial/corrective connotation. In 1 John 4:18, the construction is kolasin echei ('fear has/possesses punishment') — fear itself contains a punishing quality.

    Semantic categories: eschatological/judicial punishment, tormenting quality of fear

    Key verses: 1 John 4:18, Matt 25:46

    Relevance: CRITICAL for 1 John 4:18 interpretation. The rarity (only 2 NT uses) and the semantic field (judicial punishment) confirm that the fear cast out by love is specifically the dread of divine punishment — NOT reverential awe.

    G5046 — teleios¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G5046-teleios.md

    Occurrences: 17

    Adjective 'complete/perfect/mature.' From telos (end/goal). 17 verses, 19 tokens. KJV 'perfect' 88%. Semantic range: moral maturity (Matt 5:48), mature understanding (1 Cor 2:6), lacking nothing (Jas 1:4), perfect quality (1 John 4:18). In 1 John 4:18, teleia agape = love that has reached its goal/completion. Antonym: nepios (infant/immature).

    Semantic categories: moral/spiritual maturity, mature understanding, complete/lacking nothing, perfect quality

    Key verses: 1 John 4:18, Matt 5:48, Col 1:28, Jas 1:4

    Relevance: Defines what kind of love casts out fear: love that has reached its GOAL, love that is COMPLETE. Not abstract perfection but functional maturity — the love of one who knows God's character and trusts his purposes.

    G4352 — proskyneo¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G4352-proskyneo.md

    Occurrences: 65

    Verb 'to worship/prostrate.' From pros (toward) + kuneo (to kiss). 54 unique verses, 65 tokens. KJV always 'worship/worshipped.' The third imperative of Rev 14:7 and the verb for beast-worship in Rev 13:4,8,12,15. Same word for both: the choice is whom to worship.

    Semantic categories: worship of God, worship of Christ, false worship/beast worship, homage to humans

    Key verses: Rev 14:7, Rev 13:4, Rev 13:15, Matt 4:10, Rev 15:4

    Relevance: The behavioral expression of fearing God. Rev 14:7 climaxes with proskynesate — worship the Creator. The same verb describes beast-worship in Rev 13, making the contest explicit.

    H7812 — shachah¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H7812-shachah.md

    Occurrences: 194

    Verb 'to bow down/worship/prostrate.' 166 verses, 194 tokens across 25 books. Hishtaphel stem dominates (99%). KJV 'worship' 46%, 'bowed' 29%. The primary Hebrew behind proskyneo in worship contexts.

    Semantic categories: worship of God, social/political obeisance, idol worship

    Key verses: Ps 96:9, Exod 20:5, Dan 3:18, Matt 4:10

    Relevance: The OT worship verb behind proskyneo. The fear → worship connection (Rev 14:7) maps onto the yare → shachah pattern in the OT.

    G26 — agape¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G26-agape.md

    Occurrences: 116

    Feminine noun 'love' — 116 tokens in 106 verses. KJV 'love' 64%, 'charity' 18%. Strongest density in 1 John (13.3 per 100 verses). The teleia agape of 1 John 4:18 is love that has reached its goal. Antonym of phobos in 1 John 4:18.

    Semantic categories: God's love for humanity, human love for God, mutual love among believers, love as perfect/complete

    Key verses: 1 John 4:18, 1 John 4:8, 1 John 4:16, Deut 6:5 (LXX)

    Relevance: The love that casts out fear. John defines agape as keeping commandments (1 John 5:3) — the same obedience that flows from fearing God (Eccl 12:13). Love and fear produce the same result.

    G906 — ballo¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G906-ballo.md

    Occurrences: 127

    Verb 'to throw/cast.' 106 verses, 127 tokens. Highest density in Revelation (5.7 per 100 verses). In 1 John 4:18, ballei exo = present active indicative 3S — love CONTINUOUSLY casts out fear. The ongoing present tense suggests a dynamic process, not a one-time event.

    Semantic categories: throwing/casting objects, divine judgment, love displacing fear

    Key verses: 1 John 4:18, Matt 3:10, Rev 20:15

    Relevance: The verb for love's action against fear. Present active indicative = ongoing, continuous casting out. Love doesn't eliminate fear once; it continuously displaces servile fear.

    G1391 — doxa¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G1391-doxa.md

    Occurrences: 168

    Feminine noun 'glory/honor/splendor.' 151 verses, 168 tokens. From dokeo (to seem). Shifted from classical 'opinion' to LXX 'visible majesty' (translating kavod H3519). The second imperative of Rev 14:7: 'give glory to him.'

    Semantic categories: divine radiance/splendor, honor/reputation, praise/doxology

    Key verses: Rev 14:7, Rev 15:4, Rom 3:23

    Relevance: The middle imperative of Rev 14:7's triad (fear → give glory → worship). Giving glory is the bridge between fearing God and worshipping him.

    G2920 — krisis¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G2920-krisis.md

    Occurrences: 47

    Feminine noun 'judgment/decision.' 47 verses in NT. From krino (to judge). KJV 'judgment' 55%. In Rev 14:7: 'the hour of his judgment (krisis) is come.' In 1 John 4:17: 'boldness in the day of judgment (krisis).' Same word in both — linking the fear-of-God command to the judgment context.

    Semantic categories: divine judgment, judicial process, condemnation

    Key verses: Rev 14:7, 1 John 4:17, John 5:24, Matt 12:36

    Relevance: Connects Rev 14:7 and 1 John 4:17 — both address the 'day of judgment.' The first angel announces judgment (producing proper fear); 1 John 4:17-18 addresses judicial fear (casting out punishment-dread). Same judgment, different fear-responses.

    H4941 — mishpat¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H4941-mishpat.md

    Occurrences: 448

    Masculine noun 'judgment/justice/ordinance.' 406 verses, 448 tokens across 31 books. From shaphat (to judge). KJV 'judgment' 72%. The Hebrew counterpart to krisis. In Eccl 12:14: 'God shall bring every work into judgment (mishpat).'

    Semantic categories: divine judgment, justice/right, ordinance/statute

    Key verses: Eccl 12:14, Ps 96:13, Deut 10:18

    Relevance: The OT judgment word paralleling Rev 14:7's krisis. Eccl 12:14's mishpat maps to Rev 14:7's krisis through the LXX.

    H8104 — shamar¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H8104-shamar.md

    Occurrences: 511

    Verb 'to keep/guard/watch.' 408 tokens. Primitive root meaning 'to hedge about with thorns.' KJV 'keep' 55%. In Eccl 12:13 and Deut 10:13: 'keep his commandments.' The obedience-verb paired with yare in the fear-obedience pattern.

    Semantic categories: covenant obedience, guarding/protecting, watchfulness

    Key verses: Eccl 12:13, Deut 10:13, Gen 2:15, Ps 119:4

    Relevance: The 'keep' in 'fear God and keep his commandments.' Paired with yare in every farewell address: Moses, Joshua, Samuel, Solomon.

    H367 — eymah¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H367-eymah.md

    Occurrences: 20

    Feminine noun 'terror/dread/horror.' 20 occurrences. From unused root meaning to frighten. Concentrated in Job (6 of 20). Describes visceral, involuntary terror: the horror of Abram's vision (Gen 15:12), terror falling on nations (Exod 15:16), Job's complaint about God's terrifying presence (Job 9:34, 13:21). Also used for idols as 'bugbears' (Jer 50:38). LXX maps to phobos 11x.

    Semantic categories: terror before God's power, terror before human power, terror as oppressive force (Job), psychological anguish, idols as objects of terror

    Key verses: Gen 15:12, Exod 15:16, Job 9:34, Job 13:21

    Relevance: DISTINCT from yirah — represents the servile terror side of fear. Job wants eymah REMOVED (9:34, 13:21). This is the type of fear that 1 John 4:18 addresses (fear with punishment). Eymah contrasts with yirah as slavish fear contrasts with filial fear.

    H6343 — pachad¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H6343-pachad.md

    Occurrences: 51

    Masculine noun 'dread/sudden alarm.' 51 occurrences. From H6342 (to be startled). Concentrated in Job (11), Psalms (9). Denotes both the emotion (sudden dread) and the object feared (the dreadful thing). More visceral/physical than yare. LXX maps to phobos 35x — the LARGEST Hebrew source for phobos.

    Semantic categories: sudden dread/visceral terror, object of dread/thing feared, divine dread falling on peoples, nocturnal/irrational fear

    Key verses: Job 3:25, Job 4:14, Ps 91:5, Isa 24:17-18

    Relevance: Completes the Hebrew fear-word triad: yirah (reverence), eymah (horror), pachad (sudden dread). Pachad is the biggest LXX contributor to phobos (35 co-occ), meaning phobos inherits the alarm-side as well as the reverence-side.

    G2125 — eulabeomai¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G2125-eulabeomai.md

    Occurrences: 2

    Verb 'to be circumspect/reverent.' Only 2 NT occurrences: Heb 11:7 (Noah 'moved with fear') and Acts 23:10 (tribune 'fearing' for Paul). From eu (well) + lambano (to take) — 'to take/handle well.' Always positive: reverent caution that produces constructive action. Distinguished from raw terror by deliberate, thoughtful response.

    Semantic categories: reverent caution before God, prudent apprehension before danger

    Key verses: Heb 11:7, Acts 23:10

    Relevance: Represents the POSITIVE fear category in Greek — the fear that accompanies faith (Heb 11:7). Noah's eulabeomai is the paradigm: fear + faith = constructive action (building the ark). This is the kind of fear Rev 14:7 commands.

    G2124 — eulabeia¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G2124-eulabeia.md

    Occurrences: 2

    Feminine noun 'godly fear/reverence.' Only 2 occurrences, both in Hebrews: 5:7 (Christ 'was heard in that he feared') and 12:28 ('serve God with reverence and godly fear'). The noun form of eulabeomai. Paired with aidos (reverence/modesty) in 12:28. Grace-context: receiving an unshakable kingdom → serve with eulabeia.

    Semantic categories: reverent caution before God, worship disposition accompanying grace

    Key verses: Heb 5:7, Heb 12:28

    Relevance: CRITICAL: Christ himself had eulabeia (Heb 5:7), and believers under grace are to serve with eulabeia (Heb 12:28). If Jesus feared God this way, the command to fear God (Rev 14:7) is not sub-Christian but Christlike.

    G1169 — deilos¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G1169-deilos.md

    Occurrences: 3

    Adjective 'cowardly/timid/fearful.' Only 3 NT occurrences: Matt 8:26 and Mark 4:40 (Jesus rebukes disciples' cowardice in the storm) and Rev 21:8 (the cowardly head the list of the lost). Always negative — linked to oligopistos (little faith) and apistos (unbelief). The deil- family (G1167 deilia, G1168 deiliao, G1169 deilos) stands in CONTRAST to the phob- family.

    Semantic categories: cowardly faithlessness in crisis, condemned cowardice among the lost, contrast with commanded fear of God

    Key verses: Matt 8:26, Mark 4:40, Rev 21:8, 2 Tim 1:7

    Relevance: The OPPOSITE of the fear commanded in Rev 14:7. Deilos-fear (cowardice before circumstances) is condemned; phobeo-fear (reverence before God) is commanded. Rev 21:8 vs. Rev 14:7 — the cowardly perish while the God-fearers prevail.

    H157 — ahab¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H157-ahab.md

    Occurrences: 225

    Verb 'to love/have affection for.' 225 occurrences across 27 books. Highest density in Hosea (11.6 per 100 verses). Covers covenantal love (Deut 6:5), God's love (Deut 7:8), interpersonal love, romantic love. In Deut 10:12, ahab appears THIRD in the sequence after yare and halak — love follows fear in the covenant pattern.

    Semantic categories: covenantal love toward God, God's love toward people, interpersonal love, romantic love

    Key verses: Deut 6:5, Deut 10:12, Deut 11:13, Deut 30:6

    Relevance: The Hebrew love-verb paired with yare (fear) in Deut 10:12. Fear and love appear as CO-REQUIREMENTS, not alternatives. Moskala claims biblical Hebrew preserves this integration — fear includes love.

    H4172 — mora¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H4172-môwrâʼ.md

    Occurrences: 16

    Masculine noun 'fear/terror/awesome thing.' 16 occurrences. From yare (H3372). Used for terrifying deeds (Deut 4:34, 26:8), dread of a superior (Gen 9:2), reverent fear due to God (Mal 1:6, Isa 8:13). In Isa 8:13, God should BE Israel's mora — their object of fear. More concrete than yirah — denotes the feared object rather than the inner disposition.

    Semantic categories: terrifying object or deed, dread inspired by a superior, reverent fear due to God, human fear of threats

    Key verses: Isa 8:13, Mal 1:6, Deut 4:34, Gen 9:2

    Relevance: Fills out the H3372 yare word family. Isa 8:13 ('let him be your mora') parallels Matt 10:28 — redirect fear from human threats to God himself.

    G4576 — sebomai¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G4576-sébomai.md

    Occurrences: 10

    Verb 'to venerate/worship.' 10 occurrences (8 in Acts). Used primarily for 'God-fearers' — Gentiles who revered Israel's God (Acts 13:43, 16:14, 17:4). Also used for vain worship (Matt 15:9 / Mark 7:7 quoting Isa 29:13) and pagan worship (Acts 19:27). LXX maps from yare H3372 (3 co-occ).

    Semantic categories: Gentile God-fearers, vain worship/misdirected reverence, pagan worship

    Key verses: Matt 15:9, Acts 13:43, Acts 16:14, Acts 17:4

    Relevance: Part of the worship vocabulary alongside proskyneo and phobeo. The 'God-fearers' (sebomenoi) in Acts demonstrate what fearing God looks like in the Hellenistic world — the same category as Cornelius (Acts 10:2, using phobeo).

    G3954 — parrhesia¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G3954-parrhesia.md

    Occurrences: 31

    Feminine noun 'boldness/confidence/openness.' 31 verses across 11 books. From pas (all) + rhesis (speech) — 'all-outspokenness.' KJV 'boldness' 26%, 'confidence' 19%. In 1 John 4:17, parrhesia in the day of judgment is the FRUIT of perfected love — confidence replaces judicial fear.

    Semantic categories: boldness in speech, confidence before God, openness/public declaration

    Key verses: 1 John 4:17, Heb 4:16, Acts 4:13

    Relevance: The POSITIVE counterpart to the fear cast out in 1 John 4:18. Perfect love produces parrhesia (boldness/confidence) in the day of judgment — replacing kolasin-fear with confident trust.

    H2617 — checed¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H2617-checed.md

    Occurrences: 255

    Masculine noun 'lovingkindness/mercy/covenant faithfulness.' 241 verses, 255 tokens. Psalms dominates (127). The covenant-love that grounds the fear-love relationship. Paired with yirah 3x in collocations.

    Semantic categories: God's covenant love, human loyalty, mercy

    Key verses: Ps 136:1, Ps 130:7, Prov 16:6

    Relevance: The covenant-love context for the fear-love pairing. Prov 16:6 pairs checed/truth with yirah: 'by mercy and truth iniquity is purged; by the fear of the LORD men depart from evil.' God's checed is the GROUND for proper fear.

    G2919 — krino¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G2919-krino.md

    Occurrences: 132

    Verb 'to judge/decide/condemn.' 98 verses, 132 tokens. Root of krisis (G2920). Active voice dominates (74%). Spans judicial, evaluative, and decisional senses.

    Semantic categories: divine judgment, human judicial process, moral evaluation

    Key verses: John 5:22, Acts 17:31, Rom 2:16

    Relevance: The verb behind krisis (judgment). The first angel announces that the hour of God's krisis has come — the same judgment process that 1 John 4:17 addresses.

    G25 — agapao¶

    Source: D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G25-agapao.md

    Occurrences: 147

    Verb 'to love.' 109 verses, 147 tokens. Present active dominates (72 tokens). 11 imperative occurrences. In Deut 6:5 LXX: agapeseis = 'thou shalt love' — the love verb behind the greatest commandment.

    Semantic categories: God's love for humanity, commanded love for God, love for neighbor

    Key verses: Deut 6:5, Matt 22:37, John 3:16, 1 John 4:19

    Relevance: The verb form of agape. Deut 10:12 pairs yare (fear) with ahab (love) = phobeo with agapao in the LXX. Fear and love are co-required in the covenant.
