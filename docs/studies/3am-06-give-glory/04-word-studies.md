# Word Studies

## Question
What does dote auto doxan ("give glory to him") mean as a gospel imperative? How is glory (doxa/kabod) given to God — through confession (Josh 7:19), character acknowledgment (Isa 42:12), conduct (Matt 5:16, 1 Pet 2:12), fruit-bearing (John 15:8), bodily stewardship (1 Cor 6:20, 10:31), beholding-transformation (2 Cor 3:18), or something else? What is the relationship between giving glory and the judgment announcement that immediately follows? How does Romans 1:21 ("they glorified him not as God") describe the exact failure the first angel addresses? Why does Rev 16:9 record that under the plagues they "repented not to give him glory" — a precise verbal echo of 14:7? How does John 17:4 ("I have glorified thee on the earth: I have finished the work") establish Christ as the model of giving glory?

### G1391 — doxa

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G1391-doxa.md`

**Occurrences:** 168

Glory, honor, splendor. From dokeo (to seem/be recognized). Classical meaning 'opinion/reputation' was transformed through LXX translation of kabod (H3519, 170 co-occurrences, PMI 6.32) to carry the Hebrew meanings of weight, substance, brightness, and character. Six semantic categories: divine luminous presence, Christ's inherent glory, eschatological glory, doxological ascription, human honor/status, natural splendor. The doxa-kabod bridge is the foundational linguistic connection for this study.

**Semantic categories:** divine luminous presence, Christ's inherent glory, eschatological glory, doxological ascription, human honor/status, natural splendor

**Key verses:** Rev 14:7, John 1:14, 2 Cor 3:18, Rom 1:23, Rom 3:23, Rev 4:11

**Relevance:** The core noun of the study. 'Give glory' (dote doxan) in Rev 14:7 commands acknowledging God's full weight/substance/character. The kabod→doxa bridge means 'glory' carries OT meanings of character, presence, and radiance.

### G1392 — doxazo

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G1392-doxazo.md`

**Occurrences:** 71

To glorify, render glorious. From doxa (G1391). 54 unique verses across 13 books. John dominates (16/54 = 30%), concentrated in chapters 12-17. Six semantic categories: humans glorifying God (response to works), humans glorifying God (intentional obedience), mutual Father-Son glorification, God glorifying believers, word/gospel being glorified, negative/ironic uses. LXX bridge: kabad H3513 (28x, PMI 6.54).

**Semantic categories:** response to mighty works, intentional obedience, mutual Father-Son glorification, God glorifying believers, gospel glorified, failure to glorify

**Key verses:** Rom 1:21, John 17:4, Matt 5:16, 1 Cor 6:20, John 15:8, Acts 12:23

**Relevance:** The core verb. Used in Rom 1:21 (edoxasan — 'they glorified not'), John 17:4 (edoxasa — 'I have glorified'), 1 Cor 6:20 (doxasate — aorist imperative), Matt 5:16 (doxasosin — purpose subjunctive). Maps the full range of how glorification occurs.

### H3519 — kabowd

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H3519-kabowd.md`

**Occurrences:** 214

Glory, honor, weight. From kabad (H3513, 'to be heavy'). 189 unique verses in 27 OT books. Root meaning is literal heaviness applied metaphorically: what has 'weight' commands honor. Six semantic categories: divine theophanic presence, divine identity/exclusivity, liturgical/doxological use, human honor/wealth, glory departing/defiled, eschatological universal revelation. LXX maps overwhelmingly to doxa (G1391) with 170 co-occurrences.

**Semantic categories:** divine theophanic presence, divine identity/exclusivity, liturgical/doxological use, human honor/wealth, glory departing/defiled, eschatological revelation

**Key verses:** Exod 33:18, Exod 40:34, Isa 6:3, Hab 2:14, Psa 19:1, 1 Chr 16:28-29

**Relevance:** The Hebrew foundation. Kabod's root meaning 'weight/heaviness' grounds the concept: glory is substance, not decoration. Exod 33:18-19 equates kabod with goodness/character. The LXX kabod→doxa bridge transferred these rich meanings into the NT.

### H3513 — kabad

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H3513-kabad.md`

**Occurrences:** 141

To be heavy; in good sense: to honor, glorify; in bad sense: to be burdensome, to harden. Primitive root verb. 108 unique verses across 26 books. Verb stems: Piel (honor/glorify, 38x), Niphal (be honored, 30x), Qal (be heavy, 22x), Hiphil (make heavy/harden, 17x). LXX splits: doxazo (28x) for honor/glorify sense; baryno (25x) for physical weight/hardening sense.

**Semantic categories:** to honor persons, to glorify God, to be heavy/burdensome, to harden heart, to be distinguished, God glorified through judgment

**Key verses:** Exod 20:12, Lev 10:3, 1 Sam 2:30, Isa 24:15, Psa 22:23

**Relevance:** The root verb behind kabod (H3519). The Piel stem ('honor/glorify') is the active sense used when humans glorify God. The LXX's semantic split (doxazo for honor, baryno for weight) shows how the translation tradition handled kabad's dual range.

### G1325 — didomi

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G1325-didomi.md`

**Occurrences:** 430

To give (primary verb, prolonged form). 377 unique verses across 22 books. Revelation has highest density (12.1/100 verses). Seven semantic categories: material giving, delegated authority, God's gifts, Father giving through Son, self-giving sacrifice, granting permission, rendering/producing. Hebrew source: nathan H5414 (1330 co-occurrences).

**Semantic categories:** material giving, delegated authority, God's gifts, Father-Son giving, self-giving sacrifice, granting permission, rendering/producing

**Key verses:** Rev 14:7, Rev 16:9, Rev 11:13, Rom 4:20, John 9:24, Acts 12:23

**Relevance:** The verb in 'give glory' (dote doxan). Rev 14:7 uses the 2nd aorist active imperative (dote). The same didomi+doxa construction appears in Rev 16:9 (dounai, infinitive), Rev 11:13 (edokan, indicative), Rom 4:20 (dous, participle), Acts 12:23 (ouk edoken, negative). Tracing this construction across Revelation maps the glory-giving/refusing pattern.

### H3034 — yadah

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H3034-yadah.md`

**Occurrences:** 136

To praise, give thanks (Hiphil); to confess (Hithpael). Denominative from yad ('hand') — literally 'to use/extend the hand.' 111 unique verses. The dual meaning (praise/confess) is significant: praise and confession are two sides of the same act of acknowledging God. Hiphil dominates (~90%, praise/thanks); Hithpael is restricted to confession contexts. LXX maps to exomologeo G1843 (78x, PMI 7.58).

**Semantic categories:** praise and thanksgiving, confession of sin, communal confession, liturgical worship, personal acknowledgment

**Key verses:** Psa 136:1, Lev 16:21, Dan 9:4, Gen 29:35, Josh 7:19

**Relevance:** Critical for the confession idiom. In Josh 7:19, todah (from yadah) parallels kabod — 'give glory AND make confession.' Yadah's dual meaning shows that praise-giving and sin-confessing are unified: both acknowledge that God is right.

### G3670 — homologeo

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G3670-homologeo.md`

**Occurrences:** 26

To confess, profess, acknowledge. Compound of homos ('same') + lego ('to speak') — literally 'to say the same thing.' 21 unique verses across 10 books. Four senses: agree with, concede/promise, profess openly, praise (Heb 13:15). Present tense dominates Johannine confession-of-faith passages; aorist captures decisive confessing.

**Semantic categories:** agree/assent, confess sins, profess faith, praise/celebrate

**Key verses:** Rom 10:9-10, 1 John 1:9, 1 John 4:2-3, Phil 2:11, John 1:20

**Relevance:** The confession vocabulary. 'To say the same thing' — confession means agreeing with God's assessment. Phil 2:11 ('every tongue confess...to the glory of God') explicitly links confession with glorification.

### G1843 — exomologeo

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G1843-exomologeo.md`

**Occurrences:** 11

To confess openly, acknowledge fully, give thanks. Compound of ek ('out of') + homologeo. 11 unique verses. Almost entirely middle voice, emphasizing personal involvement. Preserves yadah's dual range: both confession of sin (Matt 3:6, James 5:16) and thanksgiving/praise to God (Matt 11:25, Rom 15:9). Primary LXX rendering of yadah (78x, PMI 33.14).

**Semantic categories:** confess sins, give thanks, praise God, confess allegiance

**Key verses:** Matt 3:6, Matt 11:25, Phil 2:11, Rom 14:11, James 5:16

**Relevance:** The LXX bridge from yadah (H3034). Preserves the dual confession/praise meaning. Phil 2:11 ('every tongue should confess') uses this word in the ultimate glorification context.

### G1504 — eikon

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G1504-eikon.md`

**Occurrences:** 23

Image, likeness, representation. 23 occurrences across 9 books. Revelation dominates (10x). Three semantic categories: imperial coin inscription (civic), Christ as image of God (Christological), human transformation into God's image (soteriological). Used in 2 Cor 3:18 ('the same image'), Rom 1:23 ('changed glory into an image'), Col 1:15 ('image of the invisible God').

**Semantic categories:** imperial coin, Christ as image of God, human transformation into image, beast-image

**Key verses:** 2 Cor 3:18, Rom 1:23, Col 1:15, Rom 8:29, Rev 13:14-15

**Relevance:** Central to the beholding-transformation mechanism. 2 Cor 3:18: believers are changed into 'the same image' (eikona). Rom 1:23: humanity exchanged glory for an image. Rev 13-14: the beast-image vs. God's image — the eschatological form of the glory-exchange.

### G4983 — soma

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G4983-soma.md`

**Occurrences:** 146

Body (as a sound whole). 122 unique verses in 18 books. Used in 1 Cor 6:19-20 where the body is declared naos (G3485, inner temple) and the arena for glorifying God. Paul uses soma theologically: the body is not merely physical but is the locus of redemption, worship, and moral agency.

**Semantic categories:** physical body, resurrection body, body of Christ, body as temple, body-soul unity

**Key verses:** 1 Cor 6:19-20, Rom 12:1, 1 Cor 15:43-44, Phil 3:21

**Relevance:** The body as arena of glorification. 1 Cor 6:20 commands 'glorify God in your body.' Rom 12:1 extends this: 'present your bodies a living sacrifice.' The purchase-price argument (agorazo) establishes that the body belongs to God and must be used for His glory.

### G3485 — naos

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G3485-naos.md`

**Occurrences:** 46

Temple, sanctuary — specifically the inner sanctuary where deity dwells (from naio, 'to dwell'), as distinct from hieron (the entire temple precinct). 40 NT occurrences. In 1 Cor 6:19, the body is called naos — not the outer temple precinct but the inner holy place where God's presence dwells through the Spirit.

**Semantic categories:** Jerusalem temple, body as temple, heavenly temple, metaphorical temple

**Key verses:** 1 Cor 6:19, 1 Cor 3:16-17, 2 Cor 6:16, Rev 21:22

**Relevance:** The naos/hieron distinction matters: calling the body naos means it is the INNER sanctuary, the dwelling place of deity — not merely a sacred precinct. This elevates bodily stewardship from practical wisdom to holy-place maintenance.

### G2920 — krisis

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G2920-krisis.md`

**Occurrences:** 47

Judgment, judicial process. From krino (G2919, to separate/judge). 47 NT occurrences. Distinguished from katakrima (condemnation) — krisis is the judicial PROCESS, not necessarily the negative verdict. LXX maps from both mishpat (H4941, verdict/ordinance, 132x) and riyb (H7379, covenant lawsuit, 29x).

**Semantic categories:** court of justice, judicial activity, evaluation, punishment

**Key verses:** Rev 14:7, John 5:24, Matt 12:36, Acts 17:31

**Relevance:** The judgment announcement following 'give glory' in Rev 14:7: 'the hour of his krisis is come.' The riyb→krisis bridge (TR-riyb-krisis.md) shows the covenant-lawsuit background: Rev 14:7 invokes God's legal right to judge creation.

### G2570 — kalos

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G2570-kalos.md`

**Occurrences:** 102

Good, beautiful, noble, fit. Of uncertain affinity. 91 unique verses across 16 books. Straddles aesthetic (beautiful) and ethical (good) domains. Distinguished from agathos (intrinsically good): kalos emphasizes observable/appreciable excellence — beauty and fitness perceived by others.

**Semantic categories:** morally good, aesthetically beautiful, fit/proper, noble/admirable

**Key verses:** Matt 5:16, 1 Pet 2:12, 1 Tim 6:12, Heb 10:24

**Relevance:** The 'kala erga' (beautiful/good works) in Matt 5:16 and 1 Pet 2:12 are the visible conduct that causes others to glorify God. Kalos means the works are aesthetically compelling — attracting glory — not just morally correct.

### G3340 — metanoeo

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G3340-metanoeo.md`

**Occurrences:** 36

To repent, change one's mind. Compound of meta ('change') + noeo ('to think/perceive'). 32 unique verses in 6 books. Fundamentally cognitive/volitional — a deliberate re-orientation of thought and will, not merely emotional remorse. Concentrated in Synoptics, Acts, and Revelation.

**Semantic categories:** change behavior, turn to God, moral reversal

**Key verses:** Rev 16:9, Rev 16:11, Acts 2:38, Acts 17:30, Rev 9:20-21

**Relevance:** Critical for the Rev 16:9 echo: 'they repented not (ou metenoasan) to give him glory.' The refusal to repent IS the refusal to glorify. Rev 9:20-21 is the precursor. Metanoeo's cognitive nature means it involves the re-orientation of mind that enables glory-giving.

### G2041 — ergon

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G2041-ergon.md`

**Occurrences:** 176

Work, deed, action. 161 unique verses in 26 books. Three senses: occupation/employment, product/accomplishment, act/deed. First occurrence: Matt 5:16 ('your good works'). In John 17:4, 'the work (ergon) which thou gavest me to do' — Christ's entire redemptive mission is one ergon.

**Semantic categories:** occupation/employment, product/accomplishment, act/deed, works of God, works of law

**Key verses:** John 17:4, Matt 5:16, 1 Pet 2:12, Eph 2:10, Rev 20:12

**Relevance:** The 'work' that Christ completed in glorifying the Father (John 17:4). The kala erga ('beautiful works') in Matt 5:16 and 1 Pet 2:12 are the conduct that causes others to glorify God. Believers' works are the visible arena of glory-giving.

### G3339 — metamorphoo

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G3339-metamorphóō.md`

**Occurrences:** 4

To transform, transfigure. From meta ('change') + morphoo ('to form'). Only 4 NT occurrences: Matt 17:2, Mark 9:2 (Christ's transfiguration), Rom 12:2 ('be ye transformed'), 2 Cor 3:18 ('are changed'). Denotes change of essential form (morphe), not merely outward appearance (schema). Contrasts with metaschematizo (G3345, change of external presentation) and syschematizo (G4964, conform to external pattern).

**Semantic categories:** physical transfiguration, spiritual/moral transformation

**Key verses:** 2 Cor 3:18, Rom 12:2, Matt 17:2, Mark 9:2

**Relevance:** The transformation verb in 2 Cor 3:18: metamorphoumetha (present passive — 'we are being transformed'). Same verb as Christ's transfiguration — the transformation is real and radical. In 2 Cor 3:18, the transformation is from glory to glory by the Spirit. In Rom 12:2, transformation is contrasted with conformity (syschematizo). This is how glory-giving becomes possible: believers are transformed into glory-bearing image.

### G2734 — katoptrizomai

**Occurrences:** 1

To behold as in a mirror; or to reflect as a mirror. Hapax legomenon — occurs only in 2 Cor 3:18. From katoptron ('mirror'). Middle voice. The 'beholding vs. reflecting' debate: Meyer and most moderns favor 'beholding as in a mirror' for the middle voice; Chrysostom and others favor 'reflecting as a mirror.' The middle voice in Greek usage favors the subject's own action of looking, not the mirror's function of reflecting.

**Semantic categories:** beholding/contemplating, reflecting

**Key verses:** 2 Cor 3:18

**Relevance:** The critical verb in the beholding-transformation mechanism. If 'beholding': believers look at God's glory and are changed. If 'reflecting': believers mirror God's glory to others. Either way, sustained engagement with divine glory produces transformation. The hapax status means the word's meaning must be determined from context and cognate usage.

**Semantic categories:** believe/trust, have faith, entrust

**Relevance:** Faith as glory-giving. Rom 4:20: Abraham was 'strong in faith, giving glory to God' — the participle dous doxan is circumstantial to pistei. Faith and glory-giving are identified. Num 20:12 confirms the inverse: Moses' unbelief = failure to sanctify/glorify.

World, ordered arrangement. 152 unique verses across 20 books. Used in John 17:5 ('the glory which I had with thee before the world was') — Christ's pre-existent glory before the kosmos existed.

**Semantic categories:** physical world, humanity, world system, orderly arrangement

**Key verses:** John 17:5, John 3:16, Rom 1:20, 1 John 2:15-16

**Relevance:** John 17:5: Christ's glory predates the kosmos. The 'before the world was' phrase establishes that glory is not a created attribute but belongs to Christ inherently.
