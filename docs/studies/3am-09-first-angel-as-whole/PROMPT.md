## Question

## Prior Research Summary
This is the first mini-capstone in the 3AM series (Study 9 of 21), synthesizing five element studies (04-08). Study 04 established that the anarthrous euangelion aionion is qualitative, that the legon participle structurally embeds the commands AS gospel content, and that the cognate accusative (euangelion + euangelisai) intensifies the gospel-ness. Study 05 showed phobethete ton theon means reverential awe (not terror), grounded in the yirah tradition, and identified the Eccl 12:13-14 template and the farewell-address pattern. Study 06 identified seven modes of glory-giving and the Rom 1:21 antiparallel. Study 07 established krisis (process) vs. katakrima (condemnation), the Dan 7:22 benefactive judgment, the prophetic aorist elthen, and three texts that explicitly label judgment as gospel (Rom 2:16, Luke 3:18, Rev 14:6-7). Study 08 confirmed the four-term Exo 20:11 allusion (poieo not ktizo), the commandment inclusio (Rev 12:17/14:12), and the Sabbath sign (owth). External corpus (EGW, Bohr, Froom) and web research (Carson, Parsons, Paulien, Perriman) provide leads to verify, especially regarding Acts 17 structure, Psalm 96 mapping, and the 'natural theology' critique.

### Focus Areas

1. **The structural unity of Rev 14:6-7 as one integrated proclamation:**
This is the controlling focus area. READ the grammar reference entries for aorist imperative ($GRAMMAR_REF/greek/mood-aorist-imperative.md) and the legon discourse connector ($GRAMMAR_REF/greek/participle-legon-discourse.md). Run greek_parser.py --verse on Rev 14:6-7 to get the full morphological breakdown. The legon participle (present active nominative masculine singular) structurally embeds v.7's content as the CONTENT of the euangelion in v.6 — the commands are not appended to the gospel but ARE the gospel. The three aorist imperatives (phobethete, dote, proskynesate) form a climactic triad: internal orientation (fear), relational acknowledgment (give glory), behavioral expression (worship). These are not three separate actions but one reality viewed from three angles. The hoti clause introduces the ground/reason for all three imperatives. The creation formula specifies WHO is to be worshipped — him that made (poieo, not ktizo). Run cross_testament_parallels_v2.py --hybrid-nt on Rev 14:7 to find NT parallels.

5. **The 'natural theology' objection and why it fails:**
The Answering Adventism critique argues Rev 14:6-7 cannot be gospel because it doesn't explicitly mention Christ. Counter this from three angles: (1) CHRISTOLOGICAL CONTENT IS IMPLICIT — 'him that made' is Christ (John 1:1-3, Col 1:15-17, Heb 1:2), 'the hour of his judgment' involves Christ as judge (Acts 17:31, John 5:22), and Rev 14:12's 'faith of Jesus' explicitly names Christ. READ the word studies for G4102-pistis ($WORD_STUDIES/G4102-pistis.md). (2) ACTS 17 PRECEDENT — Paul also delays mentioning Christ until the climax (v.31), yet his speech is universally recognized as gospel proclamation. If Paul's Athens sermon is gospel with delayed christological naming, Rev 14:7 is gospel with delayed christological naming. (3) THE TEXT SAYS IT IS GOSPEL — the angel has euangelion aionion to proclaim (v.6); the legon participle makes v.7 the content of that euangelion. The burden of proof falls on the critic who says the text is wrong about its own label. Also address Gal 1:6-9 — Paul condemns adding salvation CONDITIONS (circumcision), not adding gospel CONTENT (judgment, creation, commandments) that he himself preached (Rom 2:16, Acts 17:24, 1 Cor 7:19). Run greek_parser.py on Gal 1:6-9.

6. **The poieo vs. ktizo distinction and the fourth commandment allusion:**
READ the word studies for G4160-poieo ($WORD_STUDIES/G4160-poieo.md) and G2936-ktizo ($WORD_STUDIES/G2936-ktizo.md).
