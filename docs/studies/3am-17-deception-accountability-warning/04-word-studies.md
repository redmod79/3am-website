# Word Studies

## Question
Does everyone who receives the mark of the beast end up lost? What about those who were deceived (Rev 19:20)? Does God guarantee fair warning before the mark crisis? Can the mark be resisted (Rev 15:2)? How does 2 Thessalonians 2:10-12 explain the relationship between deception and accountability — is the "strong delusion" a cause of truth-rejection or a consequence? Trace the judicial delusion pattern: Romans 1:24-28, Psalm 81:11-12, Isaiah 6:9-10. How does graduated accountability work (Luke 12:47-48, Acts 17:30, 1 Tim 1:13)? How does the OT ignorance-vs-presumption distinction (Lev 4, Num 15:22-31) establish the framework? Is the close of probation taught in the Bible — how do Rev 22:11, Rev 15:8, and Daniel 12:1 converge?

### G4106 — plane

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G4106-plane.md`

**Occurrences:** 10

Feminine noun: error, deceit, delusion. In 2 Thess 2:11 forms energeian planes -- 'working of delusion.' Only NT instance where God governs plane. Straddles cognitive (wrong belief) and moral (perversion) domains. Antonym of aletheia.

**Semantic categories:** doctrinal error, eschatological delusion as judgment, moral corruption, consequence of sin

**Key verses:** 2 Thess 2:11, 1 John 4:6, Rom 1:27, Jas 5:20

**Relevance:** Core word in judicial delusion. energeian planes = 'working characterized by delusion' -- genitive of quality.

### G1753 — energeia

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G1753-energeia.md`

**Occurrences:** 8

Feminine noun: operative working, power in action. All 8 Pauline. Genitive construction identifies SOURCE/AGENT. 2 Thess 2:9 = Satan's energeia; 2:11 = God sends energeia of delusion. Morally neutral -- names whatever source exerts power.

**Semantic categories:** operative power of God, operative power of Satan/delusion

**Key verses:** 2 Thess 2:9, 2 Thess 2:11, Eph 1:19

**Relevance:** Establishes judicial delusion as active divine operation, not mere permission.

### G3860 — paradidomi

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G3860-paradidomi.md`

**Occurrences:** 136

Verb: hand over, deliver, betray, transmit. Triple paredoken in Rom 1:24,26,28 = progressive judicial abandonment. Same verb for Judas betraying Jesus and Christ giving Himself up.

**Semantic categories:** betrayal, judicial delivery, divine judicial abandonment, sacrificial self-giving, tradition transmission

**Key verses:** Rom 1:24, Rom 1:26, Rom 1:28, Acts 7:42

**Relevance:** Three-fold paradidomi is NT's fullest expression of judicial abandonment. Andrews confirms 'legal and punitive weight.'

### G1209 — dechomai

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G1209-dechomai.md`

**Occurrences:** 68

Middle voice verb: receive, welcome, accept. 2 Thess 2:10 ouk edexanto (Aor Mid Ind 3p) = active refusal. Middle voice emphasizes DELIBERATE choice not to welcome truth. Distinct from lambano (active seizing).

**Semantic categories:** hospitality reception, reception of divine message/truth, physical receiving

**Key verses:** 2 Thess 2:10, Acts 17:11, 1 Thess 2:13

**Relevance:** Establishes truth-rejecters' ACTIVE refusal in 2 Thess 2:10. Not passive failure but deliberate rejection.

### G5237 — hupereidon

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G5237-hupereidō.md`

**Occurrences:** 1

Hapax verb: overlook, look beyond. Sole NT use Acts 17:30 -- God 'winked at' times of ignorance. Deliberate restraint, not approval. Paired with metanoeo: overlook period ends when God commands repentance.

**Semantic categories:** divine forbearance toward sin, graduated accountability boundary

**Key verses:** Acts 17:30

**Relevance:** Critical for graduated accountability. Increased light ends the overlook period. Compare Rom 3:25 paresis.

### G4105 — planao

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G4105-planao.md`

### H7684 — shegagah

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H7684-shegagah.md`

**Occurrences:** 19

Noun: mistake, inadvertent transgression. 19 OT occurrences in Leviticus (8) and Numbers (11). Defines forgivable sin category. Contrasted with beyad ramah in Num 15. Also governs cities of refuge.

**Semantic categories:** sacrificial atonement for inadvertent sin, ignorance vs presumption framework, unintentional manslaughter

**Key verses:** Lev 4:2, Num 15:24-29, Num 35:11

**Relevance:** Central to OT ignorance category. Maps to NT distinction between ignorant sin and willful sin.

### G52 — agnoia

**Occurrences:** 4

Feminine noun: ignorance. From a-privative + nous (mind). In Acts 17:30 tous chronous tes agnoias = 'the times of this ignorance' -- the era God overlooked. Also Acts 3:17 (ignorance of crucifiers). The condition under which God restrains active judgment, per hupereidon.

**Semantic categories:** pre-gospel ignorance, mitigating condition for judgment

**Key verses:** Acts 17:30, Acts 3:17, Eph 4:18, 1 Pet 1:14

**Relevance:** Ignorance is the condition God 'overlooked' (hupereidon). The graduated accountability framework hinges on distinguishing genuine agnoia from culpable ignorance (2 Thess 2:10).
