# Word Studies

## Question
Babylon's fall: doubled epesen, proleptic aorist, Isa 21:9 template, Rev 14:8 / Rev 17-18 relationship, Jer 50-51 verbal parallels, 'in one hour' refrain, millstone imagery, progressive fulfillment.

### G4098 — pipto

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G4098-pipto.md`

**Occurrences:** 93

To fall — used 85x in 10 NT books with heaviest concentration in Revelation (20 uses, 5.0 per 100 verses). Dominant form is aorist active indicative (45 of 93 tokens, 80% are aorist). Semantic categories: prostration in worship, destruction of cities/powers, physical falling, cosmic descent, spiritual/moral falling, judgment falling upon. The 'is fallen, is fallen' formula (Rev 14:8, 18:2) is the paradigmatic prophetic aorist — grammatically past tense, describing Babylon's future fall as accomplished fact.

**Semantic categories:** destruction of cities, prostration, physical fall, cosmic descent, moral falling, judgment

**Key verses:** Rev 14:8, Rev 18:2, Rev 16:19, Rev 17:10, Luk 10:18

**Relevance:** Core verb of the study — epesen is the doubled prophetic aorist announcing Babylon's fall. The V-2AAI-3S morphology is confirmed by parser. The 80% aorist preference marks pipto as a punctiliar-action verb.

### H5307 — naphal

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H5307-naphal.md`

**Occurrences:** 539

To fall — 403 unique verses across 33 OT books. Semantic categories: literal physical fall, death in battle ('fall by the sword'), prostration in worship, judgment/overthrow of nations, lot-casting, causative 'cast down.' The prophetic perfect naphlah naphlah babel (Isa 21:9) is the direct source for Rev 14:8. LXX maps naphal to pipto 239x (60%), establishing the cross-testament bridge.

**Semantic categories:** physical fall, battle death, prostration, judgment/overthrow, lot-casting, causative casting

**Key verses:** Isa 21:9, Jer 51:8, Amos 5:2, Isa 3:8

**Relevance:** Hebrew counterpart in the Isa 21:9 template. Qal Perfect 3fs form (naphlah) is the prophetic perfect — future event described as accomplished past. The doubling (naphlah naphlah) is epizeuxis.

### G2372 — thumos

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G2372-thumos.md`

**Occurrences:** 18

Passion, fierceness, wrath — 18x in NT, 10 in Revelation (56%). From thyo (to rush/breathe hard). Distinguished from orge: thumos = passionate eruption, boiling fury; orge = settled judicial displeasure. In Revelation: divine eschatological fury (14:10, 15:1, 16:1, 19:15), Babylon's corrupting passion (14:8, 18:3), satanic rage (12:12). The 'wine of thumos' collocation is exclusive to Revelation.

**Semantic categories:** divine eschatological fury, corrupting passion, satanic rage, human outburst, vice

**Key verses:** Rev 14:8, Rev 14:10, Rev 16:19, Rev 19:15, Rev 18:3

**Relevance:** Central to the genitive chain in Rev 14:8 and the polarity flip. The thumos of Rev 14:8 is ambiguous — it could be Babylon's passionate corruption or God's responsive wrath. This deliberate ambiguity IS the theological point.

### G3709 — orge

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G3709-orge.md`

**Occurrences:** 36

Wrath, anger — 36x in NT, concentrated in Romans (12) and Revelation (6). Denotes settled judicial displeasure rather than passionate eruption. Combined with thumos in Rev 16:19 and 19:15 for maximal intensity.

**Semantic categories:** divine eschatological wrath, judicial wrath, wrath and law, deliverance from wrath, human vice

**Key verses:** Rev 6:16-17, Rev 11:18, Rev 16:19, Rev 19:15

**Relevance:** The polarity flip endpoint: Rev 16:19 substitutes orge for porneia in the terminal genitive. Combined with thumos in the longest genitive chain (19:15).

### G3631 — oinos

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G3631-oinos.md`

**Occurrences:** 33

Wine — 33 tokens in 25 verses across 9 books. Revelation has 8 uses (highest raw count), concentrated in chapters 14-19. Collocates with thumos (5x) and porneia (3x) exclusively in Revelation's judgment passages. Hebrew source: yayin (H3196, 115 LXX co-occurrences).

**Semantic categories:** literal beverage, parabolic, Babylon's fornication wine, God's wrath wine

**Key verses:** Rev 14:8, Rev 14:10, Rev 16:19, Rev 17:2, Rev 18:3

**Relevance:** Vehicle of both intoxication and judgment. The same wine that Babylon distributes (14:8) becomes the wine God distributes (14:10). The cup that intoxicates IS the cup that judges.

### G4202 — porneia

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G4202-porneia.md`

**Occurrences:** 25

Fornication, harlotry — 25x in NT, 7 in Revelation (highest concentration). From porneuo (G4203). Refers to sexual immorality broadly; figuratively to idolatry. In Revelation, characterizes Babylon's corrupting influence on nations.

**Semantic categories:** sexual immorality, idolatry, Babylon's influence

**Key verses:** Rev 14:8, Rev 17:2, Rev 17:4, Rev 18:3, Rev 19:2

**Relevance:** Terminal genitive in Rev 14:8's chain (tes porneias autes). The polarity flip swaps this for orge in 16:19. The zanah/porneia vocabulary requires prior covenant relationship — Babylon is corrupted faith, not mere paganism.

### G5331 — pharmakeia

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G5331-pharmakeia.md`

**Occurrences:** 3

Sorcery, witchcraft — only 3x in NT (Gal 5:20, Rev 9:21, Rev 18:23). From pharmakon (drug/spell). In LXX renders Hebrew kesheph (H3785). The Nahum 3:4 LXX connection is key: Nineveh as 'mistress of sorceries' who 'selleth nations' → Rev 18:23 'by thy sorceries were all nations deceived.'

**Semantic categories:** sorcery, deception, vice

**Key verses:** Rev 18:23, Gal 5:20, Rev 9:21

**Relevance:** How Babylon deceives nations. The kesheph/pharmakeia vocabulary chain from Nahum 3:4 through LXX to Rev 18:23 shows Babylon inheriting Nineveh's deceptive methods.

### G1831 — exerchomai

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G1831-exerchomai.md`

**Occurrences:** 230

To come out, go forth — 230x in NT. Compound of ek (out of) + erchomai (come/go). Used for physical departure, spirit expulsion, origin, spreading of news, and divine commands to separate from corrupt systems. In Rev 18:4 (exelthate, aorist active imperative) = the separation call from Jer 51:45.

**Semantic categories:** physical departure, spirit expulsion, separation command

**Key verses:** Rev 18:4

**Relevance:** The 'come out' call. Exelthate (V-2AAM-2P, aorist imperative) commands a decisive, complete act of separation from Babylon. Draws from Jer 51:6,45 and Isa 48:20.

### G897 — Babylon

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G897-babylon.md`

**Occurrences:** 11

Babylon — 11 unique NT verses, 12 tokens across 4 books. More than half (6 of 11) in Revelation 14-18 where Babylon functions as prophetic-symbolic entity. From H894 babel/balal (H1101, to confuse). Matthew uses for historical exile; 1 Peter 5:13 debated; Revelation uses symbolically.

**Semantic categories:** historical city, apocalyptic harlot, cup-bearer of wrath, object of final destruction

**Key verses:** Rev 14:8, Rev 16:19, Rev 17:5, Rev 18:2, Rev 18:10, Rev 18:21

**Relevance:** The name itself — from balal (mixture/confusion). The balal etymology establishes mixture/confusion as Babylon's essential character.

### H894 — babel

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H894-babel.md`

**Occurrences:** 264

Babel/Babylon — 264 tokens in 233 unique OT verses across 14 books. Heaviest in Jeremiah (149 uses, 10.9 per 100 verses). From balal (H1101, to confound/mix). Native Akkadian etymology 'gate of the god' (Bab-ilu); Scripture reinterprets polemically as 'confusion' (Gen 11:9).

**Semantic categories:** historical city, empire, object of prophecy

**Key verses:** Gen 10:10, Gen 11:9, Isa 21:9, Jer 51:7-8, Dan 5:30

**Relevance:** The OT foundation for the entire Babylon tradition. Jeremiah 50-51 alone accounts for 57% of OT usage.

### G5610 — hora

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G5610-hora.md`

**Occurrences:** 108

Hour — 108x in NT. Primarily literal hour or appointed time. In Revelation 10 uses (2.5 per 100 verses). The 'in one hour' (mia hora) refrain appears 3x in Rev 18 (vv.10,17,19) as structural marker of sudden judgment.

**Semantic categories:** literal time, appointed time, sudden judgment

**Key verses:** Rev 18:10, Rev 18:17, Rev 18:19, Rev 17:12

**Relevance:** The threefold 'in one hour' refrain structurally ties the three mourning groups. Emphasizes suddenness, not literal chronology.

### G2250 — hemera

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G2250-hemera.md`

**Occurrences:** 388

Day — 365 unique verses in 23 NT books. Used for literal daylight, calendar day, and figuratively for era/epoch. In Rev 18:8 'in one day' (mia hemera) parallels Isa 47:9.

**Semantic categories:** literal day, appointed time, judgment day, era/epoch

**Key verses:** Rev 18:8

**Relevance:** The 'in one day' construction in Rev 18:8 provides the OT-compatible timeframe (Isa 47:9) that the 'in one hour' refrain then compresses further.

### G4204 — porne

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G4204-porne.md`

**Occurrences:** 12

Harlot, whore — 12x in NT, 5 in Revelation. Feminine of pornos (G4205). From pernemi (to sell). Characterizes Babylon in Rev 17:1,5,15,16 and the great whore whose judgment is celebrated in 19:2.

**Semantic categories:** literal prostitute, figurative idolatrous system

**Key verses:** Rev 17:1, Rev 17:5, Rev 17:15-16, Rev 19:2

**Relevance:** Babylon's characterization as porne (harlot) rather than moichalis (adulteress) is significant per Perriman — though the zanah/porneia background from the OT does imply prior covenant relationship.

### H2534 — chemah

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H2534-chemah.md`

**Occurrences:** 133

Fury, wrath, poison — 117 verses, 133 tokens. From yacham (H3179, to be hot). Primary Hebrew equivalent of thumos. Heaviest in Ezekiel (30) and Jeremiah (16). Translated 'fury' (~51%) and 'wrath' (~28%).

**Semantic categories:** divine fury, human rage, poison/venom

**Key verses:** Jer 25:15, Ezek 5:13, Isa 51:17, Nah 1:6

**Relevance:** Hebrew counterpart to thumos (G2372). The cup-of-fury vocabulary chain: kowce chemah (cup of fury, Isa 51:17, Jer 25:15) → oinos tou thumou (wine of wrath, Rev 14:8).

### G3173 — megas

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/G3173-megas.md`

**Occurrences:** 212

Great — 200 unique verses in NT. The title 'Babylon the Great' (Babylon he megale) appears in Rev 14:8, 17:5, 18:2, 18:10, 18:21. Collocates with pipto (10x), forming 'great was the fall.'

**Semantic categories:** physical size, degree, status, importance

**Key verses:** Rev 14:8, Rev 17:5, Rev 18:2, Rev 18:10, Rev 18:21

**Relevance:** Part of Babylon's title — 'the Great.' The megale emphasizes the scale of both the system and its fall.

### G3458 — mylos

**Occurrences:** 4

Millstone — 4x in NT. Rev 18:21 (lithos hos mylinon megan, stone like a great millstone), Rev 18:22 (phone mylou, sound of millstone), Matt 18:6 (mylos onikos, donkey millstone), Mark 9:42 (mylos onikos). All four uses connect to judgment imagery.

**Semantic categories:** judgment instrument, domestic life ceased

**Key verses:** Rev 18:21, Rev 18:22, Matt 18:6, Mark 9:42

**Relevance:** The closing prophetic-action image in Rev 18:21. Connects Jesus' stumbling-block warning (Matt 18:6) to Babylon's judgment. The shift from Jeremiah's eben (stone) to Revelation's mylos (millstone) may intentionally evoke the Jesus tradition.

### H68 — eben

**Source:** `D:/bible/bible-studies/word-studies/H68-eben.md`

**Occurrences:** 315

Stone — 239 verses, 315 tokens across 27 OT books. Covers memorials, covenant tablets, construction, precious stones, stoning, standard weights. In Jer 51:63, the eben bound to the scroll and cast into the Euphrates is the prophetic symbolic action behind Rev 18:21's millstone.

**Semantic categories:** building material, memorial, weapon, precious stone, weight, prophetic symbol

**Key verses:** Jer 51:63, Gen 28:18, Exod 28:17-20

**Relevance:** Jeremiah's eben (stone) tied to the scroll in Jer 51:63-64 is the OT source for Rev 18:21's millstone. The stone sinking into the Euphrates symbolizes Babylon's irreversible fall.

### G1713 — emporos

**Occurrences:** 5

Merchant — 5x in NT (all in Rev 18 except Matt 13:45). Rev 18:3 ('merchants of the earth are waxed rich'), 18:11 ('merchants weep and mourn'), 18:15 ('merchants stand afar off'), 18:23 ('thy merchants were the great men of the earth'), Matt 13:45 ('a merchant man seeking goodly pearls'). Connects to Isa 23:8 LXX where Tyre's traders are emporoi as 'rulers of the earth.'

**Semantic categories:** commercial agent, wealth/economic power

**Key verses:** Rev 18:3, Rev 18:11, Rev 18:15, Rev 18:23

**Relevance:** Key figure in Rev 18 lament. The merchants-as-great-men (megistanes) of the earth (18:23) echoes Isa 23:8 LXX. Babylon's commercial network is a core dimension of her corrupting influence.
